Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-20
Handling Persons in Need of Mental Treatment
Issue Date:06 May 2004Effective Date:07 May 2004
Rescinds:S04-06
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.provides the policy and definitions for handling persons in need of mental treatment.
  • B.outlines the procedures for:
    • 1.handling arrestees in need of mental health evaluation, treatment, or hospitalization.
    • 2.handling persons requiring involuntary admission to a designated mental health intake facility.
    • 3.assisting persons seeking voluntary admission to a mental health intake facility.
    • 4.processing adult arrestees who are on an Unauthorized Absence (UA) from an Illinois Department of Human Services (IDHS) State-Operated Mental Health Center.
    • 5.handling adults who are classified as being a UA from a IDHS State-Operated Mental Health Hospital.
  • C.provides a matrix of roles and responsibilities.
  • D.refers Department members to the Department directive entitled Approved Medical Facilities for the listing of the designated mental health intake facilities and the IDHS state-operated mental health centers.
  • E.states the immunity provision of the IDHS Mental Health and Developmental Disabilities Code (405 ILCS 5).
II.Policy
The Department is committed to the dignified treatment and safety of arrestees and other persons requiring assistance from the Department to obtain mental health evaluation, treatment, or hospitalization.
III.General Information
  • A.Persons requiring mental treatment and transportation by the Department will be transported in a squadrol, when available, consistent with the Department directive entitled "Squadrol Operating Procedures."
  • B.Restraining devices will be used if necessary or required consistent with the procedures established in the Department directive entitled "Restraining Arrestees."
  • C.The Department directive entitled "Approved Medical Facilities" identifies the designated mental health intake facilities and the IDHS state-operated mental health centers for the transportation of arrestees and persons requiring mental health treatment.
    NOTE:
    If the mental health intake facility determines that a person who is not in Department custody is in need of state-operated hospitalization, that mental health intake facility will be responsible for transporting the patient to the IDHS state-operated mental health center.
  • D.A Hospitalization Case Report (CPD-11.406) will be completed and submitted whenever a Department member transports any person for mental treatment and no other case report is required consistent with the Incident Reporting Guide (CPD-63.451).
  • E.A Hospital Run Sheet (CPD-62.420) will be completed and submitted whenever a Department member has contact with any person who is transported to a hospital consistent with the Department directive entitled "Communicable Diseases and Infection Control."
IV.Statement of Law
Immunity Provision of the Mental Health and Developmental Disabilities Code - 405 ILCS 5/6-103
