Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-03-04
District Field Supervisor
Issue Date:27 December 2011Effective Date:03 January 2012
Rescinds:S03-03-01 version dated 08 January 1983
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive identifies responsibilities and duties of field lieutenants, tactical lieutenants, and sergeants assigned to district law enforcement.
II.Basic Responsibilities
  • A.The effectiveness of field supervision is reflected in the performance of subordinates. Supervisors assigned to field duty in district law enforcement will remain cognizant of district crime experience and specific problem areas, coordinate field activities by direction of personnel toward resolving these problems, and ensure the development and maintenance of an aggressive and disciplined patrol force.
  • B.District law enforcement field supervisors are responsible for:
    • 1.mentoring subordinates by helping to develop their knowledge, ability, and confidence to make decisions in conformance with existing policies;
    • 2.guiding and directing their subordinates, ensuring the achievement of basic and directed patrol on matters affecting their duties and responsibilities;
    • 3.taking appropriate action when deficiencies in performance and/or equipment are observed; and
    • 4.responding to incidents requiring a supervisor.
  • C.Incidents requiring supervisory action in the field will be performed by the appropriate district field supervisor. For example, the following incidents require supervisory action:
    • 1.all firearm discharge incidents by sworn members,
    • 2.events requiring the completion and approval of Tactical Response Reports (TRRs)(CPD-11.377),
    • 3.monitoring and reporting motor vehicle pursuits,
    • 4.allegations of impairment of Department members, and
    • 5.traffic crashes involving Department members.
  • D.Field supervisors will provide information to subordinates about available resources and will promote cooperation among members of their command with those of other units or agencies.
III.Duties
  • A.Crime Suppression
    The district field supervisors will:
    • 1.personally tour high level conflict areas and conduct missions as appropriate during their tours of duty;
    • 2.optimize community involvement and respond appropriately to community concerns;
    • 3.communicate the outcomes of directed missions and other enforcement activities to the district commander and executive officer and, when practical, at beat community meetings;
    • 4.use information originating from beat community meetings and logs, the 311 system, and the CLEAR system to monitor and evaluate community concerns.
  • B.Basic Duties
    District Field supervisors will:
    • 1.ensure that action is taken by subordinates against crime and disorder conditions and that the district commander and the executive officer are informed of the actions taken;
    • 2.develop aggressive, preventive patrol by monitoring subordinates’ daily activity and being available for assistance if required;
    • 3.monitor radio dispatch and assignments, observe responses to radio assignments, provide field supervision, and take necessary action to expeditiously reduce radio assignments pending (RAP);
    • 4.ensure excessive time is not being spent on calls for service or case reports and that errors are minimized;
    • 5.review and, if appropriate, approve all case reports generated by members under their supervision before the conclusion of their tour of duty.
    • 6.submit a daily Supervisor’s Management Log (CPD-11.455);
    • 7.participate in roll call activity with emphasis on the training of subordinate personnel;
    • 8.respond to all domestic disturbances and felony-in-progress calls as frequently as possible, giving special attention to the protection and processing of the crime scene;
    • 9.respond to all emergency plans, assuming and retaining command in conformance with existing directives;
    • 10.make the appropriate notifications to CPIC when any significant event occurs within the district in accordance with current Department directives;
    • 11.complete assigned Complaint Register (CR) investigations in a thorough, objective, and expeditious manner;
    • 12.review and, if appropriate, approve requests for search warrants consistent with the Department directive entitled “Search Warrant and Consent to Search incidents”;
      NOTE:
      Search warrants must be approved by members of the rank of lieutenant or above.
    • 13.perform station duties quickly and efficiently and keep time spent in the station to a minimum;
    • 14.conduct check-off roll calls. Field supervisors will inform the station supervisor of officers working beyond the normal tour of duty and any who have not reported off-duty properly.
    • 15.perform all duties as required by law, Department directives, and as directed by the executive officer and the district commander.
  • C.Situational Duties
    The district field supervisors will:
    • 1.respond to and assume immediate command of:
      • a.all emergency plans,
      • b.hostage/barricade/terrorist incidents,
      • c.school violence incidents,
      • d.Homicides,
      • e.Aggravated battery with a firearm incidents,
      • f.mass arrest incidents,
      • g.all other major field incidents that occur in the district.
        NOTE:
        The field supervisor will direct the performance of subordinate personnel consistent with the relevant Department directives or unless relieved by a higher ranking member of the Bureau of Patrol.
    • 2.direct and supervise the investigation when a person is charged with resisting arrest, interfering or obstructing a police officer that involves a physical act between the officer and offender, or assault and/or battery of a police officer, consistent with the Department directive entitled “Use of Force Guidelines;”
      NOTE:
      In instances when a Street Deputy's approval is not required, “Final Approval” status of TRRs will be approved by a member of the rank of lieutenant or above.
    • 3.supervise the district response whenever a police officer discharges a firearm, OC spray, or Taser and submit all reports to the district commander through the appropriate chain of command of the district of occurrence before the end of the tour of duty;
    • 4.initiate a CR log investigation and consult with the Bureau of Internal Affairs when there’s an allegation of criminal conduct by a Department member;
      NOTE:
      The field supervisor responsibility in these matters will terminate upon completion of the investigation, regardless of the hour. The responsibility will not be delegated to a subordinate or turned over to another field supervisor unless directed to do so by the Chief, Bureau of Internal Affairs, or higher authority.
    • 5.respond to and direct the protection of the scene of any incident involving Department personnel, whether those members were on or off-duty, when:
      • a.a Department member discharges a firearm,
      • b.gunfire is directed at the police,
      • c.a police officer is shot,
      • d.a person is shot or injured by a police officer.
    • 6.ensure TRRs are properly completed and forwarded for approval, consistent with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Use of Force Guidelines”;
    • 7.conform with all investigative guidelines contained in Department directives regarding complaint, disciplinary, and summary punishment procedures when allegations of impairment involving the operation of a vehicle, whether on or off duty;
    • 8.notify the OEMC operations manager regarding problems associated with dispatch and/or current field operational demands;
    • 9.in the event of conflict between supervisors of equal rank, request the matter be resolved by a higher ranking member within their chain of command or the on-duty Street Deputy, Street Operations.
IV.Conflict Resolution
If this directive conflicts with a provision in any other Department directive relative to field supervisory duties, responsibilities, notifications, or required approvals, members will seek guidance from the Chief, Bureau of Patrol.
