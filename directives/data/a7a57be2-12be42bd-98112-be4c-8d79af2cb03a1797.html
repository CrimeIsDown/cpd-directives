Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S11-06-01
Peak Performance Driver Training Program
Issue Date:25 September 1998Effective Date:26 September 1998
Rescinds:S98-07
Index Category:Training
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.revises the Peak Performance Driver Training Program conducted by the Traffic Safety and Training Unit, Traffic Section.
  • B.details the five different types of training classes offered by the Peak Performance Driver Training Program, the criteria for participation in each class, and the procedures relating to attendance.
    • 1.Recruit Training.
    • 2.Phase II In-Service.
    • 3.One Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class.
    • 4.Two Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class.
    • 5.Specialty Vehicle Training.
II.Recruit Training
This training program will:
  • A.consist of a four day training course which will combine classroom instruction with hands-on driver training. Sessions will be held from 0645-1500 hours each day.
  • B.introduce recruits to safe operating procedures while driving squad cars and squadrols, review the dynamics of safe driving strategies, and develop driving skills and accident avoidance techniques.
  • C.schedule classes in conjunction with the Education and Training Division.
III.Phase II In-Service
This training program will:
  • A.be attended by sworn Bureau of Patrol members with two to ten years of service.
  • B.be scheduled by unit / district commanders.
  • C.be a one day training program designed to reinforce the safe driving strategies, skills and accident avoidance techniques originally demonstrated in the Recruit Training and Phase I training programs. Practical exercises will be designed to demonstrate driver and vehicle limitations. Additional emphasis will be placed on the mental aspect of driving, as well as the benefits and necessity of the occupant restraint systems.
  • D.emphasize the goal of reducing Department crashes and minimizing injuries to Department members.
IV.One Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class
This training program will:
  • A.require attendance by sworn members who have been in a preventable Department traffic crash, previously attended the recruit training program, and are identified by either their commanding officer or the traffic safety officer as someone who would benefit from re-training (as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Traffic Crashes Involving Department Members.")
  • B.target members whose accidents occur during emergency response situations or whose driving habits place them in jeopardy of serious injury.
  • C.consist of a one day intensive training session which will be limited to small groups. Classes will consist of both classroom discussion and driving exercises. Analyses of student accidents or poor driving habits will be a key component of classroom instruction. Practical exercises will be designed to demonstrate driver and vehicle limitations. The benefits and necessity of the occupant restraint systems will be emphasized.
  • D.emphasize the goal of reducing Department crashes and minimizing injuries to Department members.
  • E.schedule courses as directed by the Traffic Safety Unit. Traffic Safety personnel will notify units whose members are to attend and will provide available training dates. Units will be required to contact the Peak Performance office to reserve spaces for a particular date.
V.Two Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class
This training program will:
  • A.require attendance by sworn members who have been involved in a preventable Department traffic crash and have not attended any Peak Performance Driver Training Programs.
  • B.consist of a two day course which will combine classroom instruction with hands-on driver training. Sessions will be held from 0645-1500 hours each day.
  • C.present to officers the safe driving strategies, driving skills, and traffic crash avoidance techniques that officers will find useful in avoiding future traffic crashes. Analyses of officers' traffic crashes will be a key feature of this class.
  • D.schedule courses as directed by the Traffic Safety and Training Unit. Traffic Safety personnel will notify units whose members are to attend and will provide available training dates. Units will be required to contact the Peak Performance office to reserve space for a particular class.
VI.Specialty Vehicle Training
The following one day training programs are available:
  • A.PAPV-Introduction to the proper driving methods, maintenance, and limitations of the Police All Purpose Vehicle (PAPV). Members must have the newer style, DOT approved, motorcycle helmet for training. Members can obtain the DOT approved helmet in exchange for their old one at the Equipment and Supply Section during normal business hours.
    NOTE:
    Unit commanders may contact the Peak Performance office on an as-needed basis in order to schedule an officer for training.
  • B.Commercial Drivers License (CDL)-third party certification for Department members who need a CDL "C" class license with the "Passenger" endorsement in the performance of their duties.
    • 1.Unit commanders may contact the Peak Performance Office on an as-needed basis in order to schedule an officer for training.
    • 2.Prior to training, officers must have successfully completed the written examination for the "C" class with the "Passenger" endorsement administered at the Secretary of State's Office.
VII.Scheduling
All programs will:
  • A.be presented at the training site designated by the Peak Performance Driver Training Program.
  • B.schedule classes from 0700-1500 hours under normal circumstances. Assigned members will report to the Area 5 auditorium at 0645 hours for roll call and inspection. Members are to report in the prescribed seasonal uniform with baton and helmet. Members should also be prepared for inclement weather. It will be the responsibility of a scheduled member to report a pending inability to attend a training session to his unit of assignment, so that notification to the Peak Performance Office can be made and a substitute member assigned to the training session.
  • C.be conducted by the Traffic Safety and Training Unit. The commanding officer of the Traffic Safety and Training Unit will ensure that attending members driving records reflect the classes attended, and the Education and Training Division is provided with the completed class cards.
VIII.General Information
  • A.Unit / district commanders will designate members to attend the Phase II training according to this directive. In the event a member scheduled to attend a training session is unable to do so due to illness, subpoena, etc., the unit / district commander will attempt to schedule a substitute member.
  • B.Unit / district commanders will contact the Peak Performance Office and reserve training slots for the One Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class, the Two Day Preventable Traffic Crash Class, and the Specialty Vehicle Training Class.
  • C.Unit / district commanders will ensure that members are not scheduled for training programs which conflict with a court appearance and/or jury duty requirements.
  • D.Parking will be available in the rear parking lot of Area 5 for all scheduled members.
  • E.Assigned members will enter the Area 5 Headquarters through the rear entrance door from the parking lot and will proceed to the Area 5 auditorium located on the first floor.
IX.Records
  • A.The Commanding Officer,Traffic Safety and Training Unit will report absences from the scheduled units / districts on a biweekly basis to the deputy superintendent of the member's respective bureau or to the Commander, Office of the Superintendent, if assigned to a unit within the Office of the Superintendent.
  • B.The Commanding Officer, Traffic Safety and Training Unit will be responsible for ensuring that member's driving records reflect courses attended.
  • C.The Education and Training Division will retain the class attendance cards completed by attending members and will include this information on member's training records.
