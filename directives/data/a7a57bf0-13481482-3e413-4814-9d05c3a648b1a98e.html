Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-03-03
District Station Supervisor
Issue Date:27 December 2011Effective Date:03 January 2012
Rescinds:S03-03-02 versions dated 26 September 1979
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the function of the district station supervisor.
  • B.defines the duties and responsibilities of the district station supervisor.
  • C.discontinues the role of the district watch commander.
  • D.discontinues the role of the district manager.
  • E.discontinues the role of the desk sergeant.
  • F.discontinues the Watch Commander’s Log [CPD-21.916 (Rev. 6/01)] and introduces the Watch Incident Log [CPD-21.916 (Rev 12/11)].
II.Authority
The district station supervisor has the authority to manage in-station operations, including personnel and material resources, and direct the work of watch supervisory personnel consistent with plans and strategies established by the district commander.
III.Accountability
District station supervisors are accountable for the enforcement of all laws and ordinances and the conduct and appearance of all on-duty subordinate personnel.
IV.General Duties
To efficiently manage Department personnel and resources, each district station supervisor will:
  • A.prepare the daily schedule assignment line-ups, post worksheets, and ensure they are submitted via the Police Computer Aided Dispatch (PCAD) system to the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) one hour prior to each roll call;
    NOTE:
    District station supervisors will submit the watch assignment line-ups via facsimile message only if the PCAD system is inoperable.
  • B.conduct roll calls for all watch personnel, unless other priority duties demand their presence elsewhere. In such instances, another supervisor will perform these functions. The roll call procedure will consist of:
    • 1.taking attendance and designating assignments;
    • 2.reviewing and approving the watch Attendance and Assignment Record (CPD-11.605);
    • 3.inspecting personnel, noting deficiencies, and taking appropriate corrective action;
    • 4.notifying watch personnel of court dates, scheduled training sessions, and beat team and community meetings;
    • 5.reviewing newly released Department directives, viewing streaming videos, and the delivery of other training as designated by the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division;
      NOTE:
      The district station supervisor will ensure complete and accurate entry of such viewing or training into the Office Automation Application training records for the participating members.
  • C.ensure that personnel assigned during irregular hours receive proper roll call notifications, exchanges of information and training, and properly report on and off duty;
  • D.ensure compliance with policy and procedures regarding arrestee processing and booking, including but not limited to:
    • 1.reviewing each arrest situation to determine the propriety of the charge and proper indication of initial approval of the probable cause;
    • 2.ensuring that the Cook County Felony Review Unit of the State’s Attorney’s Office and other state or federal agencies are notified when appropriate;
    • 3.request felony review override consistent with the Department directive entitled “Felony Review by Cook County State’s Attorney”;
    • 4.ensuring that vehicles subject to police action (tow, impoundment, seizure) are processed in accordance with Department procedures;
    • 5.monitoring the status of prisoners who have not been cleared by the Identification Section and requesting that particular background checks be expedited in accordance with established procedures;
    • 6.indicating the final approval of charges against the accused when the booking process has been completed.
  • E.ensure compliance with policy and procedure regarding bail bonding. The district station supervisors’ approval indicates that:
    • 1.as best as can be determined, the arrestee is not violating conditions of any bond or on parole;
    • 2.upgrades to felony charges, when authorized by law, have been sought;
    • 3.the Identification Record (IR) number recorded on the Arrest Report corresponds to that imprinted on the arrestee’s criminal history record;
    • 4.all investigative alerts and warrants have been addressed; and
    • 5.complaints are attached to the arrest report.
  • F.ensure field supervisors approve all case reports by regularly auditing those reports pending approval in the electronic reporting application.
  • G.manage district facility operations including the district desk and lock-up and:
    • 1.ensure that Department-issued equipment, such as radios, shotguns, vehicles, etc., is properly maintained, accounted for, and kept in good working order;
    • 2.conduct daily informal inspections, thereby ensuring that station facilities, grounds, and equipment are kept in good order and the security of the physical plant is maintained.
  • H.ensure the accuracy of information recorded on court and Illinois Traffic Crash transmittals, particularly with respect to IR numbers and Record Division (RD) numbers;
  • I.prepare a Watch Incident Log summarizing all important incidents that occurred during the tour of duty;
    NOTE:
    This log will be a continuous record of important incidents during a 24-hour period. A new log sheet will be prepared by each station supervisor and will be forwarded to the relieving station supervisor. All required notifications will be made in addition to completing the Watch Incident Log.
  • J.accessing their general district email inbox, at minimum, three times during their tours of duty; the first time during the first hour and the third time during the last hour of the tour;
  • K.completion of personnel evaluations consistent with the Department directive entitled “Performance Evaluation System”;
  • L.review and approve the appropriate CLEAR watch management applications including, but not limited to, the automated:
    • 1.Daily Attendance and Assignment Record;
    • 2.Medical System;
    • 3.Message In-Box.
  • M.acknowledge excellent performance and recommend appropriate recognition for outstanding achievements;
  • N.ensure the continuity of operations between watches by advising the oncoming station supervisor of the district conditions and problems that need to be addressed by the relieving station supervisor;
  • O.conduct lock-up inspections as directed;
  • P.maintain the district file for search warrants;
  • Q.perform all duties as required by law, Department directives, and as directed by the executive officer and the district commander.
V.Situational Duties
District station supervisor responsibilities include, but are not limited to:
  • A.conducting investigations and reporting unusual occurrences that take place in the lockup, consistent with the Department directive entitled “Processing Persons under Department Control”;
  • B.notifying CPIC, the area deputy chief, the district commander, and the executive officer as necessary and consistent with Department directives;
  • C.equitably dealing with personnel matters (including grievances) brought to their attention;
  • D.the final approval of Tactical Response Reports for those incidents that do not require approval by the Street Deputy, Street Operations; and
    NOTE:
    If the station supervisor is a sergeant, the station supervisor will forward the TRR to the tactical lieutenant or executive officer for final approval.
  • E.the performance of other duties as required by Department directives, the district commander, or the district executive officer.
VI.CONFLICT RESOLUTION
If this directive conflicts with a provision in any other Department directive relative to duties, responsibilities, notifications, or required approvals, members will seek guidance from the Chief, Bureau of Patrol.
