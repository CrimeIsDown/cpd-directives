Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-09
Application for Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 15 April 2004; D04-20
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.delineates the eligibility requirements, application procedures, and selection process for Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician.
  • B.introduces the Applicant Questionnaire for Evidence Technician (CPD-62.194).
II.Assignments and Duties
  • A.Police Officers Assigned as Evidence Technicians report to the Evidence Technicians Unit, Forensic Services Division, and are assigned to:
    • 1.Evidence Technician Team South, Unit 477 or
    • 2.Evidence Technician Team North, Unit 377
  • B.Police Officers Assigned as Evidence Technicians perform the following duties:
    • 1.search for, collect, mark, photograph, and preserve evidence and fingerprints at crime scenes.
    • 2.use cameras, fingerprint powders, brushes, and other tools to process crime scenes.
    • 3.conduct Breathalyzer tests.
    • 4.photograph and fingerprint hospitalized arrestees.
    • 5.photograph lineups.
    • 6.photograph arrestees or personnel relative to any allegations of battery caused by a police officer to the arrestee or battery to a police officer.
    • 7.perform other duties as required by directive or supervisory personnel.
III.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, members who are interested in applying for the position of Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician must:
  • A.be a sworn member below the rank of sergeant.
  • B.have a minimum of three years of continuous service as a sworn member with the Chicago Police Department.
  • C.be able to perform the duties and assignments of a Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • D.be able and willing to work any watch assignment.
  • E.be willing to change furlough to attend training.
  • F.have received performance ratings above the minimum rating score necessary to qualify for a step increase as defined in the Department directive entitled "Performance Evaluation System" for each of their last four performance evaluations.
  • G.have an acceptable disciplinary record. The candidate’s disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (CR) numbers for misconduct resulting in a suspension of more than ten days or a record of three or more sustained CR numbers resulting in suspensions during the past five years.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR numbers with findings of "No Disciplinary Action" will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances concerning discipline will only be allowed to apply for Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician and their application will be considered if the candidate’s disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of a successful grievance such that the outcome of the grievance results in a disciplinary record that meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • H.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago at the time of application.
  • I.if currently appointed to a D-2A grade, be willing to forgo D-2A status and pay and accept D-2 status and pay of a Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician.
IV.Application Procedures
  • A.Interested members must complete the Applicant Questionnaire for Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician attached to this directive and it must be received in the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, by the determined deadline.
    NOTE:
    Incomplete applications or applications received after the deadline will NOT be processed.
  • B.The self-addressed application receipt will be returned to the applicant within ten days of receipt by the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section.
    NOTE:
    Any member who does not receive a receipt should call the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section.
V.Selection Process
  • A.The selection process will consist of a multiple choice examination based on selected reading materials.
    NOTE:
    Before the posted date of the examination a reading list will be provided to applicants meeting the eligibility requirements for Police Officer Assigned as an Evidence Technician.
  • B.During the commencement of the application process, The Commander, Forensic Services Division will disseminate, via Administrative Message Center (AMC) message, the date, time and location for eligible candidates to report for the multiple-choice examination.
    NOTE:
    Members will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled for the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will excuse a member who has been scheduled for testing during duty hours, provided that the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor was notified by the affected member at least forty-eight hours prior to the testing.
  • C.Applicants will be placed on the eligibility list in order of their scores on the written multiple-choice examination.
    NOTE:
    In the event two or more applicants have identical final scores, seniority will be utilized as the tie breaker.
  • D.Management reserves the right to fill 20% of the training program vacancies at its discretion without regard to score, provided that the vacancy is filled with a candidate from the eligibility list.
VI.Training Program
  • A.Based upon operational needs candidates will be selected off the eligibility list to attend a designated training program consisting of two (2) phases.
  • B.Selection for training will not constitute a promotion to Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician.
  • C.Phase One of the training program will be conducted at the Education and Training Division.
    • 1.Selected candidates will be notified of the date and time of the training program.
    • 2.Upon successful completion of the Phase One of the training program, candidates must pass a proficiency test which measures the application of principles taught during Phase One before advancing to Phase Two.
  • D.Phase Two of the training program consists of a 90 day probationary period of on-the-job training.
    • 1.Only candidates who pass the proficiency test from Phase One of the training program will advance to Phase Two.
    • 2.Candidates will be detailed to the Forensic Services Division for Phase Two of the training program.
    NOTE:
    If the probationary period is interrupted for any reason, the probationary period will be suspended until the member resumes the duties related to training and probation.
  • E.The on-the-job performance will be assessed by supervisory personnel from the Forensic Services Division who will evaluate the practical application of the theories, principles, and techniques taught.
VII.Additional Conditions
  • A.Candidates selected for training must pass a drug screen test, prior to the start of the training program for Police Officers Assigned as Evidence Technicians.
  • B.At the time of selection to begin the training program, selected members must be in full duty status.
VIII.Assignment as Evidence Technician
  • A.Upon successful completion of both phases of the training and consistent with the requirements in Item VI of this directive, candidates will be promoted to Police Officers Assigned as Evidence Technicians.
  • B.A candidate selected as a Police Officer Assigned as Evidence Technician will be removed from their assignment if at any time they exhibit problematic behavior, demonstrate a lack of knowledge, skills or abilities necessary for the job, or is unable to perform the duties of the position
