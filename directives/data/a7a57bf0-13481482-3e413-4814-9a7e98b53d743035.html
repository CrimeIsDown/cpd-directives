Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-03-02
District Executive Officer
Issue Date:27 December 2011Effective Date:03 January 2012
Rescinds:S03-03-03 version dated 01 August 2011; S03-03-04 version dated 11 May 2004
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive introduces and defines the functions of the district executive officer (XO).
II.General Information
  • A.Executive officers are second in command of the district to which they are assigned.
    NOTE:
    The executive officer will not perform substantially all of the duties or assume substantially all the responsibilities of a commander without the prior approval of an appropriate deputy chief or Chief, Bureau of Patrol.
  • B.Actions performed by the executive officer in the absence of the commander will be given the same weight as if they had been performed by the district commander.
  • C.The executive officer will normally not work the same duty hours as the district commander.
  • D.The primary, but not exclusive, responsibility of the executive officer is to assist the district commander in areas of operations, administration, planning, identification of emerging crime trends, and preparing strategic plans to address crime and disorder issues impacting the community.
III.Duties and Responsibilities
Duties and responsibilities of executive officers assigned to District Law Enforcement include but are not limited to:
  • A.executing the policies and strategies designed to address crime and disorder matters in their district;
  • B.ensuring the tactical lieutenant assigns tactical personnel on daily mission-driven assignments to support specific prevention strategies;
  • C.ensuring that the district station supervisor or district field supervisor assigns field personnel consistent with the district strategic mission;
  • D.assigning supervisors the responsibility to inspect lock-up facilities a minimum of four times per tour of duty;
  • E.supervising district sworn and civilian members;
  • F.monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of crime and disorder suppression policies and strategies;
  • G.recommending adjustments/changes to policies and strategies;
  • H.monitoring, analyzing, and developing strategies to manage:
    • 1.overtime,
    • 2.elective use of time,
    • 3.balancing day-off groups,
    • 4.medical roll use,
    • 5.Department vehicle crashes,
    • 6.court deviations, and
    • 7.complaint and disciplinary proceedings.
  • I.ensuring daily work sheets and deployment of district personnel are consistent with the overall strategic mission of the district;
    • 1.The executive officer will normally assign a lieutenant to serve as the station supervisor.
    • 2.If a lieutenant is not available, the executive officer will designate a sergeant to serve as the station supervisor.
  • J.monitoring the electronic case reporting application to ensure that field supervisors and station supervisors are fulfilling their responsibility for the approval of pending reports.
  • K.approving period work sheets, motor vehicle pursuits, and traffic crash investigations involving Department members;
  • L.optimizing community involvement and responding appropriately to community priority concerns;
  • M.supervising the district CAPS office;
  • N.ensuring effective utilization of available technology, including the CLEAR system, to analyze and disseminate information and to monitor the activity of field units;
  • O.overseeing the operational and administrative functions of the district;
  • P.performing duties as directed by the district commander.
IV.Situational Duties
The district executive officers will:
  • A.assign and monitor the progress of the complaint register investigations assigned to supervisors and ensure that the investigations are completed in a timely manner;
  • B.conduct complaint register investigations as directed by the district commander;
  • C.evaluate district station supervisors and others as determined by the district commander via the Performance Evaluation System;
  • D.participate in the development of Individualized Performance Plans (CPD-62.217) for members who are identified for placement in the Behavioral Intervention System or Personnel Concerns Program, consistent with the Department directives entitled “Behavioral Intervention System” and “Personnel Concerns Program.”
V.Conflict Resolution
If this directive conflicts with a provision in any other Department directive relative to the duties, responsibilities, notifications, or required approvals, members will seek guidance from the Chief, Bureau of Patrol.
