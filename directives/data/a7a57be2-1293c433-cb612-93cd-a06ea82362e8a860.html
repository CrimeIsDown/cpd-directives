Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S07-02
Firearms Taken Into Custody or Turned In
Issue Date:30 May 1989Effective Date:31 May 1989
Rescinds:G89-06
Index Category:Processing Property
I.Purpose
This directive continues procedures for:
  • A.aiding citizens in registering their firearms.
  • B.disposing of firearms that are in Department custody.
Department members will refer to the General Order titled "Firearms Taken Into Custody or Turned In" for recording and processing firearms taken into custody

  • A.When an applicant contacts the Department by telephone to register or renew the registration of a firearm

  • B.When an applicant appears in person at a police district, desk personnel will:
    • 1.provide the applicant with a Firearms Registration Application (CPD-31.562) for each firearm to be registered.
    • 2.instruct the applicant to complete the form in its entirety and follow the instructions on the reverse side of the form.
    • 3.direct the applicant to either personally deliver or utilize the US Postal Service to deliver completed, NOTARIZED Firearms Registration Applications and required attachments to the Gun Registration Section.
  • C.Peace officers who are residents of the City of Chicago are exempt from the registration and renewal fees when registering authorized, duty-related firearms. An affidavit verifying such employment must accompany the application in order to qualify for the fee waiver. The Affidavit of Employment forms (CPD-31.563) are available at the Gun Registration Section.
  • D.Security personnel registering personally-owned, duty-related firearms that are newly acquired must apply in person at the Gun Registration Section with the proper credentials.
  • E.Members will remind applicants that registration of a firearm is not a permit or license to carry the weapon.

  • B.When a determination is made that the firearm or related property is no longer needed for evidence or further investigation, the legal owner may be entitled to the return of that property. Two methods are authorized to ensure the return of a firearm to its legal owner:
    • 1.by order of court (most efficient and correct method of turnover) in accordance with the provisions of the Department directive entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken Into Custody."
    • 2.by issuance of a Property Release Order form (CPD-34.554) when a court order cannot be obtained.
  • C.A sworn command staff member may authorize the return of a firearm to its legal owner after ensuring that a Property Release order has been prepared and all of the following conditions are fulfilled:
    • 1.That an Illinois resident to whom he is returning a firearm has in his possession a valid Firearm Owners Identification card or proof of his exempt status under the statute. The owner's identification card number will be printed in the "Illinois" space provided on the Property Release Order form. If the person to whom the weapon is being returned is exempt, print "Exempt" in that space. If the Illinois resident is also a Chicago resident, include the City firearms registration number in the "Chicago" space.
    • 2.That a non-Illinois resident to whom he is returning a weapon has satisfactory proof of such non-residency. In such case, the word "non-resident" will be printed in the "Chicago" space provided on the Property Release Order form.
    • 3.The firearm is not contraband.
    • 4.The firearm has been cleared by the Firearm / Toolmarks Unit.
    • 5.A court of law is not expected to determine the disposition of the firearm.
    • 6.A name check is made of the owner to determine if there are any outstanding warrants or criminal history which would preclude the individual from legally possessing a firearm.
  • D.Resident owners MUST present their Illinois Firearm Owners Identification card AND, if applicable, the City Firearm Registration card when claiming their weapon at the Evidence and Recovered Property Section. However, if an individual presents a judge's order releasing firearms listed on a Property Inventory, this judicial order must be honored. A Department member who releases an unregistered weapon per a judge's order will inform the person taking receipt of the unregistered firearm that it must be taken out of the city or otherwise lawfully disposed of.
