Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E06-04-03
Notification of Death or Hospitalization of Members: Non-Duty-Related, Retired Members, and Family Members
Issue Date:03 August 2004Effective Date:04 August 2004
Rescinds:S04-10-03
Index Category:Personnel Support
I.Purpose
This directive outlines responsibilities of Department members receiving notification of the hospitalization or death of active members (non-duty-related), retired members, family members of active members, and the spouses of retired members.
II.Responsibilities
  • A.Department notifications for deceased active members or the death of an active Department member’s immediate family member will be taken by:
    • 1.the member’s unit of assignment or;
    • 2.the Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) Center.
      NOTE:
      A Department member receiving a notification concerning the death or hospitalization of an on-duty Department member will immediately notify Operations Command and follow the procedures outlined in this directive.
  • B.A Department member receiving a notification concerning the non-duty-related death or hospitalization of an active Department member or the death of an active Department member’s immediate family will refer the caller to the member’s unit of assignment.
    • 1.When the member’s unit of assignment is not known, the Department member will attempt to identify the unit of assignment by reviewing the "Alpha Listing" or contacting the Human Resources Division.
    • 2.If the member’s unit of assignment is not operational or cannot be determined at the time of the notification, the Department member will take the notification and forward the information to the AMFN Center. The AMFN Center will process the notification as outlined in this directive.
  • C.Department members receiving a death or hospitalization notification will:
    • 1.complete either a Death Notification Facsimile Message (CPD11.490K) form or a Hospitalization Facsimile Message (CPD.11.490D) form as required.
    • 2.submit the appropriate facsimile message form to their unit commanding officer or watch commander for approval and signature.
    • 3.transmit the approved facsimile message form to the AMFN Center by fax.
      NOTE:
      The employment of active members must be verified by the AMFN Center.
  • D.A Department member receiving a notification concerning the death of a retired member or the death of a retired member’s spouse will furnish the caller with the AMFN Center’s telephone number. If the member’s phone has transfer capabilities, the caller will be transferred.
  • E.The AMFN Center will:
    • 1.send a facsimile message for Citywide transmission over the AMFN upon receipt of a Death Notification or Hospitalization Facsimile Message form regarding an active Department member.
    • 2.send a facsimile message for Citywide transmission over the AMFN upon receipt of a verified Death Notification Facsimile Message form for a:
      • a.Department member’s immediate family;
      • b.retired Department member;
      • c.retired member’s spouse.
        NOTE:
        Members will attempt to obtain and include in the facsimile message the date time and location of the Saint Jude Police League service, if applicable.
    • 3.notify the Chaplains Section and provide the wake information, if requested.
    • 4.forward a notice to the Office of News Affairs for inclusion of the notification in the Daily Bulletin concerning the day, time, and location of the wake, funeral services, and, if requested, the Saint Jude Police League service.
  • F.The Special Activities Section will:
    • 1.verify the completed Death Notification form of a retired member or the retired member’s spouse with the Payroll and Purchasing Section of the Finance Division or the Pension Board.
    • 2.inform the AMFN Center that the information on the retired member or the retired member’s spouse Death Notification form has been verified.
III.Other Responsibilities
  • A.In the event of the death or serious injury to a member, the Director, Office of News Affairs or a member specifically designated by the Superintendent, is solely responsible for the dissemination of information to the news media. No other member will respond to media inquiries unless directed to do so by the Superintendent.
  • B.When a Department member dies in a non-duty-related incident, the member’s unit commanding officer will:
    • 1.arrange to obtain all Department property from the deceased member’s family and forward all non-unit Department property to the Human Resources Division.
    • 2.ensure that the personal property of the deceased member is returned to the family.
