Chicago Police DepartmentGeneral Order G04-09
Communicable Diseases and Infection Control
Issue Date:06 November 2014Effective Date:06 November 2014
Rescinds:07 August 1995 Version
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes Department policy relating to infectious materials and disease control.
  • B.establishes the Department's Exposure Control Plan.
  • C.provides definitions pertaining to the Department's program.
II.Policy
It is the policy of the Department to:
  • A.provide information and safeguards for the health and safety of the public and Department members.
  • B.comply with the Bloodborne Pathogens Standard, 29 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) 1910.1030, relating to occupational exposure to blood or other potentially infectious materials.
III.General Information
  • A.The Department recognizes the potential exposure of its employees to communicable diseases in the performance of their assigned duties.
  • B.Because of the unique risks and conditions within the Forensic Services Section and the Medical Services Section, these units will prepare a unit exposure control plan.
IV.Diseases
The descriptions of the following diseases do not by any means exclude other contagious diseases from the coverage of this directive.
  • A.Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) - A condition that may result after an infection with the HIV virus.
  • B.Ebola Virus (EBOV) - a virus that causes a severe and often fatal hemorraghic fever.
  • C.Hepatitis B Virus (HBV) - A virus that causes an infection and inflammation of the liver.
  • D.Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) - The virus that attacks the body's immune system causing the disease known as Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (AIDS).
  • E.Meningitis - An acute bacterial infection of the brain and spinal cord associated with fever, severe headache, nausea, vomiting and coma.
  • F.Tuberculosis - A communicable disease caused by a bacteria causing lesions in the lungs, bones and other body parts. Tuberculosis may be contracted through respiratory secretions from the respiratory tract.
V.Exposure Control Plan
Specific information regarding the Department's Exposure Control Plan which includes:
  • A.exposure determination/universal precautions; the use of the Hospital Run Sheet (CPD-62.420) and the Report of Exposure to Communicable Disease/Hazardous Material (CPD-62.418); and post-exposure procedures may be found in the addenda to this directive.
  • B.communicable disease training may be found in the Department directive entitled "Communicable Disease Training."
  • C.Hepatitis B Vaccinations may be found in the Department directive entitled "Hepatitis B Vaccination."
