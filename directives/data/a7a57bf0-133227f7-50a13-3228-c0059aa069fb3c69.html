Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D11-14
Annual Watch and Furlough Selections, Vacation Schedules, and 2012 Operations Calendar
Issue Date:22 February 2012Effective Date:22 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 20 October 2011
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members:
    • 1.there will be a three day "adjustment" period between the 13th police period, 2011 and the 1st police period, 2012: 5 – 7 January 2012.
    • 2.police periods will no longer begin on Thursdays; each police period will begin on a Sunday.
    • 3.of the 2012 watch selection process.
    • 4.of the 2012 furlough selection process.
  • B.continues the annual bidding process for watch assignments.
  • C.continues procedures for bidding as established in the collective bargaining agreements.
    • 1.FOP: 80 percent bid and 20 percent management rights.
    • 2.PBPA, Sergeants: 5 bid positions per each of the 3 primary watches in district law enforcement.
    • 3.PBPA, Lieutenants: 1 bid position per each of the 3 primary watches in district law enforcement.
  • D.establishes the Chicago Police - 2012 Operations Calendar (CPD-11.143).
  • E.establishes dates for the annual furlough periods.
  • F.continues the practice of utilizing reverse seniority to fill positions as necessary.
II.General Information
  • A.In accordance with collective bargaining agreements, the watch selection process will precede the furlough selection process for all sworn ranks.
  • B.The granting of requests for steady watch selection and furlough selection will be the responsibility of the district commander or unit commanding officer, consistent with the operational needs of the district or unit and the applicable provisions of collective bargaining agreements.
  • C.Department members, districts, and units subject to the steady watch-selection bidding and furlough selection are identified in the existing collective bargaining agreements.
  • D.The annual steady watch and furlough selection process for civilian members with job title codes identified in the existing collective bargaining agreements will be conducted simultaneously and with the same procedures as described and set forth for the sworn members of the Department in accordance with this directive and the Department directives entitled “Furlough and Vacation” and “Steady Watch Selection and Assignment.”
  • E.Probationary police officers assigned or detailed to a unit with less than 12 months of service are not considered part of the unit complement for watch or furlough selection. Probationary police officers with more than 12 months of service are considered part of the unit complement for furlough selection only.
  • F.Department members detailed to a district or unit for twenty-eight days or more before the date of the annual steady-watch selection (on or before 13 October 2011) or furlough selection (on or before 27 October 2011) will select a watch or furlough within the unit of detail.
    NOTE:
    This does not apply to probationary police officers with 12 or more months of service who will participate in the furlough selection process within their unit of detail at the time of selection.
  • G.District/unit administrative personnel can obtain Department members' information for labels to attach to the appropriate forms from the Bureau of Patrol intranet website at "On-line Reports."
III.Watch Selection Requests Form and Seniority Number
  • A.Members submitting the Watch Selection Request form (CPD-11.629) will not fill in the "Seniority No." box.
  • B.The unit administrative staff assigned to process the watch selection requests will annotate the seniority number on the subject form with information from their unit's respective 12th police period seniority listing.
    NOTE:
    On 10 November 2011, the designated unit administrative staff will print unit seniority listing via the Department intranet: Business Objects, Public Folders, Data Warehouse Reports, Personnel Reports.
IV.Steady Watch Selection
  • A.The annual steady watch selection will be conducted per the appropriate collective bargaining agreement and the Department directive entitled “Steady Watch Selection and Assignment.”
  • B.The following police officers are not subject to the bid process for steady watch assignments: police officers assigned to district tactical teams, foot patrol in districts 001 and 018, the immediate staff of each exempt member, and District Law Enforcement community policing officers (including senior services officers). The number of excluded staff positions, per the subject collective bargaining agreement, is limited to two staff members and the number of excluded community policing officers is limited to seven; five on the second watch and two on the third watch.
V.Watch and Day Off Group Assignment for Sworn Non-Exempt Members
  • A.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.allocate the unit’s manpower between the watches in a manner that best addresses operational needs.
    • 2.ensure the day-off groups are balanced.
  • B.The watch selection process will begin on 10 November 2011 for all sworn members in all units of the Department; the watch selection requests must be submitted on or before 17 November 2011.
    NOTE:
    All watch selection requests will be submitted to unit commanding officers on the Watch Selection Request form (CPD-11.629).
  • C.The most senior captain assigned to a district or unit (one per district) may identify one watch to which the captain prefers NOT to be assigned. This one watch must be identified by submitting a Personnel Action Request (PAR) formset (CPD-11.612) to their district commander between 10 – 17 November 2011.
  • D.Between 18 November – 22 November 2011, the watch selections will be processed by the unit commanding officers; on 23 November 2011 unit commanding officers will post the watch selections.
VI.Furloughs
  • A.Between 24 November and 7 December 2011 the annual furlough selections for all members will be submitted in accordance with established collective bargaining agreements.
  • B.The furlough selection process will be conducted on 8 December 2011 in the units.
  • C.Sworn members may submit requests for a full-furlough segment or split the furlough into two equal segments.
    • 1.A full-furlough segment will commence on the first day of a police period.
    • 2.Split-furlough segments will commence on either the 1st day or the 15th day of a police period.
  • D.On or before 16 December 2011 unit commanding officers will post the 2012 furlough selections.
VII.Furlough Selections - Police Officers
Assignment of a full-furlough segment or both split segments for police officers will be determined in a one-step process by seniority.
VIII.Furlough Selections - Sergeants
  • A.In units that have two or more sergeants assigned to each watch, sergeants will select furloughs by seniority on the watch within the unit of assignment. In units that have less than two sergeants assigned to each watch, sergeants will select furloughs by seniority within the unit of assignment.
    NOTE:
    District tactical sergeants are deemed a separate watch for furlough selection purposes.
  • B.Furlough assignments for sergeants will be determined in a two-step process, using seniority as a basis for the first pick. Furlough Selection/Absence Summary (CPD-11.609) will be ranked in seniority order.
    • 1.During the first step of the process, each sergeant will be assigned a full or single split segment based upon the individual’s ranked preference and segment availability.
    • 2.When every sergeant in the process has been assigned one segment, the second step will commence involving those sergeants assigned a split segment, in seniority order. The second split segment will be assigned based upon the individual’s ranked preference and segment availability.
  • C.Sergeants who voluntarily leave a watch or unit after the furlough selection process is completed will have their furlough segment(s) changed if there is an existing sergeant on the sergeant’s new watch or unit who already has the same furlough segment(s), resulting in a surplus of sergeants on furlough for that watch or unit. In that event, the incoming sergeant will have the opportunity to select from the available furlough segment(s), subject to the unit’s manpower needs.
  • D.Sergeants who have been authorized a furlough extension that includes a holiday will be given first priority for being off on that holiday.
IX.Furlough Selections - Captains and Lieutenants
Captains and lieutenants will select furloughs by rank, by seniority, within the unit of assignment using the same two-step process as the sergeants outlined in Item VIII-B.
X.Vacation Selections - Civilians
  • A.Unit commanding officers will allocate civilian resources between the watches and balance day-off groups in a manner which best address the operational needs of the unit.
  • B.Vacations will be selected in accordance with the provisions of the Department directive entitled “Furlough and Vacation.” However, for civilian members covered by a bargaining-unit agreement, vacation selection and the number of vacation days earned will be determined in accordance with the member’s bargaining-unit agreement.
XI.Furloughs Granted - All Members
A list of furlough selections granted will be posted in each unit. Exempt unit commanding officers will forward a report listing the names of all members and their furlough/vacation selections to their bureau chief and entered into the Watch/Personnel application no later than 19 December 2011. Unless advised to the contrary, this report will be considered approved.

Due to operational needs, no full 5th period furloughs or 5B furlough segments will be authorized. XIII.Chicago Police - 2012 Operations Calendar
  • A.Copies of the Chicago Police - 2012 Operations Calendar (CPD-11.143) will be distributed concurrently with this directive.
    • 1.Department members will receive one copy.
    • 2.Unit secretaries will retain enough calendars to ensure absent members receive a copy of the calendar.
  • B.The calendar is designed to show the following:
    • 1.Calendar months,
    • 2.Police periods,
    • 3.Rotating work schedule day-off groups,
    • 4.Traffic-court key-letter groups, and
    • 5.Nontraffic misdemeanor and municipal ordinance court keys.
      NOTE:
      The key box on the calendar indicates how to interpret Items XIII-B-3 through 5 of this directive.
  • C.The calendar months are designated with dark-lined borders.
  • D.The right-hand side designates split police periods which are differentiated from the calendar months by shaded overlays.
XIV.Compensatory Time Furloughs 2012
  • A.Compensatory time furloughs are intended for those personnel who have selected a full-furlough period.
  • B.2012 vacation days cannot be used for any furlough or compensatory time furlough that extends beyond the conclusion of the 13th police period, 5 January 2013.
  • C.Those sworn members who have selected a full-furlough period are entitled to a compensatory time furlough. These compensatory time furloughs will be scheduled 6 police periods following a full furlough if the full furlough was taken during the police periods 1 through 7, or 6 periods prior to a full furlough to be taken during the police periods 8 through 13.
    NOTE:
    Officers who select an 11th period full furlough will not be entitled to a compensatory time furlough between 16 – 24 May due to operational needs.
