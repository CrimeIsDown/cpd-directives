Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-07-11
Mobile Capture and Reporting System Pilot Program
Issue Date:22 February 2007Effective Date:22 February 2007
Rescinds:D07-11
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Mobile Capture & Reporting System (MCR), an automated system for completing, submitting, and approving Illinois Traffic Crash Reports at district desks.
  • B.establishes responsibilities and procedures for the entry and approval of information into the MCR System.
  • C.continues the use of the following:
    • 1.Driver’s Accident Statement (CPD-22.111).
    • 2.Witness Accident Statement (CPD-22.112).
    • 3.Illinois Traffic Crash Report, SR 1050C.
    • 4.Illinois Traffic Crash Report, SR 1050CA.
    • 5.Illinois Traffic Crash Report Template One, SR 1000CA.
    • 6.Illinois Traffic Crash Report Template Two, SR 1000CB.
    • 7.Transfer List-Traffic Crash Report (CPD-31.660).
    • 8.Department Vehicle Traffic Crash or Damage Report (CPD-22.951).
    • 9.Traffic Accident Warning to Motorist (Secretary of State form DSD DC-157).
    • 10.Medical Reporting and Re-examination Request (Secretary of State form DSD DA-16).
II.Scope
  • A.Phase II is scheduled to become operational on 12 June 08 city-wide. Only trained sworn desk officers will utilize the Mobile Capture and Reporting System.
  • B.The MCR System Pilot Program will be used for Type “A” traffic crash. Department members will follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Traffic Crash Investigations” unless otherwise directed by this directive.
III.Officer Responsibilities
Reporting officers will:
  • A.adhere to all Department procedures and policies relative to completing a Traffic Crash Report.
  • B.select the “MCR” icon and enter their user name (PC login).
  • C.enter the word “password.”
    NOTE:
    Department members will then change their password. After the first logon, Department members will use the new password to access the system. Department members are responsible for the safeguard of any action taken under their username and password.
  • D.complete an automated Traffic Crash Report for each accident.
    NOTE:
    Complete the automated Traffic Crash Report using the on-screen prompts and training provided by the Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT). For specific procedures on completing an automated Traffic Crash Report, refer to the “User’s Guide to Mobile Capture & Reporting System” published by PSIT.
  • E.obtain an RD number and place that number in the box named “Agency Report No.”
  • F.place all approved reports in the appropriate report basket.
IV.Desk Sergeant Responsibilities
The Desk Sergeant will:
  • A.review and complete the required section of the submitted Traffic Crash Reports for approval.
  • B.print a completed copy of the Traffic Crash Report with the Secretary of State bar code.
V.Training
  • A.Training on the MCR System will be provided on-site at the affected units by a PSIT training team. Additional assistance will be available via the Help Desk, the “User’s Guide to Mobile Capture & Reporting System” (available in print and online), and a quick-reference guide.
  • B.Upon completion of the training, an officer will complete an attendance roster.
VI.Procedures in the Event of an MCR Unavailability
  • A.If a member is unable to access the MCR System application on a Department computer, that member will:
    • 1.notify the desk sergeant.
    • 2.telephone the Help Desk for assistance.
    • 3.complete a paper Illinois Traffic Crash Report following the procedures as outlined in the Department directive entitled “Traffic Crash Investigations.”
  • B.In the event of a MCR System unavailability, the Help Desk will notify all affected units via the AMFN network with information regarding the expected duration of the outage.
VII.Evaluation
  • A.The Mobile Capture and Reporting System Pilot Program Evaluation Committee will consist of the following members or their appointed designees:
    • 1.Assistant Superintendent, Operations;
    • 2.Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol;
    • 3.Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology;
    • 4.Commanding Officer, Major Accident Investigation Unit;
    • 5.Command staff member of the Research and Development Division.
  • B.The evaluation committee will be charged with the responsibility of:
    • 1.evaluating the use, effectiveness, and efficiency of the program.
    • 2.preparing a program evaluation report for review by the Superintendent.
  • C.The Superintendent will review the committee report and determine whether the pilot program will be terminated, modified, expanded, or implemented on a citywide basis.
