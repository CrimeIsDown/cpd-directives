Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-16
Processing an Adult Arrested for a Juvenile Warrant or for an Offense committed when he or she was a juvenile
Issue Date:27 October 2016Effective Date:31 October 2016
Rescinds:22 March 2016 Version
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive clarifies the procedures and in-custody time limits for the arrest of:
  • A.an adult with a juvenile warrant.
  • B.an adult with juvenile charges.
    NOTE:
    An "adult" means anyone age 18 or older.
II.General Information
  • A.As of 1 January 2014, the Juvenile Court Act expanded the jurisdiction of the Juvenile Court to include anyone under the age of 18.
  • B.Any adult arrested and being held for court on charges stemming from an act committed while a juvenile or any adult with a juvenile warrant will be processed and held in the lockup exactly as an adult consistent with the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control." The arresting officer will:
    • 1.contact the Juvenile Intervention and Support Center (JISC) to confirm the warrant.
    • 2.notify the Bureau of Detectives Area Special Victims Investigations of the valid warrant or of the charges. A detective will be assigned to process the warrant (or charges upon which the subject was arrested) and will determine if the arrestee is to be released on a station adjustment, released with a court date in juvenile court, or held for court.
  • C.If being held for court, different timeline and transportation procedures apply. The arrestee, instead of being transported automatically to the Cook County Department of Corrections (CCDOC) on the prisoner bus, will be transported by district personnel to the Cook County Juvenile Court during the regular call to appear before a Juvenile Court Judge.
    NOTE:
    Department members will refer to the Department directive entitled "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" for hours of operation and transportation to the Cook County Juvenile Court.
  • D.If it is anticipated that the arrestee cannot be brought before a Cook County Juvenile Court Judge within the 40-hour time limit set forth in Item III of this directive, the arrestee will be transported to the Cook County Department of Corrections to appear in Juvenile Court.
III.Hold time-limit for an adult arrested for a juvenile warrant or for an offense committed when he or she was a juvenile
  • A.An adult arrested for a juvenile warrant, or an adult arrested for an offense committed when he or she was a juvenile, who is not released with a station adjustment or court date in juvenile court, will appear in court no later than 40 hours from the time of arrest (including weekends and court designed holidays), consistent with the requirements of the Juvenile Court Act.
  • B.If the arrestee is also charged with offenses not under the Juvenile Court Act, then separate bond will be required for those offenses, and the adult case will take priority over juvenile offenses for court scheduling and transportation purposes.
IV.An Adult arrested for a juvenile warrant from an Illinois county other than Cook County
If the juvenile warrant is from an Illinois county other than Cook County, the subject will be held at the area of processing until pickup by the appropriate agency. If the subject cannot be picked up within the 40-hour time limit set forth, the subject will be transported to the Cook County Department of Corrections.
V.An adult arrested for an out-of-state juvenile warrant or a federal juvenile warrant
An adult arrested for an out-of-state juvenile warrant or federal juvenile warrant will be processed as an adult under the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
VI.Conflict Provisions
  • A.If the Cook County Department of Corrections refuses to accept the arrestee solely on the basis that the arrestee has a juvenile warrant or juvenile charges:
    • 1.the transporting officers will notify:
      • a.the district station supervisor of the area of processing; and
      • b.the Crime Prevention and Information Center (  CPIC
