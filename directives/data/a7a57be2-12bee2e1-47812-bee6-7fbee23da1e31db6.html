Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-05-08
Chicago Police Department/Cellular One and Communities on Phone Patrol Partnership
Issue Date:13 October 2000Effective Date:14 October 2000
Rescinds:S00-09
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the Chicago Police Department, Cellular One, and Communities on Phone Patrol Partnership.
  • B.outlines responsibilities of district commanders, beat teams, Community Policing Office, District Advisory Committee, and the Chicago Alternative Policing Strategy (CAPS) Implementation Office.
  • C.outlines specific criteria for the distribution of cellular telephones to community groups and individuals.
  • D.recommends procedures for use and establishes the cooperative procedures between the partnership participants and district personnel.
  • E.provides information for the recommended use, maintenance, programming, and replacement of the cellular telephones.
II.Policy
The Department will provide support to assist residents in addressing issues of crime and neighborhood disorder. In keeping with this policy, each district will distribute cellular telephones to community groups and/or individual citizens who volunteer to participate in efforts to enhance proactive problem-solving and information exchange between the community, the police, and other city agencies.
III.General Information
  • A.This cooperative program between the Chicago Police Department and Cellular One will provide ten cellular telephones to each police district for the exclusive use of community groups and/or citizens involved in the beat level problem-solving activities. The telephones will only be able to call pre-programmed numbers. The cellular telephones are rechargeable and are equipped with an adapter for use in an automobile; both the recharger and adapter will be signed out by the user. Community groups and/or citizens will be responsible for the care of the cellular telephone and the assigned accessories.
  • B.Partnership participants are volunteers interested in working on behalf of the community and for the benefit of the neighborhood. The Department has established guidelines for volunteer participants in the partnership program. Participants who inappropriately use the cellular phone or engage in activity beyond the recommended guidelines will be excluded from the partnership program.
  • C.Volunteer partnership participants are reminded that they have no police authority and are obligated to observe all laws and ordinances which apply to all citizens.
  • D.The Department restricts partnership members use of the telephones to proactive, long-term problem solving. However, in emergency situations, the cellular telephones will have access to 9-1-1 by dialing the Cellular One operator.
  • E.Cellular telephones and accessories will be assigned a CPD inventory number and will be listed on the unit property inventory control record. Theft, loss, or damage to any cellular phone provided under this program will be immediately reported to the CAPS Implementation Office. The loss, theft, or damage to cellular telephones and accessories will not be the subject of a complaint register investigation unless the equipment was within the control of Chicago Police Department personnel at the time of the incident. Lost, stolen, or damaged cellular telephones and accessories that are assigned to partnership members will have the appropriate case report filed to report the incident. An extra copy of the case report will be sent to the CAPS Implementation Office.
  • F.The cellular telephones will be retrieved from any community group and/or individual who:
    • 1.does not fully employ the cellular telephone(s) within the partnership guidelines.
    • 2.attempts to reprogram and/or tamper with the cellular phones, or attempts to telephone any unauthorized numbers.
    • 3.purposely damages or physically misuses the cellular telephones.
    • 4.unnecessarily places themselves at risk while monitoring neighborhood problems.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.Partnership Volunteer Members
    • 1.Partnership volunteer members will:
      • a.be at least twenty-one years of age or older.
      • b.report crime immediately.
      • c.operate in groups of two or more while performing monitoring operations.
      • d.when using a personal vehicle, the owner/operator shall possess proof of a valid State of Illinois Drivers license and plate, city sticker, insurance, and to have same available for review by community policing personnel, if requested to do so.
    • 2.Partnership volunteer members will not:
      • a.carry weapons of any kind while in the performance of partnership activities.
      • b.represent themselves as police officers to anyone, under any circumstance, nor will they wear or carry any emblem, insignia, decal or sign which would indicate that they are police officers.
        NOTE:
        Off-duty officers volunteering for the program may not wear or carry any emblem, insignia, decal or signs with "Chicago Police" or "Police" written on them.
      • c.engage in foot pursuits or automobile chases under any circumstances.
      • d.detain anyone unless ordered to do so by a police officer.
      • e.attempt to stop a crime in progress or interfere with police operations.
      • f.allow children to accompany them while on patrol.
      • g.photograph or videotape any individual while on patrol.
      • h.use police scanners while on patrol.
      • i.allow any unauthorized person use of phones provided under this program.
  • B.District Commander
    District commanders will:
    • 1.decide which community groups and/or individuals receive the cellular telephones. The District Advisory Committee may assist with the selection.
    • 2.ensure that the partnership cellular telephones are used exclusively by community groups or individual residents to address beat level problem- solving activities within the district.
    • 3.be the final arbiter for the retrieval of the cellular telephones from any group or individual that is misusing the assigned cellular telephones.
    • 4.ensure that the partnership cellular telephones, when under district control, are stored in a secure manner.
    • 5.ensure that the operations of each community group and/or individual involved in the cellular partnership program is reviewed after a sixty- day period in order to decide whether continuation of this project will serve the established goals. Information from the CAPS Implementation Office, the Community Policing Sergeant and the District Advisory Committee may assist this review.
  • C.Beat Teams
    Beat team officers will:
    • 1.identify priority beat problems that may be addressed by the cellular partnership program.
    • 2.provide Information Collection for Automated Mapping (ICAM) maps bi-weekly to cellular partnership members to help identify problem areas.
    • 3.ensure the exchange of information regarding the progress of the program is disseminated during the beat meeting.
  • D.Community Policing Office
    Members assigned to the Community Policing Office will:
    • 1.provide partnership volunteers in the Chicago Police Department/Cellular One Partnership Program the RELEASE OF LIABILITY (CPD-11.708) and the NOTICE TO PARTICIPANTS (CPD-11.709). The community policing sergeant and/or community policing officers must collect a signed RELEASE OF LIABILITY from participants indicating that they have read and understood both the NOTICE TO PARTICIPANTS and the RELEASE OF LIABILITY.
    • 2.be responsible for the training of community groups and individual residents in the use of the cellular telephones.
    • 3.distribute each cellular telephone to an individual resident or representative of a community group designated by the district commander. The partnership member will be required to furnish proper identification and affix their signature to a control sheet maintained in the community policing office.
    • 4.designate a district contact person(s) for all technical problems concerning the cellular telephones and be the liaison between the district and the CAPS Implementation Office.
    • 5.conduct a monthly physical inspection of the cellular telephones and accessories to ensure operability and physical security.
    • 6.monitor the effectiveness of the cellular partnership program at the district level and prepare a monthly report through the chain of command to the CAPS Implementation Office regarding the performance of the cellular partnership program.
  • E.District Advisory Committee
    The District Advisory Committee may:
    • 1.advise the district commander on which community groups and/or individuals should receive the cellular telephones.
    • 2.assist the community policing sergeant in monitoring the effectiveness of the cellular partnership.
  • F.CAPS Implementation Office
    The CAPS Implementation Office will:
    • 1.assist district personnel in training and advising community groups in the use of the cellular phones.
    • 2.monitor all information received from the districts and analyze the results.
    • 3.as necessary, recommend modifications of the cellular partnership to enhance the effectiveness of the program.
    • 4.serve as a liaison between the districts community policing office and Cellular One for repair, reprogramming, replacement, and data usage of cellular telephones.
    • 5.develop documentation for comments and information concerning the cellular partnership from the districts.
    • 6.ensure that positive results of the cellular partnership program are entered into the problem-solving database. All issues concerning the cellular partnership should be communicated citywide.
    • 7.ensure that the analysis of the cellular partnership program and any recommendations from the district are forwarded through the chain of command to the Chief of Patrol.
