Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-03-05
Temporary Release of Inmates from Cook County Jail
Issue Date:11 December 2001Effective Date:12 December 2001
Rescinds:S01-12
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.describes conditions when inmates from Cook County Jail may be released to Detective Division personnel for follow-up investigation.
  • B.provides guidelines that Detective Division personnel will follow to obtain the temporary release of an inmate from Cook County Jail.
  • C.outlines procedures for processing inmates temporarily released from Cook County Jail who are to be charged with another crime.
  • D.introduces the Cook County Release of Liability form. This form is issued by the Cook County Department of Corrections (CCDOC) and can be obtained at the Detective Division.
  • E.introduces:
    • 1.the Release for Temporary Custody of Inmate form (CPD-23.187)
    • 2.the Defendent Hold Notification form (CPD-23.186)
II.Policy
Only authorized Detective Division personnel will be allowed to request and obtain the release of inmates from Cook County Jail. All requests will be approved by a Detective Division command staff member, or in the absence of a command staff member, the lieutenant of the requesting member.
III.Procedures
  • A.When authorized Detective Division personnel wish to obtain the temporary release of an inmate from Cook County Jail for purposes of investigative follow-up, whether the inmate in question is a suspect or a witness, the following guidelines will be followed:
    • 1.The case being investigated must be a felony.
    • 2.The request must be made using the Request for Temporary Release of Inmate Form. Each request will be limited to a release period of twenty-four hours. When more than twenty-four hours are required to complete the investigation, an additional Request for Temporary Release of Inmate form will be completed specifying the anticipated time of return of the inmate (not to exceed an additional twenty-four hours).
    • 3.A telephonic notification to the Cook County Department of Corrections Special Operations Response Team (S.O.R.T.) must be made twenty-four hours prior to an inmates release. Notifications will be made during regular business hours, Monday through Friday, 0900 hours to 1700 hours. Requests for a release on Saturday, Sunday, or holiday are at the discretion of the Commander of the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T.
    • 4.A copy of the Request for Temporary Release of Inmate form and the Cook County Release of Liability form will be faxed to the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. The requesting unit must obtain verification that the fax was received.
    • 5.The Request for Temporary Release of Inmate Form, the Cook County Release of Liability form, as well as the Detective Division members Department identification card and star must be presented to the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. personnel prior to the inmate being released to Department custody.
      NOTE:
      In cases where an Assistant States Attorney assigned to Felony Review had previous involvement in the case under investigation, the A.S.A. from Felony Review will be notified by the responsible detective that the inmate is being taken into temporary custody.
    • 6.The inmate will be returned to the custody of the CCDOC immediately upon completion of the investigation or within twenty-four hours, whichever occurs first. When additional time is necessary for the investigation, the member responsible for the inmate will contact the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. immediately. A new Request For Temporary Release form will be completed, approved, and faxed to the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. unit detailing the reason(s) for the delay and when the inmate is expected to be returned to the custody of the Sheriff. The new Request For Temporary Release form will be presented to the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. personnel upon returning the inmate to the Cook County Jail.
  • B.Detective Division personnel authorized to accept custody of an inmate will be responsible for the inmates care from the time the inmate is released to the members custody until the inmate is returned to the custody of the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. personnel at the jail. Department personnel will immediately escort the inmate to the Department facility to which the responsible Detective Division personnel are assigned.
    NOTE:
    Bomb and Arson Section personnel will use the 11th District for processing if the inmate is subsequently charged with an additional crime.
  • C.When it is determined that the inmate will be charged with an additional crime, the Detective Division member responsible for the inmate will:
    • 1.prepare an Arrest Report (CPD-11.420) justifying the probable cause for the new charge and any other related paperwork necessary to complete the booking process. Members will include the CCDOC inmate number in the narrative of the arrest report.
    • 2.present the Arrest Report to the district watch commander for initial approval of probable cause.
    • 3.accompany the inmate to the lockup with the Arrest Report and wait while the inmate is fingerprinted and photographed.
    • 4.sign the back of the "Permanent Record - Records Division" copy of the Arrest Report indicating that the inmate is being returned to the Cook County Jail.
    • 5.return the inmate to Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. personnel at the Cook County Jail once fingerprinting and photographing are completed.
    • 6.prepare a Defendant Hold Notification form.
    • 7.notify the Cook County Department of Corrections S.O.R.T. personnel receiving the inmate that the inmate has been charged with another crime and present the following documents:
      • a.Defendant Hold Notification form.
      • b.Goldenrod (transportation) copy of the new Arrest Report.
      • c.Any additional original Request for Temporary Release of Inmate forms (if applicable) prepared for an extended hold beyond the original twenty-four hour period.
      NOTE:
      The responsible detective will ensure that any warrants or investigative alerts related to the arrestee are resolved in accordance with current Department directives prior to returning the inmate to the custody of the CCDOC.
  • D.The district watch commander of the unit where the inmate is processed will:
    • 1.ensure the proper handling and processing of the arrestee consistent with this directive.
    • 2.review the Arrest Report and sign the Initial Approval of Probable Cause box indicating the elements of the offense have been included in the narrative portion of the Arrest Report.
    • 3.waive the waiting time for the results of the fingerprint check in order to allow for the timely return of the inmate to the custody of the CCDOC.
    • 4.notify the Identification Section to expedite the processing of the fingerprints.
  • E.The on-duty district desk sergeant will ensure that all court-related documents, including the Arrest Report, complaints, criminal history record, and felony minute sheets, are processed expeditiously.
  • F.The second watch desk sergeant will ensure that these court-related documents are hand-carried to Central Bond Court. The documents are to be delivered to CPD personnel in Room 433 Monday through Friday between 0830 and 1200 hours. On weekends and holidays all court-related documents are to be hand-carried to Branch 57 in Room 100 between 0830-1200 hours.
    NOTE:
    It will be noted on the Court Complaint Transmittal Listing (CPD-11.551) that the arrestee is in the custody of the CCDOC.
