Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S05-02
Special Response Plans
Issue Date:23 February 1989Effective Date:24 February 1989
Rescinds:G89-02
Index Category:Extraordinary Responses
I.Purpose
This directive explains the procedures for notification of off-duty members. Department members will refer to the General Order titled "Special Response Plans" for an explanation of the Department's Special Response Plans Manual and assignment of responsibilities.
II.Notification of Off-Duty Members (Plan Blue Only)
  • A.News Media Notifications
    Radio and television will be used to inform members that Plan Blue has been activated. The announcement will be as follows: "All members of the Chicago Police Department are notified that Plan Blue (Phase One or Phase Two) is now activated. Members of the Department may be notified to report to designated locations."
  • B.Unit Recall Notifications
    • 1.Unit Responsibilities
      • a.In addition to news media announcements, affected units will initiate the recall notification of off-duty members.
      • b.Members assigned to make telephone recall notifications will inform off-duty members of the plan, the plan phase activated, and the reporting time and location.
    • 2.Recall Roster Content and Accuracy
      • a.The Unit of Assignment Recall and Check-Off Roster (DPOL 8010) lists all sworn and civilian members assigned to a unit. This roster will be used to recall personnel upon activation of Plan Blue.
      • b.The roster lists each member's rank or title code, the employee's name, employee number, star number and home telephone number. The roster has space for indicating day-off group, special status (limited duty, convalescent duty, arrestee processing team, lockup keeper, etc.), time contacted, reporting time and remarks.
      • c.To ensure that all information furnished on the rosters is up-to-date, members will submit a Personnel Change Notice (CPD-11.611) in conformance with the Department directive entitled "Payroll and Timekeeping."
      • d.Unit commanders will ensure that corrections are made as needed.
    • 3.The Public Safety Information Technology Responsibilities
      Public Safety Information Technology will:
      • a.furnish each unit of the Department with one copy of the "Unit of Assignment Recall and Check-Off Roster" on the first day of each police period.
      • b.replace any roster used in the event of implementation of a Special Response Plan. Any unit in need of a replacement roster will notify the Public Safety Information Technology to this effect.
    • 4.Recall Roster Accountability
      • a.Upon receipt of a revised roster as noted in Item II-B-3-a, the secretary of the unit will ensure that the old roster is removed from use and processed in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
      • b.Upon receipt of a replacement roster as noted in Item II-B-3-b, the secretary of the receiving unit will immediately file the roster used during the implementation of a Special Response Plan until its return is requested by the Assistant Superintendent, Operations.
