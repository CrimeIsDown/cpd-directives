Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S07-01-04
Bulk Currency Inventory
Issue Date:14 January 2004Effective Date:15 January 2004
Rescinds:S04-01
Index Category:Processing Property
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures for processing bulk amounts of United States currency.
II.Policy
  • A.All bulk amounts of United States currency (e.g., narcotic-related seizures) subject to inventory will be bagged immediately at the scene without a hand-count using the Currency Inventory Bag.
  • B.This policy will not apply when the recovering officer, with the approval of the on-scene supervisor, requests that the currency be held in its original form to be processed by Forensic Services Section personnel for the purposes of evidentiary testing (i.e., fingerprints, blood stain).
  • C.A recovery which ultimately yields less than the amount defined by a bulk amount of United States currency of this directive will not be considered a violation of this policy when a good faith effort was made in estimating the dollar value recovered at the scene.
III.General Information
  • A.The Equipment and Supply Section will maintain a supply of Currency Inventory Bags (CIBs) for issuance to Departmental units.
  • B.Unit commanding officers will ensure that an adequate supply of CIBs are available to unit members.
  • C.Supervisors assigned to specialized units or supervisors assigned to field duties will keep a supply of CIBs readily available to service requests from field personnel.
    NOTE:
    When there is a likelihood of a large currency seizure during the execution of a search warrant, search team supervisors will bring a supply of CIBs to the location of the warrant.
IV.Special Circumstances
Requests to hold bulk amounts of US currency in its original form for the purposes of evidentiary testing (i.e., fingerprints, blood stain) to be processed by Forensic Services Section personnel require the approval of the on-scene supervisor or the assigned member from the Bureau of Investigative Services. When it is determined that recovered currency has evidentiary value, the recovering officer will follow the directions given by the responding Forensic Services Section personnel.
  • A.The recovering officer will document the reason for the request directly in the eTrack system.
  • B.In the event of an eTrack unavailability, the recovering officer will document the reason for the request in a To-From-Subject report and forward the report to the Commanding Officer, Evidence and Recovered Property Section (ERPS).
V.Recovery Procedures
  • A.When recovering a bulk amount of US currency, the recovering officer will:
    • 1.take custody of the currency and ensure its safekeeping.
    • 2.immediately request a supervisor to the scene.
    • 3.whenever possible, establish the ownership of the currency.
    • 4.not commingle currency recoveries.
      • a.Currency recovered at the same time from the same person or exact location within a residence, building, or vehicle will be recovered using a single Currency Inventory Bag.
      • b.Currency recovered from different persons or different locations will be assigned separate Currency Inventory Bags.
      • c.All currency with a total estimated value exceeding $10,000 requires a notification to the Asset Forfeiture Unit, Monday through Friday, 0700 to 1700 hours. At all other times, notify the Narcotic and Gang Investigative Section 24-hour desk.
        NOTE:
        The recovering officer will not begin to bag the currency prior to the arrival of the supervisor or, if it is determined that the currency is suspect narcotics-related, prior to a response from personnel from the Asset Forfeiture Unit.
    • 5.secure the currency in the presence of the person from whom the currency was recovered, unless the presence of the person poses an imminent danger to Department members or others.
      • a.If the subject denies ownership, complete a Disclaimer of Ownership form (CPD-41.551).
      • b.If no person is present upon recovery, the currency will be processed as outlined in Item V-B-5 of this directive.
    • 6.upon arrival of the supervisor, place the currency in the Currency Inventory Bag.
      • a.Members will use multiple Currency Inventory Bags if the amount of currency recovered will not fit into one CIB.
      • b.Bundled currency will be placed directly into the CIB with the wrapper(s) intact (e.g., rubber bands, paper bands, envelopes) unless the bundling material has evidentiary value, in which case it will be inventoried as outlined in Item V-A-6-c of this directive.
      • c.Any container of evidentiary value (e.g., suitcase, backpack, container with secret compartment) that cannot be packaged inside an Evidence-Property Envelope (CPD-34.559 A, B, or C) will be recovered and inventoried without the sealed packaging for traditional bulk pick-up.
    • 7.in the presence of the supervisor, seal the Currency Inventory Bag(s).
    • 8.complete the required information on the CIB receipt. The person from whom the currency was recovered will be given the tear-off receipt for each CIB even if the person disclaims ownership.
    • 9.upon completion of the recovery, transport the property and, if applicable, the arrestee to the same facility to complete the processing.
  • B.The on-scene supervisor will:
    • 1.witness the bagging procedure.
    • 2.sign the appropriate field on the CIB after the recovering officer seals the bag.
    • 3.ensure the tear-off receipt is given to the person from whom the currency was recovered.
    • 4.ensure that a Disclaimer of Ownership form is completed if the person from who the currency was recovered disclaims ownership.
    • 5.when no person is present, request an evidence technician to photograph a recovery prior to bagging.
      NOTE:
      If no person is present upon recovery, the tear-off receipt will be inserted inside the CIB with the currency.
VI.Inventory Procedures
  • A.The inventory procedures outlined in the existing Department directives will be followed to inventory all CIBS.
    • 1.The recovering officer will:
      • a.process the inventory directly into the computer database.
      • b.when entering the inventory information into eTrack, select:
        • (1)"Bulk Currency Seizure" for the Item Type on the Inventory screen.
        • (2)"Recovering Unit Personnel (Hand Carry)" for the Transport Mode on the "Classification" screen.
          NOTE:
          Completing the inventory in this fashion will cause the eTrack system to automatically generate a manifest with the name of the inventorying officer as the courier. Property inventoried in eTrack cannot be transported to another location without a manifest.
      • c.record the information requested on the outside of the CIB.
        NOTE:
        The eTrack application assigns a number to each inventory upon that inventory's creation in the system. Therefore, multiple inventories for a single recovery may not be sequentially numbered.
      • d.after electronically submitting the inventory for approval, log off the system and take the CIB(s) containing the property to the desk sergeant for verification.
      • e.if the inventory is approved, sign in the signature box on the CIB and attach a printout of the inventory to the CIB in an appropriate manner.
    • 2.The supervisor will indicate approval directly into the database. Upon approval, paper copies of the inventory, as specified, and the manifest will be printed.
  • B.In the event of an eTrack system unavailability, and only after receiving authorization from the watch commander, the recovering officer will complete the inventory using the Property Inventory book formset (CPD-34.523).
    • 1.Only one CIB will be listed per line on the Property Inventory formset, if multiple CIBs were used for the recovery.
    • 2.The "USC Only / Total Cash USC" box of the Property Inventory formset will be left blank.
    • 3.The member will:
      • a.in the Description of Property section of the Property Inventory formset, describe the property as "United States currency sealed in Currency Inventory Bag number____."
      • b.record the phrase "ETRACK FAILURE" and the Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) number that informs Department members that the E-track failure in the open space on the formset marked "Evidence & Recovered Property Section Use Only."
      • c.make a photocopy of the original (white) Property Inventory formset of each inventory and place that copy in the eTrack Binder behind the tab marked "Formset Photocopies."
  • C.The desk sergeant (or in specialized units, the sergeant responsible for maintaining the eTrack Binder), upon receiving a PAX notification from ERPS that the CIBs have been received, will record the following information in the lower-right box of Copy 5 (canary) in the units Property Inventory book:
    • 1.ERPS personnel name,
    • 2.Employee number,
    • 3.Date, and
    • 4.Time of receipt.
  • D.Under no circumstances will the CIB be opened until the currency is transported to the bank.
VII.Transport Procedures (Hand-Carry Procedures)
  • A.Bulk currency inventory requires a "hand-carry procedure"; therefore, the recovering officer will follow the inventory procedures as outlined in existing Department directives.
    • 1.The desk sergeant will:
      • a.review the submitted inventory for accuracy and completeness.
      • b.ensure that the "Generate Hand Carry Manifest" box on the Print screen is checked prior to approving the inventory.
      • c.present the recovering officer with the manifest that was printed along with the inventory copy and instruct that person to present it upon arrival at ERPS.
    • 2.The recovering officer will, upon approval of the inventory, immediately transport the Currency Inventory Bag(s) and its accompanying manifest to the Evidence and Recovery Property Section.
      NOTE:
      An Incorrect inventory will not be accepted at ERPS.
  • B.In the event of an eTrack system unavailability and the recovery was inventoried using the Property Inventory Book, the recovering officer will ensure that the procedures established for transporting property to ERPS as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Inventory System For Property Taken Into Custody" is followed.
    • 1.The recovering officer will:
      • a.sign and provide the removal information requested on the back of Copy 5 of the Property Inventory formset.
      • b.transport the CIB and the original (white) and Copy 2 (buff) of the Property Inventory formset to ERPS. Copy 2 will not be sent via police mail.
      • c.return the receipt portion of Copy 2 (buff) of the Property Inventory formset to the unit of inventory.
    • 2.The desk sergeant (or in specialized units, the Department member responsible for the inventory book) at the unit of inventory will collect the receipt portion of the buff copy and ensure compliance with all the provisions of the Department directive entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken Into Custody."
VIII.Evidence and Recovered Property Section Responsibilities
  • A.The Evidence and Recovered Property Section personnel will accept custody of properly completed inventories of Currency Inventory Bags at all times, including nonbusiness hours. When receiving an inventory, ERPS personnel will:
    • 1.follow procedures outlined in existing Department property inventory directives.
    • 2.verify that the control number(s) on the Currency Inventory Bag(s) matches the number(s) recorded on the Inventory copy.
      NOTE:
      If the control numbers on the CIB(s) do not match the number(s) recorded on the Inventory copy, the inventory will not be accepted at ERPS.
    • 3.inspect all CIBs for any visual damage.
      NOTE:
      If a visually damaged CIB is discovered at ERPS, ERPS personnel will not accept the CIB.
    • 4.secure all sealed CIBs until transported to the bank for processing, in accordance with procedures established by the Commander of the Evidence and Recovered Property Section.
  • B.If the recovery was inventoried using the Property Inventory book, ERPS personnel receiving the inventory will:
    • 1.notify the desk sergeant at the unit of inventory via PAX that the inventory has been received.
    • 2.record the date, time of delivery, and PAX notification made to the unit of recovery on:
      • a.Copy 1 of the Property Inventory formset.
      • b.Copy 2 of the Property Inventory form in box 6 on the receipt side.
      • c.on the Currency Inventory Bag in the Chain of Custody field.
    • 3.upon notifying the desk sergeant at the unit of recovery that the inventory has been received and upon recording the information on the receipt side of Copy 2 of the Property Inventory formset, relinquish the receipt portion of the buff copy to the inventorying officer.
  • C.Personnel assigned to transport Currency Inventory Bags to the bank will:
    • 1.record the required information in the Chain of Custody field prior to leaving ERPS.
    • 2.separate CIBs by inventory number at the banks counting room.
    • 3.ensure that inventories are not commingled.
    • 4.witness the opening of the CIB and counting of the money within.
    • 5.recover the opened CIB.
    • 6.ensure that a deposit slip for each CIB is received from bank personnel.
    • 7.if the bank determines that there are counterfeit notes inside a CIB, obtain documentation from the bank of the amount of counterfeit notes.
  • D.If it was determined that there were counterfeit notes recovered, ERPS personnel will:
    • 1.using the eTrack system, complete the Money Work Sheet and select "Counterfeit Adjustment" as the item type. Include the following:
      • a.Name and employee number of the witnessing Department member.
      • b.Date and time of the count.
      • c.Total number of counterfeit notes.
    • 2.in the event of an eTrack system unavailability, follow procedures established by the Commander of the Evidence and Recovered Property Section.
IX.Damage to Inventoried Currency Bags
  • A.Prior to its use, the Department member will visually inspect the CIB to ensure that there is no damage to the bag.
  • B.Department members who become aware of visible damage to an inventoried CIB will immediately notify their sworn supervisor.
  • C.Upon discovery of a compromised CIB, the supervisor conducting the investigation will:
    • 1.conduct a preliminary investigation.
    • 2.initiate a Complaint Register investigation and follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Complaint and Disciplinary Procedures."
    • 3.request an evidence technician to photograph the CIB in its current condition.
    • 4.after being photographed, place the compromised CIB in its current condition inside a new CIB and inventory the new CIB as outlined according to this directive.
      NOTE:
      The RD number from the original incident will be used and the related inventory numbers and CIB control number will be cross-referenced.
    • 5.document all inventory numbers related to the above process in the appropriate case report.
