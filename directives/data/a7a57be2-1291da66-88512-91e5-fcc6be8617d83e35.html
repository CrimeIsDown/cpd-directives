Chicago Police DepartmentGeneral Order G01-02-02
Organization and Functions of Operations
Issue Date:26 January 2009Effective Date:27 January 2009
Rescinds:G09-02-02
Index Category:Department Organization
I.Purpose
This directive establishes the organizational structure and functions of Operations.
II.Responsibilities
  • A.Operations is commanded by the Assistant Superintendent, Operations, who reports directly to the Superintendent of Police. The Assistant Superintendent, Operations, oversees and coordinates the activities of the following units:
    • 1.CAPS Implementation Office
      The CAPS Implementation Office is commanded by a director who reports directly to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations. This office communicates with all City departments, ensuring coordination of City services as they apply to the Chicago Alternative Policing Strategy (CAPS) program and organizes community residents in furtherance of CAPS related activities.

      The CAPS Implementation Office includes the following:
      • a.Community Outreach Section

        The Community Outreach Section is commanded by a deputy director who reports directly to the Director, CAPS Implementation Office, and oversees and coordinates the activities of the following units:
        • (1)Domestic Violence Unit

          The Domestic Violence Unit is responsible for improving the Department's response to domestic violence.
        • (2)Senior Services Unit

          The Senior Services Unit coordinates law enforcement services provided to the senior aged community members.
      • b.Special Projects Section

        The Special Projects Section is commanded by a deputy director who reports directly to the Director, CAPS Implementation Office. The Special Projects Section oversees special city wide community based initiatives focused on violence prevention and reduction.
    • 2.Special Events and Liaison Section

      The Special Events and Liaison Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations. This section is responsible for planning police coverage at public gatherings and maintaining liaison with other municipal departments as well federal and state law enforcement agencies.
  • B.Operations coordinates and unifies the efforts of the Bureau of Patrol and the Bureau of Investigative Services.
III.Bureau of Patrol
  • A.The Bureau of Patrol is commanded by the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, who reports directly to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations.
  • B.The bureau is responsible for general field operations, including the protection of life and property; apprehension of criminals; and enforcement of traffic laws and ordinances.
  • C.The Bureau consists of Patrol Group A and Patrol Group B, each commanded by a chief who reports directly to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol.
    • 1.Patrol Group A consists of the following, each commanded by a deputy chief:
      • a.Area 1: Districts 002, 007, 008, 009, and 021;
      • b.Area 2: Districts 003, 004, 005, 006, and 022.
      • c.Special Functions Group.
        The Special Functions Group consists of the following:
        • (1)Targeted Response Unit
          The Targeted Response Unit is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Special Functions Group. This unit provides unified and flexible teams to target locations that have the highest potential for public violence.
        • (2)Mobile Strike Force
          The Mobile Strike Force is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Special Functions Group. This unit provides a force of personnel capable of ready deployment; it also provides specialized vehicles, such as the command van and light truck, as well as specialized canine services.
        • (3)Other Units
          The commanding officers of the Marine/Helicopter Unit, the Mounted Unit, SWAT, the Traffic Section, the Troubled Buildings Unit, and the Special Activities Section report directly to the Deputy Chief, Special Functions Group.

          The activities of these units support district law enforcement by providing the following services: administration of the vacant building program; support to area drug- and gang- house officers; marine and mounted patrols, as well as air support; traffic enforcement city-wide and traffic crash investigations on Lake Shore Drive and the Chicago Skyway; intersection control; administration of the Peak Performance Driver Training Program; and the control of large groups of people at public gatherings.

          The Special Activities Section arranges and conducts tours of Department facilities; administers the ride-along program for other than news media representatives; coordinates visits of Department guests as directed by the Superintendent; coordinates the Department's blood donor program; maintains liaison with Department retirees; manages and distributes all requests for poster approvals; and, on a twenty-four-hour basis, provides assistance to the families of officers seriously injured or killed. The Special Activities Section also consists of the Honor Guard, the School Visitation Unit, the Civil Rights Unit, and the Crisis Intervention Team.
    • 2.Patrol Group B consists of the following:
      • 1.Area 3: Districts 019, 020, 023, and 024;
      • 2.Area 4: Districts 010, 011, 012, and 013;
      • 3.Area 5: Districts 014, 015, 016, 017, and 025;
      • 4.Central Control Group: Districts 001 and 018.
        The Central Detention Section is commanded by a commanding officer who reports directly to the Commander, District 001. This section processes and maintains custody of arrestees held in central lockup; transports arrestees to courts and institutions; and provides transportation for persons in multiple arrest situations.
      • 5.Alternate Response Section
        The Alternate Response Section is commanded by a commanding officer who reports directly to the Chief, Patrol Group B. This section prepares case reports by telephone for local and out-of-town callers and acts as the backup call facility to the OEMC for both call-taking and dispatching of emergency personnel.
  • D.Each police district is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the appropriate deputy chief, who reports directly to the appropriate chief. Each district is provided with personnel and support service to staff three watches, tactical teams, and a community-policing office.
IV.Bureau of Investigative Services
  • A.The Bureau of Investigative Services is commanded by the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Investigative Services, who reports directly to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations.
  • B.The Bureau of Investigative Services coordinates and directs the efforts of its divisions toward completing thorough and unified investigations. The bureau consists of the Detective Division, the Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division, and the Organized Crime Division.
    • 1.Detective Division
      • a.The Detective Division is commanded by a chief who reports directly to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Investigative Services.
      • b.The Detective Division is responsible for the follow-up investigation of felonies, selected misdemeanors, and missing/found persons. Additionally, the Division is responsible for the care of juveniles who are in need of protective services and the processing of juvenile offenders.
      • c.Each Detective Division area consists of a Violent Crimes Section, Property Crimes Section, Special Victims Section, and Case Management Section.
      • d.The Detective Division consists of Field Group A and Field Group B, each commanded by a deputy chief who reports directly to the Chief, Detective Division, and the Headquarters Support Unit.
        • (1)Field Group A consists of:
          • (a)Area 1: Districts 002, 007, 008, 009, and 021
          • (b)Area 2: Districts 003, 004, 005, 006, and 022
          • (c)Youth Investigations Section
            The Youth Investigations Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Field Group A. The section is responsible for managing juvenile records; providing investigative support for missing/found persons cases; investigating child abuse cases; and coordinating and monitoring social service resources to juveniles and their families.
          • (d)Central Investigations Section
            The Central Investigations Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Field Group A. This section responds citywide using specialized investigative techniques.
        • (2)Field Group B consists of:
          • (a)Area 3: Districts 001, 018, 019, 020, 023, and 024;
          • (b)Area 4: Districts 010, 011, 012, and 013;
          • (c)Area 5: Districts 014, 015, 016, 017, and 025;
          • (d)Forensic Services Section
            The Forensic Services Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Field Group B. This section provides technical and scientific expertise for the collection, analysis, and preservation of physical evidence and the reconstruction of crimes.
        • (3)Headquarters Support Unit
          The Headquarters Support Unit is commanded by a commanding officer who reports directly to the Chief, Detective Division. This unit provides technical and behavioral crime analysis, special projects, and criminal registration.
    • 2.Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division
      The Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division is commanded by a chief who reports directly to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Investigative Services, and is assisted by the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This division is responsible for conducting tactical and analytical activities supporting effective deployment of field units; analyzing anti-terrorism intelligence; coordinating the Department’s overall anti-terrorism planning and preparation; conducting intelligence investigations; providing specialized coverage of the Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) rapid transit systems and the City’s airports and adjacent areas; conducting bomb and arson investigations; and serving as the command post for the Department. The division coordinates the functions and performances of the following sections:
      • a.Deployment Operations Center Section
        The Deployment Operations Center Section (DOC) is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division, and is responsible for the Crime Prevention Information Center Unit (CPIC) and Operations Command Unit. This section provides timely intelligence that will assist in the reduction of violent crime and assess the threat of terrorism; gathers information to assist in the effective recommendation for the deployment of Departmental resources; and functions as the command post of the Department.
      • b.Intelligence Section
        The Intelligence Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This section conducts investigations of an individual, group, or organization reasonably believed to be engaging in criminal or terrorism-related activity.
      • c.Airport Law Enforcement Section
        The Airport Law Enforcement Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This section provides specialized patrol coverage to airport terminals and outlying properties; regulates vehicular and pedestrian traffic; and works in conjunction with the Federal Bureau of Investigation, the Federal Aviation Administration, the Federal Transportation Safety Administration Bureau, and the Drug Enforcement Administration in various airport/dignitary functions.
      • d.Public Transportation Section
        The Public Transportation Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This section provides patrol security for the CTA rapid transit system.
      • e.Bomb and Arson Section
        The Bomb and Arson Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This section ensures the proper follow-up investigations of all reported fire, bomb, and arson incidents and related threats; provides bomb security to dignitaries and for the retrieval, deactivation, and disposal of illegal explosives and explosive devices.
      • f.Predictive Analytics Group
        The Predictive Analytics Group is commanded by a director who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division. This group is responsible for forecasting crime using the Predictive Analytics System.
    • 3.Organized Crime Division
      The Organized Crime Division is commanded by a chief who reports directly to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Investigative Services. The chief is assisted by the Deputy Chief, Organized Crime Division. This division focuses on the dissolution of illegal narcotic, gang, and vice activities through both street-level enforcement efforts and long-term criminal investigations. The Organized Crime Division consists of the following:
      • a.Narcotics Section
        The Narcotics Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Organized Crime Division. This section is responsible for investigation of and enforcement against large-scale, illegal narcotics activities and narcotics activities that transcend district boundaries; processes all cases that are drug-related and involve money, vehicles, real property, or other assets seized for civil and/or criminal forfeiture proceedings.
      • b.Gang Enforcement Section
        The Gang Enforcement Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Organized Crime Division. This section focuses on street-level enforcement of gang criminal enterprises by the strategic deployment of teams to areas of the City experiencing increases in shootings, narcotics, and public violence.
      • c.Gang Investigation Section
        The Gang Investigation Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Organized Crime Division. This section ensures the proper investigation of any individual, group, or organization reasonably believed to be engaging in criminal gang activity.
      • d.Vice Control Section
        The Vice Control Section is commanded by a commander who reports directly to the Deputy Chief, Organized Crime Division. This section is responsible for investigations directed toward the detection and suppression of vice activities other than narcotics (e.g., gambling, prostitution, distribution of obscene matter) and performs functions relating to City license applications, subterfuge, and organized crime involvement in legitimate business operations.
