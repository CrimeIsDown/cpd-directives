Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S12-02
Private Security Forces
Issue Date:31 March 1978Effective Date:01 April 1978
Rescinds:S78-16
Index Category:Outside Organizations
I.Purpose
  • A.This directives informs members of the laws pertaining to weapons, uniforms and licensing of private security forces.
  • B.The provisions of Chapter 117, Municipal Code of Chicago, will not be enforced as they are preempted by state law governing detectives and agencies (225 ILCS 447/5-5 et seq)
II.Security Guards, Watchmen, and Special Policemen: Definitions, Powers, and Limitations
  • A.Security Guards
    • 1.For the purpose of this directive and the unlawful use of weapons exemptions of 720 ILCS 5/24-2, a security guard is either:
      • a.a person employed by a detective agency which is licensed pursuant to 225 ILCS 447/ et seq., and whose duties are to protect persons or property, or
      • b.a person who is a member of a security force of 30 or more persons which is registered with the Illinois Department or Registration and Education, and who is regularly employed in a commercial or industrial operation for the protection of persons employed and private property related to such operation.
    • 2.Security guards are exempt from the unlawful use of weapons provisions of 720 ILCS 5/24-1 (a) (3), (4) and (10) while actually engaged in the performance of their employment or commuting between their homes and places of employment (subject to a one hour commuting restriction) provided they have met the requirements set forth in 720 ILCS 5/24-2 (a) (4) and possess documents prescribed by the Department of Registration and Education. This documentation includes the following:
      • a.A white identification card, which serves as both registration and proof of clearance of any criminal record.
      • b.A tan "Armed Guard Training Card," which indicates the completion of the prescribed training course, the security guards name and training number, and the name of the agency by which the security guard is employed.
    • 3.To qualify for an exemption from the provisions of 720 ILCS 5/24-1 (a) (3), (4) and (10), a security guard must possess both the white and tan cards. Blue cards formerly issued by the Department of Registration and Education are null and void.
  • B.Watchmen
    • 1.For the purpose of this directive and the UUW exemptions of 720 ILCS 5/24-2, a watchman is any person who is employed exclusively and regularly by one employer in connection with the affairs of such employer only and there exists an employer-employee relationship (thereby falling within the licensing and regulation exemptions of Chapter 38, section 201-2) and who is not one of a security force of 30 persons or more registered with the Illinois Department of Registration and Education as provided in item II-A-1-b of this directive.
    • 2.Watchmen are exempt from the provisions of 720 ILCS 5/24-1(a) (3), (4), and (10), while actually engaged in the performance of the duties of their employment. However a watchman, being no more than an agent of his employer, may be armed only to the extent that his employer may be armed, i.e., only on the employers land, in his abode, or at his fixed place of business. There is no mobility or commuting privilege.
  • C.Special Policemen
    • 1.Special policemen are persons appointed by the Superintendent of Police who posses all the powers of regular police officers only at the fixed places identified on their Certificates of Appointment (Municipal Code of Chicago, Ch. 4-340) Illustration (reduced from actual size):
    • 2.Appointment as a special policeman does not authorize an individual to carry a weapon beyond the certified location. However, a special policeman may also be a security guard as defined by item II-A of this directive and as such, would fall within the exemptions of that security guard classification.
  • D.Arrest Powers
    Security guards and watchmen, unless also appointed as special policemen, have no more than citizens power of arrest and are limited to the use of force that a citizen may use in making an arrest (720 ILCS 5/7-6).
III.Weapons: Generally
Officers suspecting a violation of 720 ILCS 5/24-1, will require a suspected offender to substantiate that they meet all the requirements of the exemption provisions of section 24-2 and, if they are a special policeman that they are at their fixed place of certification.
IV.Uniforms: Articles and Insignia
  • A.Chapter 2-84-500, Municipal Code of Chicago, provides:
    "No person shall falsely assume or pretend to be a policeman or a member of the department of police of this city, or ... wear in public the uniform adopted as the police uniform."... Nor shall any person use or wear any badge, sign, signal or device adopted or used by said department or any similar in appearance, without authority so to do from the (Chicago Police) Board."
  • B.720 ILCS 5/32-5, provides:
    "A person who falsely represents himself to be... a public officer or a public employee commits a Class A misdemeanor."
  • C.For the purpose of this directive, any person who wears or displays an article or insignia such as a checkerboard pattern hatband, Chicago flag shoulder patch, etc., is in violation of the above provisions if it can reasonably be inferred that in wearing or displaying such articles or insignia he is falsely representing himself to be a Chicago police officer.
V.Information Reports Required
  • A.In addition to other appropriate reports, an Information Report (CPD-11.461) will be completed whenever a security guard, watchman, or special policeman (on or off duty) is arrested for or suspected of committing any criminal or quasi-criminal offense, including licensing and regulation offenses but excluding minor traffic offenses.
  • B.The Information Report will be completed and processed in accordance with the current Department directive on Information Report System.
  • C.The Special Police Section, Human Resources Division will:
    • 1.classify Information Reports received according to employment classifications of the subject individuals, as previously defined in this directive.
    • 2.investigate those reports which involve special policemen and take appropriate action.
    • 3.forward copies of those reports which involve security guards and watchmen to the Illinois Department of Registration and Education.
