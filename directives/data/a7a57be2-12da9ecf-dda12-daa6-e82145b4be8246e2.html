Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U01-05-01
Computer Replacement Program
Issue Date:30 March 2007Effective Date:31 March 2007
Rescinds:A07-03
Index Category:Department Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive establishes:
  • A.the Computer Replacement Program.
  • B.procedures for the inventory, placement, and maintenance of all computer equipment managed through the Computer Replacement Program.
II.Policy
  • A.Unauthorized computers will not be stored, electrically connected, or network connected within any unit or any area controlled by a unit.
  • B.Equipment assigned to a unit and issued under the Computer Replacement Program will not be moved or transferred to other locations, including other locations within the assigned unit.
  • C.Units that receive 1505 funding are not eligible for computer equipment issued through the Computer Replacement Program.
III.General Information
  • A.The Computer Replacement Program operates for a three-year cycle from 2006 to 2009.
  • B.All computer equipment issued through the Department’s Computer Replacement Program agreement must be returned in good condition to the manufacturer at the end of the cycle, when updated computer equipment will be issued.
  • C.Department personnel will properly maintain computer equipment issued during the Computer Replacement Program cycle and will ensure that the equipment is not damaged.
  • D.When a change of command occurs within a unit, a separate inventory of all program-related computer equipment will be conducted consistent with the Department directive entitled "Department Equipment and Property Control System." Upon completion, the results of the inventory will be forwarded to the Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology, and Commander, Inspection Division.
IV.Procedures
  • A.The Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT), will:
    • 1.inform unit commanding officers of the installation process relative to the Computer Replacement Program.
    • 2.ensure commanding officers are notified in a timely manner regarding the installation of computer equipment.
    • 3.ensure the Commander, Inspection Division, is notified of units that are assigned computer equipment issued under the Computer Replacement Program agreement.
  • B.Upon notification from the Managing Deputy Director, PSIT, that computer equipment will be replaced, unit commanding officers will ensure that:
    • 1.all files are removed from existing computers.
    • 2.members from PSIT are granted full access to all Department-issued computers that are in locked offices and/or storage closets.
    • 3.all storage areas and closets are searched for existing computer equipment which will be turned over to personnel from PSIT prior to receiving computer equipment per the Computer Replacement Program.
    • 4.an investigation is conducted relative to any missing equipment, including the completion of a case report and the initiation of a Complaint Register (CR) investigation, if appropriate.
    • 5.personnel from PSIT supply the unit with a complete list of all issued computer equipment. Equipment issued through the Computer Replacement Program will be affixed with red Chicago Police Department inventory tags. The inventory list will be made available on the Chicago Police Department Intranet for viewing purposes only.
    • 6.computer equipment issued under the Computer Replacement Program is not:
      • a.placed on the floor (carpeted or not).
      • b.placed on chairs, printers, books, or any other unstable surface. The computer and monitor will be placed on a desk top or sturdy table.
      • c.adorned with stickers, post-it notes, or any other adhesives other than factory tags and/or Department-issued tags.
  • C.The Commander, Inspection Division, will ensure:
    • 1.audits are conducted every six months to ensure compliance relative to the Computer Replacement Program.
    • 2.proper and timely notifications are made from PSIT regarding units that have been assigned computer equipment per the Computer Replacement Program.
    • 3.appropriate disciplinary action is initiated if a violation of Department policy is determined.
