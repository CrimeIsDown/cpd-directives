Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U04-02-07
Semiautomatic Pistol-Mounted Lights
Issue Date:14 June 2012Effective Date:14 June 2012
Rescinds:U04-02-03
Index Category:Uniform and Personal Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.authorizes the use of Department-approved semiautomatic pistol-mounted lights as an optional item.
  • B.provides specifications, procedures, and training requirements for the use of Department-approved semiautomatic pistol-mounted lights.
II.General Information
  • A.The pistol-mounted light is a part of the weapons system and will only be used for illumination when, consistent with Department policy and training, members are justified in deploying the weapon.
  • B.Members authorized to carry a pistol-mounted flashlight will simultaneously carry the prescribed or auxiliary flashlight on their person at all times.
  • C.Members must complete a training and qualification course approved by the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division, to be authorized to carry a pistol-mounted light.
    NOTE:
    To continue authorization for the pistol-mounted light after completing the training and qualification course, members must have the pistol-mounted light attached to their weapon during the annual prescribed and alternate prescribed weapon qualification course.
III.Specifications
Pistol-mounted lights will be:
  • A.only utilized on pistols that have an integrated accessory rail molded into or permanently affixed to the dust cover (recoil-spring and guide-rod housing) by the pistol manufacturer during original production.
  • B.affixed to the pistol and carried in a Department-approved holster while the member is on duty. The pistol-mounted light will not be attached or removed from the pistol at any time during pistol deployment.
  • C.firmly attached to the pistol’s integrated accessory rail by means of a clamp attachment actuated by a push button or screw.
  • D.attached below and parallel to the muzzle of the pistol.
  • E.able to produce a minimum of 90 lumens with emitted light visible only in the white-light spectrum.
    NOTE:
    Laser targeting and laser/light “combo” systems are not authorized for Department use.
  • F.able to achieve a minimum continuous run time of 60 minutes.
  • G.powered by alkaline, lithium, or rechargeable batteries.
  • H.operated by a switching system that allows for “momentary on,” and may also allow for a “constant on” and/or a strobe function.
  • I.activated by a toggle, push button, or pressure switch.
  • J.not more than 4.5 ounces in weight, including batteries.
IV.Training
  • A.The Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division, will:
    • 1.develop and approve a training and qualification course for pistol-mounted lights.
    • 2.notify members when courses are made available.
    • 3.ensure that members successfully completing the course are entered into the CLEAR system.
  • B.Unit commanding officers will access the CLEAR system to verify members assigned and detailed to their unit who are authorized to carry a pistol-mounted light.
