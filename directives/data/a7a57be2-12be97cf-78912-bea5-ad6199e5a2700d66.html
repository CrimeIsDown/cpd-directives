I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the access policy for the headquarters building (HQ) and parking lot.
  • B.outlines roles and responsibilities for the maintenance of HQ building security.
  • C.assigns responsibility for the programming of Department identification cards.
  • D.continues the:
    • 1.use of the Visitor Log

II.Policy
As a public interest building, the 1st floor of the Central Police Headquarters (HQ) building is open to the public, Monday through Friday, during normal business hours. The remainder of the building is restricted in access to authorized Department members and visitors.
III.General Security Measures
  • A.All sworn and civilian Department employees are required to present their Department identification card to the officers at the security station to gain entry above the 1st floor. Department identification cards must be worn in plain view at all times while inside the HQ building. This includes sworn uniformed personnel.
  • B.It is the responsibility and duty of members assigned to the HQ building, to verbally challenge individuals, who are not displaying a Department identification card or visitor identification card

  • C.For every controlled access area there is only one command staff member who can authorize access to that restricted area. For example, one command staff member cannot authorize access to an area which is not under that command staff member’s direct control.
  • D.Those members directed to pick up payroll checks at the HQ building will enter via the double doors adjacent to the dock situated on the southwest quadrant next to the parking lot. Checks will be available at the Police Document Services Section check pick up room on the 1st floor.
  • E.Entry into the HQ building beyond the dock area via the dock area doorway is prohibited.
  • F.The Police Document Services Section will be responsible for all inquiries concerning delivery of packages to the HQ building.
    NOTE:
    Members receiving deliveries of packages which must be signed for will report to the Police Document Services Section to sign. Private sector messenger personnel will not be authorized access to the building beyond the 1st floor.
  • G.All 1st floor stairwell doors will be secured to preclude entry to the upper levels from the lobby area.
  • H.To facilitate an orderly entry to floors 2-5, individuals will enter the elevator lobby area from the west side corridor and present their identification to the officer(s) at the security station. Egress from the elevator bank area may be through the east corridor of the lobby.
  • I.Persons visiting the HQ building:
    • 1.will be required to use the Michigan Avenue entrance and pass through the magnetometer station located on the 1st floor and consent to a security screening search before gaining entrance to the elevators. All packages, briefcases, handbags, etc., carried by persons entering the HQ building through the magnetometer station security screening area are subject to a security screening check.
      NOTE:
      Civilian Office of Police Accountability (COPA) employees, City employees assigned to the HQ, City-contracted service personnel, retired Department members, and all sworn peace officers are exempt from this procedure.
    • 2.who withhold consent to a search or refuse to pass through the magnetometer station will not be permitted access beyond the HQ public access area.
  • J.Visitors will be required to present a government issued photo identification card (i.e., driver’s license, state I.D. card, passport) to the HQ security officer(s) at the security station. Visitor identification cards

  • L.Department members will meet their visitors in the HQ lobby and escort them to their intended destination. Upon completion of the visit, a member of the unit visited will ensure that visitors are escorted back to the security station. IV.Responsibilities
  • A.The Assistant Superintendent, Administration is responsible for:
    • 1.providing HQ building security and staffing the magnetometer station, the video security camera screening room, responding to requests for police service in the building and parking lot, and performing other duties as necessary.
      NOTE:
      In the event that the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) dispatches a patrol unit to an assignment at HQ, the officer(s) assigned by OEMC will perform the service.
    • 2.maintaining a Public Safety Information Technology generated listing of personnel who have received access into restricted areas of the HQ building.
  • B.The Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services is responsible for:
    • 1.establishing appropriate rules, regulations, and procedures which are necessary for the management of the physical HQ facility.
    • 2.assigning the 1st floor lockers to personnel assigned to the HQ security detail and the Police Document Services Section.
    • 3.establishing rules, as necessary, for the use of the 2nd floor physical fitness center.
    • 4.providing:
      • a.a numbered decal to authorized personnel for use of the parking lot.
      • b.interim parking placards
        Interim Parking Placard
        X
        a form issued by the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services, or designee allowing authorized personnel to use the HQ lot for thirty (30) day increments from the date of issuance.
        .
    • 5.designating spaces in the HQ parking lot.
    • 6.assigning the 2nd floor physical fitness center and basement lockers to Department personnel.
  • C.HQ building command staff members will:
    • 1.direct personnel within their command requiring access to restricted areas to:
      • a.complete the:
        • (1)Request for Identification Card form, obtaining appropriate signatures as indicated on the form.
        • (2)Request for Headquarters Secure Access Authorization form.
      • b.submit the Request for Headquarters Secure Access Authorization form through the chain of command to the authorizing command staff member as indicated on the form.
      • c.follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled, “Department Identification Cards and Photos” for replacement of lost cards.
    • 2.identify restricted area(s) under their control to which the member’s duties will require access.
    • 3.request access for themselves (or personnel under their command) by completing a To-From-Subject report when they require access to another unit’s controlled access area. In this case, the requesting command staff member will forward the To-From-Subject report through channels to the command staff member who is responsible for the subject access space.
      NOTE:
      The parking lot is the single exception to this policy. Each command staff member in the HQ building may approve requests for parking lot usage.
  • D.The Commander, Inspection Division will ensure the parking lot is monitored for compliance with this directive.
  • E.The Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology is responsible for programming Department identification cards to enter controlled access areas.
V.Off-Hours (1800-0600 hours daily and at all times on weekends and holidays)
  • A.All members, regardless of rank, who are reporting to their HQ units during a time when their unit is not normally in operation, will first report to the HQ security officer at the security station to announce their presence in the HQ building.
    NOTE:
    Should visiting members require access to restricted areas during non-traditional business hours (e.g., access to the Missing Persons Section) the officers assigned to HQ security will telephone the restricted area unit and inform the on-duty members to allow the visiting officers access. Such visitors will be required to sign the visitors’ log

    a record keeping document used to record the names of persons who visit the HQ building.
    maintained at the 1st floor security station.
  • B.Police personnel “hand delivering” reports to units which are closed will leave the reports at the Police Document Services Section on the 1st floor. These reports will be delivered by the Police Document Services Section.
VI.Parking Lot Use
  • A.The 35th Street entrance is the official gateway for entry into the HQ parking lot.
  • B.Vehicles will not be allowed to park in the HQ parking lot without a HQ parking decal

    X
    a decal affixed to a privately owned vehicle on the lower, driver’s side portion of the windshield for authorized personnel seeking to park in the HQ lot.
    or interim
    Interim Parking Placard

    /temporary parking placard
    Temporary Parking Placard
    X
    a form allowing authorized personnel to use the HQ lot for business visits of two (2) hours or less.
    .
    NOTE:
    Unmarked and marked Chicago Police Department vehicles are exempt from this policy.
  • C.Sworn and civilian members assigned to the HQ building who elect to park their personal vehicle in the HQ parking lot will:
    • 1.complete the Headquarters Parking Registration form,
    • 2.submit the Headquarters Parking Registration form to the Fleet Liaison Section, and
      NOTE:
      If a member uses more than one vehicle, a maximum of two (2) vehicles can be registered using a separate form for issuance of a decal for each vehicle. Motorcycles will not require a parking decal.
    • 3.upon receipt of the HQ parking decal, affix the decal to the lower left hand corner (driver’s side) of the vehicle’s windshield.
  • D.Sworn and civilian members temporarily detailed to the Field Services Section, Unit 166, who elect to park their personal vehicle in the HQ parking lot will:
    • 1.request an interim parking placard from the Fleet Liaison Section.
    • 2.upon receipt of the interim parking placard:
      • a.present the parking placard for inspection by the HQ security officer at the security station located at the entrance to the parking lot, and
      • b.display the placard in the windshield of the vehicle after parking in an available space.
    • 3.if applicable, request an additional interim parking placard after the initial interim parking placard expires.
  • E.Sworn and civilian members who elect to park their personal vehicle in the HQ parking lot that require a interim parking placard for special situations will:
    • 1.submit a To-From-Subject report with required justification, through channels, to the Fleet Liaison Section.
    • 2.upon receipt of the interim parking placard:
      • a.present the parking placard for inspection by the HQ security officer at the security station located at the entrance to the parking lot, and
      • b.display the placard in the windshield of the vehicle after parking in an available space.
    • 3.if applicable, request an additional interim parking placard after the initial interim parking placard expires.
  • F.Visitors entering the lot without a Department identification card (including members in Department vehicles) will first stop at the security station located at the entrance to the lot and wait for instructions from the HQ security officer. The HQ security officer will:
    • 1.issue the visitor a temporary parking placard which must be displayed in the windshield of the vehicle.
    • 2.direct the visitor where to park, or inform the member that no parking is available.
    • 3.accommodate special requests for visiting dignitaries and guests when requested by HQ personnel who have completed the Event Notification form.
  • G.Members entering the HQ parking lot will park on a “first come first served basis” in spaces indicated by white parking striping.
  • H.A limited number of reserved spaces have been specifically designated by the Bureau of Administrative Services and are indicated by yellow parking striping.
  • I.Members are prohibited from parking in:
    • 1.reserved parking spaces without authorization, and
    • 2.aisles, fire lanes, or areas of the parking lot that are not striped as designated parking spaces.
VII.Physical Fitness Center Lockers
  • A.Lockers in the male and female 2nd floor locker rooms will be assigned to sworn HQ members by the Bureau of Administrative Services.
  • B.Large lockers will be assigned to sworn members working in HQ and all command staff members, as available.
  • C.Civilian members may use the smaller lockers on a temporary basis not to exceed one (1) tour of duty at a time.
