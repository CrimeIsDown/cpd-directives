Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-07-11
Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System - Pilot Program
Issue Date:24 August 2015Effective Date:31 August 2015
Rescinds:Version issued on 30 July 2015
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System. The automated traffic crash reporting system is for completing, submitting and approving Illinois Traffic Crash Reports.
  • B.establishes procedures and responsibilities for the entry and approval of information into the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System.
  • C.introduces the Driver Information Exchange Card (CPD-22.122).
II.Scope
  • A.The pilot program is scheduled to become operational on 03 August 2015 for desk personnel in Districts 001, 015, 016, 018, 022 and Unit 608. Desk personnel will receive training 27 July - 03 August 2015.
  • B.Desk personnel from the remaining districts will receive training 31 August - 02 October 2015. The Automated Traffic Crash Reporting application will be available after desk personnel from each district have been trained.
  • C.The Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System is scheduled to be implemented Citywide 01 January 2016 and at that time will be available to all members of the Department.
  • D.The Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System will be used for Type “A" and Type "B" Traffic Crash Reports. Department members will follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Preliminary Investigations - Traffic Crashes” unless otherwise directed by this directive.
  • E.District desk personnel participating in the pilot program will receive training on the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System from the Information Services Division.
III.Officer Responsibilities
Reporting officers will:
  • A.adhere to all Department procedures and policies relative to completing a Traffic Crash Report.
  • B.select the Traffic Crash Report icon located on the Department intranet CLEAR homepage and login using their CLEAR user name (PC login).
  • C.obtain Records Division (RD) and Event numbers from the District desk Police Computer Aided Dispatch (PCAD) system or request RD and Event numbers from the Office of Emergency Management and Communication (OEMC).
  • D.complete an Automated Traffic Crash Report for each accident using the on-screen prompts and ensure:
    • 1.all involved unit information is complete.
    • 2.all applicable fields are completed. Fields not complete are indicated in the upper right corner of the Automated Traffic Crash Report screen.
    • 3.all Automated Traffic Crash Report reports have a crash diagram completed.
    • 4.completed reports are sent to the District Station Supervisor (DSS) inbox for approval.
  • E.notify DSS that report is complete and available for approval.
  • F.provide involved units a Driver Information Exchange Card (CPD-22.122) directing them to crash.chicagopolice.org to complete the Drive Information Exchange and the Illinois Motorist Report.
IV.District Station Supervisor Responsibilities
The District Station Supervisor will:
  • A.review the submitted Automated Traffic Crash Report for approval.
    NOTE:
    Upon approval, a copy of a SR 1050C, Illinois Traffic Crash Report is generated from the Automated Traffic Crash Report system and sent to the Illinois Department of Transportation (IDOT). When IDOT receives an Automated Traffic Crash Report, a control number is created and sent back to CPD for report identification and tracking.
  • B.ensure community members are provided with the appropriate support and information when requesting assistance with accessing and completing the Driver Information Exchange at crash.chicagopolice.org or the Illinois Motorist Report at http://motoristreport.illinois.gov.
  • C.ensure a copy of SR 1050C, Illinois Traffic Crash Report which includes the IDOT control number is printed and forwarded to the Records Division consistent with procedures as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Illinois Traffic Crash Reports."
V.Training
Training on the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System will be provided by ISD.
VI.Procedures When Access to the Automated Traffic Crash System is Not Available
  • A.If a member is unable to access the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System application on a Department computer, or other device, that member will:
    • 1.notify the DSS.
    • 2.notify the Help Desk at 4-DATA for assistance.
    • 3.complete a paper Illinois Traffic Crash Report following the procedures as outlined in the Department directive entitled “Preliminary Investigations - Traffic Crashes.”
  • B.In the event the Automated Traffic Crash Reporting System in not available, the Help Desk will notify all affected units via the AMC network with information regarding the expected duration of the outage.
VII.Resources
Districts will maintain a supply of the Driver Information Exchange Card (CPD-22.122) available from the Equipment and Supply Section.
