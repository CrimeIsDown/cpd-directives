Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-17
Parking Meters
Issue Date:12 April 1996Effective Date:13 April 1996
Rescinds:S96-03
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures for handling parking meters that are damaged, defective or inventoried by a Department member.
II.General Information
  • A.The Department of Revenue /Bureau of Parking will be notified whenever a member inventories a parking meter and/or meter parts. This notification will be made during normal business hours. After normal business hours, notification will be made to the Bureau of Streets and Sanitation, Communication Control.
  • B.A Department member may notify the Department of Revenue/Bureau of Parking by using the City Service Request form (CPD-21.208 ) to report:
    • 1.damaged/vandalized meters or meter parts,
    • 2.broken/malfunctioning meters, or
    • 3.missing or illegible meter numbers.
III.Inventory Procedures
Whenever a sworn member comes into possession of a meter and /or meter parts, the member will:
  • A.inventory the items according to established procedures. The green copy (copy 4) of the Property Inventory will be mailed via intra-Departmental mail to the Department of Revenue/Bureau of Parking.
  • B.notify the Department of Revenue/Bureau of Parking or the Bureau of Streets and Sanitation, Communication Control, of the recovery and provide them with the inventory number and location where the recovered property is held.
    NOTE:
    Department members will inform the Department of Revenue/Bureau of Parking that release of their property will not be made until said property is no longer needed in future court proceedings.
IV.Parking Meter Violations
  • A.When issuing a Hang-on citation for a parking meter violation, the member will ensure that the meter number is entered on the citation in the appropriate box.
  • B.When posted signs conflict with, or differ from information posted within the window of the meter, the member will be governed by the signs.
  • C.If a citizen disputes a citation issued for a parking meter violation, the member will advise the citizen to follow the instructions on the citation and/or call the  Department of Revenue

V.ParkMagic Program
  • A.Beginning on 25 September 2007, the ParkMagic Pilot Program will go into effect. This program allows motorists to pay by cell phone for parking meters and Pay and Display areas. This program is a pilot program being run by the Department of Revenue and will last approximately six months.
  • B.If a motorist is using a ParkMagic device, the device will be placed on the dashboard on the passenger side of their vehicle. When the ParkMagic device is active, it will display a green flashing light. The screen on the device will display the zone number, date, and expiration time. A motorist may also have a decal placed on the rear window in the top middle section identifying their vehicle as possibly having a ParkMagic Pilot Program device. This decal is not required to be placed on the vehicle in order for a motorist to use the ParkMagic device.
  • C.Prior to issuing a citation for a meter violation, Department members will ensure that there is not an active ParkMagic device on the dashboard and/or the meter is expired.
  • D.Parking meters and Pay and Display boxes throughout the City display stickers that reflect the parking zone number. ParkMagic users are responsible for ensuring the proper amount is paid for the zone in which the vehicle is parked. The following is a chart of the parking zones within the City of Chicago:
    Zone NumberZone Sticker ColorCostHourly Rate
    1Blue$.25/5 minutes$3.00/hour
    2Orange$.25/10 minutes$1.50/hour
    3Red$.25/15 minutes$1.00/hour
    4Green$.25/20 minutes$0.75/hour
    5Purple$.25/30 minutes$0.50/hour
    6Yellow$.25/60 minutes$0.25/hour
  • E.Officers may issue a citation for the appropriate meter violation if the motorist has underpaid the required fee by parking in a higher cost zone than is displayed on the ParkMagic device. (Example: The ParkMagic device displays payment for Zone 6 but the vehicle is parked in the higher cost in Zone 1.)
  • F.Officers will not issue a citation if the motorist has overpaid the required fee. (Example: The device displays payment for Zone 2 but the vehicle is parked in the lower cost Zone 5.)
