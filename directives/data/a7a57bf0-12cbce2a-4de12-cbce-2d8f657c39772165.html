Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-03-05
Automated Daily Attendance and Assignment Record
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:28 October 2010 Version; A10-04
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues the Automated Daily Attendance and Assignment (A&A) Record.
  • B.delineates the procedures and responsibilities of Department members assigned to complete the Daily Attendance and Assignment Record.
  • C.continues the Automated Daily Attendance and Assignment Record User Guide, available on the Department intranet.
II.General Information
  • A.The Automated Daily A&A Record is implemented to facilitate and streamline the process of completing the daily A&A records.
  • B.The Automated Daily A&A Record application system is effective citywide.
  • C.Department members’ social security numbers will not be included on the automated A&A record.
  • D.An Automated Daily A&A Record User Guide will be available in print and online with information to complete the process for Department members assigned to complete the daily A&A records.
  • E.The Automated Daily A&A Record system can be updated after final status. At the same time, this system will capture the user ID information and the date and time of the update.
  • F.The “search / view” screen option on the application will allow viewing only to designated users (e.g., Finance Division, Bureau of Internal Affairs).
III.Responsibilities
  • A.The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will:
    • 1.review, update, approve, and reject the Automated A&A Record as delineated by the Department directive entitled “Payroll and Timekeeping.”
    • 2.designate temporary role assignments, if necessary, for Department members to perform tasks in the automated daily A&A record application.
      NOTE:
      Re-approval is not required within a 24-hour period. A comment field will be completed if an A&A record sheet is updated.
    • 3.ensure that ALL Department personnel present for their tour under their command have been marked “Present” in their regularly scheduled tour of duty. Those not present require an absence code. A “start time” and “end time” are mandatory. Day off groups and furlough selection, as defined in the Watch Personnel System, must be kept up-to-date as required.
      NOTE:
      If the start and end times have been entered in the watch application, they will automatically populate on the newly generated A&A sheet.
    • 4.ensure that automated daily A&A records are saved to “Preliminary Status” by the designee within one hour of the start of each watch (i.e., 0800, 1600, and 0000 hours).
      NOTE:
      Automated daily A&A records saved to “Preliminary Status” can be updated prior to submission to the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor.
  • B.The timekeeper will:
    • 1.review, update, and change the A&A’s and submit the A&A’s for final status.
      NOTE:
      Once the A&A record is in final status, this function is considered complete.
    • 2.if the original automated A&A record is incorrect and submitted to the Finance Division, make an immediate notification to the Finance Division.
    • 3.when a member is incorrectly recorded or omitted on the automated A&A record due to change of unit or detail, request through the appropriate Bureau Chief, via telephone AND a To-From-Subject report, the appropriate change to the unit’s automated A&A record.
IV.Procedures in the Event of Automated Daily A&A Record Application Unavailability
  • A.If a member is unable to access the automated daily A&A record application on a Department computer, he or she will telephone the Help Desk for assistance.
  • B.In the event of system unavailability, the Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT) Group will notify all affected units via facsimile message with information regarding the expected duration of the unavailability.
  • C.When a station supervisor / designated unit supervisor has determined or been notified that an Automated Daily A&A Record application unavailability has occurred, he or she will:
    • 1.determine if the unavailability is the result of a planned service interruption conducted by the PSIT.
    • 2.if not a preplanned event, ensure a notification is made to the Help Desk.
    • 3.follow the instructions by PSIT Help Desk personnel relative to procedures for completing the A&A record during the system unavailability.
    • 4.direct the timekeeper to comply with the Department directive entitled “Payroll and Timekeeping.”
