Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U04-01-14
Ballistic Vest Update and Inspection; Authorization of Black Vest Cover
Issue Date:14 October 2005Effective Date:14 October 2005
Rescinds:D05-40
Index Category:Uniform and Personal Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members that bulletproof / bullet-resistant ballistic panels manufactured with Zylon® or any Zylon® base component are not approved for duty use by any sworn member.
  • B.advises members who purchased a Zylon® or Zylon® base ballistic vest panel to contact the manufacturer for specific information regarding their recall policy.
  • C.informs all sworn members that as part of the winter uniform inspection, a visible inspection of ballistic panels will be conducted.
  • D.informs Department members that the authorized black ballistic vest carrier is approved as an outer garment. The black ballistic vest carrier can be worn over the uniform shirt or approved uniform sweater.
II.General Information
  • A.After testing, the U.S. Department of Justice's Body Armor Safety Initiative (BASI) determined that the effectiveness of Zylon® - a lightweight material used in the manufacture of soft body armor - degrades quickly and severely compromises the ability of the vest to prevent penetration of the ammunition it is designed to stop.
  • B.Second Chance Body Armor and Toyobo Inc. have been named as defendants in a class action lawsuit alleging the failure of ballistic vests containing Zylon® fiber to adequately protect the vest wearer as identified by the threat level designated for the vest. This legal action affects all persons and entities in the United States and its territories that have purchased, possess, or own ballistic vest panels manufactured by Second Chance which contain Zylon® fiber. A settlement has been reached between defendants Toyobo Co. Ltd. and Toyobo American Inc. and the certified class of consumers who purchased Second Chance Ultima®, Ultimax®, and Triflex® ballistic vests.
  • C.Department members who wish to obtain more information regarding the rights of class action members can contact the claims administrator at (877) 567-2754 or check the FOP website at www.grandlodgefop.org for links to updates on the class action lawsuit.
III.Member Responsibilities
  • A.All sworn members of the Chicago Police Department will maintain a current item of ballistic-resistant soft body armor that meets or exceeds Threat Level II. Threat Level II is the minimum threat level required for body armor worn by sworn members of the Chicago Police Department.
    NOTE:
    Explanation of the different levels of threat protection can be found in Item V of this directive.
  • B.Department members will inspect their soft body armor to ensure that the ballistic panels are in compliance with current Uniform Specifications. As the specifications provide only for ballistic panels containing aramid fibers, such as Kevlar®, ballistic panels containing Zylon® fibers, such as the Second Chance Ultima®, Ultimax®, and Triflex® ballistic vests, are not authorized for wearing by Department members.
    • 1.Members possessing Second Chance ballistic panels containing Zylon® fibers will contact the claims administrator at (877) 567-2754 for information on replacement of their body armor.
    • 2.Members possessing ballistic panels containing Zylon® fibers which were not manufactured by Second Chance will contact the ballistic manufacturer immediately for directions regarding the company’s recall policy of the vest panels.
  • C.During the Department winter uniform inspection, in addition to uniform items of wearing apparel, all sworn members will present the following items to their unit commanding officer for inspection:
    • 1.Both front and back ballistic vest panels, exposed with appropriate identification patches as indicated in Item IV below, attached by the manufacturer on both.
    • 2.Ballistic panel carrier.
    • 3.Outer vest carrier, if vest is to be worn outside the uniform shirt or approved uniform sweater.
      • a.Outer black vest carriers which satisfy the requirements listed in Item III-C-3-b have been added as an optional outer garment for all sworn members.
      • b.To be approved, a black vest carrier will:
        • (1)be made of cordura nylon.
        • (2)either:
          • (a)mirror the uniform shirt with optional lower slit pockets, or
          • (b)be MOLLE compatible.
        • (3)not have any firearm holster sewn on or attached to it.
        • (4)be maintained, replaced, or repaired to ensure a professional look.
      • c.Outer black vest carriers can be worn year round.
      • d.The approved black ballistic vest carrier can be worn over the approved sweater. The following restrictions apply:
        • (1)the sweater will be tucked into the waist band of the trouser, and
        • (2)the sweater/vest combination will not be worn with the tie, and
        • (3)all other specifications regarding the approved sweater and black ballistic carrier will be followed.
  • D.The unit commanding officer will inspect each member’s ballistic panels to ensure that they meet uniform specifications. Members who present panels which do not meet uniform specifications will be advised to replace such panels with panels which afford the wearer protection equivalent to a Threat Level II.
IV.Soft Body Armor Identification
According to specifications, every ballistic panel will have a label permanently attached to either exterior surface of the panel. The label will contain the following information, written in the English language.
  • A.Name, logo, or other identification of the manufacturer.
  • B.The rated level of protection, according to the section 2.0 of the National Institute of Justice (NIJ) Standard (for Chicago Police Department members, Type II in accordance with NIJ Standard-0101.04).
  • C.Size (if custom fitted, provisions for the name of the individual for whom it is made).
  • D.Lot number.
  • E.Date of manufacture.
  • F.Date of issue line, to be filled in by the user.
  • G.A model designation that uniquely identifies the panel for purchasing purposes (panels designed to fit male and female torsos must have separate model designations).
  • H.Strike face or wear face indicator (i.e., the surface of the garment that is to face the threat or to be worn next to the body must be identified).
  • I.Serial number and the individual’s employee number for whom the vest has been supplied.
  • J.Care instructions for the ballistic material in accordance with 16 CFR 423(Part 423, Care Labeling of Textile Wearing Apparel and Certain Piece Goods, as amended by Federal Trade Commission Regulation Rule, effective January 2, 1984).
  • K.For Type I through Type IIIA body armor, a warning in twice the size of the rest of the type on the label, exclusive of the information required in Item IV-A above, stating that the armor is not intended to protect the wearer from rifle fire and, if applicable, that the armor is not intended to protect the wearer from sharp edges or pointed instruments.
    NOTE:
    Printing color changes are acceptable but cannot be substituted for the type size requirement.
  • L.For armor that has been successfully tested for compliance to standards through the NIJ voluntary Compliance Testing Office (CTO) at an NIJ testing facility, the following statement will be included on the post test production label: “The manufacturer certifies that this model of armor has been tested through NLECTC (National Law Enforcement Corrections and Technology Center) and has been found to comply with Type (insert appropriate type designation for NIJ Standards 0101.04).” This compliance statement cannot appear on armor that has failed NIJ compliance testing or on armor that has not been tested for compliance as specified by the NIJ standards.
    NOTE:
    Acceptable formats for pretest and post-test labels may be obtained by contacting the NIJ CTO.
V.Armor Classification and Body Armor Threat Levels
  • A.The level of threat protection required of soft body armor worn by Department members is Threat Level II, which offers protection against 9mm, full metal jacketed round nose (FMJ RN) bullets, with nominal masses of 8.0g (124 br), impacting at a minimum velocity of 358 meters per second (m/s), 1175 feet per second (ft/s)or less, and 357 magnum, jacketed soft point (JSP) bullets, with nominal masses of 10.2g (158 gr), impacting at a velocity 427 m/s, 1400 ft/s, or less.
  • B.NIJ Standard 0101.04 Ballistic Chart/Types of Threat Levels
    • 1.Type I: Protects against .22 caliber, long rifle lead round nose (LR LRN) bullets and ACP full metal jacketed round nose (FMJ RN) bullets.
    • 2.Type IIA: Protects against 9mm FMJ RN bullets and 40 caliber S&W FMJ bullets. It also provides protection against Type I threat levels.
    • 3.Type II (CPD requirement): Protects against 9mm FMJ RN bullets and .357 magnum JSP bullets. It also provides protection against Type I and Type IIA level threats.
    • 4.Type IIIA: Protects against high velocity 9mm FMJ RN bullets and .44 magnum Semi-Jacketed Hollow Point bullets. It also provides protection against Type I, Type IIA, and Type II level threats.
    • 5.Type III: Protects against 7.62 FMJ bullets (U.S. Military designation M80). It also provides protection against Type I, Type IIA, Type II, and IIIA level threats.
    • 6.Type IV: Protects against .30 caliber armor piercing bullets (U.S. Military M2 AP). It also provides protection against Type I, IIA, II, IIIA, and Type III level threats.
