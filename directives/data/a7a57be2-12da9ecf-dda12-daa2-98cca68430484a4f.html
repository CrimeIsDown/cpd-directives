Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U01-10
Department Telephones
Issue Date:12 November 1993Effective Date:13 November 1993
Rescinds:S93-20
Index Category:Department Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines policy and procedures for the use of Department telephones.
  • B.initiates the use of the Cook County State's Attorneys Office Grand Jury Notice of Disclosure Form.
II.Policy
  • A.Telephones are available in Department facilities for conducting official business. The use of Department telephones for personal calls is prohibited. Supervisors will ensure that the use of telephones is restricted to Department business.
  • B.Since a significant amount of police business is handled by telephone, it is essential that telephone contacts be of the same high quality and professional manner as face-to-face contacts.
III.Pax Telephones
The PAX telephone system is an internal and secure communications network which provides service to Department offices and facilities. It can also be utilized to communicate directly with numerous Municipal, County, State and Federal agencies with whom the Department interacts on a regular basis. The private line Bell telephones have been replaced with the Centrex System.
IV.Centrex System
  • A.Every unit within the Department or any other City Department that has "312-74" as the first five digits (centrex telephone) can be dialed directly. When dialing within the centrex system, dial the last five digits.
  • B.The Centrex II concept eliminates delays on incoming telephone calls by allowing the transfer of incoming telephone calls directly, bypassing the necessity of going through a switchboard.
V.Telephone Number Listings
  • A.Telephone numbers and subscriber information will be provided to Department members for official police operations only. The following classifications are used by the phone company.
    • 1.Subscriber - the person identified by name in the telephone company records as responsible for a specific telephone number. An officer may contact the phone company to obtain subscriber info. This number can be obtained by utilizing the phone lines in the watch commanders office which have been designated for Directory Assistance / Customer Name and Address Service.
    • 2.Unlisted number - a telephone number for which the subscriber is not listed in any public telephone directories but may be available through the directory assistance operator.
    • 3.Non-published number - an unlisted telephone number that is not available through the directory assistance operator. Department members may request non-published telephone information only when necessary to conduct official Department operations.
  • B.Requests for non-published telephone numbers or additional subscriber information will be processed as follows:
    • 1.Requests for non-published telephone numbers or additional subscriber information will be made only by district watch commander or unit supervisors in charge.
    • 2.Members assigned to investigations in which there are immediate emergency investigative needs for non-published telephone information will request that this information be obtained from the Office of Emergency Management and Communication (OEMC) by their watch commander or unit supervisor in charge.
      • a.Watch commanders or unit supervisors in charge will:
        • (1)contact the OEMC, via PAX and state the reason for the request, type of case, case RD number, information sought and their name, star number, unit of assignment and PAX telephone number.
        • (2)approve and forward to the Chief, Detective Division, Attention: Telephone Company Liaison Officer prior to the end of the tour of duty during which the request was made:
          • (a)a To-From-Subject report, prepared by the requesting officer, confirming the emergency telephonic request including the RD number, date, location and type of investigation.
          • (b)a Cook County State's Attorneys Office Grand Jury Notice of Disclosure Form, prepared by the requesting officer, for presentation to the Grand Jury, listing all law enforcement and government personnel who, in the performance of their sworn duty to enforce criminal law, would be deemed necessary by the State's Attorney to have access to or have disclosed unto them the restricted telephone information sought via Grand Jury Subpoena.
            NOTE:
            The listing will include the name of the Superintendent of Police, Chief, Detective Division, affected area commander, unit commanding officer, supervising sergeants, and requesting and investigating detectives. If other police personnel will be affected, include the line all other involved police personnel who may be assigned to the investigation at a later date. Also list any member of an outside law enforcement agency or any State's Attorney who is already involved in the investigation.
          • (c)an original Felony Minutes Form (101's), prepared by the requesting officer, listing the name of the subject of the investigation about whom the information is being sought. If the suspects name is not known, insert the pseudonym, John Doe, on the offender line. The Felony Minute form submitted must contain specific and detailed information as to why the information being requested, via subpoena, is pertinent to the criminal investigation in question.
      • b.A separate set of the To-From-Subject report, the Felony Minutes and the Disclosure Form will be submitted with each request for information for a specific subscriber whether by name or RD number. RD numbers and names from one investigation will not be utilized to request information relating to a different investigation.
      • c.An immediate emergency investigative need is defined as an emergency situation wherein there is an immediate threat to life, property, or circumstances in which time is of the essence, e.g., HBT incident, bomb threat, missing-found or abducted child, or an immediate arrest situation for a serious felony.
    • 3.Members assigned to an investigation in which there is no immediate investigative need for non-published telephone information will request that their watch commander or unit commanding officer obtain the necessary information from the Office of the Chief of the Detective Division in the following manner:
      • a.Watch commanders or unit commanding officers will approve and forward to the Chief, Detective Division, Attention: Telephone Company Liaison Officer: those items enumerated in Items V-B-2-a-(2)-(a), (b) and (c) of this directive.
      • b.Information requests for non-emergency investigations will ordinarily only be made by watch commanders or unit commanding officers of units which have the specific follow-up investigative responsibility for the case being investigated.
    • 4.The Office of Emergency Management and Communications will:
      • a.process and respond to emergency requests for non-published telephone information.
      • b.prepare and retain an Unlisted Telephone Information Request card (CPD-32.521)
      • c.forward a duplicate copy of the Unlisted Telephone Information Request to the Chief, Detective Division, Attention: Telephone Company Liaison Officer.
    • 5.The Detective Divisions Telephone Company Liaison Officer will:
      • a.process and respond to all non-emergency requests for non-published telephone information.
      • b.maintain a supply of and distribute as necessary to those units within the Department requiring them (i.e., all Bureau of Patrol districts and Detective Division areas), copies of the Cook County State's Attorneys Office Grand Jury Notice of Disclosure Form.
      • c.present a copy of the appropriate To-From-Subject report(s), Cook County State's Attorneys Office Grand Jury Notice of Disclosure Form(s) and the original Felony Minutes Forms to the Cook County Grand Jury for issuance of a subpoena.
      • d.serve the issued subpoena on the electronic communication service company named in the subpoena.
VI.Long Distance Telephone Calls
  • A.Long Distance telephone calls made by using Department Centrex telephone facilities will be limited to official Department business.
  • B.It is general practice for a law enforcement agency to pay for a collect call when receiving a service. It is also general practice for a law enforcement agency to refuse a collect call when it is providing a service. Generally, the Department does not accept collect calls except those from other law enforcement agencies.
  • C.Members responsibilities
    Department members will:
    • 1.obtain authorization from their supervisor / commanding officer before making a long distance telephone call or accepting a collect telephone call.
    • 2.make authorized long distance telephone calls using a designated long distance access Centrex telephone extension.
    • 3.complete the required information on the Telephone Control Log (CPD-32.705) whenever making a long distance telephone call to or receiving a collect call from outside the 312 / 708 area codes.
    • 4.inform the supervisor / commanding officer responsible for a Centrex extension of the necessity of making a long distance telephone call when making the call from a designated long distance Centrex extension which is not chargeable to their commanding officer.
    • 5.be required to pay for any unauthorized long distance telephone call(s) and may be subject to disciplinary action.
  • D.Telephone Control Log
    The Telephone Control Log(s) will be:
    • 1.utilized by all commanding officers to assist in the review and approval of Centrex long distance telephone expenses.
    • 2.retained in accordance with existing Records Retention requirements.
    • 3.available from the Equipment and Supplies Section.
  • E.Unit commanding officer's responsibilities
    Unit commanding officers will review and compare the Telephone Control Log with the monthly Centrex Billing Information report provided by the Bureau of Administrative Services to verify the business necessity of each call.
VII.Telephone Calls to Office of Emergency Management and Communications by Department Members
  • A.Calls for service
    Request for service which are received at a police facility from a citizen either in person or by telephone will:
    • 1.be relayed to the OEMC through the use of the emergency number (911), the direct line between district desks and radio zone consoles or personal radio when the immediate, on-the-scene presence of the police is necessary.
    • 2.be relayed to the OEMC via the direct line between the district desk and the radio zone console when the call is of a non-emergency nature which requires on-the-scene presence by district law enforcement personnel.
  • B.Administrative Messages
    • 1.All requests to broadcast administrative messages to field units (e.g., "call the station," "go in for service," "meet a police officer," etc.) will be directed to the appropriate radio zone via direct line or the units city wide frequency dispatcher.
    • 2.Telephone requests and notifications from Department members will be made via telephone when the:
      • a.nature of the request for information is not appropriate for broadcast over police radios.
      • b.information requested cannot be obtained elsewhere.
    • 3.The OEMC has responsibility for notification to other Department units or agencies outside the Department.
  • C.Records Division (RD) Numbers
    Under certain circumstances (walk-in complaints at police facilities, arrest situations, etc.) it may be necessary to contact OEMC via telephone to obtain a Records Division number if no other means for obtaining an RD number is available.
  • D.Personal Messages
    Requests for radio broadcasts of personal messages, or to call telephone numbers outside the Department will not be accepted by the OEMC. Such requests will be referred to the district or unit desk of the concerned member.
  • E.Status of Beat Units
    • 1.Telephone requests from patrol beat personnel affecting the units status will not be accepted by the OEMC. All such request must be made by radio.
    • 2.Patrol beat units will be taken out of service only on orders of the unit's watch commander.
