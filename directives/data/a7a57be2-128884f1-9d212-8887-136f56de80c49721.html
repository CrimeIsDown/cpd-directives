Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-02-05
Payroll and Timekeeping – Working Out of Grade
Issue Date:27 September 1994Effective Date:29 September 1994
Rescinds:G94-05-05
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.Sworn Policy
  • A.Working out of grade
    • 1.A member is considered to have worked out of grade when the member has:
      • a.been authorized by the appropriate authorizing officer to perform the duties of a higher grade than the actual grade of the member.
      • b.performed substantially all the duties and assumed substantially all the responsibilities of the higher grade for a minimum of two hours.
      • c.been assigned the out-of-grade D3 duties of a "Deceased Person Removal" by:
        • (1)the assignment from an appropriate supervisor as identified under Item I-B-4 of this directive; and
        • (2)actually performing the duties of physically handling and transporting the body of a deceased person consistent with the directive entitled "Processing and Transportation of Deceased Persons;" and
        • (3)submitting an Out of Grade Work Authorization form (CPD-11.639) consistent with the guidelines and required approvals under this directive. Additionally, the approving supervisor will ensure that the corresponding Records Division (RD) number for the incident is recorded on the Out of Grade Work Authorization form.
    • 2.No member will be authorized to work out of grade unless circumstances require that the position be filled. The authorizing officer must determine if not filling a vacancy would have an adverse effect on unit operations.
  • B.Authorization to work out of grade
    • 1.An Out of Grade Work Authorization form (CPD-11.639) must be completed by the appropriate authorizing officer before a member will be allowed to work out of grade or compensated with out of grade pay.
      • a.When a watch commander / unit commanding officer intends to authorize a member to work out of grade for a future date, the Out of Grade Work Authorization will be submitted to the unit command staff officer for his approval before the member is allowed to work out of grade.
      • b.When circumstances require that a watch commander / unit commanding officer authorize a member to work out of grade because the position must be filled immediately, the Out of Grade Work Authorization will be submitted to the member's unit command staff officer for his review and signature.
      • c.The signature of the unit command staff officer is required on all Out of Grade Work Authorization forms.
      • d.The unit command staff officer will forward the Out of Grade Work Authorization to the unit timekeeper.
    • 2.When a member is authorized to work out of grade for a number of consecutive days, within a police period, only one Out of Grade Work Authorization need be completed.
    • 3.The authorization officer for the various out of grade positions is as follows:

    • 4.Any sworn supervisor may authorize no more than two nonprobationary D1, D2, and/or D2A sworn members to perform the out-of-grade D3 duties of a "Deceased Person Removal" consistent with Item I-A-1-c of this directive. A sworn supervisor of the rank of lieutenant or higher may authorize the out-of-grade assignment of more than two nonprobationary D1, D2, and/or D2A sworn members when such additional personnel are necessary to physically handle and transport a deceased person due to the size, weight, location, or other conditions of the deceased person's body, or the unusual nature of the incident.
      NOTE:
      When additional deceased person removals are necessary within a district during the same regular tour of duty of a watch when two members have already been approved to work the out-of-grade D3 duties of a "Deceased Person Removal," supervisors will normally direct such additional removals to the members previously assigned to work out-of-grade, unless such assignment is inconsistent with the most efficient use of Department resources.
  • C.Authorized Out of Grade Positions
    • 1.Police officers, excluding probationary police officers, will be compensated for an eight hour tour of duty consistent with the member's tenure at the rate of pay for the position being filled when authorized to perform the following duties for a minimum of two or more hours within a single eight hour tour of duty:
      • a.sergeant
      • b.patrol specialist
      • c.automotive pound supervisor.
      NOTE:
      A police officer required to work overtime while working out of grade as a sergeant will be compensated with the pay differential between his actual grade and the higher grade for the overtime worked. In addition, the officer will be compensated for the overtime worked at the rate of time and one-half in either compensatory time or payment at his regular rate of pay in completed 15 minute segments. The member will not be credited with the one-half hour of administrative overtime credited to supervisors.

      A police officer required to work overtime while working out of grade as a patrol specialist, or automotive pound supervisor will be compensated with the pay differential between his actual grade and the higher grade at the rate of time and one-half for the overtime worked. In addition, the officer will be compensated for the overtime worked at the rate of time and one-half in either compensatory time or payment at his regular rate of pay in completed 15 minute segments.
    • 2.Non-command staff supervisory members will be compensated for an eight hour tour of duty, consistent with the member's tenure at the rate of pay for the position being filled when authorized to perform the following duties for a minimum of two or more hours within a single, eight-hour tour of duty.
      • a.A sergeant may be authorized to perform the duties of a district field lieutenant or a non-command staff unit commanding officer.
      • b.A lieutenant may be authorized to perform the duties of a district watch commander, a non-command staff unit commanding officer of the rank of captain or a command staff rank.
      • c.A captain may be authorized to perform the duties of a command staff rank.
      • d.Sergeants will receive one-half hour administrative overtime and lieutenants and captains will receive three-quarters of an hour administrative overtime while working out of grade.
      NOTE:
      A supervisory member required to work overtime while working out of grade as a lieutenant or captain will be compensated on an hour for hour basis. A supervisory member required to work overtime while working out of grade as a command staff member will be compensated on an hour for hour basis only when the duties which were performed would have required the presence of the command staff member.
    • 3.Nonprobationary D1, D2, and/or D2A sworn members assigned, consistent with the provisions of this directive, to the out-of-grade duties of "Deceased Person Removal" will be compensated at the D3 rate of pay consistent with the member's tenure for an eight-hour tour of duty without regard to the length of time necessary for the member to perform these duties. However, any overtime required to be worked by the member, whether related to the deceased person removal or other duties, will be compensated for the overtime worked in completed 15 minute segments at the member's normal rate of pay consistent with the relevant provisions of the applicable collective bargaining agreement.
  • D.Restrictions
    • 1.A member authorized to work out of grade will not be allowed to use compensatory time during the tour of duty except in an emergency.
    • 2.A command staff rank will not be filled unless the absence of the command staff member is to be for an extended period of time and the vacancy would impact on the operations of the unit.
  • E.Unit timekeeper responsibilities

    When a member has worked out of grade, the unit timekeeper will;
    • 1.indicate the out of grade position worked in the "Temporary Change of Watch / Assignment or Additional Assignment Explanation" column of the Attendance and Assignment Record (CPD-11.605).
    • 2.not record the number of hours worked in an out of grade position on the Time and Attendance Record (CPD-11.602) unless an Out of Grade Work Authorization has been received.
    • 3.retain the Out of Grade Work Authorization for two and one-half years.
II.Civilian Policy
  • A.General Information
    • 1.Civilian members may be authorized to work out of grade when:
      • a.circumstances require that a vacant budgeted position be filled or,
      • b.a member in a budgeted position will be absent for an extended period of time.
    • 2.Civilian members will not be authorized to work out of grade in a job title:
      • a.excluded from union representation.
      • b.covered by a different bargaining unit agreement.
      • c.not authorized in the unit's budget.
      • d.in which an employee is on layoff status.
    • 3.Pending the approval of the unit commanding officer of command staff rank, a watch commander or civilian equivalent can only authorize a civilian member to work out of grade for a maximum of 5 days.
    • 4.Chicago Accounting and Purchasing System code number necessary for completing the "Acting In a Higher Rated Position-Civilian" form (CPD-11.646) can be found in the Item III of this directive.
  • B.AFSCME Members

    The AFSCME bargaining agreement requires that when a civilian member is directed to perform and is held accountable for substantially all of the duties and responsibilities of a higher rated job title for more than ten days, the member shall be paid at the higher rate for all such time, retroactive to the first day of the assignment.
    • 1.The time limit for working out of grade is 90 days, except where the civilian member whose position is being filled is on a leave of absence, in which case it shall be six months. The time limit may be extended by mutual agreement of the Department and the Union.
    • 2.Unit commanding officers will equitably rotate out of grade assignments on the basis of seniority among the members who have the ability to perform the job without further training. However, unit commanding officers will not rotate members in order to circumvent the out of grade payment.
    • 3.If the member is required to work out of grade in a vacant budgeted position beyond 90 days or, six months, whichever is applicable, the Department will post and fill the position as a permanent vacancy as specified in Section 12.7 of the AFSCME bargaining agreement.
  • C.UNIT II Members

    The UNIT II bargaining agreement requires that a civilian member who is directed to perform and who is held accountable for substantially all of the duties and responsibilities of a higher rated job title for more than ten days will be paid at the step above their current rate of pay for all such time, retroactive to the first day of the assignment.
    • 1.The Unit II agreement does not restrict the length of the out of grade assignment.
    • 2.Unit commanding officers will not arbitrarily remove members from the higher classification solely to circumvent the out of grade payment.
  • D.Procedures
    • 1.The unit commanding officer of command staff rank will submit a completed "Acting in a Higher Rated Position-Civilian" form to his bureau deputy superintendent for approval no later than 7 days after the out of grade assignment began when it is determined that a civilian member is likely to be absent for more than 10 days, or the duration of the absence is uncertain and the member's job title must be filled by another member in a lower rated job title. For an out of grade assignment which is certain to be 10 days or less (e.g., days off, vacation, medical absence, etc.) an "Acting in a Higher Rated Position-Civilian" form need not be completed.
    • 2.The "Acting in a Higher Rated Position-Civilian" form will be completed as follows:
      Employee Data:Self explanatory.
      Current Title Data:Self explanatory.
      Fund: Preprinted number. No entry required.
      Actv:Insert unit budget number, i.e., 4126, 4173, etc.
      Sect. No.: Applies only to Data Systems Division, i.e., 01, 02, 03, 04.
      Barg. Unit:I, II, III or IV as appropriate.
      Title Code:Same as indicated on the Attendance and Assignment Record (CPD-11.605).
      Description/Position Title:Same as indicated on the Attendance and Assignment Record.
      Grade: Self explanatory.
      Payroll Number:Enter 1180 in this box.
      Current Pay Rate:Enter the monthly rate in the Amount box and the annual rate in the "Annual Amount" box. Box "FR" is to be left blank.
      Higher Rated Title Date:This portion of the form is to be completed in accordance with the instructions provided in the "Current Title Data" portion of the form.
      Justification:Provide sufficient information as to why the member must work out of grade by indicating the impact on unit and/or Department operations if the position is not filled.
      Signatures: Self explanatory.
    • 3.The bureau deputy superintendent will review the form and:
      • a.If approved, forward;
        • (1)a copy to the unit commanding officer of command staff rank.
        • (2)the original to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services.
      • b.if disapproved, return the form to the unit commanding officer of command staff rank indicating the reason for that disapproval.
    • 4.The Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services, will review the form for completeness and accuracy and, if approved, sign the form and forward it to the Office of Budget and Management, City Hall, Room 604.
    • 5.The unit commanding officer of command staff rank will ensure that the:
      • a.copy of the approved form is filed in the unit. The form will be retained in the unit for two and one-half years.
      • b.unit commanding officer, if different from the command staff member, is notified of the approval or disapproval of the out of grade authorization.
    • 6.Unit commanding officers will ensure that when a member is authorized to work out of grade, the out of grade title is recorded in the "Temporary Change of Watch Assignment Or Additional Assignment Explanation Column" on the Attendance and Assignment Record (CPD-11.605) and that the out of grade hours are recorded on the member's Time and Attendance Record (CPD-11.602) in accordance with the provisions of the Department directive entitled "Civilian Time and Attendance Record."
    • 7.When a unit receives a copy of the Acting in a Higher Rated Position Form (OBM-103) containing all required approvals for a member to work out of grade and the member has worked out of grade for more than ten days, the out of grade hours from the first day worked will be reported to the Finance Division on the Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listing (DPOL 8968 A) in accordance with the provisions of the Department directive entitled "Payroll Procedures for Reporting Overtime/Out of Grade Hours And Holiday Premium Hours Earned for Payment."
