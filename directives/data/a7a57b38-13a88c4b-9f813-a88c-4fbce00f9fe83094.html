Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D12-05
Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV) Citation Books - Version Cancellation and Issuance
Issue Date:22 October 2012Effective Date:22 October 2012
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members that effective 26 October 2012 at 0001 hours, all Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV) (CPD-11.497A [Version C - 01-05-09]) citation books are discontinued and will be replaced by the new ANOV (CPD-11.947A [Version A - 08-17-12]) citation books.
  • B.outlines the procedures for the collection and destruction of the discontinued ANOV citation books.
II.General Information
  • A.Department members will familiarize themselves with the new ANOV citation books, including the new pre-printed charge and mail-in information for citing 7-24-099(a) "Possession of Cannabis," and refer to the Department directive entitled "Municipal Administrative Hearings" when citing a Municipal Code of Chicago ordinance violation on an ANOV citation.
  • B.When using the new ANOV (CPD-11.947A [Version A - 08-17-12]) citation books, Department members will not have to issue a copy of the Possession of Cannabis (MCC -24-099) ANOV Mail-In Option (CPD-11.497-S) supplement to a respondent charged with MCC 7-24-099(a) "Possession of Cannabis."
III.Procedures
  • A.Unit commanding officers will ensure:
    • 1.the acquisition of a sufficient supply of the new ANOV citation books for distribution from the Equipment and Supply Section.
    • 2.the collection of the old ANOV citation books, either on-hand or issued to personnel, including partially used books.
    • 3.the new ANOV (CPD-11.947A [Version A - 08-17-12]) citation books, which become effective 26 October 2012 at 0001 hours, are issued to Department members and are available for use.
  • B.Department members will submit their old ANOV citation books to the designated unit member and, in turn, will be issued the new ANOV citation book.
    • 1.Once the new ANOV citation books are issued, Department members will not use any other citation from any prior series.
    • 2.Department members submitting partially used ANOV citation books will remove the “Issuing Officer, Investigator and/or Inspector Copy” (blue) of all issued ANOV citations and retain them for a one-year period from the date of issue.
    • 3.In the event that any outstanding citations cannot be accounted for by 26 November 2012, the Department member to whom the citations were distributed will, by the end of their next tour of duty:
      • a.prepare a To-From-Subject report to their unit commanding officer explaining the circumstances under which the citations cannot be accounted for.
      • b.initiate the appropriate case report and send an AMC message, incorporating the AMC number in the narrative section of the case report.
      • c.attach a copy of the case report and AMC message to the To-From-Subject report and submit the report to their unit commanding officer.
  • C.Unit citations clerks will check all ANOV citation books and complete the corresponding Administrative Citation Control Sheet (CPD-11.495), indicating both the used and unused citations which are returned. When all citations on an Administrative Citation Control Sheet are accounted for, the sheet will be placed in the unit’s inactive file. In the event an Administrative Citation Control Sheet indicates that the citations have not been returned, the supervisor of the Department member to whom the citations were distributed will be notified to have outstanding citations returned without delay.
  • D.The Transfer Listing for MCC Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violations (CPD-11.498) will be used for the return of the old ANOV citation books. Each citation book will be listed by series, in numerical order, on a single line of the Transfer Listing. The number of the first and last citation in a citation book will be clearly printed on the same line of the Transfer Listing. The Transfer Listing will be prepared in triplicate and separated according to Item III-E of this directive.
  • E.After the collected ANOV citation books have been listed on the Transfer Listing, the books will be packed by series, in numerical order, in cartons. The Transfer Listing will be separated as follows:
    • 1.The yellow copy will be packed inside the carton containing the ANOV citation books listed on that Transfer List.
    • 2.The white copy will be taped to the outside of the carton containing the ANOV citation books, in such a manner that personnel can clearly read the listing.
    • 3.the pink copy will be kept by the sending unit in accordance with the existing records-retention requirements.
IV.Retention and Disposal Procedures
  • A.All official records for the old ANOV citation books, including Administrative Citation Control Sheets and Transfer Listings, will be retained by all units in accordance with the Department directive entitled “Records Management” and the existing records-retention requirements.
  • B.All discontinued ANOV citations which are unused and have been collected and appropriately packaged by units will be disposed of following the records disposal procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Records Management."
    NOTE:
    Unit commanding officers will ensure to coordinate the disposal of their unit's discontinued ANOV citations with the Bureau of Administration, including pick-up by the Department of General Services.
  • C.The records storage warehouse personnel will coordinate the further disposal of the discontinued ANOV citation books.
