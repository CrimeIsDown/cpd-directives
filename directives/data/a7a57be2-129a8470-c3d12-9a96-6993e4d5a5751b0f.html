Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-04-09
Strategic Inspections Task Force Program
Issue Date:23 December 1997Effective Date:24 December 1997
Rescinds:S97-11
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Strategic Inspections Task Force Program.
  • B.outlines responsibilities of Department members.
II.General Information
  • A.The Strategic Inspections Task Force is a unit of specialized personnel from the Department of Police, Department of Fire, Department of Buildings, Department of Health, Department of Law and the Department of Revenue operating under the auspices of the Department of Buildings. Chicago Police Department command personnel inquiring about the Program may contact the Strategic Inspections Task Force Project Manager, Department of Buildings.
  • B.The Task Force conducts inspections and investigations of buildings which are the subject of ongoing criminal activity, especially:
    • 1.narcotics and gang activity.
    • 2.buildings located on major arterial streets which are in blighted areas and show early signs of decay.
    • 3.occupied residential properties in hazardous/dilapidated condition.
  • C.The Task Force consists of six teams of inspectors.
    • 1.Three teams will concentrate on the inspection of arterial streets.
    • 2.Two teams will concentrate on the inspection and investigation of gang/drug havens.
    • 3.One team will concentrate on properties in hazardous/dilapidated condition.
  • D.The Task Force will conduct initial inspections throughout the City on a police district by district basis. Task Force supervisors will meet with a district commander at least two weeks prior to initiating operations in the district. A set of teams, normally consisting of one gang/drug team and one arterial team, will operate together in a designated district. One set of teams will operate on the north side, while another set of teams operates on the south side of the City.
  • E.A tactical or gang tactical team, assigned to the district being inspected, will be designated to accompany each Strategic Inspections Task Force team to provide security, assist team members with tenant/witness interviews and record information on Strategic Inspection Task Force forms. The forms will be turned over to the Task Force team upon completion of the inspection.
    NOTE:
    The Strategic Inspections Task Force forms are provided and maintained by the Department of Buildings.
  • F.In addition to the initial inspections of buildings, the Task Force will also conduct reinspections of buildings previously discovered to be the subjects of ongoing criminal activity on an area by area basis. Prior to the reinspections, Task Force supervisors will contact the affected Area Deputy Chief, Bureau of Patrol to determine which district will provide a tactical or gang tactical unit to assist team members throughout the area by providing the team with security.
III.Responsibilities
  • A.Tactical or gang tactical personnel assigned to a Strategic Inspection Task Force team will:
    • 1.accompany the inspections team to provide them with security while inspections are being conducted.
    • 2.take appropriate police action and make required notifications when encountering criminal activity and/or persons in need of shelter or medical treatment while assisting a Task Force inspections team.
    • 3.as directed by the tactical lieutenant, gather district information requested by the Task Force.
  • B.The neighborhood relations sergeant will:
    • 1.meet with the district commander, tactical lieutenant and the members of the Strategic Inspections Task Force prior to an operation being conducted in the district.
    • 2.assist the tactical lieutenant by coordinating district information requested by the Task Force which is received from community members and/or the Beat Plans.
  • C.The tactical lieutenant will:
    • 1.meet with the district commander, neighborhood relations sergeant and the members of the Strategic Inspections Task Force prior to an operation being conducted in the district.
    • 2.act as the liaison with Task Force supervisors.
    • 3.ensure that the appropriate tactical or gang tactical personnel are assigned to assist the Task Force team:
      • a.conducting initial inspections within the district.
      • b.conducting reinspections within the area.
    • 4.coordinate the efforts of tactical and gang tactical personnel in the gathering of district information requested by the Task Force.
    • 5.ensure that the district commander is apprised of information relating to Strategic Inspections Task Force operations within the district.
  • D.The district commander will:
    • 1.along with the tactical lieutenant and neighborhood relations sergeant, meet with members of the Strategic Inspections Task Force approximately two weeks prior to an operation being conducted in the district.
    • 2.provide the Task Force with a list of properties identified as drug and gang houses, properties on arterial streets which are beginning to decay and residential properties in a hazardous condition. Also, a To-From-Subject report containing the list of properties will be forwarded to both the respective area deputy chief and Chief of Patrol.
    • 3.designate the tactical lieutenant to act as a liaison with Task Force supervisors.
    • 4.ensure that district personnel cooperate with and assist Task Force members as directed by district supervisors.
    • 5.when directed by the area deputy chief, ensure that the tactical lieutenant is informed of reinspection missions and the assignment of tactical or gang tactical personnel to accompany the Task Force throughout the area.
  • E.The deputy chief will:
    • 1.when notified by the Strategic Inspections Task Force of reinspections in their area, determine which district in the area will provide a tactical or gang tactical unit to accompany the Task Force team throughout the area.
    • 2.ensure that the district commander of the designated tactical or gang tactical unit is notified of the Task Force reinspections and the assignment of personnel.
    • 3.ensure that the Task Force is notified of the district designated to provide personnel to assist the team which is conducting reinspections.
