Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S05-03
Critical Incident Response Program
Issue Date:27 December 1999Effective Date:28 December 1999
Rescinds:G99-13
Index Category:Extraordinary Responses
I.Purpose
This directive provides more information about the Critical Incident Response Program and follow-up procedures. Department members will refer to the General Order titled "Critical Incident Response Program" for policies and initial procedures regarding critical incidents.
II.Critical Incident Response Program
The Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, will be responsible for the Chicago Police Department's overall management of the Critical Incident Response Program. In this capacity, the Deputy Superintendent will be responsible for:
  • A.identifying pertinent training issues and will:
    • 1.coordinate with the Commander, Bomb and Arson Section, continual information gathering relative to weapons of mass destruction and haz-mat incidents.
    • 2.coordinate with the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division ensure the appropriate level of training and dissemination of information is conducted and maintained.
    • 3.ensure the adequate training of all field personnel to a minimum of the awareness level, including units within the Counterterrorism and Intelligence Division and the Special Functions Group that are likely to be involved in critical incidents as first responders (e.g., Airport Law Enforcement Section, Public Transportation Section, Mobile Strike Force, etc.).
    • 4.ensure that personnel of the Mobile Strike Force (MSF) are trained and equipped to function as secondary responders at designated critical incidents.
    • 5.ensure members of the SWAT program are trained to respond as specialists with the capability to execute their functions while utilizing personal protective Equipment
      Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
      X
      Specialized equipment including breathing apparatus, articles of clothing, and functional equipment issued by the Department which are intended to protect the member from limited exposure to harmful hazards and substances.
      (PPE).
    • 6.coordinate with the Commander, Bomb and Arson Section training for Bomb Squad personnel to respond as specialists, with the capability to execute their function in hostile environments utilizing PPE.
  • B.all administrative functions including:
    • 1.ensuring that an appropriate supply of PPE is obtained and that members are given the proper written instructions for use and care of each piece of equipment upon completion of training and issuance of the equipment.
    • 2.supplying rosters of trained and equipped secondary responders each police period to the watch commanders of MSF and to Operations Command.
    • 3.directing the Critical Incident Coordinator to conduct semi-annual inspections of program members' PPE, including breathing apparatus to ensure proper fit and viability of the device for a fifteen minute period of time. All malfunctions will be reported to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Special Functions Group.
    • 4.monitoring the program administration and taking measures to correct deficiencies.
    • 5.submitting an annual report to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations. The written report will be submitted through channels by 31 January of each year and will include:
      • a.a summary of training conducted relating to the Critical Incident Response Program during the previous year.
      • b.a projection of anticipated training for the current year.
      • c.a summary of all implementations of the Critical Incident Response Program or utilization of its resources over the previous year.
      • d.an evaluation of current equipment performance and needs, which will include a projection of budgetary needs for the following year.
III.Procedures
  • A.Notifications
    • 1.In addition to the procedures detailed in the Department directive entitled "Hazardous Material (HAZ-MAT) Incidents," OEMC will notify Operations Command whenever a Level 2 or Level 3 haz-mat incident is declared.
    • 2.Upon the declaration of a Level 2 haz-mat incident, Operations Command will notify the watch commander of the MSF.
    • 3.The MSF watch commander will assign a supervisor trained in critical incident management to respond and assist the Officer in Charge and Incident Commander at the Level 2 haz-mat incident.
    • 4.If deemed appropriate, the Officer in Charge will contact Operations Command to request the appropriate command level authorization for the implementation of the Critical Incident Response Program if the required command level supervisor is not on the scene.
    • 5.Upon notification of a Level 3 haz-mat declaration, Operations Command will notify and direct MSF to implement the Critical Incident Response Program. Operations Command will ensure information about the incident, access route, and staging area is relayed to responding personnel.
    • 6.Should the incident occur when MSF personnel are not normally available, or manpower needs exceed the resources available from on-duty personnel, the call up system may be invoked with authorization from the On-Call Incident Commander, Assistant Superintendent, Operations, or the Superintendent.
    • 7.OEMC will dispatch a canine unit to the staging area.
    • 8.OEMC will forward requests for specialist secondary responders through Operations Command. Operations Command will be responsible for the notifications to the appropriately trained and equipped specialists.
    • 9.Isolation Teams
      Isolation Teams
      X
      Department members whose response duties are limited to establishing and maintaining the outer perimeter and access routes.
      will vigilantly monitor radio communications for changes in the situation which might affect their posts. In addition, Isolation Teams will notify OEMC and the Officer in Charge of any observed changes in conditions and be prepared to take the appropriate actions.
  • B.Critical Incident Response Teams
    • 1.MSF personnel assigned to field duties will ensure that specialized Department issued PPE
      Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
      X
      Specialized equipment including breathing apparatus, articles of clothing, and functional equipment issued by the Department which are intended to protect the member from limited exposure to harmful hazards and substances.
      is immediately available.
    • 2.Upon being notified that a Critical Incident Response Program has been implemented, MSF personnel will immediately proceed to the staging area by way of the advised route and stand by.
    • 3.Upon arrival at the scene, the Critical Incident Coordinator will seek out the Officer in Charge and assist in the implementation of the Critical Incident Response Program.
    • 4.The first MSF supervisor on the scene will seek out the Incident Commander and/or Officer in Charge and:
      • a.determine that the staging area is safe and the access route was correct and notify responding units of any change.
      • b.ascertain the role for Critical Incident Response Team members.
      • c.brief the MSF personnel on their function and ensure that the proper color signals are being employed:
        • (1)Red - High haz-mat threat level. Personnel should don their PPE and be masked and taped prior to leaving the staging area.
        • (2)Blue - Potential haz-mat threat level. Personnel should don their PPE, but carry respirator and tape with them when they exit the staging area.
        • (3)Yellow - Low haz-mat threat level. Personnel need not don PPE, but should carry their equipment bags with them when they exit the staging area.
        • (4)Green - No protective equipment needed. Deployment will be for other police purposes, PPE should be left in the staging area.
      • d.give proper consideration and instructions for the application of the buddy system to operations within the Restricted Zone.
      • e.ensure that personnel are deployed in accordance with the instructions received from the Officer in Charge.
