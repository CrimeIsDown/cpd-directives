Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-05-01
Application for Captain - SES
Issue Date:03 April 2013Effective Date:03 April 2013
Rescinds:16 January 2009 Version
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs members of the rank of lieutenant that:
    • 1.applications for Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES) are being accepted until 17 April 2013 at 2359 hours.
    • 2.the eligibility requirements for Captain - SES are delineated in the Department directive entitled “Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES).”
    • 3.the applicant list may be maintained through multiple promotional processes.
  • B.delineates the application process, selection procedures, and training program for Captain - SES.
II.General Information
The primary function of personnel appointed to the rank of Captain - SES and the eligibility requirements for Captain - SES are delineated in the Department directive entitled “Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES).”
III.Captain - SES Application Process
  • A.Candidates will:
    • 1.go online to the Taleo Police Careers site for the City of Chicago located at www.cityofchicago.org/policeapplication;
    • 2.select the link, "Captain (SES)" under "Job Openings;"
    • 3.follow the procedures for submitting an application. A click-by-click "Candidate Experience Guide" is available on the Chicago Police Department Human Resource Division's intranet site to assist in submitting an application. To access the guide, click on Bureau sites, then click on "Human Resources Division", then click on "Career Development and Performance", and then click on "Candidate Experience Guide."
      NOTE:
      Questions regarding completing a profile or application in Careers should be directed to the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources, 312-744-4976.
    • 4.attach a cover letter as part of the Taleo application including the following information:
      • a.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's Chicago Police Department assignments to the Captain - SES position.
      • b.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's non-Chicago Police Department work experience or training to the Captain - SES position.
      • c.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's supervisory and leadership experience to the Captain - SES position.
      • d.discussion of the applicant's strengths that qualify the applicant to perform the function of the Captain - SES position. The strengths should be considered in terms of the Critical Performance Dimensions identified for the Captain - SES position. Reference the Chicago Police Department, Human Resource Division's web page for specifics.
    • 5.attach a résumé as part of the Taleo application including the following information:
      • a.name,
      • b.star number,
      • c.employee number,
      • d.home address,
      • e.district of residence,
      • f.home and cellular telephone numbers,
      • g.history of Departmental assignments including dates of assignment, supervisor names, and duties performed,
      • h.non-Chicago Police Department work experience or training (e.g., military experience, special training) relevant to appointment to Captain - SES. Include the type of experience or training received and the years of your involvement, and
      • i.education, including the:
        • (1)name and location of colleges / universities attended, and
        • (2)number of semester or quarter hours completed; type of degree or certification; and year completed.
    • 6.identify three references with whom they have worked for or with in the last three years. One of the references must be an exempt member and the other two must be supervisors. The three references each must complete the standard letter of recommendation prior to the close of the application period. The three letters of recommendation must be received by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, by the close of the application period. An unfavorable reference may be the basis for disqualification from further consideration from the position.
      NOTE:
      The standard letter of recommendation form is found in the Taleo job posting.
    • 7.complete and submit a Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form. This form must be received by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, by the close of the application period.
      NOTE:
      The Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form is found in the Taleo job posting.
    • 8.obtain a statement of educational attainment from the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Employment Section. The statement of educational attainment must be received from the candidate by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, by the close of the application period.
    NOTE:
    If the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, does not receive the three letters of recommendation, the Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form, and the statement of educational attainment by the close of the application period, the member will be disqualified from continuing in the selection process for Captain - SES.
  • B.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances, such as military duty. To submit a proxy application, the candidate's proxy will contact the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, at 312-745-5318 before the close of the application period.
  • C.Members who have applied for the position of Captain - SES certify that they are willing and able to perform the duties of a Captain - SES.
  • D.The applicant list may be maintained through multiple promotional processes. Applicants will be notified by an Administrative Message Center (AMC) message of the format to update previously submitted Resume / Self Assessment forms for the duration of the applicant list.
IV.Captain - SES Selection Process
  • A.The Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division (CPD-HR), will review applications for completeness and will forward complete applications to the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources (DHR).
  • B.DHR will again review applications for completeness and will screen applications to determine whether a candidate meets the minimum qualifications for the position in accordance with the eligibility requirements delineated in the Department directive entitled “Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES).”
  • C.DHR will compile a list of all minimally qualified candidates and forward the list to CPD-HR. CPD-HR will review the list to determine if the candidates meet the appointment requirements and remove candidates, if necessary.
  • D.Eligible candidates will be further screened by the Captain's Screening Board.
    • 1.The Screening Board will be comprised of the following members selected by the Superintendent of Police:
      • a.two members currently in the rank of Captain,
        NOTE:
        The Patrolmen's Benevolent and Protective Association (PBPA), Unit 156 – Captains, may make nominations to the Superintendent of Police on which captains to select for the Screening Board.
      • b.two district commanders, and
      • c.two deputy chiefs, Bureau of Patrol (or equivalent rank).
    • 2.The Screening Board will review the Resume / Self-Assessment forms for all lieutenants on the referral list and will determine by a 2/3 vote whether to eliminate a lieutenant from further consideration. All decisions to eliminate an applicant from further consideration will be documented in writing.
    • 3.The Screening Board will forward ineligible lieutenants' Resumes / Self Assessment forms to the Chicago Police Department, Director, Human Resources Division, for retention purposes.
    • 4.The list of remaining eligible lieutenants will be placed in a sealed envelope and forwarded with all Resumes / Self Assessment forms in a sealed carton marked "Captain Selection Process – Confidential" to the chairperson of the Merit Board for further screening.
  • E.The Merit Board will review all the submissions and make recommendations to the Superintendent of Police.
  • F.CPD-HR will submit a Request for Hire Form to the Office of Budget and Management identifying the position and the number of vacancies to be filled.
  • G.The Superintendent of Police will have sole discretion to select candidates for promotion to Captain – SES.
    • 1.The Superintendent of Police may elect to conduct interviews, make decisions based on documents submitted by the candidate, seek additional information from supervisors, or any combination thereof.
    • 2.The Superintendent of Police will select which candidates shall be promoted. Promotion will be contingent in successful completion of the Captain - SES training.
    • 3.The Superintendent of Police may choose to send more candidates for training than available vacancies.
  • H.The Superintendent of Police will forward all lieutenant's Resumes / Self Assessment forms to the Chicago Police Department, Director, Human Resources Division, for retention purposes.
V.Captain - SES Training Process
  • A.The Superintendent of Police will select which candidates shall be promoted and those candidates will have to complete the Captain – SES training.
    NOTE:
    Any lieutenant who is on an approved leave of absence must return to active duty status prior to selection for training.
  • B.The Superintendent of Police may choose to send more candidates than available vacancies to the Captain – SES training and make final decisions after the completion of the training.
  • C.The completion of the training does not guarantee appointment to Captain – SES.
  • D.Selected lieutenants must undergo and pass a drug screening prior to appointment to the position of Captain – SES.
