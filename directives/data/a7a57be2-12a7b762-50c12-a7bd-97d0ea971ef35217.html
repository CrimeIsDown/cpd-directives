Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-11
Bomb, Arson, and Fire Incidents
Issue Date:08 May 2012Effective Date:08 May 2012
Rescinds:Version Dated 01 February 1991
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Policy
  • A.It is the policy of the Chicago Police Department to investigate and accurately report each fire and bomb related incident and/or threat which is brought to its attention.
  • B.Department members will extend whatever assistance is required, and cooperate to the fullest extent with members of the Chicago Fire Department at all times.
II.Bureau of Patrol Responsibility
  • A.Whenever members of this bureau are assigned to a fire or a bomb incident, they will:
    • 1.determine if the incident is bona fide, and if necessary, assist in the rescue of endangered persons.
    • 2.provide assistance if a fire or explosion has occurred, which may include the establishment of fire lines, traffic and crowd control, Chicago Fire Department personnel safety measures, and the prevention of theft from Fire Department apparatus.
    • 3.determine if the assigned police personnel / equipment is sufficient and, if not, request additional assistance as necessary.
    • 4.keep the dispatcher informed of conditions and request a supervisor, if required.
    • 5.attempt to ascertain the cause and origin of the incident. If there is a suspicion that the incident is of a criminal nature, the member will request a supervising sergeant and the Bomb and Arson Section.
  • B.Whenever members of this bureau are assigned to a bomb threat, they will:

III.Office of Emergency Management and Communications Responsibility
  • A.Upon notification, the OEMC dispatcher will immediately dispatch a patrol unit from the district of occurrence and make any other required notifications.
  • B.If Fire Department equipment is required in a police emergency, the OEMC dispatcher will notify Fire Department of the situation.
    NOTE:
    If the complaint is a bomb threat, the Fire Department will not be immediately requested.
  • C.If a bomb or suspect device is located, the OEMC dispatcher will contact the Fire Department and:
    • 1.request that an ambulance is dispatched to the scene.
    • 2. confirm whether or not a battalion chief and hazmat engine will be dispatched to the scene.

VI.Bomb and Arson Section Responsibility
  • A.The Bomb and Arson Section will be responsible for:
    • 1.the follow-up investigation of all arson and bomb threats received, both verbal and written.
    • 2.the follow-up investigation of all fire and bomb related incidents reported on a General Offense Case Report.
    • 3.the operation of the Arson Hot Line .
    • 4.crime analysis to detect activity patterns or similarities of modus operandi. This information may be disseminated throughout the Department by means of a Crime Analysis Pattern (CPD-23.110) and/or a General Information bulletin (CPD-23.107).
  • B.The Bomb and Arson Section will, upon notification, respond and provide assistance and then assume the follow-up investigation for all incidents involving:
    • 1.fires suspected to be arsons.
    • 2.explosions suspected to be criminally caused.
    • 3.known or suspected explosives or hazardous devices and/or materials. Explosives technicians will be responsible to render safe and dispose of or preserve all such materials for evidence.
VII.CAPS Division Responsibility
The CAPS Division will:
  • A.establish and develop coordinated programs for the participation of the community in arson awareness and prevention.
  • B.promote and conduct seminars and workshops in target communities to ensure that citizen awareness and education regarding arson problems are given widespread dissemination.
  • C.identify target area(s) through liaison with the Bomb and Arson Section.
  • D.distribute appropriate pamphlets and other literature to augment the programs.
  • E.encourage and support the development of Community Fire Alert Patrol in selected target areas where feasible.
  • F.maintain close and continuous liaison with the Troubled Buildings Unit to identify abandoned properties which might be potential targets for arson and to post them as appropriate with Arson Hot Line award posters or "No Trespassing" notices.
VIII.Fire Units En Route to Fires
All police units operating in the area will be alert to Chicago Fire Department units responding to or returning from an alarm and provide close scrutiny and protection against any attack directed toward them.
IX.Fire Department Quarters Left Vacant
  • A.All police units are directed to pay special attention to fire stations left unattended, to prevent thefts and acts of vandalism.
  • B.In instances where fire stations will not be occupied for an extended period of time, the OEMC will advise all police units to pay special attention to the fire stations in their areas.
  • C.District sector sergeants and field lieutenants will periodically visit Chicago Fire Department facilities within their area of responsibility and discuss current problems with fire personnel. Visits of this nature will be recorded on the Command & Supervisor's Management Log (CPD-11.455.)
