Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S10-04
Municipal Drug and Gang House Enforcement Program
Issue Date:29 February 2012Effective Date:29 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 1 November 1997; D97-36
Index Category:Gang and Narcotic Abatement
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues the Municipal Drug and Gang Enforcement Program established by the Department of Law on 1 November 1996, based upon amendments to Sections 8-4-090, 13-14-010, 13-14-145, 13-14-180, 13-14-220, and 13-14-225 of the Municipal Code of Chicago, effective 11 November 1996.
  • B.defines the scope of the program.
  • C.outlines responsibilities of Department members.
  • D.continues the use of the Department of Law Intake Form as an instrument used by Department members to submit information to the assistant corporation counsel assigned to a program district. This form is maintained by the Department of Law.
II.Scope
  • A.The program will continue until further notice, with the presence of assistant corporation counsels in the area facilities.
  • B.The program includes the following areas and districts:
    Area 1002nd District
    007th District
    Area 3019th District
    020th District
    Area 4011th District
    013th District
III.General Information
  • A.The Chicago City Council amended the Municipal Code to establish an administrative system to address property owners and managers who encourage or permit unlawful drug and gang activities and related offenses to occur on their property. To enforce the ordinance, the Department of Law established the Drug and Gang House Enforcement Section (DGHES). This Section will assist the police and local communities in combating the social problems created by drug and gang activities. For purposes of this ordinance, “property” is defined as any parcel of property and the building structure, if any, which is situated on the property, and any portion of the public way that abuts the parcel of property when it is used in conjunction with the abutting property for the commission of illegal activity.
  • B.The Department of Law, Drug and Gang House Enforcement Section will focus on eradicating drug and gang houses from Chicago neighborhoods, and address community problems which create an environment that encourages the spread of prohibited conduct and the deterioration of neighborhoods. District commanders will work in cooperation with the DGHES and designate the tactical lieutenant as the district liaison. The DGHES consists of two components: 1) the Case Initiation and Development Unit (CIDU); and 2) the Case Prosecution Unit (CPU).
    • 1.The CIDU is comprised of three assistant corporation counsels. One CIDU attorney will be assigned to each area in the program, and will be responsible for two districts in that specific area. The focus of the CIDU attorneys will be on the Municipal Drug and Gang Enforcement Program. CIDU attorneys will:
      • a.compile cases for identified problem properties and refer them to the CPU for further action.
      • b.once local drug and gang houses have been identified, assist Department members in the problem-solving process by assembling evidence to build cases against owners or managers of these properties.
      • c.provide Municipal Code roll call training to district personnel in the participating districts to enhance enforcement of ordinances related to gang and drug activities.
      • d.attend community / beat meetings to respond to community inquiries about drug and gang houses.
      • e.provide district commanders with monthly reports listing the status / disposition of cases initiated in the program.
    • 2.The CPU is responsible for:
      • a.determining corrective measures to eliminate drug and gang activity occurring on properties identified by the CIDU.
      • b.bringing action before the Code Enforcement Bureau (CEB) for administrative adjudication when property owners or managers fail to respond to Notices of Violations or comply with ordered corrective measures.
      • c.bringing actions of abatement before a court of competent jurisdiction.
      • d.notifying the affected district commander in writing when a police officer is required to attend legal proceedings relative to a CIDU case.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.District officers will:
    • 1.use a Department of Law Intake Form to report information about drug and gang houses and related criminal activity on their beats.
      NOTE:
      The CIDU attorney will provide the program districts with a supply of the Department of Law Intake Forms.
    • 2.submit completed Department of Law Intake Forms to their station supervisor for review.
    • 3.inform community members of the status / disposition of the CIDU drug and gang house cases on their beat.
    • 4.when directed by the station supervisor or tactical lieutenant, as appropriate, assist the assigned CIDU attorney in follow-up on drug and gang house information.
      NOTE:
      Police officers will only attend legal proceedings relative to the CIDU cases when notified through the district commander by the Department of Law.
  • B.Beat team leaders will, upon receipt from the district commander, review monthly reports listing the status / disposition of cases initiated in the program and ensure that beat team officers are provided with updated CIDU information relative to cases on their beats.
  • C.The station supervisor will:
    • 1.ensure that the CIDU attorney assigned to their district is provided with transportation from the district station to various community meetings within the district when requested by the CIDU attorney.
    • 2.encourage district officers to report drug and gang house information and any related criminal activity on the Department of Law Intake Form.
    • 3.review and ensure that all copies of the Department of Law Intake Form are forwarded to the district tactical lieutenant.
  • D.The tactical lieutenant will:
    • 1.regularly meet with the assigned CIDU attorney to discuss problem drug and gang houses in the district.
    • 2.ensure that appropriate tactical and gang tactical personnel are assigned to conduct follow-up investigations relative to drug and gang house activity when requested by the CIDU attorney. Follow-up may include obtaining copies of arrest reports, case reports and other related documents, retrieving ICAM information, and any other related actions necessary to assist the CIDU attorney in developing drug and gang house cases.
    • 3.encourage tactical and gang tactical officers to report drug and gang house information and any related criminal activity on the Department of Law Intake Form.
    • 4.collect Department of Law Intake Forms submitted by districts personnel and, review and forward the forms to the assigned CIDU attorney.
    • 5.ensure that the district commander is appraised of information relating to CIDU investigations.
    • 6.maintain liaison with the Special Functions Division and the Bureau of Organized Crime to obtain information relative to gang and drug arrests effected in the district.
  • E.The district commander will:
    • 1.periodically meet with the assigned CIDU attorney to discuss problem drug and gang houses in the district.
    • 2.designate the tactical lieutenant to act as the district liaison to coordinate activities with the assigned CIDU attorney.
    • 3.ensure that district personnel cooperate with and assist the assigned CIDU attorney in gathering the necessary information needed to identify problem drug and gang houses.
    • 4.ensure that beat team leaders are provided with updated information relative to the status/disposition of DGHES cases initiated by the CIDU on their beats.
    • 5.ensure police officers are informed of any written notification to attend legal proceedings relative to a CIDU case received from the Department of Law / CPU.
