Chicago Police DepartmentGeneral Order G01-02
Department Organization for Command
Issue Date:26 January 2009Effective Date:27 January 2009
Rescinds:G09-02
Index Category:Department Organization
I.Purpose
This directive establishes the organization and command structure of the Chicago Police Department.
II.Organization
  • A.The Office of the Superintendent consists of the following units:
    • 1.Chief of Staff
    • 2.General Counsel to the Superintendent
    • 3.Department Administration
    • 4.Office of News Affairs
  • B.The Department is organized into Operations and Administration, each commanded by an assistant superintendent.
  • C.Operations consists of the following units:
    • 1.Bureau of Patrol
    • 2.Bureau of Investigative Services.
  • D.Administration consists of the following units:
    • 1.Bureau of Professional Standards
    • 2.Bureau of Administrative Services.
III.Command Structure
  • A.Order of Rank
    • 1.The order of rank for Department personnel is:
      • a.Superintendent of Police
      • b.Assistant Superintendent
      • c.Deputy Superintendent
      • d.Chief
      • e.Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Deputy Chief, General Counsel to the Superintendent
      • f.Commander, Director, Coordinator
      • g.Captain
      • h.Lieutenant
      • i.Sergeant
      • j.Police Officer
        NOTE:
        A police officer assigned to a D2 or D2A paygrade position is equivalent to the rank of police officer.
    • 2.The member having the most seniority in grade within the organizational structure of a unit will be in command, unless the Superintendent of Police or the Superintendent’s representative designates otherwise.
    • 3.Command Staff members will assume control outside their own organizational structure only when failure to do so would seriously endanger Department operations, its reputation, or its ability to maintain law and order.
    • 4.When personnel from different bureaus respond to an incident, the member in charge will be the highest ranking member of the Bureau of Patrol unless otherwise directed by the Superintendent or designated by Department directive.
    • 5.When there is a dispute, the member that was overruled may appeal to a higher-ranking member from their bureau. Execution of the original order will not be delayed during the appeal.
    • 6.Department members of the ranks delineated in Item II. A. 1. a. - f. will, collectively, be known and referred to as Command Staff.
  • B.Order of Succession
    In the absence of the Superintendent of Police, the order of succession to the Acting Superintendency of the Department is:
    • 1.Assistant Superintendent, Operations
    • 2.Assistant Superintendent, Administration
    • 3.Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol
    • 4.Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Investigative Services
    • 5.Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Professional Standards
    • 6.Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services.
