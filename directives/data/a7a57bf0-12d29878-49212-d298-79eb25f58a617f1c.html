Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-01-12
Inventory of Arrestee's Personal Property
Issue Date:08 September 2011Effective Date:08 September 2011
Rescinds:14 September 2007 Version, D07-40
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the revised policy for accepting arrestees personal property issued by the Sheriff of Cook County.
  • B.clarifies and updates procedures relative to the inventory of arrestee’s personal property.
  • C.is effective as of 0001 hours on 16 SEPTEMBER 2007.
II.General Information
The Sheriff of Cook County has issued a more restrictive policy for accepting arrestee’s personal property. Procedures complying with this policy will be introduced in phases as the procedures become automated.
III.Conflict Resolution
If this directive conflicts with any other Department directive, this directive will take precedence.
IV.Policy
  • A.Lockup personnel will accept and inventory only the following items as arrestee’s personal property on a Personal Property Receipt:
    • 1.United States currency of $500 or less;
      NOTE:
      If an arrestee has more than $500, the inventorying officer will allow the arrestee to keep up to $500 and inventory the remaining currency via eTrack.
    • 2.One plain metal ring without stones. No other jewelry will be accepted;
    • 3.Government-issued identification cards;
    • 4.Credit/debit cards;
    • 5.Prescription eyeglasses;
    • 6.Prescription medications and Medical Alert Bracelets;
    • 7.Shoelaces and belts;
    • 8.Keys; and
    • 9.Court documents and eTrack receipts.
      (Item IV-A revised, 08 September 2011)
  • B.All other items of arrestee’s personal property will be inventoried by the arresting / assisting officer via eTrack.
    NOTE:
    Outer garments will remain with arrestees and not be inventoried unless they pose a safety threat to arrestees or other persons.
V.Arresting / Assisting Officer Procedures
  • A.Arresting / assisting officers will inventory all arrestees’ personal property except for those items described in Item IV-A of this directive.
    NOTE:
    The arresting / assisting officer will request that the arrestee designate one person to receive the arrestee’s property. This person will be listed in the commentary section of eTrack.
  • B.Property will be inventoried via eTrack with the item type of “Prisoner Personal Property” and the classification action of “Property Owner Pickup.”
  • C.United States currency will be inventoried separately via eTrack with the item type of “Money (USC),” the indication it is to be deposited, and the classification action taken of “Property Owner Pickup.”
    NOTE:
    For complete procedures, members will refer to the Department directive entitled “Phase 2 eTrack Inventory of Property Taken into Custody.”
  • D.Jewelry will be inventoried separately via eTrack with the item type of “Prisoner Personal Property” and the classification action of “Property Owner Pickup.”
    NOTE:
    For complete procedures, members will refer to the Department directive entitled “Phase 2 eTrack Inventory of Property Taken into Custody.”
VI.Lockup Personnel Procedures
Lockup Personnel will:
  • A.if inventorying U.S. currency:
    • 1.count the currency and seal it in a CPD letter-sized paper envelope;
    • 2.write the amount of currency on the front of the envelope;
    • 3.sign the envelope across the seal;
    • 4.request that the arrestee sign the envelope beneath the Department member’s signature; and
      NOTE:
      If the arrestee refuses to sign the envelope, the Department member will write “SIGNATURE REFUSED” in place of the arrestee’s signature.
    • 5.place clear plastic tape over the signatures and seal.
  • B.if inventorying one plain metal ring without stones:
    • 1.place the ring in a CPD letter-sized paper envelope;
    • 2.write a description of the ring on the front of the envelope;
    • 3.sign the envelope across the seal;
    • 4.request that the arrestee sign the envelope beneath the Department member’s signature; and
      NOTE:
      If the arrestee refuses to sign the envelope, the Department member will write “SIGNATURE REFUSED” in place of the arrestee’s signature.
    • 5.place clear plastic tape over the signatures and seal.
  • C.list all arrestee property on a Personal Property Receipt, drawing a line under the last item listed, and:
    NOTE:
    When listing US currency, members will list ONLY the total amount of the currency on the box labeled “CASH $.”
    • 1.complete and sign the appropriate portions on the “Officer’s Receipt” copy;
    • 2.request that the arrestee sign the form under the line on the last item listed and in the “Property Owner” box of the form;
      NOTE:
      If the arrestee refuses to sign the form, the Department member will write “SIGNATURE REFUSED” in place of the arrestee’s signature.
    • 3.place the “Owner’s Receipt Copy” of the Personal Property Receipt in the Personal Property Envelope with the listed property. The copy will be placed face-up so it is visible through the clear back panel of the property envelope;
    • 4.provide the arrestee with the “Officer’s Receipt” copy of the Personal Property Receipt.
  • D.place all arrestee personal property into a polyethylene Personal Property Envelope, including the signed and sealed envelopes described in Items VI-A and VI-B of this directive, if applicable, and:
    • 1.heat-seal the Personal Property Envelope, and
    • 2.place it in a secure location.
  • E.complete the appropriate portions of the Arrestee Property Log, available on the Department Intranet. When property is released from the Lockup, Lockup personnel will record and initial the disposition of the property in the appropriate field on the log.
  • F.prepare, print, and sign the appropriate portions of an Arrestee and Property Transport Manifest, available on the Department Intranet. A separate form will be completed for each court call for all arrestees sent to court.
    NOTE:
    Lockup personnel will print and sign three copies of the manifest: one copy will remain in the unit; one copy will remain with the transporting officer; and one copy will remain with the Sheriff of Cook County representative.
  • G.present the three copies to the transporting officer, ensure the transporting officer signs all three copies, and retain one copy for unit records.
VII.Transporting Officer Procedures
When receiving arrestees and property, transporting officers will:
  • A.complete and sign the appropriate portions of the Arrestee and Property Transport Manifest, indicating receipt of arrestees and personal property; and
    NOTE:
    The transporting officer will sign all three copies of the manifest.
  • B.upon transporting the arrestees and personal property to the Sheriff of Cook County, ensure the receiving member of the Sheriff’s office completes and signs the appropriate portions of the Arrestee and Property Transport Manifest. The member of the Sheriff’s office will sign both remaining manifests and retain one for their records. The remaining manifest will be retained in the unit of transport.
    NOTE:
    The personal property will be submitted directly to the Sheriff of Cook County representative. At no time will the transporting officer return the property directly to the arrestee.
VIII.Returning Property to Arrestees
  • A.If an arrestee is released from the lockup, lockup personnel will return personal property listed on the Personal Property Receipt by:
    • 1.requesting that the arrestee submit the “Officer’s Receipt” of the Personal Property Receipt;
    • 2.ensuring the arrestee’s signature on the “Officer’s Receipt” matches the signature on the “Owner’s Receipt Copy”;
    • 3.completing and signing the appropriate portions on the reverse side of the “Officer’s Receipt,” signing the box marked “Property Returned By”;
    • 4.requesting the arrestee to sign the “Owner (Signature)” box and complete the “Owner’s Address” box;
    • 5.returning the property (still sealed in the property envelope) to the arrestee; and
    • 6.attaching the “Officer’s Copy” to the corresponding yellow copy of the Personal Property Receipt book.
  • B.Consistent with the Department directive entitled “Phase 2 eTrack Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody,” desk sergeants will ensure that property still retained in the unit at the time an arrestee is released from the lockup will be returned to the arrestee as a Field Turnover.
    NOTE:
    The property does not need to be released by the arresting officer. For complete Field Turnover procedures, members will refer to the Department directive entitled “Phase 2 eTrack Inventory of Property Taken into Custody.”
