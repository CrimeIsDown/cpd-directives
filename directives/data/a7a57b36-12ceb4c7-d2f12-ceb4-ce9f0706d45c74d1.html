Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-09
Processing Curfew Violators
Issue Date:29 February 2012Effective Date:29 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 15 September 2011
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive outlines the procedures for:
  • A.processing curfew violators.
  • B.issuing an Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV) citation to a parent, legal guardian, or other adult having the legal care or custody of a minor who violates any provision of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance.
II.Curfew Laws
  • A.The term “curfew laws” refers to the Statute of the State of Illinois (720 ILCS 555/1) and the City of Chicago Curfew Ordinance (Municipal Code of Chicago Section 8-16-020 through 8-16-022).
  • B.The City of Chicago Curfew Ordinance - Verbatim Text
    • 1.The provisions of Section 8-16-020, “Curfew hours for minors.”
      • a.Definitions. Whenever used in this section.
        • (1)“Curfew Hours” means:
          • (a)For minors 12 through 16 years of age, 10:00 pm on any Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday until 6:00 am of the following day; and
          • (b)For minors younger than 12 years of age, 8:30 pm on any Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday until 6:00 am of the following day; and
          • (c)For minors 12 through 16 years of age, 11:00 pm on any Friday, or Saturday and until 6:00 am of the following day; and
          • (d)For minors younger than 12 years of age, 9:00 pm on any Friday, or Saturday and until 6:00 am of the following day.
        • (2)“Emergency” means an unforeseen combination of circumstances or the resulting state that calls for immediate action. The term includes, but is not limited to, a fire, a natural disaster, an automobile accident, or any situation requiring immediate action to prevent serious bodily injury or loss of life.
        • (3)“Establishment” means any privately-owned place of business operated for a profit to which the public is invited, including but not limited to any place of amusement or entertainment.
        • (4)“Guardian” means:
          • (a)A person who, under court order, is the guardian of the person of a minor; or
          • (b)A public or private agency with whom a minor has been placed by a court.
        • (5)“Minor” means any person under 17 years of age.
        • (6)“Operator” means any individual, firm, association, partnership, or corporation operating, managing, or conducting any establishment. The term includes the members or the partners of an association or partnership and the officers of a corporation.
        • (7)“Parent” means a person who is:
          • (a)A natural parent, adoptive parent, or step-parent of another person; or
          • (b)At least 18 years of age and authorized by a parent or guardian to have the care and custody of a minor.
        • (8)“Public place” means any place to which the public or a substantial group of the public has access and includes, but is not limited to streets, highways, and the common areas of schools, hospitals, apartment houses, office buildings, transport facilities, and shops.
        • (9)“Remain” means to:
          • (a)Linger or stay; or
          • (b)Fail to leave the premises when requested to do so by a police officer or the owner, operator, or other person in control of the premises.
        • (10)“Serious bodily injury” means bodily injury that creates a substantial risk of death or that causes death, serious permanent disfigurement or protracted loss or impairment of the function of any bodily member or organ.
      • b.Offenses.
        • (1)A minor commits an offense if he remains in any public place or on the premises of any establishment within the city during curfew hours.
        • (2)A parent or guardian of a minor commits an offense if he knowingly permits, or by insufficient control, allows the minor to remain in any public place or on the premises of any establishment within the city during curfew hours.
        • (3)The owner, operator, or any employee of an establishment commits an offense if he knowingly allows a minor to remain upon the premises of the establishment during curfew hours.
      • c.Defenses.
        • (1)It is a defense to prosecution under subsection (b) that the minor was:
          • (a)Accompanied by the minor’s parent or guardian;
          • (b)On an errand at the direction of the minor’s parent or guardian, without any detour or stop;
          • (c)In a motor vehicle involved in interstate travel;
          • (d)Engaged in employment activity, or going to or returning home from employment activity, without any detour or stop;
          • (e)Involved in an emergency;
          • (f)On the sidewalk abutting the minor’s residence or abutting the residence of a next-door neighbor if the neighbor did not complain to the police department about the minor’s presence;
          • (g)Attending an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civil (sic) organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor, or going to or returning home from, without any detour or stop, an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor;
          • (h)Exercising First Amendment rights protected by the United States Constitution, such as the free exercise of religion, freedom of speech, and the right to assembly; or
          • (i)Married or had been married or is an emancipated minor under the Emancipation of Mature Minors Act, as amended.
        • (2)It is a defense to prosecution under subsection (b)(3) that the owner, operator, or employee of an establishment promptly notified the police department that a minor was present on the premises of the establishment during curfew hours and refused to leave.
      • d.Enforcement. Before taking any enforcement action under this section, a police officer shall ask the apparent violator's age and reason for being in the public place. The officer shall not issue a citation or make an arrest under this section unless the officer reasonably believes that an offense has occurred and that, based on any response and other circumstances, no defense in subsection (c) is present.
      • e.Penalties. A person who violates any provision of this section is guilty of a separate offense for each day or part of a day during which the violation is committed, continued, or permitted. Each offense, upon conviction, is punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 or an order to perform community service pursuant to Section 1-4-120 of this Code, or both. Any person who violates any provision of this section three times in any twelve-month period shall be subject to up to three times the maximum monetary fine set forth in this subsection for any subsequent violation of this section within the twelve-month period, as an alternative or in addition to a requirement to perform community service.
    • 2.The provisions of Section 8-16-022, “Custody Procedure.”
      Any police officer who finds a minor in violation of 8-16-020 is authorized to take such minor into custody until such time as the minor’s parent, legal guardian, or other adult having legal care or custody of the minor is located and notified of the violation, and takes custody of the minor from the police. If no such person can be located within a reasonable period of time, the minor shall be referred to the appropriate juvenile authorities.
  • C.The Child Curfew Act of the Illinois Compiled Statutes
    The provisions of 720 ILCS 555/1, “Child Curfew Act” - Verbatim text.
    • 1.It is unlawful for a person less than 17 years of age to be present at or upon any public assembly, building, place, street or highway at the following times unless accompanied and supervised by a parent, legal guardian, or other responsible companion at least 18 years of age approved by a parent or legal guardian or unless engaged in a business or occupation which the laws of this State authorize a person less than 17 years of age to perform: 1. Between 12:01 am and 6:00 am Saturday; 2. Between 12:01 am and 6:00 am Sunday; and 3. Between 11:00 pm on Sunday to Thursday, inclusive, and 6:00 am on the following day.
    • 2.It is unlawful for a parent, legal guardian, or other person to knowingly permit a person in his or her custody or control to violate subparagraph (a) of this Section.
  • D.Traffic-Curfew Violators
    The provisions of 625 ILCS 5/6-110, “Licenses Issued To Drivers”
    • 1.If the licensee is less than 17 years of age, the license shall, as a matter of law, be invalid for the operation of any motor vehicle during any time the licensee is prohibited from being on any street or highway under the provisions of the Child Curfew Act (720 ILCS 555/1).
    • 2.A licensee younger than 17 years of age who operates a motor vehicle during any time the driver is prohibited from being on any street or highway under the provisions of the State Statute (720 ILCS 555/1) violates 625 ILCS 5/6-110 in addition to the curfew act.
III.Procedures for Processing Curfew Violators
  • A.Because the prohibited hours in the City of Chicago curfew ordinance are more restrictive than the State of Illinois Curfew Statute, the City of Chicago curfew ordinance will be used exclusively when processing a curfew violator as well as when charging his or her parent or legal guardian.
    NOTE:
    Prior to enforcing the curfew ordinance, Department members must have a reasonable belief, under the circumstances, that a violation of the curfew ordinance has occurred and that none of the nine defenses apply before enforcing the ordinance.
  • B.A member who takes a curfew violator into custody will:
    • 1.complete one Curfew Violation Report (CPD-24.111) for each curfew violator and:
      • a.record a narrative as to the circumstances of the encounter with the minor which led to the completion and issuance of the Curfew Violation Report. The narrative will include what the member observed; a description of the location where the minor was found; the duration of time the member observed the minor at the location; what the minor told the member was the reason he or she was out after curfew; and, if the minor was found in an establishment, the name of the establishment; name of the owner or operator of the establishment; and other relevant information concerning the establishment in which the minor was found.
      • b.affix his or her signature in the “Officer’s Signature” Box, affirming, under the penalty of perjury, that the facts contained within the report are accurate and it was determined that none of the defenses to prosecution apply.
      • c.not place any markings, such as “DNA,” in the boxes designated for Youth Investigations Division (YID) use only.
    • 2.run a computer name check via the PDT, if so equipped, or through OEMC to determine if the curfew violator is missing, wanted, or a runaway.
    • 3.if the curfew violator lives in the district of apprehension or lives at an address in an adjacent district which is closer than the station in the district of apprehension:
      • a.escort the curfew violator home and release the juvenile to the custody of the parent, guardian, or other adult accepting responsibility for custody.
      • b.inform the adult accepting responsibility for the custody of the curfew violator that the child has violated the City of Chicago Curfew Ordinance, enter the relationship of the adult to the curfew violator on the Curfew Violation Report, and verify the identity of the adult.
      • c.obtain the adult’s signature in the “Released to Signature” box of the Curfew Violation Report to signify receipt of the Curfew Violation Notice. If the responsible adult refuses to sign the Curfew Violation Report, the officer will enter “refused” in the “Released to Signature” box.
      • d.issue the “Parent / Guardian Copy” of the Curfew Violation Report to the adult accepting responsibility for the curfew violator.
        NOTE:
        Department members will complete the "Referral to Support Services" section on the reverse side of the "Parent/Guardian Copy" of the Curfew Violation Report prior to issuing the copy to the adult accepting responsibility for the curfew violator.
      • e.submit the completed Curfew Violation Report to the station supervisor in the member's unit of assignment.
        NOTE:
        Department members will Include the “Parent/Guardian Copy” if not accepted by the responsible adult.
    • 4.provide transportation to the district station for all curfew violators if:
      • a.no responsible adult is available to accept custody of the curfew violator. UNDER NO CIRCUMSTANCES WILL A CURFEW VIOLATOR BE LEFT AT HOME WITHOUT ADULT SUPERVISION OR RELEASED TO GO HOME ALONE.
      • b.the curfew violator does not live in the district of apprehension or the curfew violator’s residence in an adjacent district is farther than the station in the district of apprehension.
        NOTE:
        If the member takes the curfew violator to the district station, the member who takes the curfew violator into custody will prepare a Curfew Violation Report and submit it to the station supervisor. If the curfew violator is transported by another officer, the officer will give the report to the transporting officer who will submit it to the station supervisor upon arrival at the district station and complete the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody (CPD-24.518).
  • C.If a curfew violator is brought to the district station by a Department member, the station supervisor will:
    • 1.assume custody of all curfew violators brought into the district station and be responsible for their safekeeping. Curfew violators will ordinarily be detained in the desk or office area of the station until released to their parents, guardians, or other adult accepting responsibility for their custody.
    • 2.ensure receipt of one Curfew Violation Report for each curfew violator brought to the district station.
    • 3.obtain the name, address, and telephone number of the curfew violator’s parents, guardian, or other adult who will accept the responsibility for the curfew violator’s custody and notify that person of the curfew’s apprehension and requirement to appear at the district station.
    • 4.inform the parent, guardian, or other adult accepting responsibility for the curfew violator that the child has violated the Chicago Curfew Ordinance.
    • 5.obtain the adult’s signature in the “Released to Signature” box of the Curfew Violation Report to signify receipt of the Curfew Violation Notice. If the responsible adult refuses to sign the Curfew Violation Report, enter “refused” in the “Released to Signature” box.
    • 6.issue the “Parent / Guardian Copy” of the Curfew Violation Report to the adult accepting responsibility for the curfew violator.
      NOTE:
      Department members will complete the "Referral to Support Services" section on the reverse side of the "Parent/Guardian Copy" of the Curfew Violation Report prior to issuing the copy to the adult accepting responsibility for the curfew violator.
    • 7.notify the Special Victims Section, Bureau of Detectives, to complete a disposition if:
      • a.attempts to contact the parent, guardian, or other adult who will accept responsibility for the curfew violator’s custody are unsuccessful.
      • b.the parent, guardian, or other responsible adult does not arrive within a reasonable period of time after being contacted.
  • D.Special Victims Section, Bureau of Detectives, personnel will:
    • 1.follow the procedures outlined in Item III-B to process curfew violators he or she apprehends in the field.
    • 2.process curfew violators received from the district station supervisor because of the circumstances outlined in Item III-C-7 as follows:
      • a.Attempt to contact a parent, guardian, or other adult who will accept responsibility for the curfew violator’s custody and:
        • (1)inform that person to appear at the station as required, or
        • (2)determine why the responsible adult has not come to the station for the curfew violator after having been contacted.
      • b.Perform a computer name check on the curfew violator. If the curfew violator is wanted or is a missing / runaway juvenile, process him or her in accordance with current procedures relative to these types of cases.
      • c.If the parent, guardian, or other adult willing to accept responsibility for the custody of the curfew violator cannot be reached or has not come to the district station after having been contacted, the detective will proceed according to existing policy as outlined by the Juvenile Court and by Youth Investigations Division directives.
      • d.Leave the “Parent / Guardian Copy” of the Curfew Violation Report attached to the “Original Copy” when not served and return the completed report to the station supervisor.
  • E.Station supervisors will:
    • 1.verify that all Curfew Violation Reports received at the district desk are properly completed.
    • 2.affix his or her signature in the “Supervisor’s Approval” box on all Curfew Violation Reports received at the district desk.
    • 3.collect all Curfew Violation Reports from the designated repository once each day during the 1st watch.
    • 4.prepare a Transmittal / Curfew Violation-School Absentee (CPD-24.112) in duplicate, entering on it the total number of curfew arrests made by each unit. Each unit will also prepare a transmittal sheet listing the curfew violators apprehended by personnel of that unit.
    • 5.detach and file chronologically the “District” copy of the submitted Curfew Violation Reports with the duplicate copy of the Transmittal / Curfew Violation-School Absentee in the district file. The “District” copy of all Curfew Violation Reports prepared by personnel from other units will be forwarded to the district of occurrence for inclusion in the district file.
    • 6.forward the Youth Investigations Division copy (and the Parent / Guardian copy, if applicable) of all Curfew Violation Reports collected that day with the original Transmittal / Curfew Violation-School Absentee to the Youth Investigations Division, Unit 184, in time to arrive prior to 0800 hours.
  • F.The curfew review officer will process the reports and transmittals in accordance with current Youth Investigations Division procedures.
IV.Curfew Violators with Other Offenses
  • A.Traffic-Curfew Violators
    Traffic Curfew Violator
    X
    A licensed driver under the age of 17 years who operates a motor vehicle during any time he is prohibited from being on any street or highway.
    The Department member initiating a traffic stop of a curfew violator will:
    • 1.continue to initiate and conduct safe and courteous traffic and investigatory vehicle stops as outlined by existing Department procedures and training.
    • 2.process the traffic-curfew violator in accordance with the procedures established in the Department directive entitled “Citing Traffic Violations and Attending Traffic Court.”
    • 3.issue a traffic citation for a violation of 625 ILCS 5/6-110, Licenses Issued To Drivers, when a licensee under 17 years of age violates the State Curfew Statute.
      NOTE:
      The curfew violator must be in violation of the Child Curfew Act of the Illinois Compiled Statues, not the Chicago Curfew Ordinance, to be issued this traffic citation.
    • 4.process the individual as a curfew violator in accordance with Item III of this directive.
    • 5.process traffic-curfew violators in accordance with the provisions of this directive and the Bail Bond Manual.
    • 6.not impound a vehicle solely for a violation of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance. The Chicago Curfew Ordinance does not include a provision for impounding a curfew violator’s vehicle.
  • B.Other Offenses with Curfew Violations
    A Curfew Violation Report will be completed every time a juvenile is taken into custody during hours covered by the curfew laws.
V.Procedures for Issuing an ANOV Citation to the Parent / Legal Guardian of the Curfew Violator
  • A.Members assigned to field duty will not issue the parent or legal guardian of a curfew violator an ANOV citation related to any violation of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance.
  • B.If it appears that the juvenile’s parent, legal guardian, or other person knowingly permitted the juvenile to violate the curfew, as indicated by the number and/or circumstances of the curfew violations, that adult will be charged with a violation of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance. An ANOV will be issued for this offense by a sworn member of the Youth Investigations Division.
  • C.Youth Investigations Division Responsibilities
    • 1.The Youth Investigations Division (YID) will:
      • a.input into the ICLEAR database information from all Curfew Violation Reports.
      • b.generate weekly reports from the ICLEAR database.
    • 2.The Commander, Youth Investigations Division will designate a sworn member to:
      • a.review the weekly report for repeat violators.
      • b.issue an ANOV citation for MCC 8-16-020 using information on the Curfew Violation Report in accordance with the procedures outlined in V-D-1 of this directive.
      • c.update the curfew file on the ICLEAR Database inserting the ANOV ticket number in the appropriate field.
      • d.send the “Respondent Copy” of the ANOV citation to the parent and/or legal guardian by US Mail using the information on the ANOV citation.
      • e.attach a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report to the “Department Administrative Hearing Copy” of the ANOV citation and forward to the Department of Administrative Hearings, 400 West Superior, with a transmittal.
  • D.Sworn Department members assigned to YID will issue an ANOV citation to a parent or legal guardian of a curfew violator for a violation of MCC 8-16-020 in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Municipal Administrative Hearings.”
    • 1.The designated YID sworn member completing an ANOV citation will:
      • a.use the information of the parent or legal guardian from the Curfew Violation Report to complete the section for the respondent.
      • b.insert any other information about the curfew violator’s parent or legal guardian ascertained from the Curfew Violation Report.
      • c.insert the phrase, “the above respondent, having legal care or custody of, (the curfew violator’s name), a minor, violated MCC 8-16-020 in that said minor violated provisions of MCC 8-16-020,on the following date and time” followed by the date, time, and location of the curfew violation, in the section entitled “Step 2, Count 1, In That:” of the ANOV citation. Also insert the phrase “See attached Curfew Violation Report” in the same section.
      • d.submit the completed ANOV citation to the YID administrative sergeant.
    • 2.The YID administrative sergeant will:
      • a.receive and review all ANOV citations for completion.
      • b.ensure that:
        • (1)the database file is updated with the ANOV citation number entered into the appropriate field.
        • (2)a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report is attached to the “Department of Administrative Hearing Copy” of the corresponding ANOV citation.
        • (3)the “Respondent Copy” of the ANOV and a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report are sent to the parent and/or legal guardian by US mail.
