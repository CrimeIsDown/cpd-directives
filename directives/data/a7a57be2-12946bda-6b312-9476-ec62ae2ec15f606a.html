Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S12-04
Emergency Service Agencies
Issue Date:19 August 2020Effective Date:19 August 2020
Rescinds:01 July 1989 Version
Index Category:Outside Organizations
I.Purpose
This directive informs members of various agencies within the City of Chicago that provide services at the scene of major fires, natural and human-caused disasters, civil disorders, and similar emergencies. All requests for the following specified agencies can be made via the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC).
II.Agencies
  • A.The American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois
    • 1.The American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois provides food, clothing, shelter, blood services, and medical supplies for victims of fires, natural and human-caused disasters, civil disorders, and similar emergencies.
    • 2.Vehicles of the American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois are defined as "authorized emergency vehicles" (Municipal Code of Chicago 9-48-010). Under the Road Transport Regulation Act of 2017 when responding to risk-to-life emergency situations, the drivers will activate an oscillating red light, sound a siren, and drive with due regard for the safety of all persons.
    • 3.The American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois vehicles are marked with official identification place cards on the door and rear trunk sections, and with a numbered Red Cross decal attached to the windshield. The number on the decal should correspond with the number displayed on the placard.
    • 4.The American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois personnel, displaying official American Red Cross vests, will present an official American Red Cross identification card upon request. In addition, disaster relief supervisors displaying American Red Cross badges on their outer garment will present their official American Red Cross identification cards upon request.
  • B.The Salvation Army Emergency Response Team
    • 1.The Salvation Army Emergency Response Team operates mobile canteens that respond to fires, natural and human-caused disasters, civil disorders, and similar emergencies.
    • 2.Vehicles of the Salvation Army are defined as "authorized emergency vehicles" (Municipal Code of Chicago 9-48-010). Under the Road Transport Regulation Act of 2017 when responding to risk-to-life emergency situations, the driver will activate an oscillating red light, sound siren, and drive with due regard for the safety of all persons.
    • 3.Members of the Salvation Army Emergency Response Team will possess official Salvation Army Identification cards and passes issued by the Chicago Fire Department. They may respond to the scene of an emergency in their private vehicles to deliver food supplies.
  • C.Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of Chicago
    • 1.The Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of Chicago provides assistance to individuals and families whose lives have been disrupted by unplanned events such as domestic crisis, natural and human-caused disasters, fire, or who for any reason are in need of emergency food and clothing.
    • 2.Members of the Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese of Chicago can be identified by green safety-vests, green shirts, and photo identification cards issued by the Catholic Charities of the Archdiocese.
  • D.Chicago Community Emergency Response Team (Chicago CERT)
    • 1.Various hospitals within the City Of Chicago have agreed to dispatch medical teams to disaster sites to supplement first-responder efforts in search and rescue, emergency triage, community recovery, and provide on-site emergency medical treatment.
    • 2.Members of medical teams can be identified by green construction-type helmets and yellow safety-vests that bear the name of the hospital from where they were dispatched.
  • E.Private Providers Emergency Response System (PPERS)
    • 1.The Private Providers Emergency Response System (PPERS) will, upon request, provide ambulances during a disaster to supplement the public ambulances operated by the Chicago Fire Department. The number of ambulances can vary depending on availability.
    • 2.When responding to a scene, ambulances will be directed to a staging area from where they will be dispatched under the direction of a field officer, Emergency Medical Services Division, Chicago Fire Department.
  • F.The 5-11 Club of Chicago
    • 1.The 5-11 Club of Chicago responds to Chicago Police Department and Chicago Fire Department major crime scenes, large-scale training drills, lengthy investigations, dive recovery incidents, missing persons searches, line-of-duty deaths, and officer-involved investigations.
    • 2.The 5-11 Club owns and operates canteen vehicles, known as Support Service Units, stocked with food and beverages such as bottled water, gatorade, hot chocolate, coffee, and portable trailer-mounted washroom units. All food and beverages are handed out free of charge.
    • 3.Upon arrival, a representative of the 5-11 Club of Chicago will check in with the designated unit supervisor with a roster listing personnel assigned to the incident. After having established their identity by means of proper identification, members may be permitted to cross police and fire lines when their services are required.
    • 4.The designated unit supervisor will determine if an incident requires the services of the 5-11 Club of Chicago and will make a notification to the Crime Prevention and Information Center (CPIC).
III.Procedures
  • A.When the services of these agencies become necessary, they will be requested via the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC).
  • B.Representatives of these agencies, after having established their identity by means of proper identification, may be permitted to cross police and fire lines when their services are required.
