Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-01-04
Radio Identification Numbers
Issue Date:20 October 2020Effective Date:20 October 2020
Rescinds:25 October 2011 Version
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes radio identification numbers for Department member duty assignments;
  • B.identifies protocol for the issuance, modification, or termination of radio identification numbers; and
  • C.delineates off-duty considerations for the use of radio identification numbers.
II.General Information
  • A.The term "radio identification number" is frequently used interchangeably with “beat number” and “call number.” These terms refer to both how calls for services are dispatched by the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) and how the Department identifies assignments for personnel. Furthermore, these identification numbers are:
    • 1. the mechanism by which the Department delivers services to the public.
    • 2.also used as the basis for generating Department statistics, crime analyses, and managerial reports.
  • B.Each newly authorized radio identification number requires:
    • 1.the synchronization of Department electronic applications and established managerial reports; and
    • 2.coordination with OEMC to ensure there is no duplication or confusion due to the introduction of new or modified radio identification numbers.
  • C.The official radio identification numbers, the descriptions, the assigned unit, and primary radio communication frequencies are posted and maintained in Data Warehouse located on The Wire intranet homepage.
III.Responsibilities
  • A.All unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.ensure that their units have a sufficient number of radio identification numbers to successfully fulfill their mission.
    • 2.submit a written report through their chain of command to the attention of the Director, Research and Development Division, when requesting to add, modify, or delete assigned radio identification numbers, or to be assigned to a different primary radio frequency.
  • B.The Research and Development Division will:
    • 1.maintain the radio identification table.
    • 2.liaison with OEMC and other Department units as necessary in order to properly maintain the radio identification table.
  • C.Off-Duty Considerations
    When encountering an incident off-duty where an off-duty sworn Department member is required to perform duties of a Chicago Police Officer and required to complete a Departmental report or notify OEMC, Department members will:
    • 1.if designated a regularly assigned radio identification/beat number which is not rotated by watch or assigned to another Department member (e.g., Beat 5; Chief, Office of Operations), use their assigned radio identification/beat number when contacting OEMC or completing any required Department notifications or reports.
    • 2.if not designated a regularly assigned radio identification/beat number or are assigned a radio identification/beat number on a tour of duty basis, use the district of occurrence followed by 02X when contacting OEMC or completing any required Department notifications or reports (e.g., 2202X for reporting an off-duty incident occurring within the 022nd District).
      NOTE:
      Department members using the 02X designation will contact the district station supervisor in the district of occurrence to ensure proper recordation of their use of the radio identification/beat number.
