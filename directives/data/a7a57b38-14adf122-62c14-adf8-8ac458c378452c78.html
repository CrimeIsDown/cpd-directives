Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-13-12
Bicycle Registration and Reporting Procedures
Issue Date:29 September 2004Effective Date:30 September 2004
Rescinds:S04-13
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues:
    • 1.the bicycle registration program.
    • 2.the use of the Bicycle Registration Card (CPD-31.571).
  • B.introduces on-line citizen bicycle registration via the Internet.
  • C.outlines procedures for:
    • 1.the bicycle registration program;
    • 2.reporting lost, stolen, and recovered bicycles.
II.Registration of Bicycles
  • A.Bicycle registration cards are available at all district stations for bicycle owners who wish to register their bicycles with the Department. Registration cards can be mailed to the address printed on the card or hand delivered to any district station.
  • B.Bicycle owners may also register their bicycles on-line by accessing the City of Chicago Internet Home Page.
    • 1.Department members are encouraged to inform the public that bicycles may be registered via the Internet by accessing the City of Chicago Home Page at www.cityofchicago.org and following the instructions provided on the Chicago Police Department web site.
    • 2.No bicycle owner will be instructed to submit an Internet registration if the owner desires to submit a registration card at a district facility. Department members will submit a bicycle registration card for the owner in compliance with this directive.
    • 3.Internet registration will be received by the Public Sector Information Technology (PSIT) Group and forwarded to the Data Entry Group for input into the bicycle registration database.
    • 4.Bicycle registrations received via the Internet will be printed by the Research and Development Division. Copies of the Internet registrations will be forwarded and entered into the Citizen and Law Enforcement Analysis Reporting (CLEAR) System by the Data Entry Group.
III.Responsibilities
  • A.Recovering Officer (recovered stolen bicycle)
    • 1.Whenever an officer recovers a bicycle, with or without an offender in custody, and an OEMC inquiry reveals that the bicycle has been reported stolen, the officer will:
      • a.transport the bicycle into the district of recovery.
      • b.obtain the RD number of the case report for which the bicycle was reported stolen and the information necessary to contact the owner.
      • c.attempt to contact the owner to request that the bicycle be claimed at the unit of recovery.
      • d.prepare a Supplementary Report (CPD-11.411-A) to indicate that the bicycle has been recovered and record in the narrative:
        • 1.the serial number, make, model, and owners name, address, and telephone number;
        • 2.the results of the attempt to notify the owner, indicating the time and date the attempt was made.
        • 3.the disposition of the bicycle, i.e., inventoried or inventoried and returned to the owner.
      • e.inventory the bicycle and return to the owner in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directives entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken Into Custody" and "eTrack Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody."
    • 2.If unable to contact the registered owner, the recovering officer will:
      • a.inventory the bicycle in accordance with procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken Into Custody"; and
      • b.attach a copy of the Supplementary Report to Copy 1 of the Property Inventory.
        NOTE:
        Members will only use the "Hold for Investigation" field when the bicycle has been used in the commission of a crime.
  • B.Recovering Officer (bicycle not reported stolen)
    • 1.Whenever an officer recovers a bicycle and an OEMC inquiry reveals that the bicycle has not been reported stolen, the officer will check the CLEAR system to determine whether the bicycle is registered.
    • 2.If the bicycle is registered and has not been reported stolen, the recovering officer will:
      • a.prepare a Lost and Found Case Report (CPD-11.408).
      • b.attempt to contact and inform the owner of the procedures to reclaim the bicycle.
      • c.release the bicycle to the verified owner when the owner is available to reclaim the bicycle.
    • 3.If the recovering officer is unable to contact the owner or the owner is unable to immediately reclaim the bicycle in question, the officer will:
      • a.record in the narrative of the Lost and Found Case Report:
        • 1.the owners name, address, telephone number, AND the serial number, make, and model of the bicycle.
        • 2.the date and time of the notification or attempt to notify the owner.
        • 3.the condition of the bicycle.
      • b.follow the procedures outlined in Item III-A-2 of this directive.
    • 4.If the bicycle is not registered and ownership cannot be determined, the recovering officer will:
      • a.transport the bicycle to the district station.
      • b.prepare a Lost and Found Case Report, checking the found property field.
      • c.inventory the bicycle in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody" and "e-Track Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody."
      • d.attach a copy of the Lost and Found Case Report to Copy 1 of the Property Inventory.
        NOTE:
        Each bicycle will be inventoried on a separate Property Inventory.
    • 5.Bicycles that are not reclaimed within ten days at the district station will be sent to the Evidence and Recovered Property Section (ERPS).
      NOTE:
      Members will only use the "Hold for Investigation" field when the bicycle has been used in the commission of a crime.
  • C.District desk personnel will:
    • 1.maintain a supply of Bicycle Registration Cards which will be provided to citizens upon request.
    • 2.accept completed registration cards and forward them to the District Review Office.
  • D.District review officers will:
    • 1.ensure that whenever entering information from a case report that involves a stolen or recovered bicycle, the serial number is entered into CLEAR.
    • 2.review all bicycle registration cards that have been submitted to the district station for completeness.
    • 3.forward the bicycle registration cards to the Data Entry Group, PSIT.
    • 4.ensure that extra copies of the case or supplementary reports are forwarded to the Data Entry Group, PSIT.
  • E.The Data Entry Group, PSIT, will be responsible for the maintenance and accuracy of the bicycle registration database, including adding new bicycle registrations as they are received either from the districts or the Research and Development Division.
  • F.The Detective Division Case Management Section, will be responsible for notifying the owner of a recovered bicycle whenever the officer who recovers and inventories the bicycle is unable to contact the owner.
  • G.Evidence and Recovered Property Section will:
    • 1.be responsible for the storage of all inventoried bicycles.
    • 2.ensure that all bicycles eligible for the auction process are transported to the auction site.
    • 3.ensure that any person who is unable to give a complete description of a lost or stolen bicycle will be allowed to view recovered bicycles at the auction site on the day before and on the day of the auction of such property. If a person claims ownership of a bicycle, the bicycle will be placed on "hold" and will not be sold at the scheduled auction. The person making such a claim of ownership will:
      • a.have thirty days from the date of such claim to retrieve the bicycle in question before it is rescheduled to be disposed of according to law.
      • b.provide proof of ownership such as a sales receipt or other certified documentation upon which both the name and address of the claimant and the serial number of the bicycle in question are clearly printed.
IV.Release of Inventoried Bicycles
When an owner wishes to claim an inventoried bicycle, the recovering or investigating officer will:
  • A.contact the Evidence and Recovered Property Section to verify that the bicycle is available for release.
  • B.ascertain the hours during which an owner can claim the bicycle at the Evidence and Recovered Property Section and advise the owner of the location at which the bicycle may be claimed.
  • C.issue a Property Release Order (CPD-34.554) enabling the owner to claim the inventoried bicycle.
    NOTE:
    When bicycles are reclaimed at the district of inventory, desk sergeants will use discretion in releasing bicycles to owners who have not properly registered their bicycles or have no evidence (i.e., receipt, serial number) that the bicycle belongs to them.
