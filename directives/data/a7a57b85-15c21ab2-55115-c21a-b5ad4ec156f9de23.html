Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S11-11
Training Oversight Committee
Issue Date:17 October 2017Effective Date:17 October 2017
Rescinds:
Index Category:Training
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the Department Training Oversight Committee and identifies its membership, duties and responsibilities.
  • B.establishes Department Training Oversight Subcommittee membership, duties, and responsibilities.
II.Oversight Committee
  • A.The Training Oversight Committee will be composed of the following personnel:
    • 1.Voting Members:
      • a.First Deputy Superintendent
      • b.Chief, Bureau of Patrol
      • c.Chief, Bureau of Organizational Development
      • d.Chief, Bureau of Internal Affairs
      • e.Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division
    • 2.Advisory Members:
      • a.General Counsel to the Superintendent
      • b.Chief, Bureau of Technical Services
      • c.Deputy Chief, Special Functions Division
      • d.Commander, Education and Training Division
      • e.Director, Research and Development Division
      • f.Commanding Officer of Recruit Training, Education and Training Division
      • g.Commanding Officer, Force Review Unit
      • h.Sergeant of In-Service Training, Education and Training Division
      • i.Sergeant of Field Training and Evaluation section, Education and Training Division
      • j.Committee Administrator
      • k.Designated Field Training Officers, Bureau of Patrol
      • l.Designated Police Officers, Bureau of Patrol
  • B.The First Deputy Superintendent will act as the chairperson of the committee.
  • C.The Chief, Bureau of Organizational Development will act as the vice-chairperson of the committee.
  • D.Committee affairs will be conducted only when a quorum of the membership is present or contacted for a vote. A quorum will consist of at least the chairperson or the vice-chairperson and two other voting members.
  • E.The committee chairperson will designate:
    • 1.a Department member who will serve as the Committee Administrator. The Committee Administrator is responsible for coordinating and scheduling meetings, recording meeting notes and decisions of the committee, creating and distributing agendas and minutes, and creating official committee reports on behalf of the Chairperson.
    • 2.Field Training Officers and Police Officers from the Bureau of Patrol to serve as Advisory Members of the committee.
  • F.The Training Oversight Committee will submit recommendations to the Superintendent for review and implementation.
  • G.The Training Oversight Committee will convene once a month.
  • H.The Training and Oversight Committee will contain five permanent subcommittees;
    • 1.Recruit Training Subcommittee
    • 2.Training Operational Management Subcommittee
    • 3.In-Service Training Subcommittee
    • 4.Field Training Subcommittee
    • 5.Promotional Training Subcommittee
    NOTE:
    The Training Oversight Committee is not limited to the five subcommittees and may create additional ad hoc subcommittees and/or task forces to address specific needs for a limited time period as needed.
III.Duties and Responsibilities
The Training Oversight Committee will focus on:
  • A.developing short-term and long-term strategic plans that encompass the overall vision, mission, objectives, strategies, and action plans for Department training.
  • B.implementing and overseeing the Chicago Police Department's entire training program including identifying and addressing performance inefficiencies and needs for improvement.
  • C.developing a strategic plan to de-centralize in-service training to the unit and district level, create a selection process so that designated unit level supervisors can become trained qualified instructors, and develop a quality control function for monitoring and auditing.
  • D.developing and monitoring a system that ensures consistency across the the decentralized training.
  • E.reviewing and overseeing the Department's de-centralized in-service training program to ensure compliance.
  • F.conducting periodic training needs assessments for the Department.
  • G.reviewing and adjusting training priorities.
  • H.ensuring that all Department training is delivered efficiently, timely, and effectively.
  • I.ensuring a mechanism for communication is developed by the Education and Training Division to ensure the appropriate Department training support units (e.g., Office of Legal Affairs, Research and Development Division, Bureau of Internal Affairs, and the Office of Communications) are apprised of committee recommendations and actions.
  • J.receiving and reviewing training input and feedback from both internal and external stakeholders.
  • K.creating and assigning tasks to the four Training Oversight Subcommittees.
  • L.convene meetings with subcommittees, as needed.
  • M.reviewing reports, findings, and recommendations developed by the subcommittees.
  • N.ensuring that compliance audits are regularly conducted by the Inspections and Information Services Divisions, unit/district executive officers, and Bureau of Patrol area meetings.
IV.Subcommittees
  • A.Recruit Training Subcommittee
    • 1.The Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division, will be the chairperson for the Recruit Training Subcommittee. Additional members of the subcommittee will be selected by the chairperson.
    • 2.This subcommittee will be responsible for:
      • a.ensuring that the recruit curriculum is in compliance with and maintains compliance with legal mandates, Illinois Law Enforcement Training and Standards Board requirements, national best practices, and Department policy.
      • b.ensure that upon completion of Basic Recruit Training, an end of recruit training evaluation is created, implemented, and utilized to ensure that only qualified recruits are being graduated to the Field Training Program.
  • B.Training Operational Management Subcommittee
    • 1.The Commander, Education and Training Division, will be the chairperson for the Training Operational Management Subcommittee. Additional members of the subcommittee will be selected by the chairperson.
    • 2.This subcommittee will be responsible for:
      • a.ensuring that the Instructional Design and Quality Control Section continually implements multifaceted lesson delivery methods to include adult learning principles and scenario-based training.
      • b.creating and implementing a selection process for instructors, to ensure the selection of knowledgable, qualified, experienced, and motivated instructors.
      • c.creating and implementing an instructor evaluation program and an instructor evaluation form/template.
      • d.implementing regularly scheduled Education and Training Division staff meetings.
      • e.ensuring that the Instructional Design and Quality Control Section is responsible for the maintenance of all training records (curricula, lesson plans, attendance), unless otherwise authorized by the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division.
  • C.In-Service Training Subcommittee
    • 1.The In-Service Training Subcommittee will be co-chaired by the Chief, Bureau of Internal Affairs, and the Deputy Chief, Special Functions Division. Additional members of the subcommittee will be selected by the chairperson.
    • 2.This subcommittee will be responsible for:
      • a.ensuring that the Department is complying with legally mandated 50 ILCS 705/7 in-service training and other legally mandated training such as; procedural justice, civil rights, human rights, cultural competency, legal updates, and use of force.
      • b.ensuring that the in-service program includes training in de-escalation and force mitigation classes.
      • c.ensuring that policy and training are developed and implemented to assist officers in their response to individuals with known or suspected mental health conditions.
      • d.evaluating the CIT program for appropriate and necessary resources and reporting findings to the Bureau of Patrol.
      • e.conducting a comprehensive training needs assessment quarterly.
      • f.evaluate trends, findings, and conclusions regarding Use of Force incidents to effectively incorporate modifications or additions into the training curriculum.
      • g.ensuring that ALL units are following the mandated training requirements outlined in S11-10 Department Training Records Maintenance Program.
      • h.ensuring that all units are using the standardized lesson plans and attendance sheets and that all lesson plans have been vetted and approved by the Instructional Design and Quality Control Section, Education and Training Division.
      • i.monitoring and auditing units to ensure training records are;
        • (1)being sent to the Education and Training Division, or
        • (2)if authorized by the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division are being entered into the records maintenance system.
  • D.Field Training Subcommittee
    • 1.The Chief, Bureau of Patrol will be the chairperson for the Field Training Subcommittee. Additional members of the subcommittee will be selected by the chairperson.
    • 2.This subcommittee will be responsible for:
      • a.reviewing the Departments Field Training Program and Field Training Officers pre-service and in-service training.
      • b.creating a recruitment campaign to increase the number of qualified Field Training Officers.
      • c.ensuring that a new, relevant, and standardized curriculum is created for the FTO program that includes scenario-based training and training in leadership, mentoring, supervision, documentation, performance evaluation, ethics, diversity, and human resource management.
      • d.developing a communication mechanism between the Education and Training Division and the FTOs in the field in order to identify weaknesses in the pre-service training program and to update FTOs in regards to changes in law, department policy, or changes in the FTO program.
      • e.creating a mechanism for continued evaluation of the probationary police officers after they have graduated from the FTO program but are still in probationary police officer status.
      • f.creating and managing a system that clearly delineates the supervisory responsibilities and structure in the FTO program.
      • g.creating a remedial training program for probationary police officers.
      • h.evaluating the rotation cycles and field training components to ensure that they are consistent with national best practices.
      • i.regularly evaluating the Field Training Program to identify areas of improvement.
  • E.Promotional Training Subcommittee
    • 1.The Chief, Bureau of Organizational Development, will be the chairperson for the Promotional Training Subcommittee. Additional members of the subcommittee will be selected by the chairperson.
    • 2.This subcommittee will be responsible for ensuring that the pre-service promotional curriculum is continually updated and that relevant topics such as; leadership, mentoring, coaching, supervision, performance evaluation, community policing, and investigations are included in the curriculum.
