Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-19-05
Electronic and Evidentiary Search Warrants
Issue Date:14 May 2021Effective Date:28 May 2021
Rescinds:
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines the procedures for preparing Complaints for Search Warrants (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrants (CCMC-1-220) for digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary search warrants that do not involve real property or locations where occupants could be present.
  • B.outlines the procedures for obtaining, serving, returning, and retaining search warrants for digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary search warrants that do not involve real property or locations where occupants could be present.
  • C.details the requirements for the review of Complaints for Search Warrants (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrants (CCMC-1-219) by the submitting member’s chain of command and the approval by the designated Department supervisor before these documents are presented to an assistant state's attorney for review and submitted to a judge for approval.
II.Policy
  • A.All Department members will act in accordance with the Constitution of the United States of America, including the Fourth Amendment, and will comply with the law and Department policy.
  • B.All information and recorded images obtained through the use of search warrants (that do not involve real property or locations where occupants could be present) will be used strictly for law enforcement purposes and will be preserved with utmost integrity and confidentiality consistent with Department policy and legal rules governing the handling of evidence and criminal justice records.
  • C.The primary goal in seizing digital information, electronic equipment, or items of solely evidentiary value is the preservation of evidence. Department members will ensure all possible steps are taken to minimize the possibility of accidental loss or destruction of evidence.
  • D.All digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary (e.g., unoccupied vehicle, buccal swab) search warrants that do not involve real property or locations where occupants could be present are required to be approved by a sworn Department member the rank of lieutenant or above.
    NOTE:
    All search warrants that involve real property or locations where occupants could be present will require review and approval by the submitting Department member's deputy chief or above in accordance with the Department directive titled "Search Warrants."
III.Definitions
  • A.Electronic Evidence—any evidentiary information stored or transmitted in digital form (e.g., email, ATM logs, GPS systems, call/text detail records, network/location history of a mobile device).
  • B.Digital Evidence—any probative information, stored or transmitted in digital form that a party to a court case may use at trial (e.g., call detail records, mobile device data extractions, video surveillance, and synopsis videos).
  • C.Unoccupied Vehicles—a vehicle transported to a City of Chicago Auto Pound and a search warrant is executed by the appropriate Department unit (e.g., Forensics Services Division, Major Accident Investigation Unit).
  • D.Buccal Swab—a noninvasive DNA collection from the cells on the inside of a person's cheek.
IV.Search Warrant Development
  • A.Information used in the development of any search warrant will be verified and corroborated by an independent investigation prior to submission and approval of the search warrant. The preparing Department member must be able to articulate why the seizure of the digital information, electronic equipment, or items of solely evidentiary value could yield relevant evidence or information.
  • B.Prior to preparing a Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220), it is the affiant Department member's responsibility to take all the proper investigative steps to provide for the integrity of the search warrant and ensure that the information is properly verified and corroborated. This preparation will include a review of the Rules and Regulations of the Chicago Police Department, state and constitutional law, and the appropriate research, investigation, and operational planning.
  • C.The affiant Department member preparing a Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) will ensure all the information is truthful, accurate, and current to the best of the member's ability.
  • D.The affiant Department member will utilize the Department's investigative tools to verify the validity of the target of the search warrant.
  • E.The preparing Department member will document the investigation to corroborate the content of the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) and will verify the validity of the search warrant before presenting that warrant and affidavit to their chain of command for review and approval and to the State's Attorney's Office (SAO) for review.
V.Search Warrant Complaint Preparation
A Department member preparing a Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) will:
  • A.prepare one original Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and one original Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220);
  • B.ensure that both the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) accurately and specifically describe the target to be searched and articles to be seized; and
  • C.present both the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) to the member's immediate supervisor who will review both the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) and ensure they are presented to the designated approving supervisor for review and approval.
VI.Search Warrant Review and Approval
  • A.All for digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary search warrants that do not involve real property or locations where occupants could be present are required to be approved by a sworn Department member the rank of lieutenant or above.
  • B.When reviewing a search warrant, Department supervisors will ensure the search warrant meets the statutory and constitutional requirements, achieves a legitimate law enforcement objective, and the information contained in the warrant has been verified and corroborated by an independent investigation by the Department member prior to approval.
  • C.A sworn Department supervisor designated to review a search warrant will examine the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) in light of statutory and constitutional requirements and determine that:
    • 1.the facts alleged in the complaint are credible, reliable, and clearly indicate that an investigation undertaken as a result of the information received validates an assertion of probable cause.
    • 2.all facts stated in the complaint were properly obtained.
    • 3.an investigation has been thoroughly conducted in the development of the search warrant to provide for the integrity of the search warrant and ensure that the information is properly verified and corroborated. If additional investigation is determined to be necessary, the complaint will be returned to the Department member for appropriate action.
    • 4.the information concerning the target to be searched is specifically and accurately described in both the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220).
    • 5.the technical aspects of both the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) are correct (e.g., dates, times, spelling of names).
    • 6.probable cause for the issuance of the search warrant is stated in the complaint. Probable cause is present when the facts and circumstances are sufficient to warrant a person of reasonable caution to believe that a crime has been committed and that evidence of the crime can be found in the target to be searched and articles to be seized
  • D.After approval by the appropriate Department supervisor, the member seeking to obtain a search warrant will:
    • 1.present the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) for review to a Cook County Assistant State's Attorney assigned to the  Felony Review Unit.

  • E.After review by a Cook County Assistant State's Attorney assigned to the Felony Review Unit, the member seeking to obtain a search warrant will:
    • 1.present the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) to a judge for signature.

    • 3.print the name of the approving judge and the reviewing attorney in the lower-right margin on both of the originals and prepare one copy of the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and two copies of the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220).
VII.Search Warrant Service
  • A.Department members serving digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary search warrants will follow the requirements established and directed by their respective bureau chiefs, the State's Attorney Office, and the approving judge.
  • B.If during the execution of a digital, electronic, or solely evidentiary search warrant a Department member discovers information or equipment not originally listed in the original warrant, the Department member will contact:
    • 1.the Felony Review Unit of the Cook County State's Attorney's Office to obtain an additional warrant; and
    • 2.Bureau of Detectives, Crime Analysis Technical Group.
      NOTE:
      After hours, weekends and holidays, the Department member will contact the Crime Prevention and Information Center (CPIC) who will then contact the Crime Analysis Technical Group.
  • C.Following a search warrant being served, the Department member will:
    • 1.submit the appropriate case report or Supplementary Report form (CPD-11.411-A), or (CPD-11.411-B) for personnel assigned to the Bureau of Detectives, regardless of whether the search resulted in the seizure of property or contraband. This report will document that a search warrant was obtained and served.
      NOTE:
      Information, evidence, or testimony used to obtain the search warrant that is documented in the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) need not be restated in this report.
    • 2.generate a Post-Service or Returned Warrant Data record in the CLEAR system.
    • 3.submit the Post-Service or Returned Warrant Data record electronically to the designated unit supervisor the rank of lieutenant or above for approval.
  • D.The designated unit supervisor the rank of lieutenant or above will:
    • 1.review the completed Post-Service or Returned Warrant Data record in the CLEAR system and indicate his or her review, as necessary.
    • 2.ensure the designated member inventories all seized or recovered evidence using the eTrack inventory system.
VIII.Search Warrant Return
  • A.After a search warrant has been served, the original Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) and Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) will be returned to the proper court through the use of a Court Complaint Transmittal Listing (CPD-11.551).
  • B.When a search warrant has not been served within 96 hours of issuance, the search warrant will be signed by the returning Department member and returned to the clerk of the court within twenty days.
    • 1.The Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) will be returned using a Court Complaint Transmittal Listing (CPD-11.551) and identified by entering the States Attorney's Warrant Number in the space provided for the defendant's name on the transmittal.
    • 2.Both a Search Warrant Data-Part I form (CPD-41.703) and a Post-Service or Returned Warrant Data record will be created in the CLEAR system to document the existence of the warrant.
IX.Property Disposition
  • A.The recovering Department member will ensure:
    • 1.a photocopy of the original search warrant (excluding the Complaints for Search Warrants (CCMC-1-219) is inventoried, including all digital images as an attachment to this inventory, in the eTrack inventory application in accordance with the Department directive titled “Processing Property Under Department Control.”
      NOTE:
      After the digital images are entered into the eTrack system and approved by a supervisor, the designated member will delete the images from the digital camera memory card.
    • 2.all other items seized are inventoried in accordance with the Department directive titled “Processing Property Under Department Control.”
      NOTE:
      If the search warrant was for digital evidence (e.g., social media profiles, cloud services, mobile carriers), the Department member will inventory the data upon receipt.
  • B.The recovering Department member will request the prosecuting attorney obtain a court order, when necessary, to:
    • 1.authorize the retention of the seized property by the Department for the purposes of investigation or scientific analysis;
    • 2.have the seized property impounded by the court and turned over to the court clerk pending final disposition;
    • 3.return the seized property to its legal owner; or
    • 4.dispose of the seized property.
X.Search Warrant File
  • A.Search warrant files will:
    • 1.contain the information, reports, and documents from the independent investigation to verify and corroborate the information used in developing the search warrant.
    • 2.be secured and stored in the unit that obtained the search warrant and maintained in accordance with the existing records-retention schedule.
  • B.Upon the service or the return of a search warrant, the unit member who obtained the warrant will:
    • a.enter the RD number and the Property Inventory number(s), if applicable, on the copy of the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) that will be retained in the unit file; and
    • b.forward this copy of the Search Warrant (CCMC-1-220) and a copy of the Complaint for Search Warrant (CCMC-1-219) to the exempt-rank unit commanding officer.
  • C.The designated unit supervisor the rank of lieutenant or above will ensure the appropriate information is entered on the Search Warrant Log (CPD-11.426) prior to filing.
  • D.The exempt unit commanding officer will review the forms and forward them to the designated unit supervisor to be included in the files maintained in the unit.
