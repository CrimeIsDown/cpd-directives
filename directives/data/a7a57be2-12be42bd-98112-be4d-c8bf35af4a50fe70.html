Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-01-03
Recorded Voice Transmissions Requests
Issue Date:28 December 2012Effective Date:28 December 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 01 October 1999; G97-06-06A
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines procedures for:
    • 1.requesting the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) to archive recorded voice transmissions relative to a particular incident to the permanent library.
    • 2.obtaining a recording of archived voice transmissions from the permanent library.
  • B.introduces the Recorded Voice Transmissions Request (RVTR) form (OEMC-109), which is supplied by the OEMC.
II.General Information
  • A.The Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) retains recorded voice transmissions for a period of 30 days. In the performance of official police business, sworn members and authorized civilian investigators assigned to the Independent Police Review Authority (IPRA) may request that pertinent voice transmissions be archived to the permanent library and obtain a recording of these voice transmissions.
  • B.Access may be denied if an investigative unit places a restriction on certain recorded voice transmissions when their release would interfere with the unit's investigation of the related incident.
  • C.The recorded voice transmissions will be recycled after 30 days. Members should note, however, that the Police Computer-Aided Dispatching (PCAD) System Event Query Summary provides a complete and permanent record of the event, often including information that is not contained in the recorded voice transmissions.
III.Procedures
  • A.Archiving an Incident
    The following procedures will apply for requests to archive recorded voice transmissions:
    • 1.Requesting personnel will:
      • a.prepare a RVTR form, obtaining approval of the requester's district station supervisor / designated unit supervisor and checking the appropriate box, as follows:
        • (1)"Archive all voice transmissions relative to incident to permanent library," or
        • (2)"Archive all voice transmissions relative to incident to permanent library and request recording."
          NOTE:
          When it is necessary to obtain a recording of all recorded voice transmissions over a certain period of time, (rather than only those related to a particular incident), the requesting personnel will indicate this request and the time period in the "Additional Remarks/Information" Section of the RVTR form.
      • b.send the completed RVTR form to the OEMC, Tape Review Unit, Room 207 via inter-departmental mail. This request must be received by the OEMC within 30 days of the incident.
      • c.treat all recordings as privileged and confidential information. The recording will be kept with the investigative file until it has served the purpose for which it was made, at which time it will be returned to the OEMC.
    • 2.The OEMC tape researcher will:
      • a.identify each recorded voice transmission that pertains to the requested incident, using the PCAD System, and archive a permanent library copy.
      • b.when requested, prepare and send the recording and a printout of the PCAD Event Query Summary for that incident to the requester via inter-departmental mail, unless otherwise specified by the requester in the "Additional Remarks/ Information" Section of the RVTR form.
  • B.Obtaining a Recording
    A recording of a previously archived incident may be obtained at a later date by submitting a completed RVTR form through inter-departmental mail to the OEMC, checking the box, "Request recording of previously archived voice transmissions."
