Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-14
Application for Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 06 February 2007; D07-06
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.delineates the duties, eligibility requirements, application procedures, selection process and training for the position of Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer.
  • B.introduces the use of the intranet application.
II.Duties
Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer will:
  • A.consistently demonstrate the ability and skills necessary to ride as a mounted police officer.
  • B.develop and demonstrate a working knowledge of equine anatomy.
  • C.perform horse grooming duties including, but not limited to:
    • 1.cleaning and/or showering.
    • 2.shedding.
    • 3.pulling manes and tails.
    • 4.clipping the muzzle, ears, bridle path, legs, and coronet bands.
    • 5.caring for and cleaning hoofs.
    • 6.trimming chestnuts and ergot.
    • 7.removing dirt and/or clay and calcium buildups from the urinary tract (beaning).
  • D.recognize and treat basic equine ailments including but not limited to:
    • 1.taking temperature (rectal).
    • 2.administering intramuscular and intravenous injections.
    • 3.treating lacerations, abrasions, and swellings.
    • 4.treating basic hoof problems.
  • E.develop and demonstrate the ability to properly tack horses (bridle & saddle).
  • F.perform duties related to the daily cleaning and upkeep of tack.
  • G.develop and demonstrate a working knowledge of equipment and supplies related to the Mounted Unit.
  • H.develop and demonstrate a working knowledge of equine nourishment and feed.
  • I.lift and stack:
    • 1.bales of hay that may weigh up to 60 pounds.
    • 2.bags of feed that may weigh up to 50 pounds.
  • J.tolerate continual contact with equine dander, droppings and waste products.
  • K.strip and muck stalls; clean waste products; and install fresh bedding.
  • L.clean and maintain Mounted Unit vehicles, trailers, equipment, and facilities including:
    • 1.changing tires.
    • 2.switching trailers.
    • 3.cleaning / washing inside trailers as required.
  • M.perform proper trailer loading / unloading procedures.
  • N.demonstrate proficiency in operating Mounted Unit vehicles with two and four horse trailers attached.
  • O.carry out Mounted Unit assignments in all climatic conditions.
  • P.speak to a wide array of audiences relative to the history and mission of the Mounted Unit.
  • Q.maintain and demonstrate a riding proficiency level to be tested annually.
III.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, sworn members who are interested in applying for the assignment as a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer must:
  • A.be a police officer below the rank of sergeant.
  • B.have a minimum of three years of continuous service as a Police Officer with the Chicago Police Department.
  • C.be willing and able to perform the duties of a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • D.be able and willing to work any watch assignment and change work hours in accordance with the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement between the City of Chicago and the Fraternal Order of Police.
  • E.be willing to forego:
    • 1.days off on Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays during any police period.
    • 2.Furloughs during the 6B, 7th and 8th police period.
  • F.have received performance ratings above the minimum rating score necessary to qualify for a step increase for each of the last four evaluations.
  • G.have an acceptable disciplinary record. The candidate’s disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (CR) investigations during the past five years resulting in a suspension of more than 10 days, or a record of three (3) or more sustained CR investigations resulting in suspensions of any length of time.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR investigations with findings of “No Disciplinary Action” will not be considered as sustained violation for purpose of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances concerning discipline will be allowed to apply for the position of Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer. Such applications will be considered if the candidates disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of a successful grievance, such that the outcome of the grievance results in a disciplinary record that meets the disciplinary standards set in this directive.
  • H.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago.
  • I.have five (5) years retain-ability within the Chicago Police Department.
  • J.purchase and maintain uniform parts and accessories unique to the Mounted Unit.
  • K.if currently appointed to a D-2A grade, be willing to forego D-2A status and pay and accept D-2 status and pay upon appointment to the position of Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Patrol Officer.
  • L.the candidates disciplinary record will be reviewed and a check will be made for any indebtedness to the City of Chicago at the time of application, prior to selection to begin training and again prior to being assigned to the Mounted Unit.
IV.Additional Requirements
Sworn members interested in applying for assignment as Police Officer Assigned as a Mounted Unit Officer must:
  • A.possess the physical agility and stamina to pass a rigorous 14-week Mounted Patrol training program.
  • B.maintain physical condition through exercise and training to carry out the physical duties of a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Patrol Officer.
  • C.possess the physical stamina to ride year-round in all types of adverse weather (rain, snow, severe cold and heat temperatures) for extended periods of time.
  • D.possess the psychological capacity and riding skills necessary to perform crowd management techniques in dense crowds and in hostile environments.
  • E.maintain the highest level of professionalism, appearance and demeanor at all times.
  • F.possess the ability to speak to wide array of audiences relative to the history and mission of the Mounted Unit.
V.Application Procedures
  • A.The on-line application for Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer consists of four parts: on-line application, Medical Statement, Physician’s Consent form, and Unit Questionnaire.
  • B.The application can be obtained from the Department’s Intranet home page by selecting Bureau Sites, then selecting Bureau of Administration, and then, selecting Human Resources Division. The member will choose “Mounted Patrol Officer” and enter his or her PC number and star number in the format requested. A confirmation page is displayed after the member has successfully registered and should be printed for the member’s records.
  • C.The Human Resources Division must receive the candidate’s completed Medical Statement and Physician Consent form by the close of the application period. Members will be disqualified from the continuing in the selection process if completed Medical Statements and Physicians Consent forms are not received by the close of the application period.
  • D.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances such as military duty. The candidate’s proxy will contact the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, for submission of a proxy application prior to the close of the application period.
VI.Selection Process
  • A.Eligible candidates will be scheduled to participate in the Mounted Unit Physical Agility / Performance Test. Candidates must successfully complete each part of the Physical Agility / Performance Test to continue in the selection process.
    NOTE:
    If, at any time during the Physical Agility / Performance test it becomes apparent to the commanding officer, supervisor, and/or training staff of the Mounted Unit that a candidate exhibits problematic behavior traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required tasks in a safe manner, that candidate will be disqualified and must immediately leave the testing facility without debate.
  • B.Task comprising the Physical Agility / Performance test will be graded by the measure of time the candidate takes to complete the task, and/or the particular skill, proficiency, or behavior demonstrated during the course of the Physical Agility / Performance Test.
  • C.Physical Agility / Performance Test is designed to measure a candidate’s physical fitness, endurance, and suitability for working with and around horses at least 16 hands tall and weighing between 1100 and 1600 pounds.
  • D.Each candidate will be required to perform the following tasks after a brief period of instruction and/or demonstration.
    • 1.Stack 18 bags of wood shavings (each weighing 36 pounds) onto a supply cart, walk once around outside of arena, and re-stack the shavings without stopping.
    • 2.Un-stack 5 bails of hay (each weighing 60 pounds), carry each bail 50 feet without dropping, and re-stack.
    • 3.Un-stack 6 sacks of feed (each weighing 50 pounds), carry each sack 15 feet without dropping and stack in feed room.
    • 4.Push loaded waste dumpster 150 feet to dumpster area outside of barn.
    • 5.Halter and lead one horse from a stall into the training arena.
    • 6.Walk a haltered horse one lap around the inside of the training arena (140 yards).
    • 7.Trot a haltered horse one lap around the inside of the training arena (140 yards).
    • 8.Mount / dismount horse.
    • 9.While mounted, sit and maintain balance while being walked one lap around the inside of the training arena (140 yards).
    • 10.Conduct a physical check of a horse by hand of entire horse including ears, teeth, hooves, area inside sheath and under horse’s tail / dock area (rectum).
  • E.Based upon the Physical Agility / Performance Test rank scores, the top 100 scoring candidates will be scheduled for an oral interview. In the event of a tie score, ranking will be determined by seniority. The interview will be conducted by the commanding officer, Mounted Unit, a unit sergeant, and a unit training officer. The candidate’s appearance, demeanor and communication skills will be taken into consideration. Some areas of discussion during the oral interview will include:
    • 1.knowledge of patrol duties and depth of Department experiences.
    • 2.attitude and understanding of the Department’s mission, and Chicago’s Alternative Policing Strategy (CAPS).
    • 3.a candidate’s medical, complimentary, and disciplinary history.
    • 4.willingness to adapt to and accept training, duties, and responsibilities attendant with assignment as a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Patrol Officer.
    • 5.relevant non-department experience.
    • 6.oral communication skills.
  • F.Members will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled to report for any portion of the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisors / designated unit supervisors will excuse a member who has been scheduled for an interview during duty hours, provided that the station supervisors / designated unit supervisors were notified by the member at least 48 hours prior to the scheduled interview.
VII.Eligibity List
  • A.Upon completion of the Physical Agility / Performance Tests and oral interviews, the Mounted Unit commanding officer, supervisor, and trainer will total each candidate’s composite score. Candidates with a composite score of 70% or higher will be placed in rank order on the eligibility list.
  • B.The eligibility list may be stricken at any time at the discretion of the Department. Placement on the eligibility list is not a guarantee of appointment to the position of Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer.
  • C.Candidates selected to participate in the Mounted Unit Training Program have the option to waive participation in the training program by submitting a To-From-Subject report to the Mounted Unit commanding officer requesting A Waiver of Eligibility. Candidates will remain eligible for participation in future Mounted Unit Training programs only for the duration of this list.
  • D.Management reserves the right to fill 20% of the recognized vacancies at its discretion without regard to seniority or rank score, provided that the position is filled by a candidate on the eligibility list.
VIII.Training Program
  • A.As vacancies occur, candidates will be selected in rank order from the eligibility list to attend a 14 week training and evaluation program.
  • B.Selection to participate in the Mounted Unit Training Program will not constitute an appointment to the position of Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer. Selection to participate in the training program will constitute a detail only.
  • C.At the time Mounted Unit Training commences, members must be in full-duty status.
  • D.Candidates selected for the training program will:
    • 1.take and successfully pass a drug screening and a thorough medical examination, the scope of which will be determined by the Medical Services Section, immediately prior to the start of training with the Mounted Unit.
    • 2.be detailed to the Mounted Unit to undergo a 14-week training program.
    • 3.purchase the necessary uniform for training.
    • 4.adjust their furlough period(s) if a segment(s) conflict with the training program, or with the prohibition of summer furloughs.
    • 5.successfully complete all aspects of the training program before being qualified to bid for assignment.
  • E.The training and evaluation program consist of, but is not limited to:
    • 1.seven (7) hours of riding per day.
    • 2.weekly written and riding examinations.
    • 3.evaluations during field exercises.
    NOTE:
    As training progresses, the entire Mounted Unit uniform must be purchased. Expenditures may exceed $3,000.00 and will be the responsibility of the member seeking assignment to the Mounted Unit.
  • F.If, at any time during the training program it becomes apparent to the commanding officer, supervisor, and/or training staff of the Mounted Unit that an officer exhibits problematic behavioral traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required duties, or fails training program testing, the member will be terminated from the training program and returned to their district or unit of assignment. Reasons for termination include, but are not limited to the following:
    • 1.demonstrating a lack of physical coordination to the degree that the member cannot perform the duties as enumerated in Items II and Item IV of this directive.
    • 2.displaying anxiety, apprehension, or an obvious fear of horses and/or of maneuvers.
    • 3.not having the physical strength necessary to safely handle a horse.
    • 4.failing to demonstrate a sincere interest and/or concern for the horse.
    • 5.any act of cruelty and/or abuse directed at or upon a horse.
  • G.Only those members who have successfully completed their training and evaluation program will be eligible to bid for a recognized vacancy in the Mounted Unit.
  • H.If more members successfully complete the training program than there are recognized vacancies, members who are not assigned will remain eligible to bid for future recognized vacancies for a period of three (3) years from the date of successful completion of the training program but not to exceed the life of the current eligibility list.
IX.Additional Conditions
  • A.All assigned members of the Mounted Unit are required to demonstrate Mounted Unit proficiency on an annual basis. Failure to maintain proficiency will subject the member to removal from assignment to the Mounted Unit as a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer.
  • B.If, at any time after assignment to the Mounted Unit, a member demonstrates a lack of knowledge, skills or abilities necessary for the job, is unable to perform the essential duties of the position, or exhibits problematic behavior, the member will be subject to review and removal from assignment to the Mounted Unit as a Police Officer Assigned as Mounted Unit Officer.
