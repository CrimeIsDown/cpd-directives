Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E01-03
Review of Personnel Records
Issue Date:08 December 1988Effective Date:09 December 1988
Rescinds:S88-29
Index Category:Employee Rights and Responsibilities
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures by which a Department member may inspect and obtain photocopies of certain Department maintained personnel documents which pertain to him.
II.General Information
  • A.Personnel documents subject to a member’s review include documents which are, have been or are intended to be used in determining that member’s qualifications for employment, promotion, transfer, additional compensation, discharge or other disciplinary action.
  • B.Personnel documents not subject to a members review include the following types of records:
    • 1.Letters of reference; including background investigation material prepared prior to the original appointment, return from leave of absence or reinstatement.
    • 2.Any portion of a test document administered by the Department except that the member may see a cumulative total test score for either a section of or the entire test document.
    • 3.Materials relating to the Departments staff planning, such as matters relating to the development, expansion, closing or operational goals, where the materials relate to or affect more than one employee, provided, however, that this exception does not apply if such materials are, have been or are intended to be used by the Department in determining an individual employees qualifications for employment, promotion, transfer, or additional compensation, or in determining an individual employees discharge or discipline.
    • 4.Information of a personal nature about a person other than the member if disclosure of the information would constitute a clearly unwarranted invasion of the other persons privacy.
    • 5.Records relevant to any other pending claim between the Department and the member which may be discovered in a judicial proceeding.
    • 6.Investigatory or security records maintained by the Department to investigate criminal conduct by a member or other activity which could reasonably be expected to harm the Department or could by the members activity cause the Department financial liability, unless and until the Department takes adverse personnel action based on information in such records.
  • C.The Department shall retain the right to protect its records from loss, damage, or alteration in order to ensure the integrity of the records.
III.Procedures
  • A.The Department will allow a member at least two inspection requests per document in a calendar year when requests are made at reasonable intervals, unless otherwise provided in a collective bargaining agreement.
  • B.A member who wishes to inspect his personnel records must submit a request in writing to the commanding officer of the unit which maintains the record he wishes to inspect. If the members request is approved, the member will be provided with the opportunity to inspect the requested record within seven working days after the request was made in accordance with established procedures. If the commanding officer can reasonably demonstrate that the requested record cannot be made available within the seven working day deadline, the Department will have an additional seven days to comply with the request. All requests to review personnel records will be annotated in the file requested reviewed.
  • C.A request received from a former member will be forwarded to the commanding officer of the unit that maintains the requested record.
  • D.After a member has reviewed the requested record, he may obtain a photocopy of all or part of the record upon submission of a written request.
  • E.If a member disagrees with any information contained in his personnel record, a removal or correction of that information may be agreed upon by the Department and the member. If an agreement cannot be reached, the member may submit a written statement explaining his reasons for disagreeing with the information and the statement will be attached to the disputed record. The employees statement shall be included whenever that disputed portion of the personnel record is released to a third party as long as the disputed record is a part of the file.
  • F.A member involved in a grievance procedure against the Department may designate, in writing, a representative to inspect the members personnel record which may have a bearing on the resolution of the grievance (except as limited in Items II-B-1 through 6).
  • G.If a member can demonstrate that he is unable to review his personnel record in person at the location where it is maintained (e.g., the member is confined to a bed or is a former member who resides outside the City) the Department will, upon the members written request, mail a copy of the requested record to the member.
  • H.The member shall have remedy through legal action to have false information expunged.
  • I.Personnel records are maintained at the following locations:
    • 1.Human Resources Division - the members personnel jacket, computerized performance ratings and award summaries, etc.
    • 2.Internal Affairs Division - the members record of pervious disciplinary actions (summary punishment and Complaint Register investigation case files).
    • 3.Education and Training Division - training cards and Probationary Police Officer Performance Evaluations (CPD-62.158).
    • 4.Unit Assigned - Personnel Card (CPD-11.600), Deputy Clerk affidavits, Personnel Change Notices (CPD-11.611), Personnel Action Requests (CPD-11.612), etc.
IV.Release of Information to Third Parties
  • A.The Department will not release information concerning a members disciplinary history without written notice to the affected member except when:
    • 1.the member has specifically waived written notice as part of a written, signed employment application with another employer.
    • 2.the disclosure is ordered to a party in a legal action or arbitration.
    • 3.the information is requested by a government agency as a result of a claim or complaint by a member or as a result of a criminal investigation by such agency.
    • 4.the information is requested by a member of the Department authorized to review such information.
  • B.The written notice to the affected member will be sent by first class mail to the member’s last known address and will be mailed on or before the day the information is divulged.
  • C.Disciplinary history information which is more than four years old at the time a request for such information is received (more than three years old if employee is a member of AFSCME) will not be released to a third party except when the information is ordered to a party in a legal action or arbitration.
