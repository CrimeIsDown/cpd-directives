ParkChicago – Mobile Payment For On-Street Parking Program
Issue Date:14 April 2014Effective Date:14 April 2014
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive introduces the ParkChicago – Mobile Payment For On-Street Parking Program
II.ParkChicago – Mobile Payment For On-Street Parking Program
ParkChicago is a pay by cell phone parking meter payment option that allows the payment of parking meter charges via a smartphone application.
  • A.The same on-street parking rules and regulations apply and include:
    • 1.metered rates,
    • 2.time limitations and parking restrictions,
    • 3.vehicle portability.
  • B.Three payment methods, in addition to the paybox include:
    • 1.Mobile app available at smartphone application stores.
    • 2.Voice system (phone number displayed on paybox).
    • 3.Mobile web at www.parkchicago.com.
  • C.The following information is required to use the ParkChicago payment system and to start parking:
    • 1.Valid credit card (for first-time users).
    • 2.Zone number (located on signs and paybox).
    • 3.License plate of vehicle parked.
    • 4.Time purchased (up to the maximum allowed).
III.Scope
The ParkChicago – Mobile Payment For On-Street Parking Program will:
  • A.start as a 30-day pilot effective 15 April 2014 in a select area of the 12th District. During this phase of the program, training will be conducted on-site. A limited number of smartphones will be deployed along with a PDT application.
  • B.be implemented Citywide effective 15 May 2014 with an eLearning training module. Additional smartphones will be issued.
  • C.be under the command of the Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT).
IV.Parking Enforcement
  • A.Parking Monitor Application – Enforcement Tool:
    • 1.Vehicle based officers will access the Parking Monitor Application via PDT.
    • 2.Foot, mounted, and bike officers will access the Parking Monitor Application via Department-issued smartphone.
  • B.Key Parking Ordinances:
    • 1.MCC Chapter 9-64-190(a)(1), Unlawful to park in a metered parking zone without paying the designated amount.
    • 2.MCC Chapter 9-64-190(a)(3), Unlawful to stay in a metered parking zone past the expiration time on the receipt.
  • C.Enforcement Process

    Department members enforcing the meter parking ordinance will:
    • 1.identify and review the receipt displayed on the vehicle dashboard.
    • 2.if no receipt or expired receipt is displayed, open the Parking Monitor Application mobile enforcement tool and enter the vehicle license plate number.
      • a.The Parking Monitor Application will sort the active sessions and search for the license plate number entered.
      • b.Only the license plate numbers of paid vehicles will be displayed.
      • c.If there is no match to the license plate number entered, or vehicle paid for lesser zone, Department members will issue the appropriate citation.
V.Responsibilities
  • A.Department members who utilize a Department-issued smartphone to access the Parking Monitor Application – Enforcement Tool will comply with the usage and dissemination policies outlined in the "CPD Internet, Email, and Text Messaging Policy" and the Department directives entitled:
    • 1.Department-Issued Electronic Communication Devices,
    • 2.Department Telephones,
    • 3.Access to Computerized Data, Dissemination and Retention of Computer Data.
      NOTE:
      Department members will not use personally-owned electronic communication devices to access the Parking Monitor Application – Enforcement Tool.
  • B.Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT) will:
    • 1.be responsible for any technical aspects of the program.
    • 2.ensure the program is in compliance with all record retention requirements related to comparable forms of electronic data and imaging systems.
    • 3.develop training courses specifically designed for the ParkChicago program.
