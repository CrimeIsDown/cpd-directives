Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-03-07
Chicago Automated Time and Attendance System – Pilot Program
Issue Date:11 May 1998Effective Date:11 May 1998
Rescinds:D98-19
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs members that the Chicago Automated Time & Attendance (CATA) system will be implemented initially as a pilot program and then implemented throughout the entire Department on an incremental basis. The sequence of implementation and the dates of CATA activation will be announced by the Bureau of Administrative Services. The actual dates of unit training will be announced by the Education and Training Division via the Administrative Message Facsimile Network.
  • B.informs members that they will be required to use their identification cards to electronically “swipe” in and out at the identification card readers (time clocks) located in or near Department units.
  • C.establishes policy and procedures for the use of the CATA system.
  • D.identifies the duties and responsibilities for command, supervisory, sworn and civilian members.
  • E.initiates the use of the Multiple CATA Edit form (CPD-11.672).
  • F.initiates the use of the Individual CATA Edit form (CPD-11.671).
II.Policy
It is the policy of the Department to maintain accurate employee time and attendance records. Automation of this function enables the Department to collect, calculate, maintain, and retrieve time and attendance information more efficiently to the benefit of both the Department and its members.
III.General Information
  • A.All current manual timekeeping procedures will continue in conjunction with the new CATA system until further notice.
  • B.All members, whether sworn or civilian, will normally be expected to swipe in and out at the identification card readers within their respective units. Exceptions include members who are detailed to another unit, are on an operational detail (if not reporting to their unit of assignment), or are attending court/legal proceeding. For these exceptions, members will swipe in and out at identification card readers located in their unit of detail, at the Command Post of their detail (if so equipped), or in court or other city buildings where members have been requested to appear.
    NOTE:
    Members assigned to units that do not check-in at a central location, but instead go directly to their assignments, may be exempted from swiping in and out within their units of assignment. Bureau deputy superintendents may authorize two alternatives for such personnel: (1) swipe in once during their tour of duty, or (2) be completely exempted from the swiping process. Commanders / commanding officers for these particular units will submit a To-From-Subject report to their bureau deputy superintendents explaining the difficulty swiping in and out presents for their members. Bureau deputy superintendents will ensure that the requesting unit is notified when either alternative is approved. The Superintendent will be responsible for authorizing the above exemption for those units which report directly to the Office of the Superintendent. During training, timekeepers will be informed as to the procedures for these particular units.
  • C.Identification card readers may not be installed at every location at which members have reason to appear. In such instances, whenever possible, members will swipe in and out at any identification card reader within a close proximity to the reporting location. If there is no identification card reader within a close proximity, members will complete an Individual Edit form upon return to their unit of assignment and submit it to a supervisor for authorization.
    NOTE:
    Members not returning to their units on the same day they appeared in a location without an identification card reader, and who are unable to swipe at another location within a close proximity, will telephone a supervisor in their unit of assignment, and the supervisor will complete and sign a CATA Edit form for the member.
  • D.In the event the CATA system is not operable, the CATA Help Desk, will immediately be notified. If necessary, Public Safety Information Technology will forward copies of the Attendance and Assignment Record (CPD-11.605) in order to manually record member’s time until the CATA system is available.
  • E.Sworn and civilian members must have their identification cards in their possession when reporting on duty, while on duty and when reporting off duty.
  • F.A member whose identification card is lost / stolen or inoperable will obtain a replacement. During the period the member’s identification card is lost / stolen or inoperable; the member will complete an Individual Edit form and submit it to a supervisor for approval.
IV.General Swiping Procedures
  • A.Members will swipe in and out with the picture side of their identification cards facing them and the bar code (the dark area on the back of the identification card) toward the identification card reader. With the photo portion at the bottom of the identification card, slide the card from the top of the identification card reader to the bottom.
    NOTE:
    Before inserting their identification card into the identification card reader, members will ensure that the bar code on their identification cards is clean and dry.
  • B.The identification card reader will display a green light and signal one short audible beep when the system has accepted the swipe.
  • C.The system will display a red light and two audible beeps when the swipe has not been accepted.
V.Unit Procedures
  • A.All members required to swipe in and out will:
    • 1.swipe in no earlier than 7 minutes before their scheduled starting time.
    • 2.swipe out no earlier than 7 minutes before the end of their scheduled tour of duty and no later than 7 minutes after the end of their scheduled tour of duty.
      EXAMPLE:
      (1) If a member’s scheduled tour of duty is an 8 hour shift and the member works the second watch ending his tour of duty at 1630 hours, the member will only swipe out between 1623 hours and 1637 hours.
      EXAMPLE:
      (2) If a member is required to work an 8 hour shift on the second watch, and is scheduled to end his tour of duty at 1400 hours, the member will only swipe out between 1353 hours and 1407 hours.
    • 3.swipe in and out, in uniform (if applicable).
    • 4.swipe in, prior to roll call (if applicable).
    • 5.notify a supervisor and complete an Individual CATA Edit form if they are unable to swipe for any reason, or if they have forgotten to swipe.
    • 6.follow the procedures listed in Item III-F of this directive if their identification cards are lost, stolen or inoperable.
    • 7.notify a supervisor if they discover that an identification card reader is inoperable or is displaying an incorrect time.
  • B.Supervisors will:
    • 1.swipe in and out within the times prescribed in Items V-A-1 and 2.
    • 2.authorize Individual CATA Edit forms completed by members who do not have their identification cards or have forgotten to swipe in.
    • 3.authorize Individual CATA Edit forms for members who have lost / stolen or inoperable identification cards.
    • 4.ensure that procedures outlined in Item III-F of this directive for lost / stolen or inoperable identification cards are followed.
    • 5.submit Individual CATA Edit forms to authorized personnel responsible for entering daily edits into the CATA system.
    • 6.call the CATA Help Desk to report an identification card reader that is malfunctioning or displaying an incorrect time.
  • C.Watch commanders / non-command staff unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.swipe in and out within the times prescribed in Items V-A-1 and 2.
    • 2.review and approve properly submitted Edit forms received from supervisors and authorized personnel responsible for entering daily edits into the CATA system.
    • 3.review and approve the properly completed A & A report and submit it to the timekeeper.
    • 4.ensure a sign is posted directing members to another identification card reader, if an identification card reader is found to be malfunctioning or displaying an incorrect time.
    • 5.review members’ records and take corrective action when members are not swiping in or out on time. Members will be reminded that continued incidences of undocumented / unapproved late or early swiping may subject them to disciplinary action.
    • 6.review the Unapproved Overtime Report submitted by the timekeeper and determine the status of the undocumented additional time. Each incident of unapproved overtime will be disapproved by writing disapproved next to the item. The report will then be signed and returned to the timekeeper.
      NOTE:
      The Unapproved Overtime Report will also be submitted by the timekeeper to the tactical lieutenant for review of the unapproved overtime of tactical and gang tactical personnel.
  • D.command members will be required to only swipe in once during their tour of duty.
VI.Overtime Procedures
  • A.Members will:
    • 1.continue to complete an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (CPD-11.608), authorized by their supervisor before overtime is worked as outlined in the Department directive entitled “Payroll and Timekeeping.”
    • 2.follow the procedures outlined in Item VII of this directive regarding court related overtime.
    • 3.swipe in and out when working other authorized overtime situations (i.e., “call back,” “working RDO”) and reporting to a location that has an identification card reader. If members working overtime do not have access to an identification card reader, they will follow the procedures outlined in Item III-C of this directive.
    • 4.complete both an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report and an Individual CATA Edit form, if they elect to have the additional 4 hours for working a holiday credited as compensatory time instead of pay.
    • 5.be credited for overtime according to their bargaining unit contract.
  • B.Supervisors will:
    • 1.follow the procedures outlined in Items VI-A-1 through 5 of this directive when working overtime.
    • 2.continue to authorize overtime via the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report, before the overtime is worked.
    • 3.authorize completed Individual CATA Edit forms for those members who are unable to swipe or who have appeared at any location without an identification card reader.
    • 4.follow the procedures outlined in Item III-C of this directive when a member has worked overtime in a location without an identification card reader and will not be returning to the unit of assignment on that day.
    • 5.submit all CATA Edit forms to the watch commander / unit commanding officer.
    • 6.be credited for overtime according to their bargaining unit contract.
  • C.Watch commanders / unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.continue to approve the Overtime / Compensatory Time report.
    • 2.approve properly submitted Individual CATA Edit forms and Multiple CATA Edit forms.
    • 3.if the member is not assigned to his watch, indicate the overtime earned on the appropriate A&A report and forward the edit form / overtime slip to the member’s watch commander for entry into the CATA system.
  • D.Unit timekeepers will:
    • 1.continue to maintain members’ overtime through the use of the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report and the Time and Attendance Record, until further notice.
    • 2.compare the CATA system to the current manual system for accuracy, until such time as the manual system is no longer used.
    • 3.adjust the CATA system according to manual records, after supervisory approval.
    • 4.notify watch commanders if members fail to follow the overtime procedures outlined in this directive.
VII.Court Procedures
  • A.Department members will:
    • 1.continue to follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Court Call Schedule.”
    • 2.if off-duty, continue to follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Payroll and Timekeeping.”
    • 3.swipe in for all court, judicial related or legal proceeding appearances, whether on or off duty by pressing the F-12 and then the “enter” key on the keypad before swiping in.
    • 4.swipe out for all court / legal proceeding appearances whether on or off duty, by pressing the F-12 and then the “enter” key before swiping out.
      EXCEPTION:
      A member whose tour of duty ends while in court / legal proceeding, and who will not be returning to their units of assignment, will not use the F-12 and the enter key when swiping out of court. They will simply swipe out. Members whose tours of duty end while in court or other legal proceeding, and will be returning to their units of assignment, will use the F-12 and enter key when leaving court or other legal proceeding. Upon return to their unit, they will swipe out. This will signal the end of their tour of duty.
    • 5.if unable to swipe at court, complete an Individual CATA Edit form and submit it to the court supervisor / officer for authorization.
    • 6.if there is no identification card reader in a location, follow the procedures outlined in Item III-C of this directive.
  • B.Court supervisors / officers will:
    • 1.continue to comply with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Court Section Program.”
    • 2.assist members concerning court swiping procedures.
    • 3.authorize properly submitted Individual CATA Edit forms completed by members.
    • 4.attach the Individual CATA Edit form to the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report and submit it to the member’s unit of assignment.
    • 5.direct members whose identification cards are inoperable to have them replaced and comply with Item III-F of this directive.
  • C.Unit supervisors will:
    • 1.follow procedures outlined in VI-B of this directive if the court / legal proceeding appearance is outside of regular duty hours and constitutes overtime.
    • 2.follow the procedures outlined in Item III-C of this directive when a member has worked overtime in a location without an identification card reader and will not be returning to the unit of assignment on that day.
  • D.Watch commanders / unit commanding officers will follow the procedures outlined in Item VI-C of this directive.
  • E.Unit timekeepers will follow procedures outlined in VI-D of this directive.
VIII.Initial Set-Up Procedures
  • A.File set-up:
    • 1.Unit commanding officers / commanders will designate members by watch, as well as gang and tactical personnel, who have been trained in the CATA system, to be responsible for the initial set-up of member's files. This set-up will take place after the designated members have returned from training.
      NOTE:
      In smaller units, designated members will normally be the timekeeper or a member performing the duties of a timekeeper.
    • 2.Set-up will encompass entering the following information within the fields listed below in the Employee File Maintenance screen:
      • a.normal shift code,
      • b.class code,
      • c.home time clock(s) code,
      • d.beat number and vehicle number (if applicable),
      • e.furlough segments.
    • 3.Handouts outlining the codes for normal shift, class and home time clock(s) will be distributed at training.
      NOTE:
      Computer screen fields such as furlough and shift may need to be updated annually following furlough and steady watch selections. Fields such as beat and vehicle number may need to be updated on a more frequent basis.
    • 4.Initial set-up will also include verification and correction (if necessary) of the following data:
      • a.unit of assignment,
      • b.detailed to unit (if applicable),
      • c.court key,
      • d.day off group,
      • e.Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) day: (sworn = 8, civilian = 7).
    • 5.Designated desk / unit personnel will input data for:
      • a.their respective watch personnel.
      • b.second watch personnel, which includes all staff assigned to the second watch. Examples: unit commanding officer / commander’s office staff, the unit commanding officer / commander, neighborhood relations office, review, etc.
      • c.third watch personnel, which includes all personnel assigned to the third watch. Example: neighborhood relations staff working the third watch.
    • 6.Authorized tactical / gang tactical personnel will input data:
      • a.for their respective personnel.
      • b.for shift codes on a 28-day police period basis depending upon rotation.
        NOTE:
        Shift codes for the relief team will be reviewed on a daily basis to ensure that the time corresponds to the relief team’s schedule for that day. If the time does not correspond, see Item IX of this directive entitled “Edits.”
  • B.Accrual transfers. Unit timekeepers will:
    • 1.enter the accrual balances from the Time and Attendance Record into the CATA system as close to the date of implementation as possible.
    • 2.be responsible for the accurate transfer of accrual balances for compensatory time, FLSA compensatory time, vacation, baby furlough and personal days from the Time and Attendance Record to the CATA system.
    • 3.verify that the accrual balances on the Time and Attendance Report and the balances entered into the CATA system correspond.
    • 4.print a copy of the member’s accrual balances from the CATA system. Affix an Accrual Balance Verification label on both the copy of the members accrual balances and the top of the Time and Attendance Record. This copy will then be given each member.
      NOTE:
      Accrual Balance Verification labels will be given to timekeepers during their training session.
    • 5.have the sole authority to access the Accrual Screen with the exception of personnel designated by the unit commanding officer / commander.
    • 6.make any changes during the current payroll month (e.g., a personal day taken by a member is entered into the system as a baby furlough day). If the error took place during the payroll period dated 01 January, the correction must be made no later than 31 January.
      NOTE:
      For any changes that precede the current payroll month, timekeepers will submit a To-From-Subject report to the Finance Division, Payroll Section, listing the necessary change and the reason for the change.
IX.Edits
Edits will be made to members' files whenever changes occur to the data originally entered into the CATA system. Members responsible for this function will vary. In Bureau of Patrol districts and larger units in which operational personnel currently assist in the completion of the A&A Record (i.e., desk personnel, etc.), these members will be responsible for entering edits into the CATA system. In units without such personnel, timekeepers will be responsible for this function.
  • A.Designated district desk / unit personnel will:
    • 1.make edits from the daily worksheets for members assigned to their watch (i.e., assignment codes, beat assignments, pay codes and shift codes, compensatory time used, baby furlough day used, medical roll, or Injury on Duty, etc.) using the Multiple CATA Edit form.
    • 2.make edits immediately after the last roll call for their watch or immediately after being notified of a change / edit occurring during the tour.
    • 3.make these “day of” operational changes within the Full Screen Editor.
      NOTE:
      Edits for a future date will utilize the Scheduling feature.
    • 4.run the A&A report, after all worksheet edits and CATA Edit forms have been entered into the system.
    • 5.submit the A&A report to the member’s watch commander / unit commanding officer for his signature.
    • 6.investigate, and with the approval of the watch commander / unit commanding officer, correct irregularities pertaining to members time records (i.e., missing in or out swipes, incorrect use of the F-12 key while in court/legal proceeding, etc.) on a daily basis for the previous day.
  • B.Designated tactical / gang tactical personnel will:
    • 1.follow the procedures outlined in Item IX-A of this directive.
    • 2.edit assignment codes for tactical / gang tactical personnel who have tour of duty details.
    • 3.edit shift codes to reflect the actual schedule of the relief team as necessary.
      NOTE:
      If members’ starting times change to accommodate certain details or missions, member's shift codes must also be changed.
  • C.If a change occurs during the tour of duty (i.e., beat change, etc.) and the watch commander of the member requiring the change has not been relieved for the day, the supervisor authorizing the change will submit a CATA Edit form and Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (if applicable) and ensure that the change is entered into the CATA system. In addition, the supervisor will ensure the A&A report is run after all edits have been entered. The A&A report will then be submitted to the member’s watch commander / unit commanding officer for his signature and forwarded to the timekeeper who will ensure the CATA entry is correct.
  • D.Watch commanders / unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.ensure that watch level edits accurately reflect actual assignments.
    • 2.approve corrections of members time record irregularities discovered by authorized personnel or timekeepers.
    • 3.initially approve CATA edit forms submitted for personnel not assigned to their watch and forward them to the appropriate watch commander.
    • 4.if an edit is required after the watch commander of the member requiring the change has been relieved for the day, the on-duty watch commander will ensure that the edit is noted on the appropriate A&A report and the CATA Edit form and Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (if applicable) is forwarded to the member’s watch commander.
      EXAMPLE:
      (1) A member is excused (e.g. sick, excused early (non-disciplinary) on change day, 1st watch, shift change) after his watch commander is relieved for the day. The member’s supervisor will submit a CATA Edit form to the on-duty watch commander. The on-duty watch commander will authorize the edit and note it on the appropriate A&A report, then forward the CATA Edit form to the members watch commander for final approval and entry into the CATA system.
      EXAMPLE:
      (2) A member submits an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to request 2 hours compensatory time at the end of his tour of duty. The supervisor approving the use of compensatory time will submit a CATA Edit form and the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to the on-duty watch commander, who will authorize the edit and use of compensatory time. The on-duty watch commander will then indicate the edit on the appropriate A&A report, then forward both the CATA Edit form and the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to the member’s watch commander for final approval and entry into the CATA system.
    • 5.monitor and approve properly submitted CATA Edit forms.
    • 6.sign the properly submitted A & A report.
  • E.District/unit commanding officers will ensure that members comply with the procedures outlined in this directive.
  • F.Timekeepers will:
    • 1.be responsible for completing the procedures outlined in Item IX-A of this directive when assigned to a unit that lacks the operational personnel found in the districts and/or some larger units.
    • 2.receive the A&A reports with edit forms attached from watch commanders / commanding officers within districts and larger units.
    • 3.maintain both manual records and CATA system records, until the Finance Division has performed an audit and it has been determined that the CATA system is an acceptable replacement for the manual system.
X.Crossing Guards
  • A.Crossing guard coordinators will:
    • 1.swipe in and out.
    • 2.be instructed as to the procedure that will be followed regarding the exemption of swiping in and out. This procedure will be thoroughly outlined in the CATA training session.
    • 3.continue to use the manual system in addition to the CATA system, until further notice.
    • 4.be responsible for the initial file set-up within the Employee File Maintenance screen. This process will include entering the following data:
      • a.normal shift code,
      • b.class code.
        NOTE:
        Handouts outlining the codes for normal shift and class will be distributed at training.
  • B.Crossing guards will be exempt from swiping in or out, until further notice.
