I.Exit Interviews
  • A.The commanding officer, supervisor, or a designee will conduct an exit interview with a member terminating employment, including retiring and discharged members, or a member taking a leave of absence for a period of 30 days or more. Data obtained from the exit interview will be recorded on the Exit Interview Report formset (PER 78). Further explanation is required in the “Additional Comments Required” space for all separating members whether leaving voluntarily or involuntarily.
  • B.The terminating member will be requested to sign the Exit Interview Report. However, if the terminating member refuses, or is not available to sign, the interviewer will note the appropriate reason in the section designated “Employee Signature.”
  • C.When unable to conduct an exit interview (compulsory retirement, disciplinary discharge, absent without permission, etc.) the interviewer will compete the Exit Interview Report stating the reason for termination and any other pertinent data. The interviewer will state in the “Additional Comments Required” section that the member was not available.
  • D.Upon completion of the exit interview, the white and yellow copies of the formset will be forwarded directly to the Human Resources Division via police mail. The Human Resources Division will forward the white copy of the formset to the City of Chicago Department of Personnel. The pink copy of the formset will be given to the terminating member. (If the member is not available, the pink copy will be forwarded to the Police Human Resources Division.)
  • E.Data contained on the Exit Interview Report will be utilized by the City of Chicago Department of Personnel in responding to claims for unemployment compensation; therefore, the prompt return of this report is necessary.
  • F.Inquiries regarding preparation of the Exit Interview Report will be directed to the Employment Section, Human Resources Division.
  • G.Exit Interview Report forms are obtained from the Equipment and Supplies Section and are to be available for use in each district / unit.
II.Personnel Action Request
  • A.Members must complete the Personnel Action Request to initiate action for a transfer (except AFSCME), leave, resignation, separation to another City Department, and death in family.
  • B.Members must submit a City of Chicago Employee Information Form (PER 120) for all name change requests. Members completing the form will:
    • 1.check the box at the top of the form entitled “Changing Employee Information.”
    • 2.complete the employee information in the spaces provided using the new name.
    • 3.check the “Yes” box towards the bottom of the form entitled “Changing Employee Information.”
    • 4.check the “Name” box and enter the former name in the space following “Previous Information.”
    • 5.make two copies of the form. Submit one copy of the form to the unit secretary and forward the other copy to the Human Resources Division - Records Section.
    • 6.forward the completed original form to the Benefits Management Office.
  • C.All requests for a name change must include certified / legal documents.
III.Personnel Change Notice
  • A.Members will use the Personnel Change Notice (CPD-11.611) to notify the Human Resources Division of changes in the following items:
    • 1.Home address / zip code / district of residence
    • 2.Telephone number
    • 3.Savings bond payroll deductions, amounts only (all other changes are made on a CO 51 form.)
    • 4.Persons to be notified in an emergency
    • 5.Withholding tax, along with appropriate Federal and State W4 forms.
    • 6.Marital status (include name change [if any], spouse’s name, address, and telephone number)
    • 7.Additional educational achievement (particularly degrees or certificates)
    • 8.Newly acquired language skills
    • 9.Military status (change in rank, unit, service, termination date, etc.)
    • 10.Other, specify change in the remarks section of the form.
  • B.For changes in personal data reflected in Items III-A-1, 2, 4, and 6, the Personnel Change Notice must be submitted in duplicate. The original will be forwarded to the Human Resources Division and the duplicate copy will be forwarded to the district commander or unit commanding officer to ensure that the unit / district records are updated to reflect any changes.
  • C.Any Department member who reports a change of residence address must also complete and attach two copies of the Residency Declaration / Change of Address form (PER 72) to the Personnel Change Notice. The PER 72 form is subsequently forwarded to City Hall.
  • D.Personnel Change Notices, Personnel Action Request Forms, Residency Declaration / Change of Address forms and Exit Interview forms are available from the unit secretary.
  • E.Notification of all changes will be submitted no later than three days after the change.
IV.Health Benefit Changes
Members who experience a change (addition or deletion) in their dependents (spouse or children) must contact the Human Resources Division Hospitalization Insurance Unit for information on procedures to be followed to ensure health insurance coverage for such dependents.
NOTE:
Formal notification of changes must be received by the Benefits Office within 30 days of the change or coverage may be denied.
V.Beneficiary Changes
Certain personnel changes may also necessitate changes in beneficiary. Beneficiary changes may be necessary concerning: Pension Fund, Credit Union, Deferred Compensation, Union, State Duty Death Benefit (contact Special Activities Unit), City Life Insurance Providers (City Paid / Optional) and Savings Bonds. Information related to any of the organizations / programs listed above may be obtained form the Human Resources Division, Insurance Unit.
