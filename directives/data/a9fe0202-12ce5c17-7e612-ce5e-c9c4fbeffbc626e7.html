Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S08-01-03
Complaint Summary Reporting and Review Procedures
Issue Date:14 May 2013Effective Date:14 May 2013
Rescinds:17 March 2013 Version
Index Category:Professionalism
I.Purpose
This directive sets forth reporting procedures and provides for command review.
II.Procedures for Bureau of Internal Affairs Investigations
  • A.In investigations where:
    • 1.there is a finding of misconduct by a Department member(s) that results in a disciplinary recommendation that exceeds a five (5) day suspension, the investigator will prepare an automated Summary Report using the Automated Complaint System (ACS);
    • 2.the result is unfounded, exonerated, not sustained, or sustained and does not exceed a five (5) day suspension, the investigator will prepare an automated Summary Report Digest or Summary Report using the ACS.
  • B.The investigator will ensure that either the Summary Report or the Summary Report Digest, as applicable, contains the:
    • 1.employee number of the known accused member; and
    • 2.finding of the allegation. For sustained cases, the Summary Report or the Summary Report Digest must also include:
      • a.the number(s) of the Department Rules and Regulations which was violated;
      • b.the text of the rule(s);
      • c.a statement indicating when, where, and how the rule was violated by the accused member; and
      • d.the recommended disciplinary action.
  • C.For all investigations, the investigator will submit the final investigation report to their exempt unit commanding officer, or designee, who will review the investigation.
III.Command Channel Review
  • A.The first level exempt member in the accused member’s chain of command, upon receipt of a final report of an investigation, will review the investigation to ascertain the adequacy and timeliness of the investigation, determine that any recommendation is appropriate, and judge the soundness of the conclusions and the findings, including returning the investigation for further action, if necessary.
  • B.If a command channel review exempt member:
    • 1.determines that additional investigation is necessary, they will reject the investigation and identify the reason(s) for rejection and the area(s) of concern within the command channel review comments section;
      NOTE:
      For non-IPRA investigations, if the investigation is rejected by the first level exempt member, the investigation will be returned to the investigating member for immediate action.
    • 2.disapproves the findings or recommendation, they will indicate a detailed justification supporting the alternate conclusion in the command channel review comments section;
    • 3.approves the final investigation, they will indicate their concurrence on the appropriate command channel review page and submit the original investigation to the Advocate Section, BIA, for command channel review routing;
    • 4.has insufficient space in the command channel review comments section to provide detailed instructions, justifications, and/or alternate conclusions, they will attach a To-From-Subject report with any supporting documentation to the ACS;
    • 5.determines there is evidence, in sustained findings, which indicates any culpability by supervisory personnel for the violation (failure of supervisory personnel at any level of command to hold subordinates accountable requires disciplinary action), and if culpability is apparent, the exempt member will obtain a separate Log Number and the investigation will be conducted by the BIA or IPRA.
  • C.Investigations will normally require only two (2) levels of exempt member review. Upon a finding of concurrence by the second level exempt member, the investigative report will be routed to the BIA or IPRA, as appropriate.
  • D.Investigations meeting the below criteria will be subject to command channel review by the First Deputy Superintendent:
    • 1.investigations where a second level command channel review exempt member is not available in the accused member's chain of command;
    • 2.investigations where the allegation(s) is sustained with a penalty recommendation of a suspension of 16 days or more.
    • 3.where the accused member is alleged to have committed a crime, including domestic battery;
    • 4.when an allegation was or could be made to the Equal Employment Opportunity Officer (including allegations of cultural bias, sexual harassment, or other violations prohibited by prevailing employment laws);
    • 5.in which an exempt member is the accused;
    • 6.in which the accused member is assigned to a unit under the Office of the Superintendent;
    • 7.that are resubmitted through command channel review after an initial non-concurrence.
  • E.In those cases where there are conflicting determinations by the command channel review exempt members, the Advocate Section, BIA, will determine the appropriate exempt member for additional command channel review.
  • F.In those cases with a sustained finding, the original investigation and additional documentation supporting an alternate conclusion will be submitted through command channel review to the appropriate bureau chief.
    • 1.Upon review and approval by the bureau chief, the investigation will be routed to the BIA or IPRA, as appropriate.
    • 2.Following the approval of the bureau chief, no new allegation(s) may be added by anyone, except the Superintendent, without consulting the chief who approved the final CR investigation.
IV.Adjudication
Police Board Review of Suspension
  • A.The Police Board review of suspension is:
    • 1.a review of the written investigative case file only, and an accused member is not afforded the opportunity to personally appear and testify before the Board in such instances;
    • 2.conducted pursuant to Article IV of the Police Board’s Rules of Procedure. http://www.chicagopoliceboard.org.
  • B.When the Superintendent orders a suspension of six (6) days up to 30 days, the case file will be returned to BIA/IPRA who will notify the station supervisor/unit commanding officer of the member facing suspension that a Request For/Waiver of Police Board Review of Suspension (CPD-44.222) has been forwarded to the member facing suspension and requires the member’s attention. The station supervisor/unit commanding officer will instruct the member facing suspension to:
    • 1.use the ACS to either:
      • a.waive the Police Board review of the suspension, or
      • b.request for Police Board review of suspension. To do so, by filing:
        • (1)in person at the Office of the Police Board, or
        • (2)a request by certified mail sent directly to the Office of the Police Board. The date and time of the postmark of the certified mailing will be the date and time of the filing.
          NOTE:
          At the time of the filing, or within five (5) business days thereafter, a statement and documentary evidence, if any, indicating to the Police Board the reason(s) the member declines to accept the disciplinary action may be submitted by the member.
    • 2.submit the form within three (3) business days after viewing it in the ACS. A failure to file the request within three (3) business days waives the member’s right to such review.
      NOTE:
      Members are advised to print out the Request For/Waiver of Police Board Review of Suspension form (CPD-44.222) before submitting it in the ACS.
    • 3.understand that if the member elects to choose an option that requires a written report or supporting documentation, that it is the member’s responsibility to ensure the written report is received in a timely manner.
