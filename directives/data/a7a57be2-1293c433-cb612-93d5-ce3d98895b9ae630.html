Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U05-01
Unit Inspection and Audits
Issue Date:22 February 2012Effective Date:22 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 24 April 2003; S03-06
Index Category:Accountability
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines Department policy for conducting and responding to inspections and unit audits.
  • B.establishes procedures for Department inspections and designates personnel for conducting Department inspections.
  • C.establishes procedures for inspections by outside agencies.
II.Policy
To ensure the highest level of police service, command and supervisory personnel will conduct inspections of personnel, facilities, and equipment in their areas of responsibility as often as may be necessary.
III.Nature of Inspections
  • A.As designated by this directive, Department supervisory and command personnel will:
    • 1.within their area of responsibility, conduct inspections of personnel, vehicles, materials, unit operational procedures, records, and reporting practices.
    • 2.ensure:
      • a.the appropriate maintenance of Department vehicles, facilities, and equipment.
      • b.that operational procedures, records and reporting practices are in compliance with the established Departmental policies and procedures.
      • c.that corrective action is taken whenever irregularities are discovered.
    • 3.report the results of their inspections to the next level of command.
  • B.Exempt command personnel who command units which operate on more than one watch a day will personally devote a minimum of ten hours each police period to the inspection and related duties as listed in Item III-A of this directive.
  • C.Other commanding officers and supervisory personnel will conduct inspections and perform the related duties listed in Item III-A of this directive during their assigned duty hours.
IV.Inspection Responsibilities
  • A.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.conduct at least one inspection each month of their facilities and equipment.
    • 2.if an item is in need of repair or replacement, prepare and forward a Unit Inspection Report through the chain of command to the appropriate Bureau Chief.
      NOTE:
      Units within the Office of the Superintendent will forward any Unit Inspection Report to the Chief, Bureau of Administration.
  • B.Exempt command personnel will:
    • 1.review Unit Inspection Reports.
    • 2.initial and date approved reports in the appropriate space provided on the form.
    • 3.forward the report to the next command level.
  • C.Bureau Chiefs
    • 1.Bureau Chiefs (other than the Chief, Bureau of Administration) will:
      • a.review Unit Inspection Reports for those units under their respective command or responsibility.
      • b.initial and date approved reports in the appropriate space provided on the form.
      • c.forward the reports to the Chief, Bureau of Administration.
    • 2.Chief, Bureau of Administration will:
      • a.review Unit Inspection Reports for units within the Bureau of Administration.
      • b.initial and date approved reports in the appropriate space provided on the form.
      • c.forward all reports either personally approved or approved by the appropriate Bureau Chief to the Department of General Services for implementation of the necessary corrective action.
  • D.Inspections by Inspections Division
    • 1.The Inspections Division will:
      • a.conduct field inspections on behalf of the Superintendent.
        NOTE:
        These inspections will be conducted to both verify compliance with Department policy and identify procedural deficiencies and irregularities.
      • b.record unit level deficiencies and irregularities on the Inspection Report.
      • c.conduct facility audits, timekeeping audits, and special audits on behalf of the Superintendent.
        NOTE:
        When appropriate, the Inspections Division will provide the unit with a formatted computer disk for responses to timekeeping, facility, and special audits.
      • d.compile and maintain all unit responses to Inspection Reports, facility audits, timekeeping audits, and special audits.
      • e.perform follow-up inspections as needed to ensure that deficiencies and irregularities are corrected.
    • 2.Unit commanding officers will:
      • a.within 21 days of receiving an Inspection Report for deficiencies and irregularities, determine what action will be taken (i.e., training, counseling, etc.).
      • b.when necessary, request an extension to respond to an Inspection Report.
      • c.submit the original Inspection Report with necessary documentation through the appropriate chain of command.
      • d.forward a copy of the completed Inspection Report directly to the Inspections Division.
      • e.for a response to a timekeeping, facility, or special audit, utilize the formatted computer disk, supplied by the Inspections Division, to provide the required response to the Inspections Division.
    • 3.The inspection and auditing responsibilities attributed to the Inspections Division will not relieve command and supervisory personnel of their assigned responsibilities for conducting inspections.
V.Inspections by Outside Agencies
  • A.Illinois Department of Labor
    Safety inspectors from the Illinois Department of Labor (IDOL) have the statutory right to enter and inspect a Department facility without making prior arrangements.
    • 1.When a representative from the IDOL requests to inspect a Department facility, the unit commanding officer or station supervisor will ensure that:
      • a.the representative is allowed immediate access to the Department facility without delay.
      • b.a supervisor accompanies the inspector as a witness during the inspection.
      • c.a telephone notification is made to:
        • 1.the exempt member from the unit being inspected, if not present,
        • 2.CPIC, and
        • 3.the Office of Legal Affairs.
    • 2.The exempt staff member will:
      • a.if the IDOL notes an infraction regarding the facility, document the infraction on a Unit Inspection Report.
      • b.forward the report through the chain of command to the appropriate Bureau Chief.
  • B.The Illinois Department of Corrections
    The Illinois Department of Corrections (IDOC) may conduct inspections of any adult lock-up facility for compliance with the standards established by the Department of Corrections.
    • 1.When a representative from the IDOC requests to inspect an adult lock-up facility, with or without notification, the unit commanding officer or station supervisor will ensure that:
      • a.a supervisor accompanies the inspector as a witness during the inspection.
      • b.a telephone notification is made to:
        • 1.the exempt member from the unit being inspected, if not present,
        • 2.Crime Prevention and Information Center (CPIC), and
        • 3.the Office of Legal Affairs.
    • 2.The unit commanding officer of exempt rank will:
      • a.if the IDOC notes an infraction regarding the facility, document the infraction on a Unit Inspection Report.
      • b.forward the report through the chain of command to the appropriate Bureau Chief.
