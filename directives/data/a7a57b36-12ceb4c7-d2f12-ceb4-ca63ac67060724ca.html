Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-08
Processing School Absentees
Issue Date:20 May 2002Effective Date:21 May 2002
Rescinds:S02-08
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive outlines procedures for:
  • A.handling school absentees.
  • B.processing School Absentee Reports.
II.Field Procedures
  • A.When a sworn member of the Department observes a juvenile less than sixteen years of age during periods of time when school is in session, the juvenile will be questioned to ascertain the reason for the absence.
  • B.If there is no valid reason for the absence, the member will:
    • 1.advise the dispatcher of the location and nature of the stop.
    • 2.complete a School Absentee Report.
    • 3.name check the juvenile and record the results in the "ADDITIONAL INFORMATION" field of the School Absentee Report.
  • C.When a school absentee is not involved in a criminal or quasi-criminal offense, the member will, based on their investigation, determine if the school absentee should be taken home or to school, and take one of the following actions:
    • 1.transport the school absentee to the school of attendance when the school is located in:
      • a.the district of occurrence, or
      • b.an adjacent district and a supervisor authorizes the transport.
        NOTE:
        The name and address of the school official, parent, legal guardian, or other adult accepting responsibility for the school absentee is required on the School Absentee Report. A copy of the School Absentee Report will not be left at the school.
    • 2.transport the school absentee home when a responsible adult is present in the home and the home is located in:
      • a.the district of occurrence, or
      • b.an adjacent district and a supervisor authorizes the transport.
        NOTE:
        If the person accepting responsibility for the school absentee refuses to give a name, the member will attempt to establish the relationship of the person to the absentee and write the relationship of the person and the word refused (e.g., "Uncle Refused") in the "IF RELEASED, GIVE NAME AND ADDRESS OF PERSON RELEASED TO" field of the School Absentee Report. A copy of the School Absentee Report will not be left at the absentees residence.
    • 3.transport the school absentee to the district station for processing when:
      • a.the school or home is not located within the district of occurrence or an adjacent district, or
      • b.the home address or school cannot be determined.
        NOTE:
        The transport of a school absentee will additionally be governed by the provisions of Department directives concerning the requirement for proper supervisory authorization to leave an assigned location (e.g., fixed post, mission site, beat / sector).
  • D.If the school absentee is transported to the district station, the member will:
    • 1.complete the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody.
    • 2.contact the area Special Victims Section and request a name check for the minor.
    • 3.make every effort to contact the parent or legal guardian of the absentee, recording the efforts in the "ADDITIONAL INFORMATION" field of the School Absentee Report (e.g., called grandmothers home, 123-4567 at 1300 hrs, no answer).
    • 4.allow sufficient time for the parent or legal guardian to respond to the district from work or home.
    • 5.if a parent or guardian cannot be located with sufficient effort or has not responded within a reasonable amount of time:
      • a.notify the area Special Victims Section and inform the investigator that:
        • (1)there is a juvenile in the district station who is being processed as a school absentee, and
        • (2)efforts to contact a parent or legal guardian have failed or sufficient time for a response has passed.
      • b.turn the absentee over to a youth investigator for a disposition.
  • E.When the school absentee has also been apprehended for a criminal or quasi-criminal offense, members will:
    • 1.process the school absentee according to the Department directives entitled "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" and "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
    • 2.complete a School Absentee Report.
    • 3.check "YES" in the "Other Offense" field of the School Absentee Report.
    • 4.record the charge placed against the juvenile in the "Additional Information" field of the School Absentee Report.
III.Other Responsibilities
  • A.The desk sergeant will:
    • 1.ensure that the member completes the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody.
    • 2.ensure that all necessary information is contained in each School Absentee Report.
    • 3.indicate approval by signing the School Absentee Report in the lower-right corner of the narrative section.
    • 4.ensure that all reasonable efforts to contact a parent or legal guardian are exhausted and the parent or legal guardian is given a sufficient amount of time to respond to the district station.
    • 5.if applicable, arrange for transportation of the juvenile to the parent/legal guardian or alternate placement after the youth investigators have completed their investigation.
  • B.The Area Special Victims Section will:
    • 1.perform a juvenile name check when requested by Department members.
    • 2.take custody of the school absentee when a school or home address cannot be established and all reasonable efforts to contact the parent or legal guardian have failed and:
      • a.if the juvenile is released to the custody of a parent or legal guardian, enter the name and address of the person in the appropriate field of the School Absentee Report.
      • b.if the home address, school, or name of a parent or legal guardian cannot be established, proceed according to existing policy as outlined by the Juvenile Court and by Youth Investigations Section directives for temporary placement.
      • c.if a home, school, or name of a parent or guardian is established and transportation is needed, inform the desk sergeant of the need for transportation.
    • 3.complete the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody.
      NOTE:
      The transporting officer will enter the name and address of the person to whom the school absentee is being released in the If Released, Give Name and Address of Person Released To field of the School Absentee Report.
IV.Disposition
  • A.The district citation clerk will:
    • 1.review all School Absentee Reports for accuracy and completeness.
    • 2.prepare the Transmittal / Curfew Violation-School Absentee form in duplicate, attach both copies of all School Absentee Reports, and forward to the Youth Investigations Section.
    • 3.retain the duplicate copy of the Transmittal / Curfew Violation-School Absentee form in the district in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
  • B.The Youth Investigations Section Administration curfew/school absentee analyst will:
    • 1.receive and review the School Absentee Reports forwarded from the districts.
    • 2.prepare daily, monthly, and yearly summary reports and forward in accordance with existing Youth Investigations Section directives.
    • 3.retain the record copies of the School Absentee Reports in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
    • 4.forward the duplicate copies of the School Absentee Reports to the Board of Education.
