Fire and Arson Incidents
Issue Date:26 August 2020Effective Date:26 August 2020
Rescinds:19 September 2014 Version
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Policy
It is the policy of the Chicago Police Department to investigate and accurately report all fire-related incidents and threats. All Department members will extend whatever assistance is required and cooperate to the fullest extent with members of the Chicago Fire Department at all times.
II.Field Personnel Responsibilities
  • A.Department members in the field assigned to a fire incident will:
    • 1.determine if the incident is bona fide and notify the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC);
    • 2.if necessary, assist in the rescue of endangered persons;
    • 3.notify OEMC if there are any persons present in need of medical treatment;
    • 4.if necessary, establish fire lanes, traffic and crowd control, and a perimeter;
    • 5.not block arrival or departure of responding fire department apparatus or access to fire hydrants;
    • 6.determine if the assigned police personnel and equipment are sufficient and request additional assistance, as necessary;
    • 7.keep the dispatcher informed of conditions and request a supervisor, if required;
    • 8.notify a supervisor for any displaced persons and request the appropriate resources (e.g., warming bus, Illinois Department of Human Services (IDHS), The American Red Cross in Chicago & Northern Illinois); and
    • 9.attempt to ascertain the cause and origin of the incident.

  • C.A Department member that finds it necessary to assist in the rescue of endangered persons will:
    • 1.only enter a building if there is an imminent danger to life and it is necessary and
      prudent to assist;
    • 2.notify OEMC of all observations connected with the fire and the intent to enter the
      building prior to making entry; and
    • 3.proceed with caution and frequently update OEMC and responding Chicago Fire
      Department Personnel with their location.
    • 4.if necessary, use the stairs to reach the reported fire location, and:
      • a.under no circumstances use the elevator;
      • b.when possible, ascend in teams of two;
      • c.prior to ascending a staircase, conduct a visual inspection of the stairwell for
        smoke or flames;
      • d.advise OEMC of the staircase being used by direction (e.g., north, south, east, west) or any other identifying characteristic of the staircase; and
      • e.prior to opening any door where a fire may be located:
        • (1)place the back of the hand on the door that separates the possible
          fire from the member; and
        • (2)conduct a visual inspection through a door or window opening for the
          presence of smoke or fire.
        WARNING:
        An unusually warm door or the presence of smoke or fire are indicators that fire is beyond the door. This can be an extremely dangerous condition requiring personal protective equipment to enter and is considered immediately dangerous to life and health. In these cases, members should not open the door or enter the hallway as such action can draw smoke and/or fire toward the member.
III.Office of Emergency Management and Communications Responsibilities
  • A.Upon notification, the OEMC dispatcher will immediately dispatch a patrol unit from the district of occurrence and make any other required notifications.
  • B.If fire department equipment is required in a police emergency, the OEMC dispatcher will notify the Chicago Fire Department of the situation.
  • C.Rebroadcast pertinent updates on the Fire Department Zone to the Police Department Zone (e.g., numerous calls received, affected floor, persons trapped, injuries).
  • D.If Department members on scene are in immediate danger from smoke, heat, or fire, notify the Chicago Fire Department dispatcher for transmittal to on-scene fire department members.
IV.Field Supervisor Procedures
  • A.A field supervisor will respond to all fire-related incidents where:
    • 1.a Department member makes entry into a building for the purpose of rescuing endangered individuals;
    • 2.there are displaced persons;
    • 3.there is a suspicion the incident is of a criminal nature; or
    • 4.there are injuries.
  • B.If Department members have entered the building, the field supervisor will ensure that:
    • 1.fire department personnel are aware of the number and last known location of assisting members;
    • 2.all Department members who enter the building are accounted for; and
    • 3.fire lanes and an adequate perimeter have been established for the efficient movement of emergency vehicles.
  • C.In all cases, field supervisors will ensure that:
    • 1.fire lanes and an adequate perimeter have been established for the efficient movement of emergency vehicles;
    • 2.police personnel and equipment are sufficient for on-scene needs; and
    • 3.displaced persons receive necessary services.

VIII.Fire Department Quarters Left Vacant
  • A.All police units are directed to pay special attention to fire stations left unattended to prevent thefts and acts of vandalism.
  • B.If fire stations will not be occupied for an extended period of time, OEMC will advise all police units to pay special attention to the fire stations in their areas.
  • C.Field supervisors will periodically visit Chicago Fire Department facilities within their area of responsibility and discuss current issues within the district that may affect fire department personnel. Visits of this nature will be recorded on the Supervisor's Management Log form (CPD-11.455).
NOTE:
All police units operating in the area will be alert to Chicago Fire Department units en route to fires or returning from an alarm and provide close scrutiny and protection against any attack directed toward them.
