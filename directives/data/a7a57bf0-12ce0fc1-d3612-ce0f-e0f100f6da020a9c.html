Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E03-01-07
Skin Condition Aggravated by Shaving
Issue Date:25 September 2001Effective Date:26 September 2001
Rescinds:G98-02-07A
Index Category:Medical, Health, and Wellness
I.Purpose
This directive outlines procedures when a sworn member, probationary police officer, or uniformed civilian member wishes to obtain an exemption from the clean-shaven policy due to a skin condition aggravated by shaving.
II.Policy
  • A.The Department maintains a “clean-shaven” policy as defined in the directive entitled “Personal Appearance, Uniform/Citizen’s Dress and Equipment Specifications.” However, the Department recognizes that a certified medical condition may prevent some Department members from complying with the established policy.
  • B.Those members who are currently exempt from the Department’s “clean-shaven” policy will continue to be exempt until the expiration of their current Medical Excuse form (CPD-62.435).
III.Procedures
A Department member having a skin condition aggravated by shaving may apply for an exemption to the clean-shaven policy.
  • A.To obtain an exemption the affected member will:
    • 1.call the Medical Services Section and request an exemption from the clean-shaven policy.
    • 2.comply with the instructions received from the Medical Services Section.
    • 3.continue to comply with the Department’s clean-shaven policy unless an exemption has been certified by the Medical Services Section.
    • 4.submit to an examination by a Board-Certified dermatologist.
      NOTE:
      Compensatory time will not be granted for the purposes of a medical examination intended to determine if a medical exemption should be granted.
    • 5.receive a copy of the Medical Excuse form if the Medical Services Section certifies the exemption from the clean-shaven policy. If applicable, an expiration date will be noted on the Medical Excuse form.
  • B.Upon certification, the member will:
    • 1.maintain a full beard not to exceed 1/4 inch in length or as specifically authorized when on-duty.
      NOTE:
      If the skin condition requires an extension of the maximum length of 1/4 inch, the Medical Services Section will consider the requirement on a case-by-case basis.
    • 2.under no circumstances wear a goatee or other customized beard creations while on-duty.
    • 3.ensure that a copy of the certified Medical Excuse form is carried on their person when on-duty and is available to any supervisor upon request.
      NOTE:
      For the purposes of this directive, the term on-duty applies to any official function, including court appearances, regardless of attire.
  • C.The Medical Services Section will:
    • 1.forward written instructions to a member requesting an exemption.
    • 2.upon certification, ensure that the following are recorded on the member’s copy of the Medical Excuse form:
      • a.prescribed maximum length of the facial hair and
      • b.duration of the medical excuse.
    • 3.ensure that the original Medical Excuse form is placed in the member’s medical file.
    • 4.ensure that the member receives a copy of the Medical Excuse form.
    • 5.forward a copy of the Medical Excuse form to the:
      • a.commanding officer of the member’s unit of assignment.
      • b.commanding officer of the unit of detail if the member is detailed to another unit.
  • D.Unit commanding officers will ensure that:
    • 1.a copy of the Medical Excuse form is placed in the member’s unit personnel file.
    • 2.the member’s watch commander, if applicable, receives a copy of the Medical Excuse form.
    • 3.whenever a member is detailed to another unit, a copy of the Medical Excuse form is forwarded to the commanding officer of the unit of detail.
