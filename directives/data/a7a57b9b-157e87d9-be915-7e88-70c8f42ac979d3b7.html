Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D16-06
Annual Watch and Furlough Selections, Vacation Schedules, and 2017 Operations Calendar
Issue Date:21 October 2016Effective Date:21 October 2016
Rescinds:31 October 14 Version of D14-08
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members that the week of 16 July 2017 through 22 July 2017 is an Adjustment week for operational purposes.
  • B.presents options available to Department members during adjustment week concerning the use of compensatory time, baby furlough days, personal days or vacation days.
  • C.continues the selection of furlough by watch for members below the rank of sergeant represented by FOP Lodge 7.
  • D.informs Department members:
    • 1.of the 2017 watch selection process.
    • 2.of the 2017 furlough selection process.
  • E.continues the annual bidding process for watch assignments.
  • F.continues procedures for bidding as established in the collective bargaining agreements.
    • 1.FOP: 80 percent bid and 20 percent management rights.
    • 2.PBPA, Sergeants: 5 bid positions per each of the 3 primary watches in district law enforcement.
    • 3.PBPA, Lieutenants: 1 bid position per each of the 3 primary watches in 22 police districts.
  • G.establishes the Chicago Police - 2017 Operations Calendar (CPD-11.143).
  • H.establishes dates for the annual furlough periods.
  • I.continues the practice of utilizing reverse seniority to fill positions as necessary.
II.Adjustment Week 2017
  • A.The Chicago Police 2017 Operational Calendar (CPD-11.143) will include an adjustment week from 16 July 2017 to 22 July 2017.
  • B.Adjustment week falls between the 7th and 8th police periods. Department members may request to utilize compensatory time, baby furlough days, personal days, or vacation days during this week.
    • 1.Members granted a full 7th period or 7B split furlough segment may request to extend their furlough into the adjustment week following their assigned furlough segment.
    • 2.Members granted a full 8th period or 8A split furlough segment may request to extend their furlough into the adjustment week preceding their assigned furlough segment.
    • 3.All requests to extend furloughs will be granted based on operational needs, consistent with the appropriate collective bargaining agreements and existing procedures.
    • 4.Other payroll and timekeeping procedures will be followed as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Furloughs and Vacation."
  • C.Sworn members are reminded that:
    • 1.current year Vacation Days must be used by the end of the 13th period of the current year. Therefore:
      • a.2016 Vacation Days must be used by 31 Dec 2016.
      • b.2017 Vacation Days must be used by 6 January 2018.
    • 2.previous year baby furlough or personal days that were carried over for use in the current year must be used by the end of the current calendar year. Therefore:
      • a.members may not use 2016 baby furlough and personal days that were carried over from 2015 to extend on the end of a 2016 - 13B furlough segment. The 2016 - 13B furlough segment ends on 31 December 2016.
      • b.members may not use 2017 baby furlough and personal days that were carried over from 2016 beyond 31 December 2017.
III.General Information
  • A.In accordance with collective bargaining agreements, the watch selection process will precede the furlough selection process for all sworn ranks.
  • B.The granting of requests for steady watch selection and furlough selection will be the responsibility of the district commander or unit commanding officer, consistent with the operational needs of the district or unit and the applicable provisions of collective bargaining agreements.
  • C.If the member fails to participate in watch and/or furlough selection, the commanding officer will select a watch and/or furlough for that member based on operational needs.
  • D.Department members, districts, and units subject to the steady watch-selection bidding and furlough selection are identified in the existing collective bargaining agreements.
  • E.The annual steady watch and furlough selection process for civilian members with job title codes identified in the existing collective bargaining agreements will be conducted simultaneously and with the same procedures as described and set forth for the sworn members of the Department in accordance with this directive and the Department directives entitled “Furlough and Vacation” and “Steady Watch Selection and Assignment.”
  • F.Probationary police officers assigned or detailed to a unit with less than 12 months of service are not considered part of the unit complement for watch or furlough selection. Probationary police officers with more than 12 months of service are considered part of the unit complement for furlough selection only.
  • G.Department members detailed to a district or unit for twenty-eight days or more before the date of the annual steady-watch selection (on or before 21 October 2016) or furlough selection (on or before 11 November 2016) will select a watch or furlough within the unit of detail.
    NOTE:
    This does not apply to probationary police officers with 12 or more months of service who will participate in the furlough selection process within their unit of detail at the time of selection.
  • H.District/unit administrative personnel can obtain Department members' information for labels to attach to the appropriate forms and the seniority roster by using The Wire. Members will:
    • 1.Select Menu;
    • 2.Click on Applications (gray box);
    • 3. Select Jaspersoft Reporting from drop-down menu;
      • a.Log in with CLEAR credentials;
      • b.Select Personnel-Self Service folder in left pane;
      • c.Select Furlough Labels or Seniority Roster.
        NOTE:
        Unit commanding officers will ensure the appropriate unit personnel have the required access to the Jaspersoft Reporting application. Additional access may be requested via a BOS service request to the Information Services Division.
  • I.If a Department member voluntarily leaves a unit, then assignment of a watch will be at the discretion of the unit commanding officer.
IV.Watch Selection Requests Form and Seniority Number
  • A.Members submitting the Watch Selection Request form (CPD-11.629) will not fill in the "Seniority No." box.
  • B.The unit administrative staff assigned to process the watch selection requests will annotate the seniority number on the subject form with information from their unit's respective 12th police period seniority listing.
    NOTE:
    On 09 November 2016, the designated unit administrative staff will print unit seniority listing.
V.Steady Watch Selection
  • A.The annual steady watch selection will be conducted per the appropriate collective bargaining agreement and the Department directive entitled “Steady Watch Selection and Assignment.”
  • B.The following police officers are not subject to the bid process for steady watch assignments: police officers assigned to district tactical teams, foot patrol in districts 001 and 018, the immediate staff of each exempt member, and District Law Enforcement community relations officers (including senior services officers). The number of excluded staff positions, per the subject collective bargaining agreement, is limited to two staff members and the number of excluded community relations officers is limited to seven; five on the second watch and two on the third watch.
VI.Watch and Day Off Group Assignment for Sworn Non-Exempt Members
  • A.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.allocate the unit’s manpower between the watches in a manner that best addresses operational needs.
    • 2.ensure the day-off groups are balanced.
  • B.The watch selection process will begin on 10 November 2016 for all sworn members in all units of the Department; the watch selection requests must be submitted on or before 17 November 2016.
    NOTE:
    All watch selection requests will be submitted to unit commanding officers on the Watch Selection Request form (CPD-11.629).
  • C.The watch selections will be conducted on 18 November 2016 by the unit commanding officers; on 23 November 2016 unit commanding officers will post the watch selections.
  • D.Unit commanding officers will assign day off groups for members who have changed watches and will balance the remaining day off groups, which will be effective first police period of 2017. On 23 November 2016, members' 2017 day of groups will also be posted.
    NOTE:
    Consistent with the Department directive entitled “Steady Watch Selection and Assignment,” if day-off group imbalances occur during the annual steady watch selection procedure, the relevant sections of the appropriate collective bargaining agreement will apply.
VII.Furloughs
  • A.Between 28 November and 08 December 2016 the annual furlough selections for all members will be submitted in accordance with established collective bargaining agreements and Department directive entitled "Furlough Selection and Scheduling for Sworn Members."
  • B.The furlough selection process will be conducted on 09 December 2016 in the units.
  • C.Furlough selection by watch will encompass 1st, 2nd (including Community Relations Office and district commander's administrative staff), and 3rd watch.
  • D.Sworn members may submit requests for a full-furlough segment or split the furlough into two equal segments.
    • 1.A full-furlough segment will commence on the first day of a police period.
    • 2.Split-furlough segments will commence on either the 1st day or the 15th day of a police period.
  • E.On or before 15 December 2016, unit commanding officers will post the 2017 furlough selections.
VIII.Furlough Selections - Police Officers
  • A.Assignment of a full-furlough segment or both split segments for police officers will be determined in a one-step process by seniority on the watch within the unit of assignment.
    NOTE:
    District tactical officers and foot officers assigned to Unit 001 and Unit 018 are deemed a separate watch for furlough selection purposes.
  • B.Police Officers who voluntarily leave a watch or unit after the furlough selection process is completed may have their furlough segment(s) changed at the discretion of the unit commanding officer, if necessary for operational purposes.
IX.Furlough Selections - Sergeants
  • A.In units that have two or more sergeants assigned to each watch, sergeants will select furloughs by seniority on the watch within the unit of assignment. In units that have less than two sergeants assigned to each watch, sergeants will select furloughs by seniority within the unit of assignment.
    NOTE:
    District tactical sergeants, and foot team sergeants assigned to Unit 001 and Unit 018, are deemed a separate watch for furlough selection purposes.
  • B.Furlough assignments for sergeants will be determined in a two-step process, using seniority as a basis for the first pick. Furlough Selection/Absence Summary (CPD-11.609) will be ranked in seniority order.
    • 1.During the first step of the process, each sergeant will be assigned a full or single split segment based upon the individual’s ranked preference and segment availability.
    • 2.When every sergeant in the process has been assigned one segment, the second step will commence involving those sergeants assigned a split segment, in seniority order. The second split segment will be assigned based upon the individual’s ranked preference and segment availability.
  • C.Sergeants who voluntarily leave a watch or unit after the furlough selection process is completed will have their furlough segment(s) changed if there is an existing sergeant on the sergeant’s new watch or unit who already has the same furlough segment(s), resulting in a surplus of sergeants on furlough for that watch or unit. In that event, the incoming sergeant will have the opportunity to select from the available furlough segment(s), subject to the unit’s manpower needs.
  • D.Sergeants who have been authorized a furlough extension that includes a holiday will be given first priority for being off on that holiday.
X.Furlough Selections - Captains and Lieutenants
Captains and lieutenants will select furloughs by rank, by seniority, within the unit of assignment using the same two-step process as the sergeants outlined in Item VIII-B.
XI.Vacation Selections - Civilians
  • A.Unit commanding officers will allocate civilian resources between the watches and balance day-off groups in a manner which best address the operational needs of the unit.
  • B.Vacations will be selected in accordance with the provisions of the Department directive entitled “Furlough and Vacation.” However, for civilian members covered by a bargaining-unit agreement, vacation selection and the number of vacation days earned will be determined in accordance with the member’s bargaining-unit agreement.
XII.Furloughs Granted - All Members
A list of furlough selections granted will be posted in each unit. Exempt unit commanding officers will forward a report listing the names of all members and their furlough/vacation selections to their bureau chief and entered into the Watch/Personnel application no later than 16 December 2016. Unless advised to the contrary, this report will be considered approved.

XIV.Chicago Police - 2017 Operations Calendar
  • A.Copies of the Chicago Police - 2017 Operations Calendar (CPD-11.143) will be distributed concurrently with this directive.
    • 1.Department members will receive one copy.
    • 2.Unit secretaries will retain enough calendars to ensure absent members receive a copy of the calendar.
      NOTE:
      The Chicago Police 2017 Operational Calendar (CPD-11.143) is also available via the Department Directive System.
  • B.The calendar is designed to show the following:
    • 1.Calendar months,
    • 2.Police periods,
    • 3.Adjustment week, from 16 July 2017 to 22 July 2017,
    • 4.Rotating work schedule day-off groups,
    • 5.Traffic-court key-letter groups, and
    • 6.Non-traffic misdemeanor and municipal ordinance court keys.
      NOTE:
      The key box on the calendar indicates how to interpret Items XIV-B-4 through 6 of this directive.
  • C.The calendar months are designated with dark-lined borders.
  • D.The right-hand side designates split police periods which are differentiated from the calendar months by shaded overlays.
XV.Compensatory Time Furloughs 2017
  • A.Compensatory time furloughs are intended for those personnel who have selected a full-furlough period.
  • B.2017 vacation days cannot be used for any furlough or compensatory time furlough that extends beyond the conclusion of the 13th police period, 06 January 2018.
  • C.Those sworn members who have selected a full-furlough period are entitled to a compensatory time furlough. These compensatory time furloughs will be scheduled 6 police periods following a full furlough if the full furlough was taken during the police periods 1 through 7, or 6 periods prior to a full furlough to be taken during the police periods 8 through 13.
