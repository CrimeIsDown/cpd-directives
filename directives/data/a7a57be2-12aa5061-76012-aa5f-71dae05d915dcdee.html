I.Purpose
This directive continues Operations Command as a component of the Deployment Operations Center and outlines its functions.
II.Operations Command
Operations Command is the command post of the Department. It is located in the Deployment Operations Center (DOC). Operations Command will:
  • A.be staffed at all times.
  • B.take appropriate action on communications received from within and outside the Department.
  • C.receive notifications of:
    • 1.serious injuries incurred by police officers.
    • 2.implementation of any "Immediate Emergency" or "Special Response" plans.
    • 3.shots fired at or by police personnel.
    • 4.requests for use of Department Special Weapons/Equipment.
    • 5.bombings and arsons; including attempts.
    • 6.hazardous materials incidents.
    • 7.hate crimes/human rights incidents and demonstrations.
    • 8.major offenses reported to the police.
    • 9.reports of missing persons of any age where unusual circumstances exist or are potentially newsworthy.
    • 10.information concerning strikes, labor-management incidents or union controversies or the possibility thereof.
    • 11.any incident concerning a Diplomatic Officer, Consular Officer, member of the family of a Diplomatic Officer or a Consular Officer, Honorary Consul and/or their employees.
    • 12.major power outages.
    • 13.prisoner escapes.
    • 14.any extraordinary or unusual occurrence that takes place within a lockup facility (as defined in the Department directive entitled "Required Lockup Standards and Reporting").
    • 15.arrests of City of Chicago employees.
    • 16.any significant or newsworthy incident occurring within the city.
  • D.maintain a log of significant events on a 24-hour a day basis.
  • E.immediately notify the Independent Police Review Authority in the event any person in the custody of the Chicago Police Department:
    • 1.commits suicide.
    • 2.attempts to commit suicide.
    • 3.is either seriously injured, or dies as a result of actions taken by a Department member(s). Situations in which a community member dies en route to a medical facility while being transported in a Department vehicle as a result of an auto accident, inhalator call, confinement case or other assignment in which the sole function of the Department is to provide transportation during an emergency, do not constitute grounds for initiating this notification.
  • F.provide services which include, but are not limited to:
    • 1.receiving requests for emergency Facilities Management services, pertaining to outlying facilities, during other than normal business hours.
    • 2.receiving the required notification from Department members:
      • a.on the medical roll during the hours the Medical Services Section is closed.
      • b.arriving and departing at a court appearance (after 1600 hours) in a court where no court sergeant or court officer is assigned or there is no court assembly room.
    • 3.notifying the Special Activities Section as well as the Department Chaplains when a situation indicates their services may be needed.
    • 4.informing other governmental agencies (i.e., FBI, Secret Service, Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms) of certain incidents.
    • 5.processing Information Reports (CPD-11.461).
    • 6.providing personnel information on an emergency basis when the Human Resources Division is not operational.
    • 7.providing replacement equipment, e.g., handguns and ammunition, stars and shields, etc., when this equipment cannot be obtained through regular channels during other than normal operating hours.
    • 8.adjusting personnel assignments to fill existing needs during other than normal business hours, with the approval of the appropriate area deputy chief, Bureau of Patrol or their designee.
    • 9.performing such additional services as are specified in Department directives.
  • G.notify the Safety Division of the Illinois Department of Labor (IDOL) if:
    • 1.an injury on-duty is fatal to one or more Department members.
    • 2.an injury on-duty results in three or more members being hospitalized.
    • 3.an on-duty-Department member dies of a heart attack.
    NOTE:
    Notifications to IDOL will be made by telephone twenty-four hours per day, seven days a week. If an IDOL representative is not available, the notification will be left on the IDOL voice-mail system. The notification will relate the circumstances of the incident, number of fatalities, number of members hospitalized, and the extent of the injuries.
III.Authority and Responsibilities
  • A.The appropriate area deputy chief, Bureau of Patrol or their designee will:
    • 1.ensure that a complete and thorough investigation is conducted whenever notification is received that a weapon was discharged by, or shots were fired at a Department member, and submit a report of the investigation to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations. Routine animal destruction assignments involving no extenuating circumstances, need not be the subject of an additional investigatory report to the Assistant Superintendent, Operations.
    • 2.assist all members of the Department on matters of procedure by providing advice and interpreting directives and policies, when required, consistent with the Chicago Alternative Policing Strategy.
    • 3.when the situation dictates, proceed to the scene of incidents which, in their judgment, require their command presence to ensure that the resources and operations of the Department are directed, controlled, coordinated and that there is unity of command.
    • 4.ensure that the necessary steps are taken to relieve a Department member of their police powers when, and if, the nature and gravity of a complaint justifies this course of action.
  • B.The Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol will evaluate all requests by other law enforcement agencies for assistance and implement those that are consistent with the policy of the Department.
