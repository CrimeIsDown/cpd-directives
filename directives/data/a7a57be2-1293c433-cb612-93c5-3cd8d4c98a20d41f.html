Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-03-03
Building Partnerships with the Community
Issue Date:08 January 2009Effective Date:08 January 2009
Rescinds:G09-01-04
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines two primary mechanisms for building partnerships with the community: Beat Community Meetings and District Advisory Committees.
  • B.identifies the responsibilities of Department personnel regarding Beat Community Meetings and the District Advisory Committees.
II.Beat Community Meetings
  • A.Beat Community Meetings provide an opportunity for members of the community and Department personnel to:
    • 1.exchange information and provide official Department statistics, crime prevention materials, and beat activity information to attendees;
    • 2.identify, discuss, and address emerging and chronic crime and disorder problems;
    • 3.utilize the 5 step problem-solving model to develop strategies to combat identified crime and disorder problems;
    • 4.discuss and address previous and newly identified community concerns; and
    • 5.provide feedback and follow-up information on previous meeting’s discussions, identified problems, and implemented strategies.
  • B.Meeting Attendance
    • 1.The following Department members are required to be in attendance at all Beat Community Meetings:
      • a.Beat team sergeant.
      • b.On-duty 3rd watch beat team officers.
      • c.On-duty 3rd watch community policing officer.
      • d.Designated on-duty district tactical or gang-tactical officer.
      • e.CAPS Implementation Office community organizer or designee from that office.
      • f.On-duty District watch commander.
        NOTE:
        If there is a field or tactical lieutenant working the watch on the evening of the meeting, the watch commander may delegate the responsibility to attend the Beat Community Meeting to a lieutenant.
    • 2.Area and Central Control Group deputy chiefs will attend, at minimum, one Beat Community Meeting per week within their area of assignment.
    • 3.District commanders will attend, at minimum, one Beat Community Meeting per week within their district of assignment.
    • 4.The beat facilitator will attend Beat Community Meetings and assist in facilitating the meeting.
  • C.Meeting Format
    • 1.Beat Community Meetings will be held for each beat, at minimum, once every other month. If appropriate, meetings may be held more frequently at the discretion of the district commander.
    • 2.Meetings will be limited to one hour in length.
    • 3.Meetings will be discussion-based and have an emphasis on problem-solving and information sharing by the Department, the community, and City service agencies.
    • 4.Meetings will follow a set agenda developed by the beat team sergeant and, at minimum, include the following:
      • a.welcome, introductions, and announcements.
      • b.feedback and follow-up on progress made on previously identified problems, including arrest data and CLEARmap information.
      • c.identification and analysis of new problems.
      • d.discussion of strategies and coordination of responsibilities.
      • e.recap including the information on the next Beat Community Meeting.
  • D.Distribution Materials and Resource Information
    The beat team sergeant will be responsible to develop and ensure the following resource materials are distributed at Beat Community Meetings:
    • 1.meeting agenda.
    • 2.appropriate arrest data and CLEARmap queries to summarize activities that have taken place since the last Beat Community Meeting.
      NOTE:
      Members will refer to the Department directives entitled "News Media Guidelines," “Department Reports, Publications, Survey Responses, and Official Statistics,” and “Freedom of Information” for direction relative to the dissemination of sensitive information.
    • 3.appropriate Community Alerts, crime pattern, and crime prevention information.
    • 4.notifications of Department-sponsored community events, activities, and functions.
  • E.District commanders will:
    • 1.oversee the overall scheduling, management, and maintenance of the Beat Community Meetings.
    • 2.review and approve the required Beat Community Meeting Logs.
    • 3.ensure that beat team sergeants receive the support needed from district personnel.
    • 4.facilitate transfer of information and response to requests for resources between the district and outside units.
    • 5.review the schedule of Beat Community Meetings to ensure that:
      • a.a Beat Community Meeting is regularly scheduled and held for each beat within their district of command, at minimum, once every other month. If appropriate, meetings may be held more frequently.
      • b.meetings are scheduled in such a manner to minimize the impact on district operations.
      • c.the schedule of meetings for the upcoming month and any change in the regular schedule is disseminated accordingly.
      • d.information relative to Beat Community Meetings is made available to the public.
  • F.Watch commanders will:
    • 1.ensure proper beat coverage whenever a beat team is attending a meeting.
    • 2.ensure proper supervisory sector coverage whenever a sector sergeant is attending a meeting.
    • 3.ensure that citizens are provided with information regarding upcoming Beat Community Meetings and the names of officers assigned to a beat.
    • 4.review, approve, and forward the Beat Community Meeting reports, as appropriate.
    • 5.ensure the field or tacitcal lieutenant attends the Beat Community Meeting in the absence of the watch commander on the 3rd watch.
    • 6.upon being notified by the assigned beat team officers’ inability to attend a scheduled Beat Community Meeeting, assign a replacement officer from the rapid response car assigned to that sector to attend the meeting.
    • 7.allow on-duty beat team officers to attend community-sponsored meetings on their beat as often as possible, when it is determined that their presence is necessary and it does not interfere with operational needs.
  • G.The tactical lieutenant will:
    • 1.identify and provide personnel to attend each Beat Community Meeting unless operational requirements dictate otherwise.
    • 2.attend beat meetings as directed by the watch commander or district commander.
  • H.Beat team sergeants will:
    • 1.be responsible for the overall scheduling, management, and maintenance of all Beat Community Meetings in the district to which the beat team sergeant is assigned.
    • 2.ensure that the meeting is open to the public and held at a public location in compliance with the Department directive entitled "Human Rights and Human Resources." The meeting location should, whenever possible, be on the beat.
      NOTE:
      Private homes or those establishments whose primary source of income is derived from the sale or consumption of alcohol will not be used as locations for meetings.
    • 3.ensure that a schedule of meetings for the upcoming month is established, and any change in the regular schedule is provided to the district commander, the CAPS Implementation Office, and the district CLEARpath webpage events calendar.
      NOTE:
      At minimum, this schedule will include a listing of the locations, dates, and times of the meetings for each respective beat.
    • 4.ensure that at the required members and appropriate additional district personnel are notified to attend the appropriate Beat Community Meeting.
    • 5.coordinate requests for guest speakers, outside unit participation, and various resources needed for the meeting.
    • 6.develop and ensure the materials and resource information are distributed at the Beat Community Meeting.
    • 7.ensure that the meeting follows the agenda and that it proceeds in an orderly manner.
    • 8.designate a beat team officer to complete the Beat Community Meeting Log.
    • 9.ensure that notified subordinate Department members not present at the meeting are informed of issues raised at the meeting and initiate an investigation of any unexcused absence according to the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Complaint and Disciplinary Procedures.”
      NOTE:
      When appropriate, supervisors will utilize the less serious transgression No. 19, Inattention to Duty, for unexcused absences from Beat Community Meetings.
    • 10.make contact with each beat team facilitator at least twice a month.
    • 11.evaluate requests from community groups seeking the presence of Department members at community-sponsored meetings to determine if their attendance at the meeting is necessary.
  • I.Sector and rapid response sergeants will:
    • 1.as directed by the watch commander, attend meetings to support the problem-solving process.
    • 2.ensure proper beat coverage when beat team officers are out of service at a meeting.
    • 3.as assigned, provide temporary supervisory coverage for supervisors who are out of service at a Beat Community Meeting.
    • 4.be prepared to assume the duties of the beat team sergeant if so assigned by the watch commander. When providing coverage for the beat team sergeant at a Beat Community Meeting, the assigned supervisor will review, approve, and forward the Beat Community Meeting reports, as appropriate.
  • J.Beat team officers will:
    • 1.attend Beat Community Meetings when notified to attend. If a notified beat team officer is unable to attend a Beat Community Meeting, the officer will notify their watch commander prior to the meeting.
    • 2.publicize upcoming Beat Community Meetings.
    • 3.identify guest speakers and other resources needed for an upcoming meeting and notify the beat team sergeant.
    • 4.prepare for the meeting by reviewing relevant documents.
    • 5.attend meetings during their tour of duty or as designated representatives from their watch, in the prescribed seasonal field uniform.
    • 6.if on-duty, notify the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) when attending the meeting and turn off their radios while the meeting is being conducted.
    • 7.when designated, complete the Beat Community Meeting Log and submit it to the beat team sergeant for approval.
    • 8.if on-duty, return to service promptly following the meeting by informing their supervisor and the OEMC of their availability.
  • K.Rapid response, tactical, and gang tactical officers will:
    • 1.provide relevant information to beat team officers for the meeting.
    • 2.attend Beat Community Meetings as required or when notified by the beat team sergeant to assist in the problem-solving process.
    • 3.as assigned, provide temporary coverage for beat units attending a meeting.
  • L.The Beat Community Meeting liaison officer assigned to the community policing office will:
    • 1.assist the beat team sergeant in scheduling, publicizing, and preparing materials for beat community meetings.
    • 2.attend Beat Community Meetings as notified by the beat team sergeant.
III.District Advisory Committee (DAC)
  • A.Required DAC Subcommittees include:
    • 1.Court Advocacy - to identify and track court cases that are of concern to the community.
    • 2.Domestic Violence - to address needs and concerns of the community related to the preventing, identifying, and addressing Domestic Violence.
    • 3.Faith-Based - to address the needs and concerns of the faith-based community.
    • 4.Senior Citizen - to address the needs and concerns of senior citizens. This subcommittee will be staffed by the senior services officer in the community policing office.
    • 5.Youth - to address the needs and concerns of the community’s youth population including managing youth-orientated programs (i.e., Peer Jury).
  • B.District commanders will:
    • 1.meet with the DAC and work to identify issues related to crime and disorder, establish priorities, and develop district-level strategies.
    • 2.ensure the DAC follows the procedures outlined in the established DAC By-Laws.
    • 3.ensure each DAC establishes subcommittees consistent with the needs and concerns of the community relative to crime and disorder problems in accordance with the district’s operational needs. At minimum, each district will be required to have the subcommittees outlined in Item III-A.
      NOTE:
      Other subcommittees district commanders may consider are Business, Lesbian-Gay-Bisexual-Transgender (LGBT), Hospitality, Community Emergency Response Teams.
    • 4.inform Department personnel, serving as elected DAC members in districts where they reside or own a business, that:
      • a.they are not permitted to use their positions as Department members to gain access to or disclose official reports or files, or to influence the conduct of the DAC.
      • b.participation in certain DAC activities which affect their individual conditions of employment may be prohibited by the provisions of the Governmental Ethics Ordinance, Chapter 2-156, Section 010, of the Municipal Code of Chicago.
    • 5.ensure civilians volunteering for service as a District Advisory Committee member , sub-committee member, or beat facilitator or co-facilitator complete the Application for: District Advisory Committee and Subcommittee Membership/Officer Positions, Beat Facilitator/Co-Facilitator (CPD-21.707).
    • 6.complete the District Commander Interview Form (CPD-21.708) when interviewing civilian volunteers for the above described service positions.
    • 7.ensure the applications and interview forms are reviewed, approved, and retained according to establish procedures.
  • C.Beat team officers will, through the community policing office, inform the Court Advocacy Subcommittee of any cases which are of concern to the community and where the subcommittee’s attendance in court might be beneficial to the successful outcome of the case.
  • D.Department members assigned to the community policing office will:
    • 1.provide assistance to the DAC and its subcommittees.
    • 2.maintain records as directed by the district commander.
