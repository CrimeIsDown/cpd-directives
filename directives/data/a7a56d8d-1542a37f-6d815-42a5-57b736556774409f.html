Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-03
Community Partnerships and Engagement Strategies
Issue Date:09 September 2019Effective Date:09 September 2019
Rescinds:08 December 2017 Version
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
  • A.This directive:
    • 1.contains addenda which describe various Department community partnerships and engagement strategies supporting the implementation of the Department's community relations strategy.
    • 2.introduces the Engaging Youth and Emerging Adults Evaluation Report (CPD-21.135) satisfying CALEA Law Enforcement Standard Chapter 44.
    • 3.outlines the documented evaluation of the Department's crime prevention programs, including the Department-wide community partnerships and engagement strategies, satisfying CALEA Law Enforcement Standard Chapter 45.
  • B.Department members will refer to the General Order titled "Community Relations Strategy" for the policy governing the Department's community relations strategy.
II.Program Evaluations
  • A.As identified in the General Order titled "Community Relations Strategy," the Department has established a variety of community partnerships and engagement strategies which are designed to encourage positive community interactions and relationships. The Department will work with the community to identify their concerns, develop effective crime prevention activities, and coordinate the collaborative response.
  • B.The Office of Community Policing oversees the juvenile prevention programs. Annually the Deputy Chief or his/her desginee will complete the Engaging Youth and Emerging Adults Evaluation Report (CPD-21.135) and submit it to the Superintendent for review and approval.
  • C.The Deputy Chief, Office of Community Policing, will ensure an analysis is conducted of the implemented crime prevention programs, including the Department-wide community partnerships and engagement strategies.
    • 1.The analysis will be conducted by program or strategy and will include identified emerging and chronic crime issues and neighborhood disorder, the geographic areas impacted by the programs, and the community perceptions, or misperceptions, of crime and the crime prevention programs.
    • 2.The analysis will document the evaluation of crime prevention programs and strategies and will be conducted, at least once every two years.
