Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D13-08
Processing Curfew Violators - Pilot Program
Issue Date:13 June 2013Effective Date:15 June 2013
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive
  • A.informs Department members of the implementation of a pilot program for processing curfew violators.
  • B.outlines procedures for:
    • 1.processing curfew violators.
    • 2.issuing an Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV) citation to a parent, legal guardian, or other adult having the legal care of custody of a minor who violates any provision of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance.
  • C.introduces the
    • 1.Curfew Violation Report {(CPD-24.111) Rev. 6/13}.
    • 2.Proof of Service by Mail (CPD-24.146) form.
II.Scope
This program is operational in districts 010, 011, 015, and 025 and is effective beginning 15 June 2013.
III.Procedures for Processing Curfew Violators
  • A.Prior to enforcing the curfew ordinance, Department members must have a reasonable belief, under the circumstances, that a violation of the curfew ordinance has occurred and that none of the nine defenses (MCC 8-16-020 (b)) applies .
    It is a defense to prosecution under subsection (b) that the minor was:
    • 1.Accompanied by the minor’s parent or guardian;
    • 2.On an errand at the direction of the minor’s parent or guardian, without any detour or stop;
    • 3.In a motor vehicle involved in interstate travel;
    • 4.Engaged in employment activity, or going to or returning home from employment activity, without any detour or stop;
    • 5.Involved in an emergency;
    • 6.On the sidewalk abutting the minor’s residence or abutting the residence of a next-door neighbor if the neighbor did not complain to the police department about the minor’s presence;
    • 7.Attending an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civil (sic) organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor, or going to or returning home from, without any detour or stop, an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor;
    • 8.Exercising First Amendment rights protected by the United States Constitution, such as the free exercise of religion, freedom of speech, and the right to assembly; or
    • 9.Married or had been married or is an emancipated minor under the Emancipation of Mature Minors Act, as amended.
  • B.A member who takes a curfew violator into custody will:
    • 1.complete the Curfew Violation Report and follow procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Curfew Violators."
    • 2.verify if the minor has previous curfew violations by:
      • a.entering his or her PC number and password into the CLEAR system via the PDT.
      • b.selecting "Juvenile Curfew" in the Record Management box. Click on "Query Juvenile Curfew" and then enter the minor's information in the requested fields.
    • 3.if the violator has three or more violations (including the current violation) within twelve months from the current date, issue an ANOV citation to the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of a minor following the procedures outlined in Item IV of this directive.
      NOTE:
      The ANOV will only be served upon the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of a minor. However, the curfew violator may still be released to an adult accepting responsibility for custody of the curfew violator consistent with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Curfew Violators."
IV.Procedures for issuing an Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV) Citation
  • A.If it appears that the juvenile’s parent, legal guardian, or other adult having the legal care or custody the minor knowingly permitted the juvenile to violate curfew three or more times (including the current violation) within twelve months from the current date, that adult will be charged with a violation of the Chicago Curfew Ordinance.
  • B.An ANOV will be issued to the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care of custody of a minor for a violation of MCC 8-16-020(b)(2) in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Municipal Administrative Hearings.” The member completing an ANOV citation will:
    • 1.use the information of the parent or legal guardian from the Curfew Violation Report to complete the section for the respondent.
    • 2.insert any other information about the curfew violator’s parent or legal guardian ascertained from the parent or Curfew Violation Report.
    • 3.insert MCC 8-16-020(b)(2) in the "OFFENSE" box.
    • 4.insert the phrase, “the above respondent, has legal care or custody of, (curfew violator’s name), a minor, who violated MCC 8-16-020(b)(1) on (date and time of curfew), at (location of curfew)" in the section entitled “Step 2” of the ANOV citation. Also insert the phrase “See attached Curfew Violation Report” in the same section.
    • 5.be aware of the Curfew Violation Exceptions listed in the Department directive entitled "Exemptions and Restrictions for Issuing ANOV Citations" including that the respondent or person served with an ANOV citation written for curfew-related violations is not required to:
      • a.produce a valid picture identification card, or
      • b.sign the ANOV citation.
    • 6.provide the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of a minor with the "Respondent Copy" of the ANOV Citation and the "Parent / Guardian Copy" of the Curfew Violation Report.
      • a.If the curfew violator is not released to the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of the minor, members will follow the procedures outlined in Item IV-C for distributing and serving the ANOV citation.
        NOTE:
        This restriction also includes if the minor is a ward of the State of Illinois.
      • b.An ANOV citation will not be served to an adult accepting responsibility for custody of the curfew violator if that adult is not the the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of the minor.
    • 7.attach a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report to the "Department Administrative Hearing Copy" of the ANOV citation.
    • 8.complete the ANOV citation information on the Curfew Violation Report.
    • 9.submit the completed reports and citations to their station supervisor/designated unit supervisor.
  • C.If the member is unable to serve the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care or custody of a minor with the ANOV citation, Department members will:
    • 1.complete a Proof of Service by Mail (CPD-24.146) form.
    • 2.send the "Respondent Copy" of the ANOV citation, a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report, and a photocopy of the Proof of Service by Mail form to the parent and/or legal guardian by U.S. Mail via the Police Documents Section.
      NOTE:
      If the minor is a ward of The State of Illinois, the above documents will be mailed via U.S. Mail to: Representative for DCFS, 17 N. State Street, 7th Floor, Chicago, IL 60602.
    • 3.attach a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report and the original Proof of Service by Mail form to the "Department Administrative Hearing Copy" of the ANOV citation.
    • 4.complete the ANOV citation information on the Curfew Violation Report.
    • 5.submit the completed reports and citations to their station supervisor/designated unit supervisor.
  • D.The member's station supervisor/designated unit supervisor will:
    • 1.receive and review all submitted reports and citations for completion.
    • 2.ensure a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report is attached to the "Department of Administrative Hearing Copy" of the corresponding ANOV citation.
    • 3.if the member is unable to serve the parent, legal guardian, or adult having the legal care of custody of a minor with the ANOV citation, ensure:
      • a.the "Respondent Copy" of the ANOV, a photocopy of the Curfew Violation Report, and a photocopy of the Proof of Service by Mail form are sent to the parent and/or legal guardian by U.S Mail.
      • b.the original Proof of Service by Mail form is attached to the "Department of Administrative Hearing Copy" of the corresponding ANOV citation.
    • 4.approve and sign the properly completed reports and citations.
      NOTE:
      If an ANOV citation was issued by the member taking the curfew violator into custody, the station supervisor will ensure the ANOV Information is included on the Curfew Violation Report.
    • 5.ensure the reports and citations are processed according to existing procedures.
