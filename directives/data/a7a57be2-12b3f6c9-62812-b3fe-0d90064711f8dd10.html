Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S08-03
Traffic Review Board
Issue Date:24 April 2003Effective Date:01 June 2003
Rescinds:G03-02-03
Index Category:Professionalism
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the Traffic Review Board and delineates its responsibilities.
  • B.introduces the:
    • 1.Traffic Review Board Summary of Findings (CPD-44.249)
    • 2.Traffic Review Board Complaint Register Initiation (CPD-44.244) form.
II.The Traffic Review Board
  • A.Board Membership, Quorum, and Voting
    • 1.The Traffic Review Board will consist of the following members:
      • a.Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division;
      • b.A deputy chief or other command staff member of the Bureau of Patrol;
      • c.Commander, Traffic Section;
      • d.Commanding Officer, Traffic Safety and Training Unit.
    • 2.On an annual basis, the Superintendent of Police will designate a deputy chief or other command staff member of the Bureau of Patrol to serve on the board.
    • 3.The designated deputy chief or other command staff member of the Bureau of Patrol will serve as chairperson of the board.
    • 4.A quorum of the board will consist of the three command staff members identified in Item II-A-1 of this directive or their surrogates formally designated by the Superintendent.
    • 5.The command staff members comprising the board will be equal voting members.
    • 6.Decisions of the board will be made by a majority vote of the board's command staff members and will be authenticated by the signature of the board chairperson.
    • 7.The Commanding Officer, Traffic Safety and Training Unit, will serve as the board secretary and a nonvoting member.
  • B.Responsibilities
    The Traffic Review Board will:
    • 1.meet on a monthly basis.
    • 2.review all:
      • a.motor vehicle pursuits as defined in the Department directive entitled "Emergency Use of Department Vehicles";
      • b.traffic crashes resulting from any emergency vehicle operation as defined in the Department directive entitled "Emergency Use of Department Vehicles";
      • c.traffic crashes involving Department vehicles occurring during nonemergency vehicle operation that result in any personal injury
      • d.other incidents as directed by the Superintendent.
    • 3.determine whether the actions of the involved Department member(s), in those cases subject to the board's review, were in compliance with Department policy and procedures.
    • 4.determine preventability in the case of a Department vehicle traffic crash for those crashes subject to the board's review.
    • 5.when appropriate:
      • a.certify, document, and report that the actions of the involved member(s) were within the policy and procedures of the Department.
      • b.direct the member's command staff officer to initiate specific summary punishment.
      • c.initiate a complaint register (CR) investigation, using the Traffic Review Board Complaint Register Initiation form, to be conducted by the Internal Affairs Division.
      • d.direct retraining through the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division or the member's command staff officer, as appropriate.
III.Additional Responsibilities
The Chief, Internal Affairs Division, will:
  • A.ensure all CR investigations initiated by the Traffic Review Board are conducted by investigators assigned to the Internal Affairs Division.
  • B.inform the Chairperson of the Traffic Review Board of the final disposition of all CR investigations initiated by the board.
