Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-26-14
Damage to Public Property
Issue Date:21 March 2013Effective Date:22 March 2013
Rescinds:
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive informs Department members of the enforcement procedures for Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC) Chapter 8-4-120: Damage to Public Property
II.Pertinent Ordinance
Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC) 8-4-120: Damage to Public Property states “No person shall cut, injure, mark, or deface any public building, sewer, water pipe, hydrant, or other city property, fixture or personal property, or any tree, grass, shrub, or walk in any public way or public park.”
III.Procedures
  • A.Department members will no longer use an Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violation (ANOV), Personal Service Citation, or Violation Notice Citation to enforce MCC 8-4-120: Damage to Public Property.
    NOTE:
    Department member may continue to use ANOV citations to enforce property specific ordinances such as MCC 11-12-080: Hydrants.
  • B.When responding to an incident which involves damage to City of Chicago property (e.g., public buildings, street signs, vehicles), Department members will:
    • 1.complete a Cost Recovery Incident Form (CPD-61.230)
    • 2.forward the completed form, along with any supporting documentation, to the station supervisor or designated unit supervisor following the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Potential Cost Recovery Incidents – City of Chicago."
  • C.Additionally, if the cause of the damage was:
    • 1.ACCIDENTAL (e.g., traffic crash, non-criminal, unintentional), Department members will complete the appropriate case / traffic crash report and take no enforcement action in respect to Damage to Public Property, MCC 8-4-120.
    • 2.CRIMINAL (e.g., graffiti, criminal damage to property, DUI traffic crash), Department members will complete the appropriate case / traffic crash report and, if an offender is arrested, enforce Damage to Public Property, MCC 8-4-120 using the Class "C" Misdemeanor/Ordinance Violation complaint, consistent with existing procedures.
