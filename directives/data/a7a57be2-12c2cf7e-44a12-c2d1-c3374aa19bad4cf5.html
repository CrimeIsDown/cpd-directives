  • A.Members
    • 1.Director, Finance Division, Chair
    • 2.Chief, Special Functions Division
    • 3.Chief, Bureau of Organized Crime
    • 4.Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division
    • 5.Commander, Bomb and Arson Division
    • 6.Commanding Officer, SWAT
    • 7.Commanding Officer, Equipment and Supply Section
    • 8.A member designated by the Chief, Bureau of Patrol
    • 9.A member designated by the Chief, Bureau of Detectives
    • 10.LESO Coordinator identified by the Uniform and Personal Equipment Committee
  • B.The LESO sub-committee will meet at least quarterly, or more often if so convened by the sub-committee chair, for the purpose of identifying equipment needs and review of the listing of items obtained in the previous quarter.
III.RESPONSIBILITIES
  • A.The Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee will identify Department members to function as LESO screeners
    LESO Screener
    X
    A Department member designated by the Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee to access, review, and place equipment orders in the federal government's Law Enforcement Support Office (LESO) Program.
    .
    NOTE:
    LESO screeners will be selected from units which are the most likely to be end-users of the items.
  • B.The Director, Finance Division, is responsible for overseeing the LESO Program. The Director will ensure that:
    • 1.designated Department members are trained to serve as LESO screeners.
    • 2.an annual shipping budget is set for the LESO Program, including funds for shipping, handling, and charges for the purchase of miscellaneous accessories for the acquired items.
    • 3.a LESO screener is designated to:
      • a.draft Department-level reports mandated by the federal LESO program and submit same to the Finance Division for approval.
      • b.prepare and submit a monthly, itemized shipping cost report to the Chair, Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy committee.
    • 4.all equipment requests that have been approved by the appropriate command staff member and all cost estimates are received.
    • 5.all approved LESO orders are completed.
    • 6.the original transmittal forms for requested items are received and maintained.
    • 7.an annual listing of the equipment obtained and the financial savings realized by the Department due to acquiring property via the LESO Program is generated and provided to the Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee.
  • C.Designated LESO Screeners will:
    • 1.maintain the security of their assigned LESO Program identifier.
      NOTE:
      Only designated LESO screeners are permitted to screen or request items on behalf of the Department.
    • 2.access the LESO web site using the Department-assigned LESO Program personal identifier.
      NOTE:
      LESO Screeners will identify themselves after logging into the LESO web site by entering their star number into the LESO system.
    • 3.review the LESO web site on a daily or regular basis to determine if any items that may be of benefit to the Department is available.
    • 4.reserve items as appropriate.
    • 5.within 72 hours of reserving any item, directly contact the Department command staff member who has authority on the reserved item and request approval to forward the request to the Director, Finance Division.
    • 6.forward approved requests, with a cost estimate, to the Director, Finance Division.
    • 7.maintain records of the status of LESO items which has been reserved.
    • 8.maintain records of perishable items

    • 10.submit a quarterly report of all received items and cost of shipping to the designated LESO Coordinator, who will submit a single comprehensive Mandatory Quarterly Reconciliation Report to the Finance Division.
    • 11.conduct these responsibilities as an ancillary duty of their normal assignment, rather than as a full-time assignment.
  • D.The command staff member, General Support Division will:
    • 1.determine which items will be inventoried within the Department inventory system.
    • 2.assign CPD property inventory control tags to designated items, including all nonperishable items

    • 4.inform the appropriate command staff member who has authority on the delivered item that, upon delivery, the original transmittal form is to be immediately forwarded to the Director, Finance Division.
  • E.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.consult with the Finance Division to coordinate with a LESO screener should they want to acquire specialized equipment or uniform items through the LESO program for their unit and advise the designated LESO screener with a description of the type of equipment or uniform item sought.
    • 2.in the event that the property is being field-tested, submit an evaluation report to the Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee monthly until the items are approved or disapproved for operational use.
    • 3.should the delivered property be of a personalized / perishable items category, i.e., items like vests, clothing, or boots, maintain unit level records to identify to whom the items were issued.
    • 4.inform both the Equipment and Supply Section and the unit’s designated LESO screener when the property is no longer needed or functional and submit the destruction documents as advised by the LESO screener.
  • F.The Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee will:
    • 1.establish and maintain a matrix which identifies which Departmental units will be serviced by which LESO screeners and inform the Finance Division of the selected persons’ names, units and contact information.
    • 2.ensure LESO screeners are provided with a listing of equipment needs for designated units which may be available through LESO.
    • 3.review the Finance Division-submitted reports for the purpose of determining what equipment has been acquired.
    • 4.monitor all pilot programs associated with LESO-acquired uniforms and equipment.
  • G.The Firearms Training Unit, Education and Training Division, will maintain all records of LESO weapons for the Department and submit the annual weapons reconciliation report to the Law Enforcement Support Office's Illinois State Coordinator.

GLOSSARY TERMS:
1.
LESO Program
-
The federal government's Law Enforcement Support Office (LESO) Program that provides military equipment and uniform items to local law enforcement agencies at no cost, other than shipping.
2.
LESO Screener
-
A Department member designated by the Uniform and Personal Equipment Policy Committee to access, review, and place equipment orders in the federal government's Law Enforcement Support Office (LESO) Program.
3.
Perishable Equipment
-
Any LESO (Law Enforcement Support Office) equipment that is determined by unit commanding officers to be individually issued and disposable, such as clothing items. Perishable equipment can be issued to individual members when the commanding officer determines that doing so will enhance the member's ability to perform the member's assignment.
4.
Nonperishable Items
-
Any item acquired through the LESO (Law Enforcement Support Office) program that is determined by the Unit commanding officer to have substantive value and does not fit into the perishable goods category. These items include, but are not limited to, night vision devices, weapon optics and fitness equipment.
