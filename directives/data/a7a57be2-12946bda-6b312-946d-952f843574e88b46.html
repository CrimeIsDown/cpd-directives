Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-03-06
Court Advocacy Subcommittee
Issue Date:24 August 1994Effective Date:24 August 1994
Rescinds:S94-13
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.Introduces the Court Advocacy Subcommittee.
  • B.sets forth policy regarding the Court Advocacy Subcommittee.
  • C.initiates the use of a computer program called the Production Customer Information Control System (PRODCICS).
  • D.outlines district personnel responsibilities relative to the Court Advocacy Subcommittee and PRODCICS.
II.Policy
The Department will provide support and staff resources to assist citizens in addressing issues of community concern. In keeping with this policy, each district will have a Court Advocacy Subcommittee as a part of the Chicago Alternative Police Strategy (CAPS) Advisory Committee.
III.General Information
  • A.The Court Advocacy Subcommittee Chairperson will be elected by members of the subcommittee. The goals of this subcommittee is to impact the criminal and civil justice systems by gaining meaningful access to the courts and by becoming active participants in the administration of justice in their community. Subcommittee members will attend court hearings, thereby making the courts and state’s attorneys aware that they, a community as a whole, are also victims of crimes. The increased community interest and awareness provided by the subcommittee will facilitate a working partnership between police officers on the beat and community members.
  • B.The subcommittee will also provide the mechanism for citizens and Department personnel to access various public records from the Clerk of the Circuit Court of Cook County and to develop a court tracking system. Subcommittee members will track the progress of various court cases and endeavor to make the courts more accountable to community interests. Court record information will be obtained by computer via PRODCICS at the district level.
IV.District Commander Responsibilities
The district commander will be responsible for the general oversight of the activities of the Court Advocacy Subcommittee.
V.Chicago Alternative Police Strategy (CAPS) Responsibilities
  • A.District CAPS personnel will provide staff resources and work closely with the subcommittee chairperson.
  • B.The CAPS sergeant will coordinate all training needs of district personnel.
VI.Subcommittee Chairperson Responsibilities
The subcommittee chairperson will::
  • A.ensure that subcommittee activities are coordinated through Court Advocacy Services.
  • B.coordinate all training needs of subcommittee members through Court Advocacy Services.
VII.District Desk Personnel Responsibilities
District desk personnel will direct all citizen’s requests for court record information to the district CAPS Office, Monday through Friday, during the 2nd and 3rd watches.
VIII.Clerk's Information System
  • A.The court record information provided by PRODCICS is a matter of public record..
  • B.District CAPS personnel will be trained in the use of the PRODCICS and provided with personal access codes.
  • C.Access to Department computers will be limited to Department personnel.
