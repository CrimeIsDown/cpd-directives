Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-03
Performance Ratings - Civilian Members
Issue Date:21 June 1999Effective Date:22 June 1999
Rescinds:S99-06
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.requires supervisory personnel to rate the performance of all Police Department civilian members, with the exception of those serving in a command staff rank, twice yearly.
  • B.requires unit commanding officers to ensure that civilian members under their command are notified that they will be evaluated during the two periods.
  • C.outlines procedures for the notification, preparation and submission of performance ratings.
II.Authority
  • A.By authority of the Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC 2-84-400), the Police Department is required to rate its civilian members, except crossing guards, twice a year. The two rating periods are: 1 January through 30 June and 1 July through 3l December.
  • B.Crossing guards will be rated for the 1 January through 30 June rating period only.
III.Specific Information
  • A.Civilian members in the Bureau of Administrative Services will be rated utilizing the Civilian Performance Evaluation System (CPES). All affected employees are to refer to the current Bureau of Administrative Services Special Order entitled "Civilian Performance Evaluation System (CPES)."
  • B.Non-supervisory civilian members are to be rated on the City of Chicago Performance Evaluation Employee Rating Form (PER-95). Supervisory civilian members are to be rated on the City of Chicago Performance Evaluation Supervisory Employee Rating Form (PER-96). Rating forms will be completed by the employee's immediate supervisor.
  • C.At the beginning of each rating period, the civilian member's immediate supervisor is required to discuss the evaluation system and appropriate rating form with the employee, and to indicate the performance traits, behavioral descriptions, rating categories and any applicable supervisory accountabilities which relate to the employee's job. The employee will sign and date the rating form in the space provided to indicate that this discussion has taken place. This same form will be completed by the employee's immediate supervisor at the end of the rating period.
  • D.The last supervisor who has supervised the employee for at least 30 days is responsible for rating non-supervisory civilian members who have worked under more than one supervisor during the rating period. However, any such rater should consult with previous supervisors, if possible, before completing the rating form. The employee's performance over the entire rating period should be considered when the rating form is completed.
  • E.Non-supervisory civilian members who are transferred to a new unit, or who are newly hired must be given the appropriate rating form at the beginning of the new assignment to sign and date for the later evaluation. Any rating form signed earlier will be attached to the new rating form.
  • F.If an employee has worked as a supervisor for 90 days or more during a rating period, that employee will be rated on the Supervisory Employee Rating Form. The rating should be done by the person who supervised the employee in that capacity, after consulting with any other members who supervised the employee during the rating period.
  • G.At the conclusion of the rating period, each civilian employee will be rated by his immediate supervisor. The supervisor will record the evaluation on the form previously signed by the employee.
  • H.A Department directive will be issued and will provide information specific to the rating period, including the due date for the submission of forms. The unit commanding officer will ensure that all required procedures have been completed and that all rating forms are submitted to the Employee Development Section, Human Resources Division by the due date. After the rating forms are processed by the Employee Development Section, they will be returned to their respective units.
IV.Performance Rating Notification Procedures / Responsibilities
  • A.Human Resources Division
    At the beginning of each civilian rating period, the command staff member of the Human Resources Division will forward to each unit commanding officer two copies of a control sheet containing the names of civilian members assigned to his unit and sufficient numbers of Employee Rating Forms and Supervisory Employee Rating Forms.
  • B.In a timely manner, unit commanding officers will, at the beginning of the civilian rating period, ensure that:
    • 1.the appropriate rating form is utilized for each civilian member under their command, and will indicate which civilian members are supervisors by placing an "X" after each supervisory employee's name on the control sheets. Questions as to whether an employee should be rated utilizing the Supervisory Employee Rating Form can be directed to the Employee Development Section, Human Resources Division during normal business hours.
    • 2.the immediate supervisors of civilian employees are familiar with the Employee Rating Form and Supervisory Employee Rating Form, and are cognizant of the fact that the rating an employee receives at the end of the rating period will be utilized in considering merit salary increases.
    • 3.prior to the due date, the immediate supervisors:
      • a.complete, for each civilian member they supervise, the identification information at the top of the rating form which will be used to evaluate the member once the rating period has ended.
      • b.discuss the performance evaluation system and rating form with each civilian member they supervise, as described in this directive. Upon conclusion of this discussion, the supervisory member will instruct the civilian member to sign and date the rating form and control sheets. The member's signatures will serve as a record that the discussion has taken place.
      • c.submit, to their unit commanding officer a signed rating form for each civilian member under their supervision, in alpha order.
    • 4.one copy of a signed control sheet is returned to the Employee Development Section, Human Resources Division no later than the due date, once all civilians under their command have signed and dated the appropriate rating forms.
    • 5.the signed rating form is retained in the unit personnel file of each civilian member.
  • C.The unit commanding officer will also ensure that the evaluation system is explained to a civilian newly assigned to the unit and that the appropriate rating form is signed and dated as indicated in Item III-C of this directive.
V.Performance Traits
  • A.Civilian members, both supervisory and non-supervisory, will be rated on the following eight factors:
    • 1.quality of work
    • 2.quantity of work
    • 3.ability to work with others
    • 4.ability to learn
    • 5.ability to work safely
    • 6.initiative and acceptance of responsibility
    • 7.care and use of equipment, tools, materials and supplies
    • 8.attendance and punctuality.
  • B.Supervisory civilian members will be rated on one additional factor: supervisory accountabilities. Under this factor are seven accountability areas. The rater will write specific descriptions of the employee's performance in each area that applies. The seven accountability areas are:
    • 1.delivery of services
    • 2.assistance to superiors
    • 3.budgeting and/or cost control
    • 4.staff selection and development
    • 5.dealing with the public
    • 6.planning and organizing
    • 7.control and follow-up.
  • C.If a supervisory employee is accountable for an area not listed, the rater should write in the accountability area next to "OTHER" and describe the employee's performance.
VI.Procedures for Raters
  • A.Raters will ensure that for each civilian member they are rating:
    • 1.the correct identifying information appears at the top of the rating form which will be used to rate the employee.
    • 2.they are using the rating form signed by the employee at the beginning of the rating period or assignment.
  • B.There are eight factors listed on the Employee Rating Form, and nine factors listed on the Supervisory Employee Rating Form. For the first eight factors listed on each form, the rater is to check one box to the left of the item which best describes the employee in that area. If none of the descriptions provided applies, the rater will check the box to the left of "OTHER" and write in the specific description of the employee's performance.
  • C.The ninth factor on the Supervisory Employee Rating Form is "Supervisory Accountabilities." The rater of a supervisory employee is to write in specific descriptions of the employee's performance for those areas that apply to the employee's job in the spaces labeled "COMMENTS." If a supervisory employee is accountable for an area not listed, the rater should write in the accountability area next to "OTHER" and describe the employee's performance.
  • D.The rater is to indicate whether the performance description checked for each factor reflects a level of performance that is Excellent, Good, Marginal or Unsatisfactory by checking the appropriate box on the right hand side of the form. The following descriptions apply to the levels of performance for each factor:
    • 1.EXCELLENT
      Well above acceptable performance; very little improvement is possible.
    • 2.GOOD
      Acceptable performance; improvement is possible.
    • 3.MARGINAL
      Below acceptable performance; improvement is essential and administrative action may be required.
    • 4.UNSATISFACTORY
      Far below acceptable performance; performance so unacceptable administrative action must be taken.
  • E.The rater is to determine the Overall Rating by reviewing the boxes checked on the right hand side of each page on the rating form. The ratings for all factors are to be considered, but some factors may be given greater weight than others due to their importance to the particular job. For example, an employee may receive "Good" ratings in four factors and "Marginal" in four others. The Overall Rating could be either "Good" or "Marginal," depending on which factors are the most important areas of the employee's job. In all cases, the Overall Rating must be a logical outcome of the ratings for individual factors. After all factors have been reviewed, the rater is to check the box under Overall Rating which describes the employee's overall rating. PLEASE NOTE THE FOLLOWING DESCRIPTIONS FOR THE OVERALL RATING:
    • 1.EXCELLENT
      Well above acceptable performance; very little improvement is possible.
    • 2.GOOD
      Acceptable performance; improvement is possible in some or all areas.
    • 3.MARGINAL
      Below acceptable performance, although performance in some areas may be "Good," in other areas it is "Marginal" or "Unsatisfactory." Improvement is essential and administrative action may be required.An Overall Rating of "Marginal" requires that specific, descriptive comments be provided by the rater. This rating denies the employees merit increase, and the unit command staff officer must indicate this by checking the box at the bottom of the form and signing on the line provided. An employee who receives a "Marginal" Overall Rating is expected to improve by the next rating period. Administrative action may be necessary. An employee who receives two consecutive "Marginal" ratings is subject to further administrative action.
    • 4.UNSATISFACTORY
      Far below acceptable performance; performance so unacceptable that administrative action must be taken.An Overall Rating of "Unsatisfactory" indicates serious performance problems. Specific, descriptive comments must be provided by the rater. This rating denies the employee a merit increase, and the unit command staff officer must indicate this by checking the box at the bottom of the form and signing on the line provided. An Overall Rating of "Unsatisfactory" requires immediate administrative action which can include discipline or discharge.
  • F.If it becomes apparent during the rating period that a member's performance is not at a satisfactory level and that his performance rating may be lower than it was for the previous rating period, THE MEMBER IS TO BE INFORMED and CORRECTIVE ACTION INITIATED. The rater should inform the member of areas where improvement is needed and counsel him as to how to attain an improved level of performance. Weaknesses and the member's substandard activity should be readily apparent to the rater, and there should be no difficulty in localizing the subject of discussion with the member.
    • 1.The method of discussion is probably the most important step in achieving good results from a counseling session. Guidelines for effective counseling sessions include the following:
      • a.The discussion should be informal and private.
      • b.The good points and/or potential of the member should be mentioned first.
      • c.Criticism should be of a constructive nature.
      • d.The member should be allowed to express himself and to explain his own reasons for the performance in question.
      • e.The rater should be a patient listener and avoid permitting the discussion to become argumentative.
      • f.The counseling session should be documented with a report to the unit commanding officer.
    • 2.An interview between the rater and employee should be looked upon as a training session, primarily to benefit the employee and improve his job performance.
    • 3.In any case, when a member is to be assigned a performance rating of "Marginal" or "Unsatisfactory," there should be a written record of the incidents and conduct during the rating period that made necessary the assigning of such mark. In addition, there should be a written record of the dates of all interview and counseling sessions when the member was advised of deficiencies.
    • 4.When a member is assigned a grade of "Marginal" or below, command staff officers will recommend to the command staff member of the Human Resources Division that the member be placed in the Behavioral Intervention System or Personnel Concerns Program, as appropriate. (Refer to the Department directives, entitled "Behavioral Intervention System," or "Personnel Concerns Program.")
  • G.An employee may request a review of a performance evaluation. Any request for review of the performance evaluation must be made in writing to the unit command staff officer within seven calendar days of the date the employee signed the evaluation. A review of the evaluation shall be made under the direction of the unit command staff officer.
  • H.The rater is required to discuss the completed rating form with the employee and to explain the employee's right of review in accordance with Item VI-G of this directive. At the end of the discussion, both the employee and the supervisor will sign and date the form. The employee's signature indicates only that the rating was discussed and the rights of review explained.
  • I.Unit command staff officers will ensure that the rating form is retained in the employee's unit personnel file in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
