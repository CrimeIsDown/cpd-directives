Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-04-02
Vacation Selection and Scheduling for Civilian Members
Issue Date:21 October 2002Effective Date:22 October 2002
Rescinds:S02-17-02
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.Purpose
This directive established procedures for the selection and scheduling of vacation days by civilian Department members.
II.Civilian Department Members (Except Crossing Guards)
  • A.The allotted vacation days for civilian Department members, excluding crossing guards, will be as follows:
    • 1.Thirteen working days of vacation for one through five years of continuous service.
    • 2.Eighteen working days of vacation for six through thirteen years of continuous service, if the sixth anniversary is prior to 1 July in the sixth year.
    • 3.Twenty-three working days of vacation for fourteen years or more of continuous service, if the fourteenth anniversary is prior to 1 July in the fourteenth year.
    • 4.Twenty-four working days of vacation for twenty-four years or more of continuous service.
    • 5.Twenty-five working days of vacation for twenty-five years or more of continuous service.
  • B.No vacation leave will be available to a new member in the calendar year of appointment.
  • C.Vacation is computed on the basis of time earned during the prior working year.
    • 1.The amount of pro rata vacation due to a member who did not have twelve months of continuous service in the prior year and who is on the payroll as of 1 January of the current year is determined by dividing the number of months of continuous service worked in the prior year by twelve.
    • 2.The resulting figure is multiplied by the number of vacation days authorized. Any fraction is rounded off to the nearest whole number of days.
    • 3.Any month in which the member worked at least 50% of the time will be credited for purposes of computing vacation leave.
  • D.Members returning from leave of absence during the year will receive vacation based on the time earned during the prior calendar year.
    • 1.No member while on leave of absence or leave without pay status may earn vacation credit except where such leave is for duty disability.
    • 2.Members on duty disability are to be treated the same as active employees.
    • 3.Members will be credited with vacation while on duty disability.
      NOTE:
      The one year vacation carryover limitation of the City's Salary Resolution also applies to employees on duty disability.
  • E.The granting of requests for vacations within districts or units will be the responsibility of the district or unit commanding officer, consistent with the operational needs of the unit, and the applicable provisions of existing collective bargaining agreements.
III.Crossing Guards
  • A.Crossing guards will be credited with vacations as follows:
    • 1.Thirty-six hours of vacation time will be credited after one year of service.
    • 2.Sixty hours of vacation time will be credited after two years of service.
    • 3.Seventy-two hours of vacation time will be credited after nine years through the thirteenth year of service.
    • 4.Ninety-two hours of vacation time will be credited after fourteen years of service.
  • B.Crossing guards shall receive half their vacation pay no later than May 25, and the other half of their vacation pay no later than December 10.
  • C.Vacation pay shall be computed by multiplying the employee's regular hourly rate of pay by the number of hours of vacation to which the employee is entitled.
  • D.Crossing guards may use twenty hours of their vacation as days off. Such days(s) off shall be approved by the employee's supervisor; approval shall not be unreasonably withheld.
IV.Vacation Selections
Vacation selection for civilian members is as follows:
  • A.AFSCME members and members not covered by a bargaining unit agreement will select their vacations on the basis of continuous service.
  • B.UNIT II members will select their vacations on the basis of seniority (time-in-title) by watch in accordance with the existing collective bargaining agreement.
