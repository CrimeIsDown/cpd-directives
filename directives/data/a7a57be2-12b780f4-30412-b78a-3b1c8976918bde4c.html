Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-01-05
Financial Exploitation of Elderly or Disable Persons
Issue Date:03 September 1997Effective Date:04 September 1997
Rescinds:S97-06
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This order:
  • A.informs Department members of the: Financial Law Enforcement And Government (FLAG) program.
  • B.outlines procedures for responding to incidents of alleged financial exploitation of elderly or disabled persons.
  • C.institutes the mandatory reporting of financial exploitation of elderly or disabled persons.
II.General Information
FLAG
The FLAG program is an initiative that teams public and private sector resources to address financial exploitation of elderly or disabled persons. The team includes representatives from the banking industry, Cook County Public Guardians Office, Cook County States Attorneys Elder Abuse Unit and the Chicago Police Department. The goals of the FLAG program are to increase awareness of financial crimes targeting the elderly or disabled; to more fully document crime and suspicions of crime; and, to coordinate the efforts of the banking industry, agencies which assist the elderly or disabled, and law enforcement to protect the elderly and disabled from financial exploitation.

IV.Procedures
  • A.Preliminary Investigator
    When responding to an incident of alleged financial exploitation of an elderly or disabled person, the officer will:
    • 1.conduct a preliminary investigation.
    • 2.if it is determine that a criminal act has occurred or was attempted and the offender is on the scene, place the offender under arrest in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
    • 3.prepare an appropriate case report. The preparation of a case report is not contingent upon the presence of the victim/offender or an actual arrest situation.
      NOTE:
      When a responding officer has not been provided with an adequate amount of information to substantiate a crime, the officer will complete a General Offense Case Report (CPD-11.380), include the information available and insert "Non-criminal Service" in the primary classification box and 5081 in the I-UCR offense code box. A General Offense Case Report is being prepared to document information and provide follow-up investigators with possible patterns of criminal activity in an effort to prevent the victim from suffering future/further financial loss.
    • 4.make appropriate notifications in accordance with the Department directive entitled "Preliminary Investigations."
  • B.Review Officer
    Based upon the district of occurrence, the review officer will forward a copy of the case report to the neighborhood relations officer assigned to work with elderly or disabled citizens.
  • C.CAPS Officer Assigned to Elderly/Disabled Citizens
    The district neighborhood relations officer assigned to work with elderly or disabled citizens will review the case reports regarding the financial exploitation of elderly and disabled persons. Victims will be interviewed by assigned neighborhood relations officers to determine the need for additional intervention from social service agencies, such as the : Chicago Department of Human Services, Cook County Public Guardians Office, Chicago Department on Aging, Cook County States Attorneys Elder Abuse Unit, or the Mayor s Office for Persons With Disabilities . The assigned neighborhood relations officers will make appropriate referrals and will lend their expertise in dealing with elderly and disabled citizens to personnel conducting the investigation, as needed.
  • D.Detective Division
    Detective Division members assigned to cases involving the financial exploitation of elderly or disabled citizens will conduct prompt investigations and provide the neighborhood relations officer assigned to work with elderly or disabled citizens in the districts of occurrence with case dispositions, as they become available.
