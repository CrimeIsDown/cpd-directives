Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-01-02
Evaluation of Members the Rank of Captain
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 10 May 2010; A09-02-02
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues the Performance Evaluation System (PES) for sworn members at the rank of Captain.
  • B.provides procedures for the preparation and submission of performance evaluations within the PES.
II.Performance Evaluation Criteria
  • A.Sworn members at the rank of Captain:
    • 1.will:
      • a.be evaluated by the district commander / unit commanding officer of the unit the member was assigned / detailed to for at least the twenty-eight (28) calendar days prior to the evaluation period.
      • b.not be evaluated who have been:
        • (1)on the medical roll for an illness or injury, on or off duty, or have been on a leave of absence for six (6) consecutive months or more immediately preceding the evaluation period.
        • (2)separated from the Department for any reason (i.e., retired, resigned, discharged).
    • 2.who have worked for a district commander / unit commanding officer for less than twenty-eight (28) days will be evaluated by their previously assigned district commander/unit commanding officer.
  • B.Performance Dimensions
    There are six (6) Performance Dimensions used in the evaluation of a member’s job performance which include:
    • 1.Leadership / Motivating / Mentoring Others:
      • a.Promotes a work environment where all can perform to their potential.
      • b.Provides opportunities for subordinates to obtain new knowledge and experiences.
      • c.Demonstrates sufficient knowledge of officers to be aware of their personal situations.
      • d.Shows compassion and empathy by asking officers what they need.
      • e.Turns events that happened in the district into learning opportunities to be shared with the watch.
      • f.Seeks out and informs officers of training opportunities that may be useful for them.
      • g.Is constantly training officers through personal examples and ongoing discussion.
      • h.Engenders loyalty from members.
      • i.Motivates subordinates.
    • 2.Analysis / Problem Solving:
      • a.Monitors conditions on the watch continuously to identify and respond to emerging crime patterns.
      • b.Allocates resources to an area before a problem escalates.
      • c.Considers the impact of problems on other watches and provides support to those other watches in addressing them.
      • d.Creates formalized processes for officers to share information about concerns and tactics taken on various beats with others on the watch.
      • e.Actively monitors the medical roll to identify and prevent problematic use.
    • 3.Judgment / Decision Making:
      • a.Recognizes when decisions will set precedent or cause unintentional liability.
      • b.Can justify decisions.
    • 4.Initiative / Dedication:
      • a.Consistently looks for ways to improve own effectiveness or task performance.
      • b.Anticipates next step in the process.
      • c.Voluntarily handles assignments outside of job activities when necessary.
      • d.Exerts extra effort to meet Department goals.
      • e.Researches crime trends to address the underlying causes.
      • f.Maintains focus on issues across the district when addressing problems.
      • g.Takes an active role in maintaining awareness of members’ activities and situations to ensure that problems are addressed promptly and appropriately.
    • 5.Managing Time and Resources:
      • a.Anticipates and removes obstacles to plans before they block progress.
      • b.Develops contingencies and fall-back plans.
      • c.Considers outcomes when planning.
      • d.Thinks ahead to prevent long-term negative consequences.
      • e.Prepares information for use by members on the watch.
      • f.Tracks personnel levels to ensure appropriate coverage.
      • g.Gathers information from multiple sources to pass along at roll call.
    • 6.Communication:
      • a.Adapts verbal and written language to the specific audience.
      • b.Uses everyday occurrences as a training tool to which members may refer for future reference.
      • c.Proactively disseminates updated information to the watch.
      • d.Discusses new information with members, both collectively and individually, to ensure that members understand when, how, and why a policy or procedure should be implemented.
  • C.Evaluation Categories
    • 1.There are four (4) categories used to evaluate a member’s job performance for each of the Performance Dimensions which include:
      • a.Exceeds Expectations: The member consistently displays performance of the duties and responsibilities of the job at a level that exceeds performance expectations for this dimension.
      • b.Meets Expectations: The member meets job performance expectations for this dimension.
      • c.Requires Improvement: The member must improve job performance to meet expectations for this dimension.
      • d.Unacceptable: The member fails to meet job performance expectations for this dimension.
    • 2.Example behaviors for each evaluation category and dimension are provided in the Performance Evaluation Procedure Manual to assist supervisors in making job performance evaluations.
  • D.Overall Performance Evaluation Category
    • 1.A member’s Overall Performance Evaluation Category:
      • a.will be based on the evaluations received for each of the six (6) Performance Dimensions.
      • b.is not a percentage evaluation, (i.e., not a mathematical calculation). Rather, it is based on certain policies established by the Department.
    • 2.Described below is a summary of policy statements associated with a member’s Overall Performance Evaluation Category.
CategoryPolicy Statements
Exceeds ExpectationsAt least half of individual dimension evaluations are “Exceeds Expectations.”No individual dimension evaluations of “Requires Improvement.”No individual dimension evaluations of “Unacceptable.”- Eligible to be considered for specialized training.- Will have priority consideration for assessor assignments.
Meets ExpectationsMajority of individual dimension evaluations are at least “Meets Expectations.”No more than two individual dimension evaluations at “Requires Improvement.” No individual dimension evaluations at “Unacceptable.”- Eligible to be considered for specialized training.- Will be considered for assessor assignments.
Requires ImprovementMajority of individual dimension evaluations are “Requires Improvement.”No more than two individual dimension evaluations of “Unacceptable.”A second period of Overall Evaluation in “Requires Improvement” will result in the initiation of the Captain SES removal process.- Not eligible for specialized training.- Not eligible for assessor assignments.- Not eligible for assignments outside district law enforcement.
UnacceptableThree or more individual dimension evaluations of “Unacceptable.”An Overall Evaluation of “Unacceptable” in a rating period will result in the initiation of the Captain SES removal process.- Not eligible for specialized training.- Not eligible for assessor assignments.- Not eligible for assignments outside district law enforcement.
III.Responsibilities
District commanders / unit commanding officers will:
  • A.be notified of those members who are to receive their yearly performance evaluation one (1) month prior to the month the evaluations are due.
  • B.independently consider the job performance of each identified sworn member under their command.
  • C.review performance information contained in the member's portfolio within the Performance Recognition System and contact the member's supervisors in previous units of assignments / details, if the member was assigned / detailed to another unit for a period of twenty-eight (28) days or more during the evaluation period, to provide an accurate assessment of job performance.
  • D.independently provide preliminary evaluations for members designated for their review and enter a preliminary rating for each dimension.
  • E.participate in a Performance Evaluation Review Session with the member under review, to:
    • 1.review and discuss:
      • a.job performance and the member’s evaluation.
      • b.the specific events that support the preliminary performance rating they gave for specific dimension(s) in question.
    • 2.support their ratings with specific examples as to why they gave a particular rating for the member under discussion.
  • F.submit the evaluation for the member's acceptance or rejection after the completion of the Performance Evaluation Review Session.
  • G.prepare a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) if any member receives a rating for any Performance Dimension in the "Requires Improvement" or "Unacceptable" category.
IV.Review of the Evaluation
  • A.Evaluated members:
    • 1.will have access to review their evaluations for each dimension and their Overall Performance Evaluation.
    • 2.whose job performance has been evaluated will accept or reject their performance evaluation in the PES.
  • B.If the evaluated member does not accept a rating for an individual dimension or the Overall Performance Evaluation, district commanders / unit commanding officers will document that the evaluated member has rejected the evaluation in the PES.
  • C.District commanders/unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.inform the member of the appeal process that may be exercised within seven (7) calendar days of the Performance Evaluation Review Session.
    • 2.assign all rejected performance evaluations, within the PES, to the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command if the member requests a primary appeal.
V.Appeal Process
  • A.Primary
    • 1.Evaluated members may:
      • a.appeal their Overall Performance Evaluation or individual Performance Dimension assessments.
      • b.request a review of their performance evaluation by the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command. The member will document the reasons for rejection of the evaluation within seven (7) calendar days of the date the member reviewed and refused to accept the Overall Performance Evaluation rating.
    • 2.The appropriate exempt member will:
      • a.review rejected performance evaluations.
      • b.conduct an appeal meeting with the requesting member to discuss the performance evaluation.
      • c.assign all eligible, rejected performance evaluations, within the PES, to the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command if a final level review is requested.
    • 3.In response to a request for a review of a performance evaluation, the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command may require written reports from any individual deemed appropriate. The decision of the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command as to the appropriate rating is final for any rating other than “Unacceptable” and will be documented in the automated system.
  • B.Final Level Review
    • 1.If a member's Overall Performance Evaluation rating remains "Unacceptable" after the primary appeal with the appropriate exempt member, the member may request a secondary review, via the PES, within seven (7) calendar days of the date the member reviewed and refused to accept the Overall Performance Evaluation rating by the next level exempt member in the member’s chain of command. This Final Level Review will be conducted with the appropriate chief or deputy chief.
    • 2.In response to a request for a review of a performance evaluation, the appropriate chief or deputy chief may require written reports from any individual deemed appropriate. The decision of the appropriate chief or deputy chief as to the appropriate rating is final and will be documented in the system.
