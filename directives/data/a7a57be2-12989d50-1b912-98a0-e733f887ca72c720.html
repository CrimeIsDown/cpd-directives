Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-05
Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES)
Issue Date:09 December 2016Effective Date:09 December 2016
Rescinds:21 February 2012 Version; 21 February 2012 Version of E05-05-01.
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive informs Department members:
  • A. of the rank of lieutenant that applications for Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES) are being accepted until 6 January 2017 at 1500 hours.
  • B.of the duties and eligibility requirements for appointment to Captain - Senior Executive Service (SES).
  • C.of the application process, selection procedures, and training program for Captain - SES.
  • D.that the applicant list may be maintained through multiple appointment processes.
  • E.of removal procedures of incumbents from Captain - SES positions, the consequences of removal, and additional conditions.
II.Duties
The primary function of a Captain - SES will be to:
  • A.serve as the executive officer of a Department unit, or
  • B.serve as the executive officer and be second in command of a district. If assigned to District Law Enforcement as an executive officer, a Captain - SES will:
    • 1.typically not work the same duty hours as the district commander.
    • 2.primarily assist the district commander in areas of operations, administration, planning, identification of emerging crime trends, and preparing strategic plans to address crime and disorder issues impacting the community
    • 3.perform duties and responsibilities outlined in Special Order S03-03-02 entitled District Executive Officer and Bureau of Patrol Special Order 12-06 entitled "District Executive Officer."
  • C.perform related duties as required.
III.Eligibility
To be appointed to Captain - SES, candidates must:
  • A.be a lieutenant in an active-duty status or on an approved leave of absence at the time of application.
  • B.have obtained a bachelor’s degree by the close of the application period.
  • C.be a lieutenant in an active duty status for two years at the time of appointment.
    NOTE:
    Lieutenants on an approved leave of absence at the time of application can still be appointed as long as the above criterion is met and they are otherwise eligible to return to an active duty status.
  • D.be able to perform the functions of a Captain - SES at the time of appointment.
  • E.be able to work any watch assignment at the time of appointment.
  • F.if applicable, have received performance ratings above the minimum rating score necessary to qualify for a step increase for each of the last four evaluation periods at the time of appointment.
  • G.have an acceptable disciplinary record at the time of appointment. The candidate’s disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (CR) investigations for misconduct resulting in suspensions of more than seven (7) days during the preceding 12 month period; or a record of three (3) or more sustained CR investigations resulting in suspensions of any length of time within the preceding five years prior to appointment.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR investigations with findings of “No Disciplinary Action” will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances or Police Board cases concerning discipline will be allowed to apply. Such applications will be considered if the candidate’s disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of the grievance or the Police Board hearing so that the member’s disciplinary record meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • H.have an acceptable attendance record at the time of appointment. The candidate’s attendance record cannot reflect any unauthorized absences within the preceding 12 month period prior to appointment.
  • I.meet acceptable medical roll usage guidelines at the time of appointment. If the candidate’s medical roll usage reflects nine (9) or more medical incidents and/or 45 days or more on the medical roll within the preceding three (3) year period, absent exceptional circumstances, or demonstrates a pattern of medical roll abuse or misuse (i.e., behaviors including, but not limited to: the candidate goes on the medical roll when assigned to an involuntary detail; the medical roll use is linked to furloughs, RDOs, or other time due to extend time off; or the candidate is found to have used the medical roll in an unauthorized manner), the candidate will be disqualified.
    NOTE:
    Medical time used in relation to approved FMLA leaves, ADA accommodations, or IOD injuries cannot be the basis for disqualification.
  • J.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago at the time of application and at the time of appointment.
  • K.complete the Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form at the time of application.
IV.Captain - SES Application Process
  • A.Candidates will:
    • 1.go online to the Taleo Police Careers site for the City of Chicago located at www.cityofchicago.org/policepromotions;
    • 2.select the link, "Captain (SES)" under "Job Openings;"
    • 3.follow the procedures for submitting an application. A click-by-click "Candidate Experience Guide" is available on the Chicago Police Department Human Resource Division's web page to assist in submitting an application. To access the guide, click on Bureau sites, then click on "Human Resources Division", then click on "Career Development and Performance", and then click on "Candidate Experience Guide."
      NOTE:
      Questions regarding completing a profile or application in Careers should be directed to the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources, 312-744-4976.
    • 4.attach a cover letter as part of the Taleo application including the following information:
      • a.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's Chicago Police Department assignments to the Captain - SES position.
      • b.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's non-Chicago Police Department work experience or training to the Captain - SES position.
      • c.discussion of the relevancy of the applicant's supervisory and leadership experience to the Captain - SES position.
      • d.discussion of the applicant's strengths that qualify the applicant to perform the function of the Captain - SES position. The strengths should be considered in terms of the Critical Performance Dimensions identified for the Captain - SES position. Reference the Chicago Police Department, Human Resource Division, Documents and Forms subsection web page for specifics.
    • 5.attach a résumé as part of the Taleo application including the following information:
      • a.name,
      • b.star number,
      • c.employee number,
      • d.home address,
      • e.district of residence,
      • f.home and cellular telephone numbers,
      • g.history of Departmental assignments including dates of assignment, supervisor names, and duties performed,
      • h.non-Chicago Police Department work experience or training (e.g., military experience, special training) relevant to appointment to Captain - SES. Include the type of experience or training received and the years of your involvement, and
      • i.education, including the:
        • (1)name and location of colleges / universities attended, and
        • (2)number of semester or quarter hours completed; type of degree or certification; and year completed.
    • 6.identify three references who are current members of the Department with whom they have worked for or with in the last three years. One of the references must be an exempt member and the other two must be supervisors. The three references each must complete the standard letter of recommendation prior to the close of the application period. The three letters of recommendation must be received by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Administration Section, by the close of the application period. An unfavorable reference may be the basis for disqualification from further consideration from the position.
      NOTE:
      The standard letter of recommendation form is found on the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division's web page under the Documents and Forms Subsection.
    • 7.complete and submit a Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form. This form must be received by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Administration Section, by the close of the application period.
      NOTE:
      The Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports is found on the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division's web page under the Documents and Forms Subsection.
    • 8.obtain a statement of educational attainment from the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Employment Section. The statement of educational attainment must be received from the candidate by the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Administration Section, by the close of the application period.
    NOTE:
    If the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Administration Section, does not receive the three letters of recommendation, the Consent to Obtain Consumer Reports form, and the statement of educational attainment by the close of the application period, the member will be disqualified from continuing in the selection process for Captain - SES.
  • B.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances, such as military duty. To submit a proxy application, the candidate's proxy will contact the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, at 312-745-5318 before the close of the application period.
  • C.Members who have applied for the position of Captain - SES certify that they are willing and able to perform the duties of a Captain - SES.
  • D.The applicant list may be maintained through multiple appointment processes. Applicants will be notified by an Administrative Message Center (AMC) message of the format to update a previously submitted Resume / Cover Letter for the duration of the applicant list.
V.Captain - SES Selection Process
  • A.The Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division (CPD-HR), will review applications for completeness and will forward complete applications to the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources (DHR).
  • B.DHR will again review applications for completeness and will screen applications to determine whether a candidate meets the minimum qualifications for the position in accordance with the eligibility requirements delineated in Section III of this directive.
  • C.The City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources (DHR) will compile a list of all minimally qualified candidates and submit a certified list of eligible applicants identifying those applicants that meet the eligibility requirements to the Chicago Police Department, Director, Human Resources Division (CPD-HR).
  • D.CPD-HR will review the certified list of eligible applicants to determine if the candidates meet the appointment requirements and remove candidates, if necessary. The Director, Human Resources Division, will ensure the list is processed as specified in the Department directives entitled “Merit Board.”
  • E.Eligible candidates will be further screened by the Captain's Screening Board.
    • 1.The Screening Board will be comprised of the following members selected by the Superintendent of Police:
      • a.two members currently in the rank of Captain,
        NOTE:
        The Patrolmen's Benevolent and Protective Association (PBPA), Unit 156 – Captains, may make nominations to the Superintendent of Police on which captains to select for the Screening Board.
      • b.two district commanders, and
      • c.two deputy chiefs, Bureau of Patrol (or equivalent rank).
    • 2.The Screening Board will review the Resume / Cover Letter for all lieutenants on the referral list and will determine by a 2/3 vote whether to eliminate a lieutenant from further consideration. All decisions to eliminate an applicant from further consideration will be documented in writing.
    • 3.The Screening Board will forward ineligible lieutenants' Resumes / Cover Letters to the Chicago Police Department, Director, Human Resources Division, for retention purposes.
    • 4.The list of remaining eligible lieutenants will be placed in a sealed envelope and forwarded with all Resumes / Cover Letters in a sealed carton marked "Captain Selection Process – Confidential" to the chairperson of the Merit Board for further screening.
  • F.The Merit Board will review all the submissions and make recommendations to the Superintendent of Police as delineated in the Department directive entitled "Merit Board".
  • G.CPD-HR will submit a Request for Hire Form to the Office of Budget and Management identifying the position and the number of vacancies to be filled.
  • H.The Superintendent of Police will have sole discretion to select candidates for appointment to Captain – SES.
    • 1.The Superintendent of Police may elect to conduct interviews, make decisions based on documents submitted by the candidate, seek additional information from supervisors, or any combination thereof.
    • 2.The Superintendent of Police will select lieutenants to attend training and make the final selection for all appointments to Captain - SES.
    • 3.The Superintendent of Police will select which candidates shall be appointed. Appointment will be contingent in successful completion of the Captain - SES training.
    • 4.The Superintendent of Police may choose to send more candidates for training than available vacancies.
  • I.The Superintendent of Police will forward all lieutenant's Resumes / Cover Letters to the Chicago Police Department, Director, Human Resources Division, for retention purposes.
VI.Captain - SES Training Process
  • A.The Superintendent of Police will select which candidates shall be appointed and those candidates will have to complete the Captain – SES training.
    NOTE:
    Any lieutenant who is on an approved leave of absence must return to active duty status prior to selection for training.
  • B.The Superintendent of Police may choose to send more candidates than available vacancies to the Captain – SES training and make final decisions after the completion of the training.
  • C.The completion of the training does not guarantee appointment to Captain – SES.
  • D.Selected lieutenants must undergo and pass a drug screening prior to appointment to the position of Captain – SES.
VII.Removal of Incumbents from Captain - SES Positions
  • A.District commanders or appropriate exempt member may, with the approval of the appropriate deputy chief, remove a Captain - SES during the first six months that such member holds the position of Captain - SES for such reason(s) as the district commander or appropriate exempt member deems appropriate. This recommendation will be subject to command channel review and the final written approval of the Superintendent of Police.
  • B.After a period of six (6) months, a Captain - SES can only be removed in accordance with the following process:
    • 1.the Captain - SES must be advised, in writing, specifically by the district commander or other appropriate exempt member in the chain of command, of the reason(s) that warrant(s) removal;
    • 2.the district commander or other appropriate exempt member must provide the Captain - SES with an opportunity to respond to those reason(s) in writing;
    • 3.the district commander’s or other appropriate exempt member’s reason(s) and the response from the Captain - SES must be submitted, in writing, to the Captain - SES Review Board convened by the Superintendent of Police. The Review Board will be composed of nine Department members, consisting of seven exempt members appointed by the Superintendent of Police and two captains recommended by the Captain’s Association;
    • 4.the Captain - SES must be afforded an opportunity to appear before the Review Board to respond to the reason(s) for removal. The district commander, or other appropriate exempt member, may also be required to appear before the Review Board to respond to questions that may be put forth by the Review Board;
    • 5.the Review Board will make and forward a written recommendation to the Superintendent of Police.
    • 6.the Superintendent of Police, within sixty days (60), will either:
      • a.approve the Review Board’s recommendation,
      • b.reject the Review Board’s recommendation, or
      • c.modify the Review Board’s recommendation.
    • 7.written copies of the Review Board’s recommendations and the decision of the Superintendent of Police will then be given to the affected Captain - SES and district commander, or other appropriate exempt member.
  • C.The Superintendent of Police has the authority to temporarily remove a Captain - SES, provided that the salary of the Captain - SES is not affected, prior to the completion of the formal removal process.
VIII.Consequences of Removal
A removed Captain - SES will be returned to their permanent career service rank of lieutenant and assigned to a position customarily held by a lieutenant.
IX.Additional Conditions
  • A.The removal and reassignment processes are administrative in nature, not punitive, and not part of a disciplinary process. Nothing in this directive, including, but not limited to, the removal and reassignment process, will affect the Department’s right to initiate and impose disciplinary action against such employee in accordance with the appropriate Department directives, rules and regulations, including the rules and procedures of the Police Board.
  • B.An Ad Hoc Committee will monitor and assess the effectiveness of the process outlined in this directive. The committee will meet, as needed, and may make recommendations to the Superintendent of Police and/or the Union, if appropriate, for modifications to the process. The committee will be comprised of:
    • 1.three (3) captains appointed by the union, and
    • 2.three (3) exempt members appointed by the Superintendent of Police.
