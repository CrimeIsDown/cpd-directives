Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D16-02
Web-Based Roll Call System
Issue Date:10 June 2016Effective Date:13 June 2016
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of the implementation of an updated standardized roll call utilizing applications and informational databases available to Department members.
  • B.introduces the:
    • 1. Web-Based Roll Call System on the Department intranet site home page.
    • 2. Chicago Police Department Wire Roll Call Post Creator User Guide, available on The Wire through the Department intranet.
II.General Information
  • A.The Web-Based Roll Call System:
    • 1.is an electronic resource intended to be used at roll calls for the purpose of disseminating accurate and timely district intelligence of current crime conditions, safety concerns, gang conflicts, community relations, and administrative information. The system enables members to optimize patrol efforts in the locations that are most problematic and towards the subjects described or identified as offenders.
    • 2.contains information that will be disseminated to Department members during roll call. The Web-Based Roll Call System consists of information from several different applications, databases, and bulletins that will be updated daily by designated personnel to ensure the most accurate information is relayed to Department members at roll call.
  • B.The highest ranking supervisor available or a designee of the district commander/unit commanding officer will conduct roll call utilizing the Web-Based Roll Call System for each watch.
  • C.The following Department members are authorized to access the Web-Based Roll Call System:
    • 1.district commanders/unit commanding officers;
    • 2.executive officers;
    • 3.lieutenants;
    • 4.district intelligence officers;
    • 5.designees of district commanders/unit commanding officers.
  • D.Authorized Department members can access the Chicago Police Department Wire Roll Call Post Creator Guide for detailed instructions on creating and editing posts.
III.Scope
The pilot program will be operational city-wide on 13 June 2016.
IV.Web-Based Roll Call System
The system, updated daily either automatically or by designated Department members, depending on the type of post, disseminates current intelligence and other appropriate information to Department members during roll call. The following are examples of information that may be included in the Web-Based Roll Call System.
  • A.Automatically updated posts:
    • 1.five-day weather forecast post;
    • 2.911 priority calls post—This post contains active 911 calls that are immediately visible, while coded calls are accessible by clicking the link next to the active calls.
  • B.Posts that need manual updating daily:
    • 1.Unit Intelligence Posts—Consist of information gathered according to the directive entitled "District Intelligence Officer";
    • 2.Commanding Officer's (C/O) Book Posts—Consists of information deemed appropriate by the unit commanding officer; and
    • 3.District News Posts—Consists of information of interest to district personnel.
V.Procedures
  • A.The highest ranking supervisor available or a designee of the district commander/unit commanding officer conducting roll call utilizing the Web-Based Roll Call System for the watch will:
    • 1.prior to conducting roll call, ensure that the posts requiring manual updates listed in item IV-B of this directive are current and inclusive of all intelligence received from the previous watch.
    • 2.ensure all Commanding Officer, Unit Intelligence, and District News Posts are presented to Department members during roll call.
    • 3.ensure all current directives governing roll call procedures are followed except when delineated otherwise within this directive.
  • B.The Watch Operations Lieutenant will provide for the continuity of operations between watches by ensuring the oncoming watch is advised of the district conditions and problems that may impact the relieving watch by inputting the information into the Unit Intelligence Posts.
    NOTE:
    Documentation into the Web-Based Roll Call System is required whether or not any significant conditions or problems have occurred.
  • C.District commanders/unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.ensure the procedures for the Web-Based Roll Call System are conducted.
    • 2.designate appropriate and sufficient personnel to update the following posts of the Web-Based Roll Call System daily:
      • a.Unit Intelligence Posts;
      • b.Commanding Officer's (C/O) Book Posts; and
      • c.District News Posts.
  • D.The Information Services Division will ensure all authorized Department members have access to the Web-Based Roll Call System.
    NOTE:
    Department members who require access will submit a B.O.S. Service Request (CPD-60.107) to the Information Services Division requesting a user account for The Wire.
