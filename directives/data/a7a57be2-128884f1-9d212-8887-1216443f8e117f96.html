Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-02-02
Payroll And Timekeeping-Overtime / Compensatory Time
Issue Date:27 September 1994Effective Date:29 September 1994
Rescinds:G94-05-02
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.Overtime Authorization
  • A.No member is authorized to work overtime without the prior approval of the member's appropriate supervisor. Prior approval may be verbal. Final approval will require the signature and star number of the authorizing supervisor on the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (CPD 11.608) in the space provided for entitled OVERTIME AUTHORIZED BY (RANK - NAME - STAR NO.).
    NOTE:
    When a Department member is assigned as a Field Training Officer and eligible for the one-half (1/2) hour of overtime compensation, the appropriate supervisor will indicate both authorization and approval of the earned overtime by recording their signature, PC login ID number, date, and time in the "Approved By: On-Duty W/C / Unit CO." section of the report.
  • B.No watch commander / unit commanding officer may authorize his own overtime. District watch commanders and unit commanding officers must obtain authorization from the appropriate higher ranking command staff member, in those instances when the unit command staff level commanding officer or other command staff member in the chain of command is not available to authorize the member working overtime. Such authorization will not be required when a Department directive prohibits the member from relinquishing his responsibility for the conduct of investigations requiring his personal direction.
  • C.No member is authorized to earn overtime compensation, while appearing in court, during off-duty hours without a notification through the Automated Court Notification Program or prior approval from the member’s watch commander / unit commanding officer.
    • 1.If a member is requested to appear in court during off-duty hours:
      • a.through the Automated Court Notification Program, with a court notification record number, the member will enter the court notification record number on the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report in the space provided. The member’s watch commander/unit commanding officer’s signature is not required.
      • b.through another means of notification, without a court notification record number from the Automated Court Notification Program, the member will obtain the signature, star number, and PC Login ID number of their appropriate supervisor on the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report in the space provided.
    • 2.If an arrestee fails to post bond and the member’s presence in court is required, the authorizing supervisor for the court appearance will be the desk sergeant (or acting desk sergeant) making the notification.
  • D.If the member's unit of assignment is not operational during the time an arrestee is being processed, the member will present an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (CPD-11.608) to his watch commander / unit commanding officer the first day the member returns to duty at his unit of assignment. The Overtime / Compensatory Time Report must be approved by the watch commander on duty in the district / unit at the time the arrestee was processed.
  • E.Prior to authorizing a member to work overtime, the member's appropriate supervisor will:
    • 1.evaluate the necessity for the member working overtime.
    • 2.when practical obtain relief or replacement for the member seeking authorization to work overtime, provided that such relief will not adversely affect the police function being performed.
    • 3.sign his name and enter his star number on the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report in the space provided. If prior verbal approval was given, sign the report prior to submitting it to the watch commander.
  • F.When overtime is worked:
    • 1.within the member's unit of assignment, the supervisor authorizing the overtime will be responsible for notifying the on-duty watch commander prior to the conclusion of the supervisor's tour of duty.
    • 2.outside of the member's unit of assignment, the watch commander or equivalent where the overtime is worked is responsible for signing the "approved by" box on the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report. He will ensure that the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report is forwarded to the member's unit of assignment as outlined in Department directives.
  • G.Whenever the watch commander / unit commanding officer leaves the unit facility for any reason, he will designate another supervisor to be responsible for authorizing overtime for off-duty arrests or court appearance during his absence.
II.The Office Automation Overtime and Out of Grade Computer System
District / Unit Commanding Officers will ensure that all completed Overtime / Compensatory Time Reports (CPD-11.608) and Out of Grade Work Authorization Reports (CPD-11.639) are entered into the Office Automation Overtime System within seven days after the end of the pay cycle.
NOTE:
In the event, the individual in charge of entering such information at the district / unit level is absent, the District / Unit Commanding Officers shall find a substitute to enter the above information into the system within seven days after the end of the pay cycle.
III.Compensatory Time Earned
  • A.A member who has worked overtime will, upon completion of the overtime, personally present and Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (CPD-11.608) to the:
    • 1.on-duty watch commander / unit commanding officer when overtime is worked within the member's unit of assignment.
    • 2.supervisory member present at the completion of the overtime who can attest to the accuracy of the information submitted by the member when the member has worked overtime outside of his unit of assignment.
      NOTE:
      Supervisory members are responsible for reviewing Overtime / Compensatory Time Reports for accuracy and completeness prior to signing the report indicating their approval.
  • B.A member submitting an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report will ensure that sufficient information is contained in the "Required Explanations" section of the report to justify working the overtime (i.e., RD Number, court branch, etc.).
IV.Responsibilities of Members Attending Court
  • A.A member will not:
    • 1.appear in court during non-duty hours unless they have:
      • a.been notified to appear in court through the Automated Court Notification Program with a court notification record number, or
      • b.been notified to appear in court and received authorization to appear by the appropriate supervisory member, or
      • c.in the case of an off-duty arrest where the arrestee has not been let to bail, received prior authorization from a supervisory member of the district in which the arrest was made.
    • 2.schedule court appearances on his regular day off (Exception: scheduled court key date) or on a day for which the member requested the use of elective time which was approved.
    • 3.normally attend court for a duty related incident while on suspension without prior approval.
    • 4.request authorization for overtime to attend court if an arrest was the result of a member's participation in secondary employment. This restriction does not apply to "special" employment.
  • B.When a member must appear in court the member will complete the Court Log (CPD-21.600 or CPD-21.606) in courts where a court sergeant / officer is present or in facilities containing an assembly room.
  • C.When the member must appear in a court where no court sergeant / officer is assigned or there is no court assembly room, the member will:
    • 1.notify the Court Section, Inspection Division if the court appearance is prior to 1600 hours.
    • 2.notify Operations Command if the court appearance is after 1600 hours.
    • 3.notify either the Court Section or Operations Command (as appropriate) when the member first arrives for the court appearance and when the court appearance is completed.
      NOTE:
      Unit supervisors may verify a member's court appearance prior to approving overtime requests for such appearances by contacting either the Court Section prior to 1600 hours or Operations Command after 1600 hours. Operations Command will continue to maintain Court Attendance Telephone Logs (CPD-21.610) initiated by the Court Section. An Assistant State's Attorney or Assistant Corporation Counsel cannot approve overtime. The officer will enter the attorney's name and telephone number on the overtime report for verification purposes by supervisors.
V.Supervisory Responsibilities
  • A.Watch commanders:
    • 1.will ensure that all Arrest Reports (CPD-11.420) contain the name of the officer to appear in court, his star number and his day off group and will ensure that the officer does not schedule court appearances for his assigned day off (Exception-scheduled court key dates).
    • 2.will ensure that if more than one officer is appearing in court, the presence of the additional officer is necessary.
    • 3.will not grant elective time off to members during the period of time they are required to appear in court. A voluntary change in the scheduled day off of an officer will not be allowed if it results in an officer being required to appear in court on his regular day off.
    • 4.may change watch assignments of officers for court appearances in excess of two 2 consecutive days.
    • 5.may adjust starting times of officers plus or minus two (2) hours from the designated starting time to preclude or minimize overtime earned for court appearances.
  • B.Only unit commanding officers or watch commanders may authorize a member to voluntarily work his regular day off.
    NOTE:
    Watch commanders / unit commanding officers will not indiscriminately grant voluntary tours of duty. The granting of voluntary tours of duty may impact the Fair Labor Standards Act restriction of 171 hours work in a 28 day police period.
VI.Overtime Compensation
  • A.Overtime for a court appearance which does not exceed eight (8) hours will not be credited to a member if he is on the medical roll, absent due to sickness or death in family, military leave, marriage leave or summary punishment. When the court appearance exceeds eight (8) hours:
    • 1.agreement members will be compensated at the overtime rate on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
    • 2.sergeants, lieutenants and captains will be credited in accordance with the provisions of Item VI-C-11 of this directive.
    • 3.probationary police officers will be credited in accordance with the provisions of Item VI-G-3 of this directive.
    • 4.civilian members will be credited in accordance with the provisions of Item VI-D, E or F of this directive.
      NOTE:
      Any member on suspension or leave of absence who is required by the Department to appear in court for less than eight hours will be compensated for the hours in court at the straight time rate. The member will appear in conservative business attire.
  • B.Overtime hours will be credited for Agreement members as follows:
    • 1.Overtime earned will be credited at the rate of time and one-half on the basis of completed 15 minutes segments except:
      • a.when the member has attended court during the use of elective time and the member knew of the court date before the request for the elective time was approved.
      • b.when the member has been compensated for the time by a secondary employer.
      • c.When the member is a defendant or witness in a non-duty related incident.
    • 2.When working overtime in an authorized out of grade position, the member will be compensated in accordance with Item I-C of the Department directive entitled "Payroll And Timekeeping - Working Out Of Grade".
    • 3.Voluntary tours of duty will be credited at the rate of hour for hour for the first eight hours, in compensatory time only. Time worked in excess of the first eight hours of a voluntary tour will be credited at the rate of time and one-half in either compensatory time or payment. In this instance, a separate Overtime / Compensatory Time Report must be submitted for the hours in excess of the first eight hours worked.
    • 4.Agreement members will not be allowed to voluntarily work his regular days off on a designated holiday. If a member is required to work on the holiday the officer will be compensated in accordance with the provisions of the Agreement and the Item III-B-1-b of the Department directive entitled "Payroll and Timekeeping - Holidays / Personal Days."
    • 5.Call-back assignments will be credited at the appropriate overtime rate for a minimum of two hours. Call back assignments which exceed two hours will be compensated on the basis of completed 15 minute segments at the appropriate overtime rate.
    • 6.Members required to "stand by" at times when they are not on duty shall be compensated in accordance with the provisions of Section 20.8 of the Agreement.
    • 7.A member who has worked a sixth or seventh consecutive day within the calendar week (Sunday through Saturday) will be compensated at the rate of time and one-half for work performed on the sixth or seventh day. Voluntary schedule changes will be exempt from this provision.
    • 8.An officer shall normally not be required to work more than four hours on the first watch on change day if he has worked a full tour of duty on the third watch. If he is required to work more than four hours on a change day on the first watch, he shall be compensated at the rate of time and one-half for the hours worked on the first watch on change day.
    • 9.Court appearances during off-duty hours, including instances where the officer's tour of duty ends at exactly the same time the officer is required to attend court, will be credited at the rate of time and one-half, with a minimum of two hours when the actual time spent in court is two hours or less except for those court appearances indicated in subsections a, b and c of this Item. When the actual time spent in court exceeds two hours, overtime will be computed on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
      EXAMPLE:
      An officer whose tour of duty ends at 0900 hours and who is required to attend court at 0900 hours will receive a minimum of 2 hours credit at the overtime rate (3 hours).
      • a.Officers required to attend authorized court or pre-trial conferences within one hour immediately preceding their normal tour of duty will be compensated at the overtime rate for one hour.
      • b.Officers required to attend authorized court or pre-trial conferences commencing during their tour of duty and extending beyond the normal tour of duty will be compensated at the overtime rate on the basis of completed 15 minute segments for the overtime worked. The overtime will be computed from the end of the normal tour of duty to the sign out time at court or at the conclusion of the pre-trial conference.
      • c.Officers who have completed their attendance at court or pre-trial conferences and are required to work beyond the authorized court time, (i.e., transport witnesses home, return evidence or return Department vehicle or radio) will notify the on duty watch commander of their unit of assignment or Operations Command prior to leaving the court facility. The officer will explain the circumstances to the watch commander and obtain authorization for overtime. A separate compensatory time report will be prepared and submitted to the watch commander or unit commanding officer. The narrative portion of the compensatory time slip will indicate that the overtime is a continuation of the original court overtime and the reason the overtime was worked will be explained. Only if the court appearance and continuation are more than 2 hours will additional overtime be earned.
        NOTE:
        Members do not have the option of calling either their unit of assignment or Operations Command. They are to call Operations Command only when their unit is not operational.
    • 10.Appearances at more than one court on the same day will be compensated at the rate of time and one-half in the following manner:
      • a.When the time between court appearances exceeds 2 hours (sign out time from the first court to sign in time at the next court), a minimum of 2 hours will be credited for each court appearance.
      • b.When the time between court appearances is 2 hours or less, overtime will be computed on the basis of completed 15 minute segments for the total time between sign in time at the first court to sign out time at the last court. A minimum of 2 hours will be credited when the total time is 2 hours or less.
        NOTE:
        A separate Overtime / Compensatory Time Report for each court appearance is required. The submitting member will insert, in the explanation section, the words "Multiple Court Appearances" and indicate the court branch in which the member appeared.
  • C.Overtime hours for sergeants, lieutenants and captains will be credited in the following manner:
    • 1.All overtime worked (excluding call-back assignments) will be credited in compensatory time only.
    • 2.Supervisors who are required to report on a regularly scheduled work day (for a call-back assignment) will be credited at straight time for 3 hours- or straight time for the actual time worked, whichever is greater. Compensation will be in pay or compensatory time at the member's option.
    • 3.Supervisors who are required to report to the employer's premise at the specific time on a regularly scheduled day off will be compensated in compensatory time at a straight time rate in completed 1 hour segments.
    • 4.Supervisors required to report to the Medical Services Section at a specified time on their regular day off will be compensated at straight time for 3 hours (time only). When the total actual time spent in the Medical Services Section is 3 hours or more, overtime will be computed on the basis of completed 1 hour segments plus an additional one-half hour of compensatory time for each completed work hour.
    • 5.Overtime worked as an extension of a tour will be credited in compensatory time at a straight time rate in completed 1 hour segments.
    • 6.A supervisor will normally not be required to work more than 4 hours on the first watch on change day if he has worked a full tour of duty on the third watch on the preceding day. If the supervisor is required to work more than 4 hours on a change day on the first watch, he will be credited with one-half hour compensatory time for each completed hour worked on the first watch on change day.
    • 7.Supervisory members who are required to appear in court during off duty hours as a result of being certified as a breath test operator will be compensated in the following manner: Court appearances during off duty hours will be computed at the rate of hour for hour with a minimum of 3 hours. When the total actual time spent in court is 3 hours or more, overtime will be computed on the basis of completed 1 hour segments plus an additional one-half hour of compensatory time for each completed work hour.
    • 8.Overtime hours worked during a voluntary tour of duty will be credited on an hour for hour basis.
    • 9.Supervisory members who are "regular day off" on a designated holiday will normally not be allowed to voluntarily work on a holiday. If a supervisor is required to work on a holiday the member will be compensated in accordance with Item III-B-2-b of the Department directive entitled "Payroll and Timekeeping - Holidays / Personal Days".
    • 10.One-half hour administrative overtime will be credited automatically for each tour of duty worked after the member has worked a minimum of four hours. No authorization or Overtime / Compensatory Time Report is required. Members attending training outside the Department (i.e., Northwestern University Traffic Institute, FBI Academy, etc.) will not be credited with one-half hour administrative overtime during the training period.
    • 11.Court appearances during off-duty hours will be credited at the rate of hour for hour, with a minimum of 3 hours. Appearances at more than one court on the same day will be credited at the rate of hour for hour, with a minimum of 3 hours when the total actual time spent in all the courts attended is 3 hours or less. When the total actual time spent in all the courts attended exceeds 3 hours, compensation will be computed on the basis of completed 1 hour segments.
    • 12.A member who is required to work a full tour of duty on a cancelled day off will be credited with 8 hours and a premium of 4 additional hours plus the one-half hour administrative overtime. A member who works more than an 8 hour tour of duty on a canceled day off will be credited for the completed hours worked plus an additional one-half hour for each overtime hour worked. A member who works less than a full tour of duty on a canceled day off will be credited with the number of hours actually worked plus an additional one-half hour compensatory time for each completed hour worked.
    • 13.Sworn supervisors who are assigned to work a minimum of a 12 hour tour of duty due to an emergency declared in writing by the Superintendent of Police will receive an additional one-half hour compensatory time for each hour worked beyond an eight hour tour of duty.
  • D.Overtime for civilian members covered by an AFSCME bargaining unit agreement will be credited in the following manner:
    • 1.Members not exempt from the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
      • a.Overtime worked during the normal work week will be credited at the rate of time and one-half, in payment or compensatory time, on the basis of completed 15 minute segments for time worked in excess of 40 hours in a calendar week. Hours worked beyond 35 hours but not in excess of 40 hours will be credited in compensatory time at the straight time rate on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
      • b.All work performed on Saturday or Sunday, when Saturday and Sunday are not part of the employee's workweek, will be credited at the rate of one and one-half times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment.
      • c.When employees whose days off are Saturday and Sunday have worked overtime on Saturday and Sunday, they will be credited at the rate of one and one-half times the regular hourly rate of pay for overtime worked on Saturday, in either compensatory time or payment, and they will be credited at the rate of two (2) times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment, for overtime worked on Sunday.
      • d.When employees whose regular days off are other than Saturday or Sunday are required to work on their days off, work on their sixth day will be credited at one and one-half times the regular hourly rate of pay and work on the seventh consecutive day will be credited at two (2) times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment for the seventh day.
        NOTE:
        The Chicago Police Department discontinues its current policy to pay at the rate of two (2) times for any employee who works overtime on a Sunday or the seventh day but did not work overtime on the previous day (Saturday or the sixth day).
    • 2.Members exempt from FLSA and the Illinois Minimum Wage Law

      Members exempt from FLSA and the Illinois Minimum Wage Law will not be eligible for overtime compensation. However, such employees will be given compensatory time on an hour by hour basis for all overtime worked.
  • E.Overtime for civilian members covered by the Unit II bargaining unit agreement will be credited at the rate of time and one-half, in payment or compensatory time, on the basis of completed 15 minute segments for time worked in excess of 40 hours in a calendar week. Hours worked beyond the regular duty hours but not in excess of 40 hours will be credited, in compensatory time, at the straight time rate on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
    • 1.All work performed on Saturday, when Saturday is not part of the employee's workweek, will be credited at the rate of two times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment.
    • 2.All work performed on Sunday, when Sunday is not part of the employee's workweek, will be credited at the rate of two times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment.
    • 3.When employees whose regular days off are other than Saturday or Sunday are required to work on their days off, work on their first scheduled day off shall be credited at one and one-half times the regular hourly rate of pay and work on the second scheduled day off shall be credited at two times the regular hourly rate of pay, in either compensatory time or payment.
  • F.Overtime for civilian members not covered by a bargaining unit agreement will be credited as follows:
    • 1.Members not exempt from FLSA

      Overtime will be credited at the rate of time and one-half, in compensatory time, on the basis of completed 15 minute segments for time worked in excess of 40 hours in a calendar week. Hours worked beyond 35 hours but not in excess of 40 hours will be credited in compensatory time at the straight time rate on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
    • 2.Members exempt from FLSA
      • a.Civilian members Grade 12 and G-4 and up are not compensated for overtime worked. Such members are eligible for administrative leave which will be granted at the discretion of the member's immediate command staff officer.
      • b.Civilian members Grade 11 and G-3 and below will be credited in compensatory time in completed one hour segments at the straight time rate for all hours worked in excess of 35 hours in a calendar week.
  • G.Overtime for probationary police officers (PPOs) will be credited in the following manner:
    • 1.Overtime earned will accrue in 15-minute increments once the PPO works at least eight (8) minutes in a 15-minute period.
    • 2.Overtime will be compensated at straight time for overtime hours of 171 or less worked during a FLSA pay cycle and credited in compensatory time only.
    • 3.Overtime hours worked in excess of 171 during a 28-day FLSA pay cycle will be calculated by the timekeeper in accordance with FLSA provisions and compensated in pay at the rate of time and one-half.
    • 4.Court appearances and CAPS meetings during off-duty hours will be credited at the rate of hour for hour, with a minimum of 3 hours. Appearances at more than one court on the same day will be credited at the rate of hour for hour, with a minimum of 3 hours when the total actual time spent in all the courts attended is 3 hours or less. When the total actual time spent in all the courts attended exceeds 3 hours, overtime will be accrued in 15-minute increments once the PPO works at least eight (8) minutes in a 15-minute period.
  • H.Command staff members (sworn and civilian) are not compensated for overtime worked. Such members are eligible for administrative leave which will be granted at the discretion of the bureau or division head as appropriate.
VII.Compensatory Time Use
  • A.Compensatory time off will be requested in 1 hours segments only.
  • B.The request for compensatory time off will be submitted on an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report.
    • 1.Requests to use compensatory time will be submitted to the member's watch commander / unit commanding officer at least twenty-four hours in advance. Watch commanders / unit commanding officers may make exceptions in emergency cases.
    • 2.Watch commanders / non-command staff unit commanding officers may not approve their own Overtime / Compensatory Time Report requests to use compensatory time. Such requests must be submitted to the member's immediate commanding officer for approval. Approval of the appropriate higher ranking command staff member will be required when the member's unit commanding officer or other command staff member in the chain of command is not available to approve the request.
      NOTE:
      When a district watch commander is authorized compensatory time use prior to the completion of his tour of duty and such compensatory time use requires a lieutenant to work out of grade, the lieutenant working out of grade must complete his normal tour of duty after being relieved by the succeeding watch commander.
  • C.A member will not request elective time off for the time and date on which the member is required to appear in court in a Department related matter.
  • D.A member appearing in court as a defendant in a non-duty related incident will be required to request the use of elective time if the court appearance is during his regular tour of duty. The member will not appear in uniform in these instances.
  • E.Members will not request nor will district/unit commanding officers authorize the granting of elective time when the requesting member would incur a negative elective time balance.
  • F.At the discretion of the unit commanding officer, a member may be permitted to use elective time to extend a furlough beyond the authorized extension, a full furlough (28 days), a furlough segment (14 days), or the compensatory time furlough.
  • G.Requests for the use of elective time which do not exceed fourteen consecutive days may be granted at the discretion of the watch commander as long as the number of days off is consistent with unit needs, the provisions of this directive and do not adversely affect Department operations. Requests for use of elective time which exceeds fourteen consecutive days off (including regular scheduled days-off) will be submitted through the chain of command to the respective bureau deputy superintendent's office for approval. This section does not apply to members applying for Disability Pension.
VIII.Holiday Compensation
Sworn and civilian members will be compensated for holidays in accordance with the policy and procedure outlined in the Department directive entitled "Payroll and Timekeeping - Holidays / Personal Days".
IX.Overtime / Compensatory Time Report
All overtime worked and any requests to use elective time will be reported on the Overtime Compensatory Time Report.
  • A.The Overtime / Compensatory Time Report consists of the following:
    • 1.Member Information
    • 2.Overtime Earned
    • 3.Reason for Working Overtime
    • 4.Request to use Compensatory Time / Personal Day / Vacation Day / Baby Furlough Day
    • 5.Required Explanation
    • 6.Overtime Authorized By
    • 7.Request for Compensatory Time / Payment
    • 8.Signatures
    • 9.Time Stamp Verification
    • 10.Time and One-Half Conversion Table.
  • B.The following information is required in the below indicated sections:
    • 1.Member Information Section
      • a.Name: Self-explanatory
      • b.Employee No: Self-explanatory
      • c.Present Watch: Enter the watch to which you were assigned when the overtime was worked or the compensatory time, personal day, baby furlough day or, if applicable, a vacation day is requested.
      • d.Day Off Group: Enter the day off group to which you were assigned when the overtime was worked or the compensatory time, baby furlough day, personal day or, if applicable, a vacation day is requested.
      • e.Unit No. Assignment: Enter the number of the unit to which you are assigned or detailed (the unit which maintains your Time and Attendance Records).
    • 2.Overtime Earned Section
      • a.Date Overtime Worked: Enter the actual date the overtime was worked. If the overtime worked overlaps two dates, enter both dates (e.g., 5-6 June 1994).
      • b.Day Of Week: Enter the day of the week the overtime was worked. If the overtime worked overlaps two days, enter both days (e.g., Mon-Tue).
      • c.Regular Duty Hours: Enter the regular duty hours for the date the overtime was earned.
      • d.Enter the time the overtime began and the time the overtime ended.
      • e.Actual Hours Total: Enter the total amount of actual overtime worked expressed in decimals (e.g., 15 minutes - .25, 45 minutes - .75, 1 hour and 30 minutes - 1.50, etc.).
      • f.Credited Hours Total: The member submitting the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report will leave this space blank. The Credited Hours Total box will be completed by the watch commander (or equivalent) who is present when the overtime is completed except when the member has earned overtime outside his unit of assignment (e.g., court, parade detail, off-duty arrest, etc.). In those instances, the Credited Hours Total box will be completed by the member's own watch commander (or equivalent).
      NOTE:
      A time and one-half conversion table has been provided on the reverse side of the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to assist in determining the correct number of hours in the Credited Hours Total Box.
    • 3.Reason For Working Overtime Section: The form lists eight categories of overtime. One of the boxes must be checked. In the event that the overtime encompasses more than one category, only the category showing the primary reason the overtime was worked will be checked.
      The categories are explained as follows:
      • a.Extension of Tour: Any overtime worked immediately preceding or immediately following a tour of duty.
      • b.Court Appearance: Any overtime worked for required off-duty attendance in Circuit Court, Federal Court, Grand Jury or at a Liquor Commission Hearing. The attendance must be required because of a duty related incident.
      • c.Day Off Group Change: Any overtime worked as a result of an officer's day off group being changed by the Department after change day, causing a loss of a regular day off.
      • d.Call Back: Any overtime worked as a result of an official assignment which does not immediately precede or follow an officer's regularly scheduled work hours, excluding court appearances; or who are required to report to the Medical Services Section.
      • e.Worked Regular Day Off (required): Any overtime worked as a result of an officer working his regular day off at the direction of a watch commander or unit commanding officer.
      • f.Special Event: Any overtime worked resulting from assignment to parades, details ,etc., by Department directives, including facsimile orders.
      • g.Worked Regular Day Off (voluntary): Any overtime worked voluntarily on a member's regular day off.
      • h.Other: Any overtime worked which does not fall into any of the above categories. Explanation is required.
    • 4.Request To Use Compensatory Time / Personal Day / BFD / V Day Section:
      • a.Date(s) Requested Off: Self explanatory. A separate report must be submitted for each date requested off, unless the dates are consecutive and within the same category.
      • b.From-To: Enter the time requested off, in one hour segments.
      • c.Total Hrs. Req. Off: Enter the total time requested off, in one hour segments.
      • d.Day(s) of Week: Insert an "X" in the day box on the line indicating the type of elective time requested. Vacation days not needed for the annual furlough or split furlough may be used individually or consecutively.
    • 5.Required Explanations Section: Information entered in this area is necessary to support the overtime earned.
      • a.Overtime Authorized By: The authorizing supervisor will sign their name, enter their star number, and PC Login ID Number.
      • b.Court Notification Record No: If notified to appear in court through the Automated Court Notification Program during off-duty hours, the notified member will enter the court notification record number.
      • c.RD No., CB No: In all applicable situations (investigations, arrests, etc.), this information MUST be entered.
      • d.Explain Assignment: Provide further explanation for working overtime (i.e., reason for extension of tour, reason for call back, type of special event, reason for working regular day off, etc.).
      • e.Court Key: Enter court key if court appearance is for a misdemeanor/ordinance complaint or a traffic citation.
      • f.Testified: Self explanatory.
      • g.Court Branch / Location, Charge, Docket No: Self explanatory.
      • h.Defendant: Self explanatory.
      • i.Disposition: Enter the disposition of the case when immediately available. If the case is continued, enter the continuance date if immediately available.
        NOTE:
        Officer's will not remain in court pending the final disposition of the case. Final disposition of the case is no longer required for completion of the Overtime / Compensatory Time Report or the Court Log.
    • 6.Request For Compensatory Time / Payment Section
      One of these boxes MUST be checked when the overtime earned report is submitted by a member.
    • 7.Signatures Section
      • a.Submitting Member: The submitting member will sign his name and star number and enter the date and time of his signature.
      • b.Approved By:
        • (1)The approving supervisor will be the supervisory member who can attest to the accuracy of the information submitted by the member.
        • (2)The supervisor’s signature, PC Login ID number, star number, date, and time will be obtained immediately upon the completion of the overtime.
        • (3)Requesting to Use Compensatory Time: An approving supervisor's signature is required as dictated by unit structure / policy.
      • c.Watch Commander / Unit C.O.:

        Reporting Overtime Earned: The watch commander, unit commanding officer, or equivalent, has the ultimate responsibility for reviewing and approving a report of overtime earned or request to use compensatory time. The approving watch commander / unit commanding officer will sign their name and enter their PC Login ID number, star number, and the date the report was approved.
        NOTE:
        Watch commanders are reminded of their responsibility to enter the proper “Credited Hours Total” as noted in Item IX-B-2-f.
      • d.Timekeeper: The timekeeper will enter his initials and the date when the requested action is posted on the Time and Attendance Record. No reports are to be accepted without the proper signatures and/or explanations.
    • 8.Time Stamp Verification Section: Members appearing in court branches during off-duty hours that are equipped with time stamp verification machines (e.g., Traffic Court, Criminal Court Building) must have their Overtime/Compensatory Time Report stamped by Court Liaison Section personnel.
      • a.IN: Members will provide their Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to Court Liaison Section personnel for time stamp verification prior to reporting to the court room they have been notified to appear in.
      • b.OUT: Members will provide their Overtime / Compensatory Time Report to Court Liaison Section personnel for time stamp verification immediately upon completion of the court appearance.
    • 9.Time and One-Half Conversion Table: A reference table providing a time and one-half conversion at quarter hour increments when earning overtime.
