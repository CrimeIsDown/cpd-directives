I.Purpose
This directive provides guidelines for the processing of a person who is seventeen years of age and arrested for a status offense

II.General Information
Effective 1 January 1993, Public Act 87-1154 brought Illinois law into compliance with the Federal Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention Act. This law provides that a person 17 years of age who is charged only with a status offense will not be securely detained in county jails and/or municipal lockups. This amendment to the Illinois Juvenile Court Act specifically addresses police processing of a person seventeen years of age who is charged with offenses such as underage drinking and underage possession of alcohol.

  • B.The procedures for processing a seventeen year old arrestee who has been charged with a non-status offense or a combination of a non-status offense and a status offense are unaffected by the provisions of this directive.
  • C.When a seventeen year old arrestee taken into custody for a status offense exhibits behavior/conduct which results in a member placing a non-status charge against the arrestee, the arrestee may be placed in secure custody.
  • D.A person seventeen years of age may be issued an ordinance complaint when the circumstances permit (Ref: Department directive entitled "Ordinance Complaint Form").
  • E.A person seventeen years of age charged with a status offense and ineligible for an ordinance complaint form will be:
    • 1.transported into a district facility for processing.
    • 2.escorted into the lockup for the exclusive purpose of fingerprinting and photographing if required.
    • 3.detained within the desk area or a nearby unlocked room pending the completion of the booking process and being made eligible to post a bond.
