Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-04-11
Elder Abuse Coordinated Response Initiative (CRI) - Pilot Program
Issue Date:11 June 2009Effective Date:11 June 2009
Rescinds:D09-21
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Elder Abuse Coordinated Response Initiative (CRI) – Pilot Program
  • B.outlines Department members’ responsibilities during the pilot program.
II.General Information
  • A.The Chicago Police Department and Metropolitan Family Services, an Elder Abuse Provider Agency (EAPA), are participating in a coordinated effort to respond to and target allegations of elder abuse.
  • B.The Metropolitan Family Services offers programs to provide and mobilize the services needed to strengthen families and communities including investigating allegations of elder abuse and assisting elderly crime victims.
  • C.The goal of this Pilot Program is to hold abusers accountable in the legal system for abuse, neglect, and/or financial exploitation of senior citizens.
III.Scope
The Elder Abuse CRI pilot program will:
  • A.involve reported allegations of elder abuse, neglect, and/or financial exploitation occurring within the geographical boundaries of:
    • 1.Area 2 beginning on 15 June 2009.
    • 2.Area 1 beginning on 06 Oct 2009.
  • B.end on 15 Dec 2010.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.Department members will continue to follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Senior Citizens at Risk” when investigating allegations of abuse, neglect, and/or financial exploitation of senior citizens.
  • B.When completing a case report for an elder abuse incident within the scope of this pilot program, the preliminary investigator will:
    • 1.fax a copy of the report to the Senior Citizen Services Section, CAPS Implementation Office, Attention: Elder Abuse CRI.
    • 2.provide a copy of the report to the Senior Service Officer assigned to the community policing office in the district of occurrence.
      NOTE:
      For reports generated using the Automated Incident Reporting Application (AIRA), the preliminary investigator will print an approved copy of the report to distribute as outlined above.
  • C.When notified of a reported incident within the scope of this pilot program, the Senior Service Officer from the district of occurrence will contact the Senior Citizen Services Section, for a follow-up consultation or to provide any additional information.
  • D.The Department will contribute to this program by participating in an interdisciplinary policy team and practice team.
    • 1.The policy team will:
      • a.recommend policy and practice protocols and provide oversight of the project.
      • b.consist of the following Department personnel or their appointed designee:
        • (1)Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol.
        • (2)Chief, Detective Division.
        • (3)Director, Research and Development Division.
        • (4)the commanding officer, Senior Citizen Services Section.
      • c.meet, as necessary, to provide overall guidance and direction to the project.
    • 2.The practice team will:
      • a.plan and carry out coordinated investigations and care planning.
      • b.consist of the following Department personnel:
        • (1)Senior Service Officer assigned to the community policing office in the district of occurrence, as designated by the appropriate district commander.
        • (2)a detective assigned to the Special Victims Unit, as designated by the Commander, Area 2, Detective Division.
        • (3)a member assigned to the Senior Citizen Services Section, as designated by the commanding officer, Senior Citizen Services Section.
      • c.meet, as necessary, to:
        • (1)conduct a thorough investigation of the referred incident.
        • (2)ensure appropriate access to witnesses, alleged victims, and alleged offenders.
        • (3)ensure the preservation and prevent the corruption of evidence.
        • (4)prevent the corruption of witness and alleged offenders’ statements.
  • E.Metropolitan Family Services will be responsible for contacting the commanding officer, Senior Citizen Services Section when an incident and/or victim are referred to be included in the pilot program.
  • F.Upon receiving notification from Metropolitan Family Services of a referral to the pilot program, the commanding officer, Senior Citizen Services Section will:
    • 1.schedule a practice team meeting in a timely manner.
    • 2.ensure the appropriate team members are identified and notified to appear at the practice team meeting, including:
      • a.the appropriate Department members,
      • b.the EAPA investigator and supervisor, and
      • c.an Elder Abuse Domestic Violence Court Advocate from the Senior Division of the Chicago Department of Family and Support Services.
  • G.When a Department member is notified of their required participation in a practice team meeting, the member will attend the meeting and:
    • 1.share investigative information and discuss the ongoing status of the investigation only as permitted by law and established Department policy.
      NOTE:
      Department members are reminded that the responsibility of investigative confidentiality still remains with the Department. However, the Elder Abuse, Neglect, and Exploitation Act specifically permits the EAPA to provide information to law enforcement.
    • 2.identify obstacles to prosecution and strategize to confront them, as appropriate.
    • 3.identify, develop, and implement an ongoing service plan for the victim.
V.Conflict Provision
None of the procedures in this directive will preclude a Department member from performing their duties and responsibilities as outlined in established Department directives including “Senior Citizens at Risk.”
VI.Evaluation
A pilot program evaluation committee will be established by the Department relative to this program. The committee will meet as needed and make recommendations to continue, expand, modify, or terminate this pilot program under the direction of the Director, Research and Development Division.
