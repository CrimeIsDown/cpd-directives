Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S11-07
Communicable Disease Training
Issue Date:07 August 1995Effective Date:27 August 1995
Rescinds:G95-02-01
Index Category:Training
I.Purpose
The purpose of this directive is to set forth training guidelines and responsibilities.
II.Education and Training Division
The Education and Training Division will:
  • A.provide all members in Risk Levels I and II categories, referenced in the Department directive entitled "Exposure Determination/Universal Precautions," with:
    • 1.initial bloodborne pathogens training, in accordance with OSHA Rules.
    • 2.supplemental training, whenever changes in the exposure control plan occur.
    • 3.annual refresher training on blood borne pathogens.
  • B.provide training that will include the following elements:
    • 1.the OSHA standard for bloodborne pathogens (29 CFR 1910.1030);
    • 2.general explanations of the epidemiology and symptoms of blood borne diseases;
    • 3.the modes of transmission of bloodborne diseases;
    • 4.the Department's Exposure Control Plan;
    • 5.the appropriate methods for recognizing tasks and other activities which may involve exposure to blood and other potentially infectious materials;
    • 6.the use and limitations of methods that will prevent or reduce exposure, including appropriate engineering controls, work practices, and personal protective equipment (PPE)
      Personal Protection Equipment (PPE)
      X
      Specialized equipment including breathing apparatus, articles of clothing, and functional equipment issued by the Department which are intended to protect the member from limited exposure to harmful hazards and substances.
      ;
    • 7.information on the types, proper use, location, removal, handling, decontamination and disposal of PPE;
    • 8.information on the Hepatitis B Vaccine;
    • 9.the procedure to follow if an exposure incident occurs.
  • C.maintain training records.
III.Human Resources Division
The Human Resources Division will be responsible for tracking members with Risk Level I and II assignments.
IV.Unit Commanding Officers
Unit commanding officers will ensure that all members under their command are scheduled to receive the required training.
