Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D14-03
Domestic Violence Assessment - Pilot Program
Issue Date:25 April 2014Effective Date:01 May 2014
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of the implementation of the Domestic Violence Assessment Pilot Program.
  • B.introduces the:
    • 1.revised Domestic Incident Notice (DIN) [CPD-11.443 (Rev. 4/14)].
    • 2.Chicago Police Officer's Guide to Domestic Violence Incidents (CPD-11.811) (Palm Card).
    • 3.Domestic Violence Assessment form (CPD-11.812).
II.Scope
The pilot program is effective 01 May 2014 and will continue until further notice in the 014th District.
III.General Information
  • A.The goals of the pilot program are to:
    • 1.break the cycle of domestic violence through a coordinated response involving law enforcement, prosecutors, and domestic violence service providers.
    • 2.have an incident report for every domestic violence call for service.
    • 3.ensure completed case reports contain complete incident information to assist in evidence-based prosecutions of domestic violence offenders.
    • 4.develop a proactive response to domestic violence incidents where there may be an elevated risk for harm.
  • B.The Domestic Violence Assessment form has been adapted to assess whether a domestic violence victim may be at an elevated risk for harm.
IV.Procedures
  • A.Preliminary investigating officers will respond to, investigate, and report all domestic incidents in accordance with Department directives entitled "Domestics Incidents" and "Preliminary Investigations."
    • 1.Department members will issue a Domestic Incident Notice (DIN) (CPD-11.443) to the complainant/victim as outlined in Department directive entitled "Domestic Incident Notice," explaining the contents of the DIN and sources of assistance which are available, including the City of Chicago's toll-free confidential 24-hour Domestic Violence Help Line (877-863-6338; TTY: 877-863-6339).
    • 2.In addition to the Records Division (RD) number and the reporting officer's information, Department members will record on the DIN the beat of occurrence for the reported domestic incident and whether the Domestic Violence Assessment Protocol was triggered.
  • B.If a domestic incident occurred and the offender is not on-scene:
    • 1.the investigating Department member will immediately send a flash message of the offender's description and offense.
    • 2.a field supervisor in the district of occurrence will ensure special attention is given to the household and that the information is disseminated to continue the special attention into the following watch.
  • C.If during the investigation it is determined that a case report WILL NOT be generated:
    • 1.the investigating Department member will notify their immediate supervisor and inform them of their investigation and the decision not to complete a case report.
      • a.If their immediate supervisor approves not to complete a case report, the investigating member will complete the "Incident Information" section of the Domestic Violence Assessment form (CPD-11.812), indicating the supervisor notification and approval, and submit the form to their supervisor for approval.
        NOTE:
        The investigating members WILL NOT complete the "Assessment" or "Protocol" sections of the form.
      • b.If their immediate supervisor indicates that the completion of a case report is necessary, the investigating member will complete the appropriate case report and follow the procedures outlined in Item IV-D of this directive.
    • 2.the investigating member's supervisor will, based on the facts of the incident, determine if the completion of a case report is warranted and be prepared to justify their decision.
  • D.If during the investigation it is determined that a case report will be generated, the investigating Department member will:
    • 1.complete the appropriate case report, using the Chicago Police Officer's Guide to Domestic Violence Incidents (CPD-11.811) (Palm Card) as a guide.
      • a.The case report will include complete incident information to assist in evidence-based prosecution, including the documentation of any visible injuries, out-cry witnesses, statements made by the victim and/or alleged offender, and other pertinent information as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Domestic Incidents."
      • b.The narrative of the case report will include whether or not the Domestic Violence Assessment Protocol was triggered in accordance with the Pilot Program.
    • 2.identify the crime scene and determine whether there is any evidence present. If there is visible injury to the victim or physical evidence of the crime which requires processing, Department members will notify their supervisor and request an evidence technician, following the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Crime Scene Protection and Processing."
    • 3.complete the "Incident Information" and "Assessment" sections of the Domestic Violence Assessment form (CPD-11.812).
    • 4.based on the victim's responses or the observations of the investigating member, determine whether the assessment triggers the Department's Domestic Violence Assessment Protocol.
      • a.If the protocol is triggered, the investigating Department member will complete the "Protocol" section of the assessment form and:
        • (1)advise the victim of the assessment, the possible risk or harm, and the sources of assistance that are available.
        • (2)advise the victim to call the City of Chicago's toll-free confidential 24-hour Domestic Violence Help Line (877-863-6338; TTY: 877-863-6339).
          NOTE:
          Department members will indicate on the assessment form if the victim contacted the Domestic Violence Help Line.
        • (3)notify their immediate supervisor of the assessment and the triggered protocol.
        • (4)notify the Bureau of Detectives, Area North, by phone at 312-744-8200, of the assessment and the triggered protocol.
        • (5)as soon as practical but prior to the end of their tour of duty, forward the completed Domestic Violence Assessment form to the Bureau of Detectives, Area North, via email (630DVForms@chicagopolice.org).
          NOTE:
          Department members may use a network connected multi-purpose copier (e.g., Xerox Workcentre) to scan and email the Bureau of Detectives Area North.
        • (6)submit the completed assessment form to their supervisor for approval.
      • b.If the protocol is not triggered, the investigating Department member will:
        • (1)not complete the "Protocol" section of the assessment form.
        • (2)submit the completed assessment form to their supervisor for approval.
  • E.District supervisors will:
    • 1.review case reports submitted for domestic violence incidents for completeness and thoroughness to assist in evidence-based prosecutions of domestic violence offenders.
      • a.Supervisors may use the Chicago Police Officer's Guide to Domestic Violence Incidents (CPD-11.811) (Palm Card) as a guide to assist in the review of the submitted domestic violence case reports.
      • b.When the protocol has been triggered, the reviewing supervisor will ensure the case report is reviewed and approved as soon as practical in order to ensure the dissemination of information for the follow-up investigation.
    • 2.review, and if appropriate, approve submitted Domestic Violence Assessment forms to ensure the proper notification protocol was followed.
    • 3.ensure approved Domestic Violence Assessment forms are forwarded to the District Review Office daily.
  • F.District review officers will:
    • 1.compile the submitted Domestic Violence Assessment forms daily and forward, prior to 0900 hours, a copy of the approved assessment forms via email to the:
      • a.Research and Development Division, RDAnalysis@chicagopolice.org.
      • b.Office of the Cook County State's Attorney, DV.info@cookcountyil.gov.
      NOTE:
      District review officers may use a network connected multi-purpose copier (e.g., Xerox Workcentre) to scan and email the assessment forms, as a group, to the above recipients.
    • 2.retain the Domestic Violence Assessment forms (CPD-11.812) in chronological order until the final evaluation of the pilot program is complete.
      NOTE:
      The Research and Development Division will announce the continuing records retention schedule for the assessment forms after the evaluation of the pilot program is complete.
  • G.The Bureau of Detectives will retain the forwarded Domestic Violence Assessment forms and the associated emails within the 630DVForms@chicagopolice.org mailbox until the final evaluation of the pilot program is complete.
V.Follow-up Investigative Responsibility
The Bureau of Detectives is responsible for conducting the follow-up investigations on criminal domestic violence incidents and implementing the Domestic Violence Assessment Protocol based on the responses indicated on the assessment forms.
VI.Program Evaluation
  • A.A pilot program evaluation committee will be established by the Department relative to this program. The committee will meet as needed and make recommendations to continue, expand, modify, or terminate this pilot program under the direction of the Chief, Bureau of Patrol.
  • B.The Research and Development Division will compile a report to determine the effectiveness of the pilot program and provide the report results to the evaluation committee. This report will evaluate domestic incident response and operational matters, including, but not limited to the number of domestic incidents responses, assessments completed, protocols triggered, and case reports generated.
  • C.The Chief, Bureau of Patrol will review the evaluation committee report and make a determination to continue, expand, modify, or terminate this pilot program.
