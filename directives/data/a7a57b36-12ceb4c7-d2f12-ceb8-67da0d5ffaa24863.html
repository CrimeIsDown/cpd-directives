Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-05
Dependent, Neglected, or Abandoned Children Coming Under Department Control
Issue Date:31 December 1998Effective Date:01 January 1999
Rescinds:G98-11-05
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures for processing dependent, neglected, or abandoned children who come under Department control.
II.Responsibilities
  • A.In child abuse or neglect incidents the preliminary investigating officer will:
    • 1.take protective custody of the victim and render assistance to the victim by providing protection and medical treatment, as necessary.
    • 2.determine the relationship between the offender and the child/victim in order to determine the proper offense classification and investigative responsibility.
    • 3.prepare the appropriate case report to record the facts of the incident and document the required notifications to:
      • a.the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC).
      • b.the member's supervisor.
      • c.the Youth Investigations Section.
        The Youth Investigations Section is responsible for the investigation and administrative processing of incidents classified as Offenses Involving Children:
        • (1)Dependency of a child.
        • (2)Child abductions when the offender is a parent or when the offender is in violation of a valid court order of custody.
        • (3)Missing/found persons.
        • (4)Offenses of physical and/or sexual abuse of a child under 18 years of age by a family member.
        • (5)Offenses of physical and/or sexual abuse of a child in an institutional setting (day-care centers, schools, other child care facilities).
        • (6)Offenses of physical and/or sexual abuse by a caretaker, of a child under 18 years of age, within the child's home (i.e., babysitter).
      • d.the Department of Children and Family Services (DCFS).
      • e.the appropriate Detective Division Area.
    • 4.request the services of an evidence technician, if necessary, noting the request in the appropriate box of the case report.
  • B.In dependency cases, the preliminary investigating officer will:
    • 1.render assistance to the victim by providing protection and medical treatment as necessary.
    • 2.determine the circumstances contributing to the dependent status of the minor child.
      • a.If the dependent status has resulted from non-criminal inability of the parent, guardian or custodian to provide basic care:
        • (1)prepare a General Offense Case Report (Primary Classification: Offense Involving Children (OIC); Secondary Classification: Dependency).
        • (2)contact the Youth Investigations Section, explaining the pertinent details of the situation.
        • (3)notify DCFS to initiate emergency assistance.
      • b.If the dependent status has resulted from a "lockout" (the child is not permitted by his parents to remain in the home):
        • (1)take protective custody of the child.
        • (2)notify the Youth Investigations Section for assignment of an investigator to interview the parents and the child.
        • (3)if attempts to reunite the parents and child fail, prepare a General Offense Case Report (Primary Classification: OIC; Secondary Classification: Neglect).
        • (4)turn the child over to the assigned investigator for follow-up investigation and intervention by DCFS.
  • C.Officers arresting a parent/guardian for a crime other than abuse or neglect will attempt to find temporary alternative placement of a minor child with a relative, as directed by the parent. If this is not possible, the officer will transport the child to the district station to seek placement, with the assistance of the Youth Investigations Section, through DCFS.
