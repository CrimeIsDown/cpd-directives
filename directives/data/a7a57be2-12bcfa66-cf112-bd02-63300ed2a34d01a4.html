Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S09-08-01
CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application
Issue Date:11 January 2010Effective Date:12 January 2010
Rescinds:S10-03-01
Index Category:Information Management
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application.
  • B.delineates the procedures and responsibilities of Department members to accept, assign, address, update, and close CLEARpath Community Concerns submitted by residents of Chicago.
II.General Information
  • A.The CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application:
    • 1.is a proactive problem solving strategy encouraging residents of the City of Chicago to identify community concerns and inform the Department of chronic issues in order to reduce crime and fear within their communities.
    • 2.has the capability to assign community concerns to specialized units within the Department for identification and investigation of neighborhood problems.
  • B.In an effort to automate and streamline the process, the CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application process, from assigning to closing the community concern, is virtually paperless.
  • C.Members of the community that:
    • 1.submit community concerns via the CLEARpath Community Concerns Webportal:
      • a.will be issued a unique control number and private pin number that will allow the resident(s) to inquire about the status of their concern(s).
      • b.containing lewd, obscene, or derogatory content will not have their concern accepted or investigated.
    • 2.provide their e-mail address upon submitting a community concern will be automatically updated upon every status change of the concern.
  • D.Community concerns:
    • 1.that are similar in nature and in the same geographic location will be associated to one primary community concern to prevent duplicate investigations.
    • 2.can only be extended once during the initial seven (7) day period for an additional seven (7) days (i.e., if the community concern is extended on day three [3] during the original investigational period, seven [7] days will be added to the remaining days from the original investigational period, thus making the new due date of the community concern in eleven [11] days from date of the extension request).
    • 3.that have been extended:
      • a.cannot be extended an additional seven (7) days until the investigation period reaches the due date.
      • b.require justification.
  • E.Access to the CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application shall be subject to any privileges and limitations as delineated in the Department directive entitled, “Access to Computer Data, Dissemination and Retention of Computer Data.”
  • F.Members unable to access the CLEARpath Community Concerns Computer Application on a Department computer may telephone the Help Desk for assistance.
III.Procedures
  • A.Sworn, district community policing officers assigned to monitor CLEARpath Community Concerns will:
    • 1.access the application through the CLEARpath Worklist Dashboard at least once every tour of duty to review submitted community concerns to determine the validity of submissions and recommend assignments to the most appropriate unit(s) listed within the application.
      NOTE:
      Sworn officers assigned to the District’s community policing office will have thirty-six (36) hours to accept / not accept a community concern submitted through the CLEARpath Webportal.
    • 2.enter community concerns related by members of the community attending monthly beat meetings.
    • 3.electronically recommend:
      • a.all building related community concerns to the Troubled Buildings Section (Unit 142).
      • b.all other community concerns to the most appropriate unit(s) listed within the application.
    • 4.immediately notify their sworn supervisor in the event a community concern is of a criminal nature requiring a case report (e.g. threats, reports of abuse, harassment).
  • B.District community policing supervisors will:
    • 1.access the application through the CLEARpath Worklist Dashboard, at least once every tour of duty, to review and approve recommended assignments of community concerns as determined by CAPS officers under their command.
      NOTE:
      Community policing supervisors will have forty-eight (48) hours to approve or re-assign community concerns.
    • 2.re-assign incorrectly assigned community concerns.
    • 3.monitor the community concerns in the CLEARpath Worklist Dashboard which have not been addressed.
    • 4.close community concerns which are criminal in nature and require immediate action by:
      • a.determining the necessary case report to document the incident.
      • b.directing a sworn Department member to conduct a preliminary investigation.
      • c.ensuring the proper notifications are made.
    • 5.complete the community concern closing summary, when appropriate, and submit it to the watch commander for final approval.
  • C.Watch commanders and unit commanding officers designated to address community concerns will:
    • 1.access the application through the CLEARpath Worklist Dashboard at least once every tour of duty to monitor, review, and resolve community concerns assigned to their respective watch/unit.
    • 2.re-assign incorrectly assigned community concerns.
    • 3.assign a supervisor to designate the appropriate members to take action to resolve the community concern within seven (7) days of assignment by the district community policing supervisors.
      NOTE:
      Units assigned to investigate community concerns must close the investigation within seven (7) days after assignment. In the event the assigned unit is conducting a long term investigation or cannot complete the investigation within the seven (7) day time frame (i.e., street corner conspiracy, gathering intelligence, etc.), the unit supervisor, or their designee, will have the option to extend the investigation in additional seven (7) day increments.
    • 4.update and maintain community concerns within the application that require investigation past the seven (7) day time-frame.
      NOTE:
      All extensions will require justification.
    • 5.ensure appropriate action was taken and complete the community concern closing summary.
  • D.Assigned supervisors will:
    • 1.access the application through the CLEARpath Worklist Dashboard at least once every tour of duty to monitor if they are responsible for designating subordinate members to complete community concerns assigned by their watch commander/unit commanding officer.
    • 2.assign the appropriate members to take action to resolve the community concern within seven (7) days.
    • 3.change the status to “Investigation Complete” if they are the last supervisor to document and submit the action(s) taken by the designated members to resolve the community concern.
    • 4.document and submit the action(s) taken by the members assigned to resolve the community concern to the watch commander/unit commanding officer for final approval.
      NOTE:
      Non-district units will not require final approval by the watch commander.
  • E.The appropriate area deputy chief, or their designee will:
    • 1.access the application at least once every tour of duty to review community concerns assignments.
    • 2.notify the appropriate unit commanding officer in the event a community concern has:
      • a.been re-assigned over three (3) times and remains unassigned.
      • b.not been resolved and closed by the assigned investigating unit within the seven (7) day time-frame without an authorized extension.
