Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S11-02
Field Training and Evaluation Program
Issue Date:21 November 2001Effective Date:22 November 2001
Rescinds:S01-10
Index Category:Training
I.Purpose
This directive delineates the Field Training and Evaluation Program.
II.Administration
The Field Training and Evaluation Program will be administered through a combined effort of the Education and Training Division and the Bureau of Patrol. The responsibility for the program will remain with the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, in partnership with the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol. Any questions concerning the procedures to be followed in this directive should be directed to the Field Training and Evaluation Section, Education and Training Division.
III.Objective
The primary objective of the Field Training and Evaluation Program is to ensure that all police recruits are well trained and thoroughly evaluated to ensure that only competent, motivated, and ethical individuals become Chicago Police Officers.
IV.Reports and Related Forms
The following materials will continue to be used in conjunction with the program:
  • A.Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual.
  • B.Field Training and Evaluation Program Performance Checklist.
  • C.Field Training and Evaluation Program Daily Observation Report (CPD-63.210).
  • D.Field Training and Evaluation Program Training Cycle Summary Report (CPD-63.211).
  • E.Field Training and Evaluation Program Final Summary Report (CPD-63.212).
  • F.Field Training and Evaluation Program Remedial Summary Report (CPD-63.213).
  • G.Field Training and Evaluation Program Narrative Continuation Report (CPD-63.214).
  • H.Field Training and Evaluation Program Review Board Request (CPD-63.215).
  • I.Field Training and Evaluation Program Review Board Notice (CPD-63.216).
  • J.Field Training and Evaluation Program Field Training Officer and Program Critique (CPD-63.217).
  • K.Field Training and Evaluation Program Probationary Police Officer Assignment Log (CPD-63.218).
  • L.Field Training Officer Evaluation (CPD-63.219).
V.Duties and Responsibilities
  • A.Field Training and Evaluation Review Board
    The Field Training and Evaluation Review Board is responsible for maintaining the standards of the Field Training and Evaluation Program and ensuring thorough and consistent evaluation of probationary police officers and field training officers.
    • 1.The board will be comprised of an equal number of Department members from the Education and Training Division and Bureau of Patrol. The board will consist of a minimum of the following:
      • a.Three designees of the Assistant Deputy Superintendent of the Education and Training Division:
        • (1)A lieutenant assigned to the Education and Training Division.
        • (2)A sergeant assigned to the Education and Training Division.
        • (3)A field training and evaluation liaison officer.
      • b.Three designees of the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol:
        • (1)A lieutenant assigned to the Bureau of Patrol Administration Group.
        • (2)A sergeant assigned to the Bureau of Patrol.
        • (3)An FTO assigned to district law enforcement.
      • c.A lieutenant assigned to Bureau of Professional Standards, as designated by the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Professional Standards.
    • 2.When requested, it will be the responsibility of the board to review a PPOs performance during the entire probationary period. The board will convene if any substandard performance or unethical or problematic behavior is identified at any time during the probationary period. When it is determined that separation is the appropriate course of action, the board will submit its recommendation through the appropriate chain of command to the Superintendent of Police.
    • 3.The board will review the semiannual evaluation of field training officers (FTOs) and make recommendations through the appropriate chain of command.
    • 4.All board meetings will be convened at the direction of the Assistant Deputy Superintendent of the Education and Training Division.
  • B.Education and Training Division
    • 1.The Education and Training Division will:
      • a.provide training to FTOs and supervisors.
      • b.conduct the semiannual evaluation of FTOs and submit it to the board for recommendations.
      • c.publish, approve, and maintain the Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual.
      • d.distribute the Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual to all appropriate Department personnel involved in the Field Training and Evaluation Program.
      • e.inform the appropriate Department personnel of any changes and updated information to the manual.
    • 2.The Assistant Deputy Superintendent of the Education and Training Division will ensure that the Field Training and Evaluation Manual will provide guidelines, supporting documents, and procedural information consistent with this directive.
  • C.Field Training and Evaluation Liaison Officer
    The liaison officer will:
    • 1.serve as the Field Training and Evaluation Review Board secretary.
    • 2.coordinate the flow of information pertaining to the Field Training and Evaluation Program.
    • 3.track the evaluation process and maintain all reports and related forms.
  • D.Probationary Police Officer
    For the purposes of this directive, a PPO is a candidate who has successfully completed the Academic Training Phase and has entered the Field Training and Evaluation Program. The PPO must successfully complete the field training and evaluation cycles to become field qualified.
    • 1.PPOs will rotate through three field-training cycles.
      NOTE:
      Changes in watch assignments can be made at any time.
    • 2.If remedial training is requested, an additional cycle may be added.
    • 3.With the approval of the Field Training and Evaluation Review Board, at least one of the training cycles may be conducted in an alternate district to provide the PPO with a wider range of experience. The assignment to an alternate district is not mandatory.
    • 4.At the conclusion of the training cycles, the PPO will critique the Field Training and Evaluation Program and submit the completed Field Training and Evaluation Program Field Training Officer and Program Critique to the Education and Training Division, Attention: Field Training and Evaluation Program.
  • E.Field Training Officer
    • 1.Field training officers will maintain their Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual and keep it current.
    • 2.The assigned FTO will:
      • a.evaluate the performance of the PPO.
      • b.complete a Field Training and Evaluation Program Daily Observation Report for each tour worked with a PPO. This report will be submitted by the next tour of duty worked.
      • c.at the end of a PPOs first and second training cycles, complete a Field Training and Evaluation Program Training Cycle Summary Report.
      • d.at the end of a PPOs third training cycle, complete a Field Training and Evaluation Program Final Summary Report.
      • e.perform other duties and adhere to the standards as outlined in the Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual.
    • 3.The FTO assigned to a PPOs third training cycle will review the PPOs Field Training and Evaluation Program Training Cycle Summary Reports for guidance and make a recommendation regarding the PPOs field- qualification rating.
      NOTE:
      If at the end of a PPOs third training cycle a remedial training cycle is recommended or a board is requested, an immediate telephone notification will be made to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division. The FTO will also submit a written request to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, Attention: Field Training and Evaluation Program.
    • 4.At the conclusion of a remedial-training cycle approved by the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, the FTO will submit a Field Training and Evaluation Program Remedial Summary Report. This report will either indicate that the PPO be "field qualified" in all ten rating areas or request that the Field Training and Evaluation Review Board be convened to determine the future status of the PPO.
  • F.Field Training and Evaluation Sergeant
    Each district commander will designate at least one sergeant on each watch as the field training and evaluation sergeant.
    • 1.Each designated sergeant will:
      • a.monitor the progress of PPOs as well as the performance of the FTOs on a continual basis.
      • b.approve the completed Field Training and Evaluation Program Daily Observation Reports.
      • c.approve the Field Training and Evaluation Training Cycle Summary Reports when submitted at the conclusion of the training cycles.
      • d.complete the semiannual Field Training Officer Evaluation.
      • e.perform other duties as outlined in the Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual (e.g., counseling PPOs).
    • 2.Sergeants will ensure the confidentiality of the information maintained at the district level.
  • G.Field Training and Evaluation Lieutenant
    Each district commander will designate one lieutenant on each watch as the field training and evaluation lieutenant.
    • 1.Each designated lieutenant will:
      • a.monitor the progress of PPOs as well as the performance of the FTOs on a continual basis.
      • b.complete the semiannual Field Training Officer Evaluation.
      • c.perform other duties as outlined in the Field Training and Evaluation Program Manual (e.g., counseling PPOs).
    • 2.The field training and evaluation lieutenant on the 2nd watch will be responsible for:
      • a.maintaining the Field Training and Evaluation Program Probationary Police Officer Assignment Log.
      • b.the retention of the original Field Training and Evaluation Program Daily Observation Reports.
      • c.the retention of the original Field Training and Evaluation Program Training Cycle Summary Reports.
      • d.upon conclusion of the Field Training and Evaluation Phase, submit all original reports pertaining to the training of the PPO and the Field Training and Evaluation Program Performance Checklist as a final packet to the district commander for approval.
    • 3.Lieutenants will ensure the confidentiality of the information maintained at the district level.
  • H.Watch Commander
    The watch commander will ensure:
    • 1.the assignment of PPOs and FTOs during a PPOs field training and evaluation cycles in the following manner:
      • a.Each PPO will be assigned to work with an FTO. If operationally possible, each cycle will be conducted with a different FTO to allow the PPO to experience different policing styles.
      • b.In the absence of the assigned FTO (e.g., furlough, compensatory time, medical) a PPO will be assigned to:
        • (1)another FTO, or
        • (2)a police officer assigned out of grade, only if no other FTO is available.
    • 2.that PPOs will not be assigned to desk or lockup duties during the field training and evaluation period.
    • 3.that all FTOs and PPOs assigned to the watch are performing at acceptable standards.
    • 4.that the assigned field training and evaluation supervisors on the watch are performing the duties as outlined in Items VI-F and VI-G of this directive.
  • I.District Commander
    • 1.The district commander will ensure that:
      • a.FTOs are engaging in field training when there are PPOs detailed to the district for their training cycles.
      • b.the final packet of all reports pertaining to the training of a PPO and the Field Training and Evaluation Program Performance Checklist are submitted at the conclusion of a PPOs third training cycle.
      • c.upon approving the final packet, the final packet is forwarded to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, Attention: Field Training and Evaluation Program.
      • d.the semiannual Field Training Officer Evaluations are submitted to the Education and Training Division on the due dates of 01 April and 01 October.
    • 2.When deficiencies are noted in the performance of an FTO or when disciplinary action is taken against an FTO, the district commander will submit notification in the form of a To-From-Subject report through the chain of command to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, Attention: Field Training and Evaluation Program.
      NOTE:
      The Field Training and Evaluation Manual will be referenced for guidance under this program.
VI.Evaluation Process of Probationary Police Officers
  • A.A PPO must obtain a field qualified rating in all ten rating areas.
  • B.If at any time during the training cycles, acceptable performance of a PPO has not been demonstrated, the FTO will request that the Field Training and Evaluation Review Board be convened to review the PPOs performance.
  • C.If a PPO displays an inability to successfully complete the program due to undesirable traits or behavior, a supervisor will:
    • 1.make an immediate phone notification to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division.
    • 2.submit a To-From-Subject report through the chain of command to the Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, Attention: Field Training and Evaluation Program.
      NOTE:
      Undesirable traits or behaviors may include, but are not limited to, the PPO being the subject of a Complaint Register investigation, medical roll abuse, or disciplinary action.
  • D."Field Qualified" is not the only determining factor as to whether a PPO is qualified to serve as a Chicago police officer. In the event problematic behavior or performance is documented during the probationary period and a review board is requested, the board will convene. The board will review the totality of the PPOs performance throughout the probationary period and make a determination as to whether or not the PPO is qualified to become a Chicago police officer. Any recommendation will be forwarded by the board through the chain of command to the Superintendent of Police.
