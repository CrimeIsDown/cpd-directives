Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U04-01-03
Prescribed Uniform / Equipment Items: Police Officers and Sergeants
Issue Date:24 November 1998Effective Date:25 November 1998
Rescinds:G98-10-03
Index Category:Uniform and Personal Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive sets forth the prescribed uniform / equipment items for police officers and sergeants.
II.General Information
  • A.For purposes of this order, the rank of police officer is synonymous with all sworn career service titles below the rank of sergeant.
  • B.All police officers and sergeants are required to own and maintain the prescribed uniform/equipment items listed in this directive.
  • C.The authorized wearing/use of uniform / equipment items may be found in this directive or in the Department's Uniform and Equipment Specifications.
  • D.Winter uniform garments and equipment will be worn/used from 1 November through 30 April.
  • E.Spring/fall uniform garments and equipment will be worn/used from 1 May through 31 May and 1 October through 31 October.
  • F.Summer uniform garments and equipment will be worn/used from 1 June through 30 September.
  • G.On duty members, unless otherwise specified, will use only those uniform/equipment items listed in the Department's Uniform and Equipment Specifications or this directive.
III.Prescribed Uniform / Equipment Items
  • A.Clothing
    • 1.Blouse - 100% Wool (dress uniform)
    • 2.Cap - 100% Wool, Round Crown
    • 3.Cap - Cloth, Round Crown (field uniform)
    • 4.Coat - 100% Wool Reefer (dress uniform - police officers; alternate field uniform - sergeants)
    • 5.Footwear - Military Oxford
    • 6.Gloves - White
    • 7.Jacket - cloth (field uniform)
    • 8.Necktie
    • 9.Shirt - Long and Short Sleeve, (Police Officer - Light Blue, Sergeants - White)
    • 10.Socks (black)
    • 11.Trousers - 100% Wool.
  • B.Uniform Accessories
    • 1.Ammunition pouch and/or magazine case / speedloader
    • 2.Baton holder
    • 3.Belt - Trouser
    • 4.Belt - Equipment
      NOTE:
      When assigned to uniformed field duties, members will wear the equipment belt in conjunction with the trouser belt. The equipment belt will be worn so that it completely covers the trouser belt. Additionally, it is recommended that a minimum of four belt keepers be worn with the two-belt system: one in front of the holster, one behind the holster, one behind the magazine pouch or speed loader pouch, and one at the back of the belt system.
    • 5.Handcuff case (when handcuffs are carried)
    • 6.Holster
    • 7.Insignia - Cap shield, star, field training officer patch insignia (field training officers only), chevrons (sergeants only)
    • 8.Nameplate
    • 9.Patches - CPD and City Flag shoulder patches
    • 10.Personal aerosol OC chemical device holder
    • 11.Portable radio transceiver attachment
    • 12.Raincoat/cap cover
    • 13.Service bar/star
    • 14.Unit assignment designator.
    • 15.Traffic Safety Vest
  • C.Equipment
    • 1.Ammunition - Department authorized/issued
    • 2.Ball point pen (black ink)
    • 3.Baton - Department-issued
    • 4.Body Armor - Department-issued/authorized
    • 5.Handcuffs - (members hired after 1 December 1991)
    • 6.Helmet - Department-issued
    • 7.Firearm
      • a.Revolver (members hired on or before 1 December 1991).
      • b.Semi-automatic pistol (members hired after 1 December 1991).
    • 8.Prescribed flashlight
    • 9.Personal aerosol OC chemical device.
  • D.Miscellaneous
    • 1.Baton tassel (dress uniform - sergeants only)
    • 2.Beat Binder (Portfolio)
    • 3.Department Photo Identification Card
    • 4.Watch
    • 5.Whistle.
