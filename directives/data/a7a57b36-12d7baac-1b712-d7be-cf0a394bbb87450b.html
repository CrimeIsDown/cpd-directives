Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E02-02-07
Bereavement Leave - Domestic Partners
Issue Date:08 August 1994Effective Date:08 August 1994
Rescinds:D94-32
Index Category:Payroll and Timekeeping
I.PURPOSE
This directive informs Department members of the Bereavement Leave for Domestic Partners.
II.GENERAL INFORMATION
Effective 15 December 1993, the City Council of the City of Chicago amended the annual Appropriation Ordinance so as to provide bereavement leave for domestic partners. This benefit is now available to all City of Chicago employees; this includes all employees covered by a collective bargaining agreement. Employees represented by the American Federation of State County and Municipal Employees (AFSCME) should be treated in accordance with collective bargaining agreement, specifically Article 11, Section 11.1, Side letter 1, item number 3 of the agreement which addresses the issue regarding bereavement of a “domestic partner.”
III.DEFINITION
Domestic partners are defined as two persons regardless of their gender, who have a close personal relationship, sharing the same regular and permanent residence for at least six months; are each eighteen years of age or older, not married to anyone, not related by blood closer than would bar marriage in the State of Illinois, and are each other’s sole domestic partner, responsible for each others common welfare and jointly sharing their financial responsibilities.
IV.PROCEDURES
Any Department member choosing to utilize this benefit must register their domestic partner with the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources using the Domestic Partner Registration Form. This Department of Human Resources form can be obtained from the unit timekeeper or the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources. All information on the Domestic Partner Registration Form is CONFIDENTIAL and is not subject to casual inquiry.
  • A.Bereavement leave will be granted to Department members in accordance with the following guidelines.
    • 1.Sworn members - Leave with pay as a result of the death of a domestic partner will be granted to sworn members not to exceed three consecutive days, including regularly scheduled days off, immediately following the death of a domestic partner.
    • 2.Civilian members (represented by a union) - In the event of the death of a domestic partner said employee shall be entitled to a paid leave of absence up to a maximum of three consecutive days including the day of the funeral.
    • 3.Civilian members (non-union) - Leave with pay as a result of the death of a domestic partner may be granted to employees not to exceed three consecutive days following the death of a domestic partner.
  • B.When an employee is entitled to bereavement leave and the funeral is to be held out of Illinois and beyond the states contiguous thereto, the employee will be entitled to a maximum of five consecutive days. For purposes of this section, those states contiguous to the State of Illinois are: Missouri, Iowa, Wisconsin, Indiana, Kentucky and Michigan.
  • C.The member requesting the leave will, if time permits, prepare a Personnel Action Request form (CPD-11.612) indicating “Death in Family.” The member will indicate “Domestic Partner” in the “Remarks” section.
  • D.The completed form will be submitted to the unit commanding officer who, after approving the request, will ensure that the form is forwarded to the Employment Section, Human Resources Division.
  • E.In the event that time does not permit the member to complete the Personnel Action Request form, the member will be responsible for completing the form upon returning to duty.
  • F.Annual or compensatory time furloughs will not be extended as a result of the death of a domestic partner during such furlough unless the death occurs during the last three days of the furlough period, at which time the procedure outlined in Item IV-B will be followed, if applicable.
V.BEREAVEMENT LEAVE APPROVAL
Upon request of a Department member to utilize bereavement leave for the death of a domestic partner, the unit commanding officer or designee will:
  • A.contact the City of Chicago, Department of Human Resources during normal business hours.
  • B.request the section responsible for domestic partner registration.
  • C.determine if the member has registered a domestic partner.
  • D.approve the leave if the registration is verified, or
  • E.disapprove the leave if the registration cannot be verified.
  • F.indicate in the “Remarks” section on the Personnel Action Request form the name and title of the person contacted and the date and time.
  • G.forward the complete Personnel Action Request form to the Employment Section, Human Resources Division.
