Chicago Police DepartmentUniform and Property U01-08
Child Safety Seats
Issue Date:22 February 2012Effective Date:22 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 23 November 1999; S99-10
Index Category:Department Equipment
I.Purpose
This directive informs Department members of the:
  • A.laws regarding child safety seats as found in the Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) and in the Municipal Code of the City of Chicago (MCC).
  • B.access for Department members to child safety seats when transporting a child.
  • C.distribution, operational procedures, care and maintenance of Department child safety seats.
II.References
  • A.625 ILCS 25/4 - Transporting children under the age of 4.
  • B.625 ILCS 25/4a - Transporting children between the ages of 4 and 6.
  • C.MCC Chapter 9-76 Section 180(a) - Vehicle equipment
III.Department Use of Child Safety Seats
  • A.To address those situations where there may be the necessity for a Department member to transport a child of six years of age or younger, select Bureau of Patrol field supervisors and members of the Youth Investigations Division will have at their disposal child safety seats.
  • B.These devices can be installed by a trained field supervisor or Youth Investigations Division member in a Department Ford Crown Victoria automobile only, provided it is not equipped with a rigid prisoner security screen mounted between the front and back seat.
  • C.Installation training for Bureau of Patrol field supervisors will be conducted by the Traffic Safety and Training Unit and will consist of a four-hour course and a Training Bulletin. The course will begin with a classroom lecture; then the attendees will pair off and participate in hands-on training installing each type of child safety seat.
  • D.Bureau of Patrol field supervisors will be scheduled for training by their district commanders / unit commanding officers.
IV.Bureau of Patrol Responsibilities
  • A.Distribution
    • 1.Initially each district will receive three of each type of child safety seat, which are:
      • a.Convertible Car Seat for infants and toddlers up to 20 pounds,
      • b.Booster Car Seat for children up to 70 pounds.
    • 2.District commanders will ensure that the seats will be retrieved by a representative from each district at the Equipment and Supply Section.
    • 3.The district commander will designate a regularly staffed supervisor's vehicle to be used for child transports. The child safety seats will be available in the field 24 hours a day. When not in use, the child safety seat will be kept in a protective wrapper and stored in the trunk.
    • 4.A sworn supervisor having completed training on installation and assigned to the vehicle equipped with the child safety seats will ensure the child safety seats are in the trunk of the vehicle and in working order.
    • 5.The four remaining seats will be secured in the district facility and accessible to the station supervisor when needed.
  • B.Operational Procedures
    • 1.Watch lineups transmitted to the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) will indicate that a properly trained field supervisor is assigned to field duty and is in possession of the child safety seats by use of the designation "C".
    • 2.Department members having to transport a child six years of age or younger will request a supervisor via the OEMC and notify the dispatcher of the number of children to be transported as well as their estimated age(s).
    • 3.The designated Bureau of Patrol supervisor will respond to the location, determine the type of child safety device needed, ensure that the device is properly installed and the child is properly secured within the child safety seat, and document each use and request for service on the Supervisors Management Log (CPD-11.455).
    • 4.If field personnel need to transport more than two children at a time, the assigned field supervisor will make the appropriate determination.
    • 5.When transporting a child in a police vehicle in a child safety seat, the child will be accompanied by a parent, legal guardian, or other such responsible adult whenever possible.
  • C.Care and Maintenance
    • 1.District custodians will be responsible for cleaning the child safety seat should a child soil it in any way.
    • 2.The Department of Fleet Management will be responsible for cleaning the child safety seat if the child is found to have a communicable disease.
    • 3.The child safety seats will be taken out of service when a seat is damaged, inoperative, soiled, or was in use during a traffic crash.
    • 4.When a seat is taken out of service, the appropriate field supervisor will notify the station supervisor of the circumstances for which the seat was taken out of service.
      • a.The station supervisor will:
        • 1.ensure that a replacement seat is put in service.
        • 2.review the To-From-Subject report indicating why the seat was taken out of service and forward the report to the district commander.
      • b.The district commander will review the report submitted and ensure that:
        • 1.a copy of the report is forwarded to the Traffic Section, Traffic Safety and Training Unit, Attention: Certified Child Safety Seat Technician, and
        • 2.the seat is returned to the Equipment and Supply Section between 0700-1600 hours Monday through Friday.
    • 5.The Equipment and Supply Section will notify the Traffic Safety and Training Unit when a child safety seat is damaged so that they can determine proper disposition of the device.
      NOTE:
      Under no circumstances will a damaged child safety seat be used for transporting a child.
    • 6.The child safety seats will be kept for no longer than six years. The child safety seat will be returned to the Equipment and Supply Section for a replacement.
    • 7.The Equipment and Supply Section will monitor product recalls and then will notify each district of the recall. Recalled products will be returned to the Equipment and Supply Section, which will then follow manufacturer's instructions relating to the product recall.
    • 8.During the weekly vehicle / equipment inspection, assigned personnel will use the notation "2C" indicating that both seats are in the trunk of the designated vehicle and in working order within the Condition of Vehicle column on the Vehicle / Equipment Inspection Report (CPD-11.457).
    • 9.If one or both child safety seats are missing or damaged, the station supervisor will be notified, and a To-From-Subject report will be submitted before the end of the Department members regularly scheduled tour of duty.
    • 10.The station supervisor will ensure that a complaint register (CR) investigation is initiated if it is believed that the damage or loss was not of an accidental nature or was due to negligence.
