Bears in Blue Foundation - Pilot Program

I.Purpose
This directive introduces the Bears in Blue Foundation - Pilot Program.
II.Scope
The city-wide pilot program is effective beginning 10 March 2013, and will continue until 31 December 2013.
III.Bears in Blue Foundation
Founded by police officers of several jurisdictions, the Bears in Blue Foundation is a not-for-profit charity, separate from the Chicago Police Department, which is organized to supply teddy bears and journals to first responders, school officials and crisis workers who come in contact with children who have experienced or been exposed to abuse and/or trauma.
IV.General Information
The Chicago Police Department is committed to serving the the youngest members of the community and recognizes that working with children who have suffered abuse is a skill that every Department member must possess.
Gestures of an officer’s natural concern and caring for victims of child abuse will promote the process of healing. Officers have the opportunity to give an abused child the hope of a childhood, the joy of play, and the sense of being cared for by others. Psychological studies have proven that transitional objects have a significant calming effect on children in stressful or traumatic circumstances.
Child abuse is more than physical and can have damaging effects not only on the children who suffer it, but on communities that must address the aftermath of abuse. While physical abuse might be the most visible, other types of abuse, such as emotional abuse and neglect, also leave deep, lasting scars. The earlier abused children get help, the greater chance they have to heal.
V.Procedures
  • A.The Bureau of Patrol, CAPS Division designee will:
    • 1.act as the Department liaison with the Bears-In-Blue Foundation during the pilot program.
    • 2.formulate the distribution plan for the teddy bears and journals for delivery to the district level.
  • B.Upon receipt of the Bears-In-Blue teddy bears and journals, district commanders, or designee, will:
    • 1.ensure all necessary program information is collected on the Bears-In-Blue form including but not limited to:
      • a.the number of teddy bears initially received from the Bears-In-Blue Foundation.
      • b.the RD number associated with any specific distribution.
      • c.the number and type of distribution (either bear or journal) to children under the age of (7) seven.
      • d.the number and type of distribution (either bear or journal) to children (7) seven years and older.
    • 2.fax the Bears-In-Blue form on the last business day of each month to the Bureau of Patrol, CAPS Division at 312-745-0588.
    • 3.ensure teddy bears and journals are stored in each sector sergeant vehicle in their respective district.
  • C.Sector sergeants will:
    • 1. be responsible for individual distribution.
    • 2.record the information listed in Item V-B-1-b,c, and d, on the Bears-In-Blue form for every bear or journal distributed.
