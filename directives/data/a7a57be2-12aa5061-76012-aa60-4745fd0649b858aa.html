Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-04-01
Canine Teams
Issue Date:16 June 2005Effective Date:17 June 2005
Rescinds:S05-08
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.outlines procedures for:
    • 1.the canine teams duties, responsibilities, and capabilities.
    • 2.requesting canine teams.
  • B.establishes responsibilities for a canine handler after a canine bites or inflicts injury upon a person.
II.Canine Teams Responsibilities and Tasks
  • A.A canine team consists of an on-duty canine police officer and their assigned Department canine.
  • B.Patrol and Narcotics Detection Canine Teams are available to respond to calls for service twenty-four hours a day by request through the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) dispatcher.
  • C.Airport Law Enforcement state certified Explosive Detection Canine Teams are available based on approval as delineated in Item IV-D of the directive.
  • D.Canine handlers are responsible for determining whether a situation (other than crowd control) justifies canine use and the appropriate tactical methods that will be employed. If the on-scene supervisor disagrees with the handlers tactical assessment, a canine team supervisor will be notified to make a final determination.
    NOTE:
    When time or exigent circumstances necessitate action, the directions of the on-scene supervisor will be followed.
  • E.If a canine deployment results in the arrest of a suspect, the suspect will be turned over to the requesting unit for processing. The responding canine team functions as a tool for the successful completion of the investigation.
  • F.Police canines will only be handled or given commands by their assigned handler. If the assigned handler is injured or unable to command the canine, another canine handler will be notified for assistance.
  • G.When utilizing canine teams, Department members will adhere to procedures that properly control use of force and channel the canine teams specialized capabilities into legally acceptable crime prevention and crowd-control activities. Deployment may include, but is not limited to:
    • 1.conducting building searches for hidden offenders who have or are alleged to have committed a felony offense.
    • 2.locating lost, missing, or incapacitated persons.
    • 3.tracking suspects who have abandoned their vehicles or have fled crime scenes.
    • 4.conducting crime-scene and area searches for discarded evidence, such as clothing, weapons, etc.
    • 5.detecting the presence of narcotic substances or explosives.
    • 6.assignment to high-crime areas for patrol and enforcement.
    • 7.assisting in the establishment of probable cause in asset forfeiture cases.
    • 8.providing security for Department vehicles and equipment when requested during emergency situations.
    • 9.conducting public service canine demonstrations.
III.General Procedures When Requesting Canine Teams
  • A.When a sworn member conducting a preliminary or follow-up investigation determines that the services of a canine team is required, with the exception of crowd control or explosive material searches, the officer will notify the OEMC dispatcher and request that a canine team be assigned.
  • B.Since the success of the canine teams efforts depends on the canines ability to identify and track a specific scent, it is imperative that officers or other persons refrain from walking about the crime scene or handling objects and/or evidence connected with it.
    NOTE:
    In building searches, every attempt should be made to minimize human traffic inside the building prior to a canine search. The presence of conflicting scent trails may reduce the ability of a canine and hinder a successful apprehension.
  • C.When a canine team is conducting a building search or tracking a suspect:
    • 1.the occupants of the premises being searched should be removed before the search.
    • 2.officers at the scene should guard all possible exits on the outside of the building while a canine search is in progress.
    • 3.if possible, inside ventilation should be turned off prior to performing the search.
  • D.Unless specific circumstances dictate otherwise, only the canine handlers and their dogs will be allowed inside a building during searches for secreted suspects.
  • E.When a canine team is requested to track a person who has abandoned an automobile, no one will be allowed to enter the vehicle until after the canine team has arrived and the tracking has begun.
  • F.Preplanned requests for a canine team in nonemergency situations will be made by a district or unit commanding officer to the Special Functions Group. Request may also be submitted in writing to the Deputy Chief, Special Function Group, by completing an Intra-Department Support Service Request form.
IV.Specific Procedures When Requesting Canine Teams
  • A.Crowd Control Situations
    • 1.A supervisor may request the assignment of canine teams in a crowd control situation. However, only a sworn member of command staff rank will authorize the use of canine teams when the crowd becomes so unruly that it cannot be contained by other police methods and the crowd is likely to cause personal injury or property damage.
    • 2.Requests from supervisors to utilize canine teams in crowd control situations will be made via radio or telephone to the OEMC.
    • 3.The OEMC will dispatch canine teams as requested and notify the appropriate area deputy chief and the deputy chief, Special Functions Group of the request.
      NOTE:
      If no canine teams are available, OEMC will contact the Special Operations Section to determine their status.
    • 4.Upon arrival at the scene of an emergency situation, district or unit supervisors and canine handlers will confer regarding the placement of their canine teams.
      NOTE:
      Canines will remain in their assigned vehicles during the consultation.
    • 5.Depending on the situation, canine teams may:
      • a.remain on patrol in the area adjacent to, and not in view of, the incident scene; or
      • b.move directly to the scene to exercise a psychological deterrent on the crowd.
        NOTE:
        Canines will be leashed at all times when used for crowd control.
  • B.Narcotics Detection
    • 1.The use of canine teams in a narcotics detection or asset forfeiture capacity is authorized when a search warrant is to be executed for controlled substances and the supervisor from the unit executing the warrant requests their assistance.
    • 2.An officer or supervisor assigned to the unit executing the search warrant will accompany the canine team as they conduct the search, noting the time, location, quantity, and other related specifics of all contraband recovered by the canine team.
    • 3.In the absence of a search warrant, a supervisor from the requesting unit will confer with the canine team supervisor relative to the legality of the canine search.
    • 4.Canines may be used to sniff the exterior of a motor vehicle during a lawful traffic stop provided the driver is detained no longer than is necessary to complete the traffic stop or issue a citation. The driver cannot be detained, beyond the reasonable period of time it would take to complete the traffic stop, in order for a canine and handler to arrive at the scene. The sniff must be completed before the citation(s) has been given.
  • C.Asset Forfeiture
    • 1.The unit initiating a forfeiture action will be responsible for ensuring that legal guidelines are followed.
    • 2.An asset forfeiture proceeding is a civil action and does not fall under the same guidelines and requirements as criminal proceedings.
    • 3.Department personnel should refer to existing Department directives and bulletins relative to asset forfeiture proceedings.
      • a.Asset forfeiture money line-ups are to be conducted in a Department or agency facility, NOT at the scene.
      • b.A member from the requesting unit will be present when performing the "money line-up" and ensure that all appropriate information as to amount, personnel present, etc., are recorded on appropriate reports.
  • D.Explosive Detection Canine Teams
    • 1.Airport Law Enforcement state-certified explosive detection canine teams are available to respond to calls for service off airport property twenty-four hours a day only with authorization from a command staff member delineated in Item IV-D-4.
    • 2.When it is determined that the services of an Airport Law Enforcement explosive detection canine team are necessary, the requesting unit will contact the OEMC dispatcher to request the assignment of an explosive detection canine team. The OEMC dispatcher will contact the Airport Law Enforcement Section Watch Commander to determine the availability of an explosive detection canine team.
    • 3.If a suspect package is located before the arrival of an explosive detection canine team, the Bomb and Arson Section will be notified for removal and the procedures as delineated in the Department directive entitled "Bomb, Arson and Fire Incidents" will be followed.
      NOTE:
      With the exception of airport property, under no circumstances will a canine be used to verify the existence of an explosive substance after a package has been located.
    • 4.The following members are the only individuals who are authorized to request the dispatch of explosive detection canine teams from the Airport Law Enforcement Section to be used for other than Department of Aviation matters:
      • a.the Superintendent,
      • b.the Assistant Superintendent, Operations
      • c.the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol,
      • d.the Deputy Chief, Special Functions Group.
    • 5.The requesting member will provide the name of the authorizing command staff member to the OEMC dispatcher.
V.Canine Bites and Other Inflicted Injuries
  • A.When a Department canine is involved in a bite incident while on or off-duty, the handler will:
    • 1.seek medical treatment for the bitten person, as needed.
    • 2.notify:
      • a.Special Functions Group (SFG) 24 hour desk, if the handler is assigned to SFS.
      • b.their watch commander, if the handler is assigned to the Airport Law Enforcement Section or the Public Transportation Section.
    • 3.request an evidence technician to take photographs of the injury.
      NOTE:
      If possible, photographs should be taken both before and after medical treatment.
    • 4.complete a Dog Bite Incident Report (CPD 21.421) and obtain a bite number from the Administrative Desk Control Center.
    • 5.contact Animal Care and Control (ACC) and provide a concise account of the events surrounding the bite.
    • 6.ensure the appropriate reports are completed and submit a complete package along with the original Dog Bite Incident Report to their unit commanding officer for review.
    • 7.contact the Arboretum View Veterinary Specialists during normal business hours. The canine will be examined by:
      • a.the veterinarian within twenty-four (24) hours of the bite incident.
      • b.an Arboretum View veterinarian ten (10) days after the initial examination, if the handler is assigned to SFS.
      • c.the designated veterinarian ten (10) days after the initial examination, if the handler is assigned to the Airport Law Enforcement Section or the Public Transportation Section.
  • B.When a person is accidentally bitten by a Department canine at the handlers residence and the bite results in broken bones or requires sutures, and the person is a resident of the handlers home, the handler will kennel the canine at the Canine Training Center (CTC).
    NOTE:
    The handler will pick up the canine from the CTC at the beginning of their tour of duty and return the canine to the CTC at the end of their tour during the ten (10) day observation period.
