Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S10-05
Street Narcotics Abatement Program (SNAP)
Issue Date:14 April 1997Effective Date:15 April 1997
Rescinds:S97-02
Index Category:Gang and Narcotic Abatement
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the Street Narcotics Abatement Program (SNAP) as a joint Bureau of Patrol - Narcotics Section, Organized Crime Division (OCD) program that will provide enhanced city-wide enforcement of narcotic laws with a focus on street / open air market sales.
  • B.coordinates the efforts of the Bureau of Patrol and Narcotics Section personnel towards the increased enforcement of narcotic laws.
  • C.identifies the duties and responsibilities of command, supervisory personnel and members assigned to or involved in this program.
  • D.provides for the publication of the results of these efforts to affected community members in order to heighten community awareness of the Department’s narcotic enforcement efforts.
II.Program Coordinator
The Commander, Narcotics Section, will appoint a lieutenant from the Narcotics Section to coordinate this program. The coordinator will:
  • A.be responsible for day to day operational decisions relative to the program.
  • B.receive location requests from district commanders, review same with the affected district commanders and tactical lieutenants and select those appropriate for SNAP enforcement activity.
  • C.liaison with district commanders and tactical lieutenants as necessary to facilitate the collaboration of Bureau of Patrol and Narcotics Section personnel.
  • D.be responsible for collecting arrest data and related statistical information generated by this program.
  • E.review, approve and process all requests submitted by SNAP Narcotics Section sergeants for use of "1505 Account" funds.
  • F.maintain liaison with other agencies/department units that may be affected by this program.
  • G.provide the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, and the Chief, Organized Crime Division with a monthly report of cumulative results.
III.District Procedures
  • A.District commanders will:
    • 1.utilize all reliable sources of information to identify prioritized locations within their districts where narcotic law violations are prevalent by reviewing the District Plan and Beat Plans with district and sector management team members and by conferring with local community groups.
    • 2.submit requests for missions to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, through their area deputy chief, identifying these locations and the nature and scope of the problem. These requests, in priority order, will include the following information, if available:
      • a.method of operation, type and hours of activity
      • b.gang affiliation
      • c.vehicle(s) used
      • d.a synopsis of a suggested approach to alleviate the problem.
  • B.Tactical lieutenants will:
    • 1.identify the tactical or gang tactical team to be utilized for this program.
    • 2.confer with the Narcotics Section lieutenant regarding the selection of street / open air market enforcement locations, the execution of the program and whether to focus on street narcotics dealers, buyers or a combination of both.
    • 3.personally ensure that statistical information (i.e., how many arrests, location (s) /beat (s) of arrests, charges, vehicles/ assets seized, etc.) is forwarded to the district neighborhood relations sergeant for community publication.
    • 4.submit a report to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, through channels, on the 5th day of each calendar month, excluding weekends and holidays, which:
      • a.summarizes the activity of the district SNAP Program for the preceding month.
      • b.indicates the number of court cases for the preceding month, including pending court cases and final dispositions, if available.
      • c.summarizes overtime earned as a result of the SNAP Program, including operations and court attendance, for the preceding month.
  • C.Neighborhood relations sergeants will:
    • 1.prepare a SNAP flier (see sample on page 3) which:
      • a.lists SNAP results, by beat, from the most recent Program implementation.
      • b.includes a request that community members notify the police if renewed narcotic sales activity is observed at a previously targeted location.
    • 2.ensure that statistical information by beat is disseminated in a timely manner to the District Advisory Committee (DAC), all DAC subcommittees and appropriate local citizen / community organizations.
    • 3.ensure that filers are given to each beat team leader for distribution at the next scheduled beat community meeting.
