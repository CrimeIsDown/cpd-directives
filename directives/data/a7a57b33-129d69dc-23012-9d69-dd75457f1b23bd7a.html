Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-04-06
Safe Start Project Program
Issue Date:25 November 2002Effective Date:25 November 2002
Rescinds:D02-53
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the:
    • 1.Safe Start Project Program.
    • 2.use of the Safe Start card issued by the Chicago Department of Public Health.
  • B.establishes goals for the Safe Start Project Program.
II.Scope
The Safe Start Project Program will:
  • A.be conducted in the 5th and 7th Districts.
  • B.commence 02 December 2002 and continue until further notice.
III.Program Information
  • A.The Safe Start Project Program is a federally sponsored initiative directed at providing intervention to children ages 0-5 who witness domestic and nondomestic violence. As a partner in this project, the Chicago Police Department, in conjunction with the Chicago Department of Public Health, will provide assistance and intervention to children within this target age range.
  • B.The goals for the project include, but are not limited to:
    • 1.providing appropriate and accessible services to children ages 0-5 who witness domestic and nondomestic violence.
    • 2.reducing the effects to children who witness violence by providing effective intervention.
  • C.Training for this program will consist of a series of roll call training sessions conducted by the Domestic Violence Program, Office of the Superintendent, explaining the effects of witnessing violence and the Department’s response to incidents in which very young children witness violence.
IV.Procedures
  • A.Members who are assigned to a call for service involving domestic violence where a crime has occurred and where children aged 0-5 are present or witnessed the incident will:
    • 1.follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled “Domestic Incidents.”
    • 2.inform the victim that:
      • a.the Safe Start Project can provide services and support to address domestic violence and other violent crimes where children aged 0-5 are present.
      • b.additional services can be accessed by calling the Domestic Violence Help Line.
    • 3.explain to the victim the effects on children who witness violence.
    • 4.provide the victim with a Safe Start card and a Domestic Violence Help Line palm card.
    • 5.complete the Domestic Violence Referral card.
    • 6.record the number of children aged 0-5 present on the scene in the narrative section of the General Offense Case Report (CPD-11.380) and in the “Additional Information” section of the Domestic Violence Referral card.
    • 7.submit all completed referral cards to their supervisor prior to the completion of the tour of duty.
    • 8.follow procedures as outlined in Item V of this directive to make the notification to the Domestic Violence Help Line.
    • 9.document the notification procedure as required in Item V in the narrative section of the case report.
    • 10.in the “Extra Copies Required” box of the case report, note that a copy must be sent to the Domestic Violence Program.
  • B.Members who are assigned to any violent incident (i.e., “drive-by shooting” or “man shot”) where children aged 0-5 are present or witnessed the incident will:
    • 1.follow all procedures as outlined in the Department directive entitled “Preliminary Investigations.”
    • 2.follow procedures as outlined in Items IV-A-2 through 10 of this directive, including the notification to the Domestic Violence Help Line.
  • C.Bureau of Patrol supervisors will ensure that the:
    • 1.Safe Start card and the Domestic Violence Help Line palm card is provided to the victim.
    • 2.responding member makes the required notification as outlined in Item V of this directive.
    • 3.following information is recorded in the narrative section of the case report:
      • a.The documentation of the notification as required in Item V of this directive;
      • b.The number of children aged 0-5 who were present or witnessed the violent incident.
    • 4.Domestic Violence Referral card is completed.
  • D.District commanders of the appropriate districts will ensure that the district review officers forward all Domestic Violence Referral cards for the previous 24-hour period to the Domestic Violence Program.
  • E.Personnel assigned to the Domestic Violence Program will:
    • 1.compile information contained on case reports submitted to them under the requirements of this directive.
    • 2.forward the information to the Department of Public Health Safe Start Project.
V.Notifications to the Domestic Violence Help Line
  • A.Notifications to the Domestic Violence Help Line are crucial to the success of the Safe Start Project. The Project team has been authorized to collect this data to ensure the necessity of the program.

    • 1.If the victim grants permission, the member will place the call and:
      • a.inform the call taker:
        • (1)of the member’s identity as an officer and as a participant in the domestic or nondomestic violence Safe Start Project. If the incident was nondomestic-related, the member will also inform the call taker of the nature of the incident.
        • (2)of the number of children aged 0-5 present at the incident and their ages.
          NOTE:
          Members will not disclose the name or address of the victim and children.
      • b.surrender the call to the victim for further assistance from the help line.
      • c.document that the call was made in the narrative section of the case report.
      • d.in the ‘Extra Copies Required box of the case report, note that a copy must be sent to the Domestic Violence Program.
    • 2.If the victim declines permission for the member to use the telephone, and does not wish to be linked to the Domestic Violence Help Line, the member will:
      • a.document the declination in the narrative section of the case report.
      • b.in the “Extra Copies Required” box of the case report, note that a copy must be sent to the Domestic Violence Program.
  • C.If the victim has no phone service, the member will:
    • 1.document the lack of phone service in the narrative section of the case report.
    • 2.in the “Extra Copies Required” box of the case report, note that a copy must be sent to the Domestic Violence Program.
VI.Program Evaluation
The Domestic Violence Program will act as the Department liaison and evaluate the effectiveness of the Safe Start Project Program.
