Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S01-01-08
Complimentary Communications
Issue Date:24 November 2010Effective Date:24 November 2010
Rescinds:S10-11
Index Category:Department Organization
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces:
    • 1.procedures ensuring that complimentary communications received by the Department are processed by the Human Resources Division and that members are informed of such recognition.
    • 2.procedures ensuring automated compliments received by the Independent Police Review Authority (IPRA) are forwarded and processed by the Human Resources Division and that members are informed of such recognition.
  • B.continues the use of the Summary of Complimentary Letters form (CPD-11.627).
  • C.discontinues procedures ensuring that complimentary communications received by the Department are processed by the Office of the Superintendent.
II.General Information
  • A.Complimentary letters that are mailed or hand carried to the Department are acceptable for recognition.
    NOTE:
    Complimentary letters not postmarked by the United States Postal Service are acceptable.
  • B.Anonymous or letters without the author’s name, address, and/or contact information for verification will not be acceptable for recognition.
  • C.Complimentary communications will be accepted from any non-Department member.
III.Procedures
  • A.Any supervisor receiving orally communicated compliments from members of the community regarding members of the Department will:
    • 1.encourage the member(s) of the community to:
      • a.compose a letter and send it to the Chicago Police Department, Human Resources Division, Attention: Awards and Recognition Section, Room 2020 SW, 3510 South Michigan Avenue, Chicago, Illinois 60653 or
      • b.compose and send an e-mail to the Department’s official e-mail address, as listed under the “Contact Us” link located in the Department’s CLEARPath Webportal on the World Wide Web (WWW), or
      • c.visit IPRA’s website on the WWW to complete an automated compliment.
    • 2.recognize the Department members actions or accomplishments as outlined in the Department directive entitled “Department Awards.”
  • B.Department members receiving e-mail correspondence or letters from members of the community complimenting members of the Department directly will forward the e-mail correspondence (electronically) and letters, with their original envelopes, with a completed Summary of Complimentary Letters form, to the Human Resources Division, Awards and Recognition Section.
  • C.Department members responsible for maintaining and responding to e-mail correspondence sent to the Department’s official e-mail address will electronically forward the compliments to Human Resources Division, Awards and Recognition Section.
  • D.The Human Resources Division, Awards and Recognition Section will:
    • 1.send a letter or e-mail response, as applicable, to the member(s) of the community or IPRA, as applicable, acknowledging receipt of the complimentary communication.
    • 2.enter one (1) complimentary letter credit into a member’s computerized award record.
    • 3.stamp the Summary of Complimentary Letters form with the word “ENTERED” and return it to the unit commanding officer, of the complimented member.
    • 4.direct a copy of the automated IPRA compliment, e-mail, or letter containing the acknowledgment to the member(s) complimented.
