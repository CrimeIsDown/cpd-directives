II.Holiday Assignment
  • A.Agreement Members
    • 1.Commanding officers and watch commanders will ensure that personnel performing non-essential services do not perform those services on a holiday. Essential services will be defined by individual unit needs.
    • 2.When operational considerations require that only some members on a watch are needed to work on a holiday, the most senior member will be given the option to work provided that:
      • a.the holiday is not the member's regular day off.
      • b.the member possesses the necessary skills or special qualifications to perform the duties required.
  • B.Civilian Members
    • 1.For members whose regularly scheduled work week does not include Saturday or Sunday, a holiday which falls on a Saturday will be observed on the Friday before the holiday; a holiday which falls on a Sunday will be observed on the Monday after the holiday.
    • 2.For members whose regularly scheduled work week includes Saturday and/or Sunday, holidays which fall on a Saturday or a Sunday will be observed on that day.
    • 3.When operational considerations require that only some members on a watch are needed to work on a holiday, the option to work will be offered on a rotating seniority basis first to volunteers who normally perform the work. When there are not enough volunteers, the work will be assigned on a rotating basis of reverse seniority. In either case the:
      • a.holiday cannot be the member's regular day off.
      • b.member who volunteers or is assigned must possess the necessary skills or qualifications to perform the work.
III.Holiday Compensation
  • A.Holiday compensatory time will not be credited to a sworn member on a holiday if the member is on the medical roll (excluding IOD), absent due to sickness or death in family, on military leave, suspended, excused non-disciplinary, or on a leave of absence.
  • B.Compensation for holidays will be credited as follows:
    • 1.Agreement Members:
      • a.A member who is regular day off or who is on furlough on a holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time.
      • b.A member who is required to work a regular tour of duty (eight hours) on a holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time and four hours of additional pay. If the member works:
        • (1)less than an eight hour tour of duty, the member will be credited with one hour compensatory time and one-half hour of additional pay for each hour worked.
          EXAMPLE:
          If an agreement member only works 4 hours on a holiday, he will be credited with 4 hours compensatory time for the holiday and 2 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)more than an eight hour tour of duty, the member will be compensated for the overtime in compensatory time or money at the rate of time and one-half on the basis of completed 15 minute segments.
      • c.A member whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a regular tour of duty (eight hours) on the holiday will be credited with four hours of pay and twenty hours of compensatory time.
      • d.Agreement members whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday will not be allowed to voluntarily work the holiday.
    • 2.Sergeants, Lieutenants and Captains
      • a.A supervisor who is regular day off or who is on full furlough or furlough segment on a holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time.
      • b.A supervisor who is required to work a regular tour of duty (eight hours) on a holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time and four hours of additional pay. If the supervisor works:
        • (1)less than an eight hour tour of duty, the supervisor will be credited with one hour compensatory time and one-half hour of additional pay for each hour worked.
          EXAMPLE:
          If a supervisor only works 4 hours on a holiday, he will be credited with 4 hours compensatory time for the holiday and 2 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)more than an eight hour tour of duty, the supervisor will be credited with compensatory time at the rate of time and one half for each completed hour beyond the normal tour of duty.
          EXAMPLE:
          If a supervisor works a total of ten hours on the holiday (8 hours regular tour of duty plus 2 hours overtime) he will be credited with 8 hours of holiday time, 3 hours CE and 4 hours of holiday pay.
      • c.Supervisors attending training outside the Department (i.e., Northwestern University Traffic Institute, FBI Academy, etc.) will not be credited with the four hours of additional pay. However, if the supervisor is required to attend class on the designated holiday, he will submit verification of his attendance at class to his commanding officer. The attendance will be noted on the member's Time and Attendance Record and the supervisor will be credited with the holiday time.
      • d.A supervisor whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a regular tour of duty (eight hours) will be credited with 20 hours of compensatory time and four hours of additional pay.
      • e.The one-half hour administrative overtime for sergeants and the three-quarters of an hour administrative overtime for lieutenants and captains will be credited in accordance with Item VI-C-10 of the directive entitled "Payroll And Timekeeping - Overtime / Compensatory Time."
    • 3.Civilian Members
      • a.A member covered by the AFSCME bargaining unit agreement (both FLSA exempt and FLSA non-exempt) will be compensated for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty on an established holiday will be credited with 10.5 hours of additional pay or, at their option, 7 hours of compensatory time and 3.5 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)A member who has vacation scheduled, and an established holiday falls during the vacation period, will be carried DOH instead of being carried V-day and will not be compensated with 7 hours of compensatory time. The member’s unused V-day can be used another time.
        • (3)For members who are not scheduled on a regular Monday through Friday workweek, when a holiday falls on a member’s day off, the member will be granted another day off. If a holiday falls on the member’s first day off, the member will be granted the day off immediately prior to the holiday. If a holiday falls on the member’s second day off, the member will be granted the day off immediately after the holiday.
        • (4)For members who work in a unit that is operational on all shifts,
          including holidays, the member will receive 7 hours of compensatory time, which will be used within 90 days, in accordance with Section 16.11 of the AFSCME contract.
        • (5)A member whose regular day off coincides with an established
          holiday and who is required to work a full tour of duty on a holiday will be credited with 17.5 hours of pay or, at their option, 7 hours of compensatory time and 10.5 hours of pay.
      • b.A member covered by the Unit II bargaining unit agreement (except Crossing Guards and Police Dispatcher Aides) will be compensated for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty on an established holiday will be credited with 10.5 hours of additional pay or, at his option, 7 hours of compensatory time and 3.5 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)A member who is on vacation or whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday will be credited with 7 hours of compensatory time.
        • (3)A member whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a full tour of duty on a holiday will be credited with 14 hours of compensatory time and 3.5 hours of pay.
      • c.Police Dispatcher Aides will be compensated for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty on an established holiday will be credited with 12 hours of additional pay, or at his option, 8 hours of compensatory time and 4 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)A member who is on vacation or whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday will be credited with 8 hours of compensatory time.
        • (3)A member whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a full tour of duty on a holiday will be credited with 16 hours of compensatory time and 4 hours of pay.
      • d.Crossing guards will be paid for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty (4 hours) on an established holiday will be credited with 6 hours of additional pay.
        • (2)A member whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a full tour of duty (4 hours) on a holiday will be credited with 10 hours of pay.
      • e.A member not covered by a collective bargaining unit agreement and exempt from FLSA will be compensated for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is a Grade 12 or higher or G-4 or higher will not receive additional compensation for working on a holiday. These members are eligible for administrative leave which will be granted at the discretion of the member's immediate unit command staff officer.
        • (2)A member who is below a Grade 12 or G-4 who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty on an established holiday will be credited with 7 hours of compensatory time.
          • (a)A member who is below a Grade 12 or G-4 who is on vacation or whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday will be credited with 7 hours of compensatory time.
          • (b)A member who is below a Grade 12 or G-4 whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work full tour of duty on a holiday will be credited with 14 hours of compensatory time.
      • f.A member not covered by a collective bargaining unit agreement and not exempt from FLSA will be compensated for an established holiday in the following manner:
        • (1)A member who is scheduled to work a regular tour of duty on an established holiday will be credited with straight time in compensatory time only up to a total of 40 hours for the workweek. After 40 hours, all time worked on a holiday will be compensated for at time and one-half in compensatory time only.
        • (2)A member who is on vacation or whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday will be credited with 7 hours of compensatory time.
        • (3)A member whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work a full tour of duty on a holiday will be credited with 15 hours of compensatory time.
    • 4.Probationary Police Officers
      • a.A probationary police officer who is on his regular day off or who is on furlough on a holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time.
      • b.A probationary police officer who is scheduled to work on an established holiday will be credited with eight hours of compensatory time.
      • c.A probationary police officer whose regular day off coincides with an established holiday and who is required to work on the holiday will be credited with sixteen hours of compensatory time. Probationary police officers who are required to work beyond their normal tour of duty on a holiday will be compensated in compensatory time at the straight rate computed in 15 minute segments.
    • 5.Command Staff Members
      • a.Command staff members will normally be carried day-off holiday and will not receive additional compensation if required to work on a holiday.
      • b.Command staff members are eligible for administrative leave which will be granted at the discretion of the bureau or division head as appropriate.
IV.Religious Holidays
  • A.When a sworn member requests a day off to celebrate a religious holiday on a day not otherwise designated by City ordinance, the member will be given the option to utilize any of the following:
    • 1.a personal day.
    • 2.compensatory time.
    • 3.a baby furlough day.
    • 4.a vacation day if applicable.
    • 5.the voluntary change of regular day off.
    • 6.excused without pay, non-disciplinary.
  • B.When a civilian member requests a day off to celebrate a religious holiday on a day not otherwise designated as a holiday by City ordinance, the member will be given the option to utilize any of the following:
    • 1.compensatory time.
    • 2.a vacation day.
    • 3.administrative leave if authorized.
    • 4.voluntary change of regular day off when the member's unit is operational seven days a week.
    • 5.excused without pay, non-disciplinary.
  • C.District / unit commanding officers will approve requests of sworn and civilian members for a day off to celebrate a religious holiday utilizing any of the above options provided the granting of time off does not adversely affect unit operational needs.
V.General Policy Governing Holidays for Civilian Members
  • A.If a civilian member is absent with the employer's permission (i.e., excused absence), on the last working day preceding or the first working day following a holiday, the member will be carried as day off holiday on the unit's Attendance and Assignment record for such holiday.
  • B.If a civilian member is in an unexcused absence status on the last working day preceding or the first working day following such holiday, the holiday shall be considered as an additional day absent without pay.
VI.Personal Days
  • A.Agreement members and sworn supervisors are authorized four personal days in the calendar year and the following procedures will apply:
    • 1.A personal day taken as a day off is equivalent to a normal scheduled tour of duty.
    • 2.A personal day will not be requested by a member for a date on which the member is required to appear in court.
    • 3.A personal day request will be given priority over requests for compensatory time off, remaining furlough days or baby furlough days.
    • 4.A personal day request will not be approved if Department operations will be adversely affected.
    • 5.Personal days must be utilized during the current calendar year. Members may elect to be paid for two unused days in lieu of taking the time off. Personal days are equivalent to 8 hours when sold back. Personal days cannot be carried over for use in the next calendar year.
    • 6.Members will not be required to work on a personal day provided that:
      • a.an Overtime / Compensatory Time Report (CPD-11.608) is submitted to the unit commanding officer ten days prior to the personal day.
      • b.the request has been approved.
      • c.a holiday is not selected as a personal days.
    • 7.Personal days may be used to satisfy summary punishment days.
    • 8.Unused personal days will not be compensated at the time of retirement or resignation.
  • B.Any dispute within a unit regarding the use of a personal day by Agreement members will be resolved by seniority when more than one personal day request is submitted for a particular date in accordance with the provisions of the Agreement.
  • C.When more than one personal day request is submitted for a particular date by a supervisory member of the same rank, within the same unit and based on operational needs one or more requests have to be denied, the watch commander will refer to the unit seniority listing. The seniority date (time in rank) will be used first and if necessary, ties in seniority will be broken by continuous service date.
VII.Payroll Procedures For Reporting Overtime / Out Of Grade Hours And Holiday Premium Hours
  • A.DISTRIBUTION
    • 1.An Overtime / Out of grade Pay Listing (DPOL 8149 A) which identifies eligible sworn members currently assigned or detailed to their unit will be sent to all unit commanding officers.
    • 2.An Overtime / Out of Grade Pay listing (DPOL 8968 A) which identifies eligible civilian members currently assigned or detailed to their unit will be sent to all unit commanding officers.
    • 3.A Holiday Premium Pay Listing (DPOL 8795 A) which identifies all eligible sworn and civilian members who are currently assigned or detailed to their unit for holiday premium pay will be sent to all unit commanding officers.
  • B.PREPARATION OF THE OVERTIME / OF GRADE PAY LISTINGS (DPOL 8149 A and DPOL 8968 A)
    Unit timekeepers will:
    • 1.examine the time and Attendance Record (CPD-11.602) for each member listed on the DPOL Listings who is assigned or detailed to their unit to ensure that those members who worked overtime hours and/or out of grade were credited with the correct number of hours for pay purposes.
    • 2.enter the unit of detail in the “Detail Status” column of the Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listings (DPOL 8149 A or 8968 A) for those members who are detailed to another Department unit if the printed list does not so indicate. No other entries are to be made on the Listings for members detailed to another unit.
    • 3.enter the total number of overtime hours earned (monthly for civilian members or police period for sworn members) as recorded in the overtime column under the heading “Pay Hours” of the Time and Attendance Record in the “Total Overtime Pay Hours” column of the appropriate Overtime / Out of Grade pay Listings (DPOL 8149 A, 8968 A).
    • 4.record the total number of hours those members were authorized to work out of grade in the “out of Grade Hours” column of the appropriate Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listing. (See the directive entitled “Payroll and Timekeeping - Working Out of Grade.")
      NOTE:
      No hours for out of grade pay can be made to a pay listing without a completed authorization form being on file in the unit.
    • 5.record the out of grade position worked (e.g., D-2, D-3, D-4, 10, 11, 12, etc.) In the “Pay grade Worked” column of the appropriate Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listing.
    • 6.circle the number of overtime hours and out of grade hours on the members Time and Attendance Record for which payment is to be made to indicate that this data was recorded on the appropriate listing.
    • 7.sign their full name in the lower right hand corner of each completed page of the Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listings before submitting them to their unit commanding officer.
  • C.PREPARATION OF THE HOLIDAY PREMIUM PAY LISTING (DPOL 8795)
    Unit timekeepers will:
    • 1.examine the Time and Attendance Record of each sworn and civilian member listed on the Holiday Premium Pay Listing who is assigned to their unit of ensure that those members who were required to work on holiday (s) were credited with the correct number of hours for pay.
    • 2.examine the Time and Attendance Record of each sworn and civilian member listed on the Holiday Premium Pay Listing who is detailed to their unit to ensure that those members who are required to work on a holiday(s) were credited with the correct number of hours for pay.
    • 3.enter the unit of detail In the “Detail Status” column of the Holiday Premium Pay Listing for those members who are detailed to another unit if the printed list does not so indicate.
    • 4.record on a blank line of the Holiday Premium Pay Listings the unit number, title code, name and social security number of those members who names are not printed on the listing and are detailed into their unit.
    • 5.record the total number of holiday premium hours earned member on the Holiday Premium Pay Listing.
    • 6.circle the number of holiday premium hours on the members Time and Attendance Record for which payment is to be made to indicate that this data was recorded on the Listing.
    • 7.sign their full name in the lower right hand corner of each completed page of the Holiday Premium Pay Listing before submitting them to their unit commanding officer.
  • D.RESPONSIBILITIES OF UNIT COMMANDING OFFICERS
    Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.review the Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listings and the Holiday Premium Pay Listing prepared and submitted by the unit timekeeper to ensure legibility, accuracy and completeness.
    • 2.sign their full name on the last page of each Listing to indicate their review and approval.
    • 3.forward completed Overtime / Out of Grade Pay Listing as follows no later than four (4) working days (excluding Saturday and Sunday) following the distribution of the listings to Department units.
      • a.Original - Finance Division - Payroll Section
      • b.Photocopy 1 - Inspection Division
      • c.Photocopy 2 - Retained in unit files for six (6) months.
  • E.GENERAL INFORMATION
    Questions concerning procedures for reporting overtime and out of grade hours earned for pay purposes may be directed to the Finance Division.
