Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-17
Parking Meters
Issue Date:16 May 2014Effective Date:16 May 2014
Rescinds:13 April 1996 version
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures for handling parking meters and meter pay boxes that are damaged, defective or inventoried by a Department member.
II.General Information
  • A.The Department of Finance will be notified whenever a member inventories a parking meter and/or meter parts, or meter pay box/meter pay box parts. This notification will be made during normal business hours. After normal business hours, notification will be made to the Department of Streets and Sanitation.
  • B.A Department member may notify the Department of Finance by using the City Service Request form (CPD-21.208 ) to report:
    • 1.damaged/vandalized meters or meter parts,
    • 2.broken/malfunctioning meters,
    • 3.missing or illegible meter numbers, or
    • 4.damaged/vandalized meter pay boxes.
III.Inventory Procedures
Whenever a sworn member comes into possession of a meter/meter parts, or meter pay box/meter pay box parts, the member will:
  • A.inventory the items according to established procedures. The inventorying member will complete the eTrack inventory process and mail a printed inventory sheet via intra-Departmental mail to the Department of Finance.
  • B.notify the Department of Finance or the Department of Streets and Sanitation, of the recovery and provide them with the inventory number and location where the recovered property is held.
    NOTE:
    Department members will inform the Department of Finance that release of their property will not be made until said property is no longer needed in future court proceedings.
IV.Parking Violations
  • A.When issuing a violation notice for a parking meter violation, the member will ensure that the meter number is entered on the citation in the appropriate box.
  • B.When posted signs conflict with, or differ from information posted on the meter or the meter pay box, the member will be governed by the signs.
  • C.In situations which parking enforcement is required and no meter pay box receipt is displayed, Department members will refer to the current directive entitled ParkChicago — Mobile Payment For On-Street Parking Program.
  • D.If a citizen disputes a citation issued for a parking meter violation, the member will advise the citizen to follow the instructions on the citation and/or call the  Department of Finance
