Chicago Police DepartmentGeneral Order G03-04
On-Call Incident Commander
Issue Date:22 May 2009Effective Date:01 June 2009
Rescinds:G09-04
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the role of the On-Call Incident Commander.
  • B.defines the authority and responsibilities of the On-Call Incident Commander.
II.Policy
The command of the Chicago Police Department is structured to ensure appropriate command response to any incident. The role of the On-Call Incident Commander is established to respond to critical unplanned incidents where neutrality and independence are required to ensure the most appropriate and timely use of resources and to allow for independent decision making.
III.Authority of the On-Call Incident Commander
The On-Call Incident Commander (OCIC) operates with the authority of the Superintendent of Police to any incident the OCIC is assigned when the Superintendent is not readily available or on-duty.
NOTE:
When the OCIC is assigned to an incident where there is a potential for conflict of interest, (e.g., member under immediate supervision of the OCIC, relative of the OCIC, etc.), the Assistant Superintendent, Operations, will be consulted for appropriate resolution.
IV.Responsibilities of the On-Call Incident Commander
  • A.The OCIC will assume command over the following incidents:
    • 1.Any police involved shooting where a person has been injured or shot as a result of the discharge.
      NOTE:
      Police involved shootings where no person has been shot or injured as a result of the discharge, unintentional weapon discharges with no hits, and routine animal destruction incidents involving no extenuating circumstances will be the responsibility of the district watch commander in the district of occurrence.
    • 2.Serious injury or death of citizens subsequent to interactions with a Department member.
      NOTE:
      Investigations of suicides or attempted suicides by persons in custody will be coordinated by the appropriate Detective Division Area Commander.
    • 3.Serious injury or death of an on-duty Department member.
    • 4.The determination to go to paper reporting when the automated systems are not functioning.
    • 5.Other incidents which in the judgment of the OCIC require their command presence to ensure that the resources and operations of the Department are directed controlled, coordinated and that there is unity of command.
  • B.Reporting:
    • 1.Whenever a Tactical Response Report (TRR) is required for any incident enumerated in Item IV-A of this directive, the OCIC will personally conduct an investigation into the circumstances surrounding the incident and document their preliminary determination as to whether the conduct of the member conformed to Department policy in the narrative portion of the TRR by completing the “Watch Commander/OCIC” section of the TRR.
    • 2.The OCIC will ensure that the necessary steps that ultimately provide for and facilitate a complete and thorough investigation are initiated for any incident where the OCIC assumes command.
  • C.Assignment:
    • 1.The OCIC will be notified of an incident for assignment via Operations Command.
    • 2.The OCIC will be available for assignment between the hours of 2000 and 0600, seven days per week. At all other times, the appropriate Bureau of Patrol, Area Deputy Chief will fulfill the role of the OCIC for any incident enumerated in Item IV-A of this directive.
      NOTE:
      If an acting Bureau of Patrol, Area Deputy Chief is below the rank of deputy chief, the OCIC will respond outside of the normal OCIC hours to any incident enumerated in section IV-A of this directive. The acting Bureau of Patrol, Area Deputy Chief will continue to have responsibility for all non-OCIC related duties.
    • 3.The Assistant Superintendent, Operations will ensure appropriate scheduling of personnel to fulfill the role of the OCIC.
