Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-13-01
Officer's Battery Reporting Procedures
Issue Date:28 December 2012Effective Date:28 December 2012
Rescinds:2 November 2007 Version
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
  • A.The Federal Bureau of Investigation and the State of Illinois Uniform Crime Reporting Systems require data from the Department when the offense of murder, aggravated battery, battery, aggravated assault, or assault is committed against a police officer or a detention aide in the performance of their duties.
  • B.This directive outlines procedures to be followed in preparation of an Officers Battery Report.
II.Procedures
  • A.An Officer's Battery Report will be completed for all incidents when:
    • 1.a sworn member is the victim of a murder, aggravated battery, battery, aggravated assault, or assault while performing a police function either on-duty or off-duty.
    • 2.a detention aide is the victim of a murder, aggravated battery, battery, aggravated assault, or assault while in the performance of their duties.
      NOTE:
      These incidents will be reported consistent with the Incident Reporting Guide (CPD-63.451). Detention aides are designated as protected employees. The requirement to complete an Officer's Battery Report will not depend on what charges are placed on an offender. There need not be an offender in custody to trigger the reporting requirement.
  • B.An Officer's Battery Report will be completed:
    • 1.in addition to the appropriate case report, Tactical Response Report (CPD-11.377), Injury on Duty Report (CPD-62.375), Report of Exposure to Communicable Disease (CPD-62.418), or other reports which are required for the incident.
    • 2.whether or not the sworn member or detention aide has sustained a physical injury.
    • 3.by the involved member or a supervisor designated by the station supervisor of the district of occurrence if the member is incapacitated.
    • 4.using the CLEAR A-TRR application. For Officer's Battery Reports completed using the CLEAR A-TRR application, one copy will be included in the station supervisor's Tactical Response Report package which is distributed as outlined in the Department directives entitled "Incidents Requiring the Completion of a Tactical Response Report" and "CLEAR Automated Tactical Response Report."
  • C.In the event of a CLEAR A-TRR application unavailability and the use of a paper Tactical Response Report and Officer's Battery Report has been approved following the criteria established in the Department directive entitled "CLEAR Automated Tactical Response Report:
    • 1.a paper Officer's Battery Report will be completed following the guidelines established in Item II of this directive.
    • 2.the completed and approved paper Officer's Battery Report will be distributed in the following manner:
      • a.The original will be forwarded to the Director, Records Division, attached to the original corresponding case or supplementary report.
      • b.A photocopy will be included in the unit station supervisor's Tactical Response Report package forwarded to:
        • (1)the Independent Police Review Authority.
        • (2)the Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT), to ensure data entry in to the CLEAR A-TRR application.
    • 3.the Managing Deputy Director, PSIT, will ensure the incident reported on a paper Officers Battery Report is data-entered in to the A-TRR application once the application becomes available.
