Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D13-01
Cancellation of Violation Notice Citation Books
Issue Date:09 January 2013Effective Date:09 January 2013
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive outlines the procedures for the collection and destruction of the discontinued Violation Notice citation books.
II.General Information
  • A.The new Version 7.0 Violation Notice citation books will become effective Saturday, 12 January 2013 at 0001 hours. After that time, Department members will not issue a Violation Notice citation from any previous versions.
  • B.Department members will refer to the Department directive entitled "Parking and Compliance Violations" when citing a Municipal Code of Chicago ordinance violation on a Violation Notice citation and will familiarize themselves with the new Violation Notice citation books including:
    • 1.the new color for the face of the Citation book is blue,
    • 2.the new version number for the Citation book is 7.0,
    • 3.the fine amount for violation code 9-64-190(b), Central Business District - Meter has increased from a $60.00 fine to a $65.00 fine,
    • 4.the wards have been updated for 9-64-170(a) - Special types of vehicles, and
    • 5.the list of plates has been revised to include - Low Speed Vehicle (LSV), Expanded Use Antique (EUA), Share the Road (STR) and Illinois Chicago Police Memorial Foundation (CPM).
  • C.The Department of Finance will deliver the new Version 7.0 Violation Notice citation books to each ticket issuing agency by 10 January 2013.
III.Procedures
  • A.Unit commanding officers will ensure:
    • 1.the acquisition of a sufficient supply of the new Violation Notice citation books for distribution from the Department of Finance.
      NOTE:
      Version 7.0 citation books will be distributed to each ticket issuing agency beginning Monday, 7 January 2013. Citation books will be delivered through Thursday, 10 January 2013. For questions on delivery, refer to Item:IV.B.6 of this directive.
    • 2.the collection of the old Violation Notice citation books, either on-hand or issued to personnel, including partially used books.
    • 3.the new Violation Notice citation books, which become effective 12 January 2013 at 0001 hours, are issued to Department members and are available for use.
  • B.Department members will submit their old Violation Notice citation books to the designated unit member and, in turn, will be issued the new Violation Notice citation book.
    • 1.Once the new Violation Notice citation books are issued, Department members will not use any other citation from any prior series.
    • 2.Department members submitting partially used Violation Notice citation books will remove the “Member's Copy" or "Issuing Officer, Investigator, and/or Inspector” of all issued Violation Notice citations and retain them for a one-year period from the date of issue.
    • 3.In the event that any outstanding citations cannot be accounted for by 15 February 2013, the Department member to whom the citations were distributed will, by the end of their next tour of duty:
      • a.prepare a To-From-Subject report to their unit commanding officer explaining the circumstances under which the citations cannot be accounted for,
      • b.initiate the appropriate case report and send an AMC message, incorporating the AMC number in the narrative section of the case report,
      • c.attach a copy of the case report and AMC message to the To-From-Subject report and submit the report to their unit commanding officer.
  • C.Unit citations clerks will check all Violation Notice citation books and complete the corresponding Citation Control Sheet (CPD-11.496), indicating both the used and unused citations which are returned. When all citations on a Citation Control Sheet are accounted for, the sheet will be placed in the unit’s inactive file. In the event a Citation Control Sheet indicates that the citations have not been returned, the supervisor of the Department member to whom the citations were distributed will be notified to have outstanding citations returned without delay.
  • D.The Transfer Listing for Canceled/Unused Citations (CPD-22.805) will be used for the return of the old Violation Notice citation books. Each citation book will be listed by series, in numerical order, on a single line of the Transfer Listing. The number of the first and last citation in a citation book will be clearly printed on the same line of the Transfer Listing. The Transfer Listing will be prepared in triplicate and separated according to Item III-E of this directive.
  • E.After the collected citation books have been listed on the Transfer Listing, the books will be packed by series, in numerical order, in cartons. The Transfer Listing will be separated as follows:
    • 1.The original copy will be packed inside the carton containing the citation books listed on that Transfer List.
    • 2.A copy will be taped to the outside of the carton containing the citation books, in such a manner that personnel can clearly read the listing.
    • 3.A copy will be kept by the sending unit in accordance with the existing records-retention requirements.
IV.Retention and Disposal Procedures
  • A.All official records for the old Violation Notice citation books, including Citation Control Sheets and Transfer Listings, will be retained by all units in accordance with the Department directive entitled “Records Management” and the existing records-retention requirements.
  • B.All discontinued Violation Notice citation books which have been collected will be delivered to the Department of Finance:
    • 1.All unused Version 6.0 (green) Violation Notice citation books must be collected from each officer or ticket issuing agent, packaged and delivered to the Department of Finance for destruction.
