Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-06
Juvenile Intervention and Support Center
Issue Date:31 January 2013Effective Date:03 February 2013
Rescinds:Version dated 04 March 2006; S06-03
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues the Juvenile Intervention and Support Center (JISC) model for juvenile delinquency intervention and support.
  • B.continues the JISC as a prototype facility under the command of the Commander, Youth Investigations Division, Bureau of Detectives.
  • C.modifies the processing procedures for juvenile arrests occurring within those police districts serviced by a JISC facility.
II.Policy
It is the policy of the Chicago Police Department to closely examine each juvenile arrest and, in all cases where probable cause exists that the involved minor has committed an offense, determine, on an individual basis, the disposition approach that provides the greatest opportunities for delinquency intervention. In establishing the JISC model, the Department seeks to enhance the effectiveness of its juvenile delinquency intervention and prevention efforts through the use of a multi-disciplinary approach and expanded interagency partnerships with other juvenile justice agencies, as well as key agencies within the social service, health care, and education systems.
III.General Information
  • A.The Commanding Officer, JISC is responsible for directing and managing all police operations at the JISC; enhancing the effectiveness of the Department's operational partnerships with the juvenile justice, social service, and community agencies participating in JISC operations and programs; and coordinating concept development and expansion of the JISC model. In meeting these duties, the JISC commanding officer will:
    • 1.ensure that juvenile arrestees are processed consistent with this and related directives.
    • 2.maintain a manual of JISC Standard Operating Procedures.
    • 3.direct Department activities conducted under the JISC model.
  • B.The JISC service area includes Districts 002, 007, 008, 009, 010, 011, and 012.
  • C.JISC Arrest Processing Cases
    The JISC will be responsible for processing all juvenile arrests within the JISC service area except for incidents involving:
    • 1.the death or great bodily harm to any person;
    • 2.shots fired at or by the police;
    • 3.the possession or use of a firearm;
    • 4.any sex offense;
    • 5.all traffic citations, traffic warrants, and DUI investigations;
      In cases where a juvenile is to be processed at the JISC, as a result of delinquent criminal acts, in addition to traffic citations and/or warrants, ALL traffic citations will be sent to Traffic Court. Department members will:
      • a.complete all traffic related incidents in the district of arrest prior to the juvenile being processed on criminal charges.
      • b.follow procedures outlined in the Department directives entitled. "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" and "Traffic Court Citing and Scheduling."
      • c.list the court date, time, room number, citation number(s), and violation number(s) in the narrative portion of the Arrest Report.
    • 6.charges requiring the juvenile to be processed as an adult.
    • 7.Child Protection Warrants.
  • D.All juveniles arrested for a criminal warrant within the JISC service area will be processed at the JISC.
    EXCEPTION:
    Adults arrested for a juvenile warrant will not be processed at the JISC.
  • E.Only JISC case responsibilities will be handled at a JISC facility. "Walk-in" requests for service will be referred to the closest district station, except for the following:
    • 1.Emergency situations;
    • 2.Compliance with the Department directive entitled "Abandoned Newborn Infant Protection Act";
    • 3.JISC-related situations.
  • F.JISC Facilities and Adult Arrestees
    Adult arrestees will not be processed at a JISC facility. When adult offenders are arrested with one or more juvenile offenders in an incident that falls within the JISC scope, the arresting officers will ensure that:
    • 1.both adult and juvenile arrestees are transported to the district of arrest or area center, as appropriate, for initial processing; and
    • 2.juvenile arrestees are then transported to the JISC for final processing.
  • G.Area Special Victims Investigation Cases
    The corresponding area Violent Crimes Section, Bureau of Detectives, based upon the district of arrest, will retain the responsibility to process any juvenile offenders arrested from an incident listed in Item III-C, as well as any adults arrested on outstanding juvenile arrest warrants.
  • H.Transport of Juvenile Arrestees from the Field
    Arresting officers, regardless of their unit of assignment, will ensure all juvenile offenders arrested within the JISC service area are transported, without unnecessary delay, to the appropriate Department facility for processing consistent with this directive.
  • I.Unit Responsible for Follow-up Investigation
    Any required follow-up investigation will continue to be the responsibility of the appropriate investigative unit as set forth in existing Department directives (i.e. the area Violent Crimes Section, Property Crimes Section, Special Investigations Unit, Bomb Unit, Arson Unit, or the Central Investigations Division).
  • J.JISC Cases and District of Occurrence Investigations
    For incidents requiring an immediate investigation by the district of occurrence (for example, TRR Reports and vehicle pursuit investigations):
    • 1.the juvenile(s) will be transported directly to the district of occurrence until the district investigation is completed; and
    • 2.the juvenile(s) will then be transported to the JISC for processing.
  • K.Juvenile Arrests by Department Personnel Occurring Outside of Chicago
    When the district closest to the suburban location of a juvenile offenders arrest is within the JISC service area, arrest processing will occur at:
    • 1.the JISC, only when the nature of the incident is not among those listed in Item III-C.
    • 2.the appropriate Bureau of Detectives Area, when the nature of the incident is among those listed in Item III-C.
IV.Member's Responsibilities in JISC Juvenile Arrest Incidents
Department members, regardless of their unit of assignment, who arrest or transport a juvenile offender within the JISC service area, as identified in Item III of this directive, will:
  • A.ensure that any immediate medical needs of the involved arrestee are addressed, consistent with existing Department directives, prior to transporting the juvenile to the JISC.
  • B.transport the juvenile offender(s) directly from the location of arrest to the JISC facility.
    NOTE:
    Refer to the Department directives entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control," "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control," and "Hospitalized Arrestees" for additional information.
  • C.ensure that all required preliminary investigative activities, including all original case and supplementary reports, notifications, evidence-collection, inventory, and other required reports, are properly completed consistent with existing Department directives.
    NOTE:
    The notification requirement specifically includes a timely notification to the parent / guardian of the juvenile arrestee. Arresting officers will record the results of the parental notification in the narrative of the Arrest Report (CPD-11.420).
  • D.upon completion of the Arrest Report, report to the assigned JISC watch coordinator for review and acceptance of the report.
  • E.provide the JISC watch coordinator with all information and supporting documentation necessary for the JISC watch coordinator to confer with the on-duty station supervisor from the district of arrest relative to the initial approval of probable cause.
  • F.retain custody and direct responsibility for each juvenile arrestee brought to the JISC until initial approval of probable cause has been received and custody of the involved juvenile has been properly transferred to the assigned JISC personnel.
  • G.promptly return to service upon notification that JISC personnel have accepted custody of the juvenile.
V.JISC Watch Coordinator Responsibilities
The JISC Watch Coordinator will:
  • A.review for acceptance all case, arrest, and other pertinent reports and coordinate the initial approval of probable cause with the district of arrest station supervisor.
  • B.ensure juvenile arrestees are processed and, when necessary, coordinate transport to the Cook County Juvenile Detention Center or alternate, non-secure detention facility designated by the court in an expeditious manner.
  • C.conduct roll-calls and oversee all Department operations occurring at the JISC during the tour of duty.
  • D.conduct hourly inspections of the JISC Secure Waiting Area and at least one periodic inspection of the remainder of the facility during each tour of duty.
  • E.oversee the safety and security of the entire JISC facility and all persons at the center.
  • F.review and approve, as appropriate, all inventories prepared at the JISC for juvenile cases processed at the center.
  • G.prepare a daily log report for the tour of duty that:
    • 1.identifies any major or newsworthy incidents that occurred;
    • 2.lists any unusual occurrences;
VI.District of Arrest Station Supervisor Responsibilities
District of arrest station supervisors within a JISC service area will ensure that field personnel arresting juvenile offenders are directed to the appropriate Department facility and juvenile arrest processing occurs consistent with this directive.
  • A.Non-JISC Juvenile Arrests Occurring Within the District
    For juvenile arrests occurring within their assigned district, but not within the JISC scope as identified in Item III-C, the district of arrest station supervisor will review the corresponding arrest report for initial probable-cause and final-charge approval consistent with existing directives.
  • B.JISC Juvenile Arrest Processing Duties
    For each juvenile arrest processed at the JISC, the district of arrest station supervisor will:
    • 1.ensure the procedures established in Items VII and VIII of this directive are followed.
    • 2.review the Arrest Report for initial probable-cause and/or final-charge approval, as appropriate.
    • 3.consistent with the provisions of the directive entitled "Releasing Arrestees Without Charging and Waiving Fingerprint Results":
      • a.authorize release without charging any juvenile when there is insufficient reason to further detain said juvenile.
      • b.waive the fingerprint results for any juvenile when necessary to effectively process said juvenile and the expedited release is otherwise authorized.
  • C.After an initial approval of probable cause, the district of arrest station supervisor will review requests to modify the charges placed against a juvenile arrestee prior to final-charge approval.
VII.JISC Arrest Approval Procedures
Upon the review and acceptance of an arrest report for a juvenile processed at the JISC, the JISC watch coordinator will:
  • A.contact the district of arrest station supervisor via the PAX and summarize any additional and relevant information gathered during the preliminary review of the case at the JISC.
  • B.request the release without charging of any juvenile arrestee when there appears to be insufficient reason to further detain the arrestee.
  • C.when appropriate, request an approval from the district of arrest station supervisor to:
    • 1.modify the charges when subsequent facts and circumstances indicate alternate charges may be more appropriate.
    • 2.waive the fingerprint results for a juvenile arrestee processed at the JISC when necessary to effectively process the arrest and the identity of the juvenile has been otherwise established with certainty.
  • D.document the approvals or other actions by the district of arrest station supervisor consistent with Item VI of this directive.
VIII.JISC Arrest Approval and Release Without Charging Documentation
Determinations made by the district station supervisor of initial probable cause, final charge approvals, waiver of fingerprint results, and release without charging for JISC arrest cases, will be documented in the Automated Arrest Report following the procedures within the ICLEAR system.
IX.JISC Secure Waiting Area Procedures
Police officers and detention aides assigned to the JISC Secure Waiting Area will:
  • A.not accept any juvenile arrestee in need of emergency medical attention.
    NOTE:
    In meeting this duty, the "Screening Arrestee" provisions of the directive entitled "Detention Facilities General Procedures and Responsibilities" will be followed.
  • B.maintain continual care and custody of all juvenile arrestees within the JISC Secure Waiting Area.
    NOTE:
    While the JISC Secure Waiting Area is not a lockup, the provisions of the directive entitled "Detention Facilities General Procedures and Responsibilities" apply relative to the care and custody of juveniles within the Secure Waiting Area of the JISC.
  • C.perform those duties established for:
    • 1."lockup personnel" and "booking officers" in the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control" that relate to a corresponding function at the JISC.
    • 2.the district desk officer relative to the return of personal property to a juvenile arrestee that was inventoried at the JISC via the Personal Property Receipt Book.
  • D.follow any additional procedures established within the JISC Standard Operating Procedures manual.
X.JISC Processing Detective Responsibilities
The JISC Processing Detective is responsible for conducting a thorough review of each assigned juvenile arrest case relative to the facts and circumstances of the current arrest and the juveniles prior history of delinquency to determine the appropriate intervention approach. In meeting these duties, the assigned detective will complete a Bureau of Detectives Screening Instrument and:
  • A.if upon review of the case, a determination is made suggesting that the charges should be modified or the juvenile should be released without charges, the assigned detective will immediately notify the JISC watch coordinator.
  • B.when the facts of the case require referral of the case to Juvenile Court, follow existing procedures relative to detention screening and court referral.
  • C.for formal and informal station-adjustment cases that indicate the need for case management and support and upon the final approval of charges, conduct a parent/guardian station-adjustment conference and determine the referral.
  • D.cases requiring detention will continue to be processed through the Probation / Detention Screening System.
  • E.immediately submit the completed arrest package with all supporting documents for supervisory review, approval, and processing.
XI.JISC Processing Overflow and Bypass Procedures
In mass-arrest situations or other occurrences resulting in an influx of juvenile arrestees that have the potential to exceed the capacity of the JISC to safely operate and process juvenile offenders within a reasonable time:
  • A.The on-duty JISC watch coordinator will immediately notify and consult with the JISC commanding officer.
  • B.The Commanding Officer, JISC will:
    • 1.evaluate the conditions and make a determination whether:
      • a.JISC personnel can handle the influx through the use of extended tours of duty or additional Bureau of Patrol and/or Bureau of Detectives resources as needed; or
      • b.the JISC needs to be placed on a bypass status, diverting any additional juvenile arrestees to the corresponding Bureau of Detectives Area or an alternate police facility.
    • 2.notify and advise the Commander, Youth Investigations Division.
  • C.The Commander, Youth Investigations Division, will advise the Deputy Chief, Bureau of Detectives, and as appropriate, request:
    • 1.additional Bureau of Patrol and/or Bureau of Detectives resources; and/or
    • 2.the JISC be placed on a bypass status.
  • D.The Deputy Chief, Bureau of Detectives, will:
    • 1.in consultation with the Chief, Bureau of Detectives, direct the assignment of any additional Bureau of Detectives resources needed at the JISC.
    • 2.in consultation with the appropriate Area Deputy Chief, Bureau of Patrol:
      • a.request and coordinate the assignment of additional Bureau of Patrol resources.
      • b.if necessary, place the JISC in a bypass status, and:
        • (1)designate the alternate juvenile arrest processing site(s).
        • (2)ensure immediate notifications are made to:
          • (a)all involved units via the Administrative Message Center (AMC) message;
          • (b)the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC);
          • (c)Crime Prevention Information Center (CPIC).
  • E.The appropriate Area Deputy Chief, Bureau of Patrol, will ensure adequate Bureau of Patrol resources are made available to the Deputy Chief, Bureau of Detectives, relative to the juvenile arrestee influx.
XII.Conflicts Resolution
If a procedure in this directive conflicts with the Department directives entitled "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" or "Processing Persons Under Department Control," this directive will take precedence.
