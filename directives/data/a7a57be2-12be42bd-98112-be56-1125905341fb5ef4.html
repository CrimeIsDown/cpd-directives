Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-05
Unlawful Use of Weapons Arrests
Issue Date:15 April 2000Effective Date:15 April 2000
Rescinds:S00-05
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.defines the statutes for Unlawful Use of Weapons (UUW) violations.
  • B.informs Department members of the procedures to be followed when processing persons charged with UUW offenses.
II.Relevant Statutes
  • A.UUW Violation By a Convicted Felon - 720 Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) 5/24-1.1 (a)
  • B.UUW Violations - 720 ILCS 5/24-1(a)
  • C.Enhanced Penalties - UUW Violations at Specific Locations - 720 ILCS 5/24-1(c)
    • A.720 ILCS 5/24-1(d); The presence of an automobile
    • B.Firearm Owners Identification (FOID) Card
      • 1.FOID Card violations are misdemeanors.
      • 2.An arrestee being charged with a weapon violation will be asked to produce a FOID Card. The arrestees response, if any, will be recorded on the Arrest Report. If the arrestee claims to have a valid FOID Card and a custodial search of the arrestees person fails to locate an FOID Card, the arresting officer will clearly state this in the narrative portion of the Arrest Report. An arrestees registration status can be checked via the Hot Desk computer system.
    • C.Aggravated Unlawful Use of a Weapon - 720 ILCS 5/24-1.6.
III.Procedures for Processing Misdemeanor UUW Arrests
It is the policy of the Department to charge an arrestee to the fullest extent of the law that the facts of the case warrant. In the event the circumstances surrounding the incident do not warrant a felony UUW charge at time of arrest, the investigating officer will determine if a felony upgrade is possible based on the arrestees criminal history. In those instances, the following procedure will be followed.
  • A.Arresting officers will:
    • 1.conduct an Identification Record (IR) name check of the arrestee, by contacting the Identification Section by PAX line.
    • 2.request a copy of the Criminal History Sheet (CPD-31.903) from the Identification Section via fax.
    • 3.contact the Records Services Division, Instant Update Unit (IUU). Provide IUU with the arrestees name, address, date of birth, and social security number. IUU will advise arresting officer on previous convictions for possible upgrade in charges.
    • 4.notify the watch commander that a felony upgrade is likely based on the preliminary response of previous convictions.
      NOTE:
      If the arresting officer is unavailable when the Criminal History Sheet warranting an upgrade in charges arrives in the district, the desk sergeant will assume the responsibility of notifying the watch commander and ensure that the Assistant States Attorney, Felony Review is notified. Felony Review notification procedures will be followed as outlined in Department directive entitled "Felony Review by Cook County State's Attorney."
    • 5.contact the Gun Desk to check the registration of the gun.
    • 6.fax a completed copy of the Arrest Report to the Gun Desk.
    • 7.in the event of a felony upgrade prior to the end of their tour, complete all necessary paperwork for felony charges.
    • 8.in the event of a felony upgrade, fax a copy of the arrest report reflecting upgraded charges to IUU. Arresting officer will contact IUU via Pax or Citynet to advise them of the upgrade in charges.
  • B.The desk sergeant will:
    • 1.ensure that arresting officers complete a criminal history name check for anyone arrested for UUW violations.
    • 2.confirm that the watch commander has been notified that a felony upgrade may be likely, based on the preliminary response of previous convictions.
    • 3.ensure that arrestees who may have charges upgraded from a misdemeanor to a felony will not be let to bail, as directed in the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
    • 4.ensure that Felony Review is contacted for an upgrade when the arresting officer is no longer available.
    • 5.ensure that, in the event charges are upgraded, felony paperwork is completed and submitted with the arrest report when the arresting officers are no longer available.
    • 6.ensure that, in the event charges are upgraded, procedure delineated in item III-A-8 of this directive are followed.
    • 7.ensure that the arresting officer is notified when charges are upgraded and there is a change in the "Arr. Off. Desired Court Date / Branch-Call" box on the Arrest Report.
  • C.The Instant Update Unit will:
    • 1.provide information regarding arrestees criminal history convictions as requested by arresting officers.
    • 2.receive updated charges from districts and update CHRIS system accordingly.
