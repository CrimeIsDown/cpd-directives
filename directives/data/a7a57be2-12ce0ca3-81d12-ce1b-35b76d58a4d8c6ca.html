Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-22
Application For Police Officer (Assigned As Explosives Detection Canine Handler), Title Code 9153
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 09 May 2008; D08-17
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.delineates the duties, eligibility requirements, application procedures, selection process and training for the position of Police Officer (Assigned as Explosives Detection Canine Handler), title code 9153, hereinafter referred to an Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
  • B.informs Department members of reference material for the selection process for Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
  • C.continues the use of the intranet application.
II.Duties
The duties of an Explosives Detection Canine Handler vary by unit of assignment. An Explosives Detection Canine Handler, assigned as a:
  • A.Transportation Security Administration (TSA) certified explosives detection canine handler assigned to the Airport Law Enforcement Section will:
    • 1.conduct patrol throughout the airports.
    • 2.perform related duties consistent with the handler’s level of training.
    • 3.interact with the public during the performance of duties.
    • 4.interact with Federal, State, and local government agencies.
    • 5.utilize their canine to detect the presence of explosive substances.
    • 6.meet TSA annual certification standards.
    • 7.respond to high-risk incidents where there is a likelihood of an encounter with:
      • a.explosives or an explosives device.
      • b.aircraft incidents of an emergency nature.
    • 8.respond to in-progress and other calls, when feasible, including but not limited to:
      • a.ambulance requests.
      • b.assault and/or battery in progress.
      • c.assist checkpoint security.
      • d.assist outside agencies.
      • e.traffic stops.
      • f.suspicious materials on and off aircrafts.
      • g.unattended baggage.
      • h.vehicle searches.
      • i.weapons violations.
      • j.aircraft fuel spill.
      • k.auto fire on airfield.
      • l.bomb threat on and off aircraft.
      • m.disturbance on and off aircraft.
      • n.Level 1 hazardous material.
      • o.security breach.
    • 9.provide for the daily care and maintenance of their canine at work and at home through proper grooming, exercise, food, water, shelter, and veterinary care.
    • 10.maintain a level of physical fitness ensuring the handler is capable of performing long foot patrols with their canines, and are able to lift and carry their canines as necessary.
    • 11.engage in training exercises several times per week on aircraft, in terminals, in vehicles, in warehouses, and on airport property.
    • 12.transport their canine to the scene of search incidents.
    • 13.be on call 24 hours a day and subject to call back for circumstances including but not limited to:
      • a.bomb threats.
      • b.dignitary security searches.
      • c.unattended packages and/or bags.
      • d.searches in secured or TSA controlled areas of the airport.
      • e.responses to requests from Federal agencies for work anywhere within the City.
    • 14.respond to requests for service outside the City of Chicago and occasionally the State of Illinois with proper authorization.
    • 15.demonstrate a working knowledge of:
      • a.airport logistics.
      • b.airport operations.
      • c.FAA rules.
    • 16.provide emergency first aid for their canine, as necessary.
    • 17.address a variety of audiences relative to the function and mission of the Canine Unit.
    • 18.utilize a computer to complete and forward a daily worksheet to TSA.
  • B.A TSA certified explosives detection canine handler assigned to the Public Transportation Section will:
    • 1.conduct patrol throughout the public transit system including locations below ground or below water, at street level, and on elevated structures.
    • 2.conduct security sweeps of critical locations including but not limited to subway tunnels, elevated structures, platforms, trains, rail yards, buses, and bus terminals.
    • 3.utilize their canine to detect the presence of explosives substances.
    • 4.respond to dignitary protection assignments and search requested locations to locate and identify dangerous materials.
    • 5.participate in Visible Intermodal Protection and Response (VIPR) Team Missions to provide counter measures at critical locations. VIRP missions may be conducted in cooperation with TSA personnel.
    • 6.perform related duties consistent with the handler’s level of training.
    • 7.meet TSA annual certification standards.
    • 8.interact with the public during the performance of duties.
    • 9.interact with Federal, State, and local government agencies.
    • 10.provide the daily care and maintenance of their canine at work and at home through proper grooming, exercise, food, water, shelter, and veterinary care.
    • 11.maintain a level of physical fitness ensuring the handler is capable of performing long foot patrols with their canines, and are able to lift and carry their canines as necessary.
    • 12.respond to high-risk incidents where there is a likelihood of an encounter with explosives or explosives devices.
    • 13.respond to in-progress and other calls, when feasible, including but not limited to:
      • a.assault and/or battery.
      • b.assisting outside agencies.
      • c.suspicious materials.
      • d.unattended articles and packages.
      • e.bomb threats.
    • 14.engage in training exercises several times per weeks.
    • 15.be on call 24 hours a day and subject to call back for circumstances including but not limited to:
      • a.bomb threats.
      • b.dignitary security searches.
      • c.unattended packages and/or bags.
      • d.responses to requests from Federal agencies for work anywhere within the City.
    • 16.respond to requests for service outside the City of Chicago and occasionally, the State of Illinois with proper authorization.
    • 17.demonstrate a working knowledge of public transit system logistics which includes rail lines, el stations, and bus routes.
    • 18.provide emergency first aid for their canine.
    • 19.complete the CTA Rail Safety Class.
    • 20.utilize a computer to complete and forward a daily worksheet to the TSA.
    • 21.address a variety of audiences relative to the function and mission of the Canine Unit.
III.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, sworn members who are interested in applying for assignment as an Explosives Detection Canine Handler, must:
  • A.be a police officer below the rank of sergeant.
  • B.be willing and able to perform the duties of an Explosives Detection Canine Handler, as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • C.be able and willing to work any watch assignment and change work hours in accordance with the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement between the City of Chicago and the Fraternal Order of Police.
  • D.have a minimum of three years of continuous service as a police officer with the Chicago Police Department.
  • E.not have any sustained Complaint Register (CR) investigations during the past five years resulting in a suspension of more than ten days, or a record of three or more sustained CR investigations within five years resulting in suspensions of any length of time.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR investigations with findings of “No Disciplinary Action” will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances concerning discipline will be allowed to apply for the Explosives Detection Canine Handler position. Such applications will be considered if the candidate’s disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of a successful grievance such that the outcome of the grievance results in a disciplinary record that meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • F.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago.
  • G.be willing to forego days off on Saturdays, Sundays, and Holidays, if required.
  • H.have received performance ratings above the minimum rating score necessary to qualify for a step increase for each of the last four evaluations.
  • I.if currently appointed to a D-2A graded position, be willing to resign D-2A status and pay and accept D-2 status and pay upon appointment to the position of Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
  • J.purchase and maintain uniform parts and accessories unique to the Explosives Detection Canine Handler position.
IV.Additional Requirements
Sworn members interested in applying for assignment as an Explosives Detection Canine Handler, must:
  • A.meet residency requirements that include:
    • 1.providing documentation that large dogs are not prohibited by lease agreement or association rules if the candidate does not reside in a dwelling owned by that member.
    • 2.having a five foot fenced-in yard with sufficient space for a 4 x 12 kennel provided by the Department at the time of appointment to the position of Canine Handler.
    • 3.consenting to an inspection of the candidate’s yard and/or kennel to ensure the proper care and treatment of the Department canine.
      NOTE:
      The inspection will be conducted by a canine training supervisor. Inspections will be conducted annually. Unscheduled inspections will be conducted when just cause exists.
  • B.meet the following criteria if other canines are present in the residence:
    • 1.provide annual city dog license registration documentation to the unit commanding officer.
    • 2.provide annual certification documentation to the unit commanding officer for the following: Rabies, Distemper Combo (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus) Bordetella.
    • 3.maintain animals in kennels separate and secure from the Department canine’s kennel.
  • C.maintain his or her uniform and equipment as outlined in Department directive entitled “Uniforms/Citizen’s Dress and Personal Equipment.”
V.Application Procedures
  • A.The application for an Explosives Detection Canine Handler, can be obtained from the Department’s “Intranet Services” main page by selecting Bureau Sites, then under Bureau of Administration, select Human Resources Division. The member will select “Explosives Detection Canine Handler,” review and then accept or deny the terms of application and enter his or her PC number and star number in the format requested. A confirmation page will be displayed after the member has successfully applied and should be printed for the member’s records.
  • B.The application for an Explosives Detection Canine Handler consists of the on-line application (including the Terms of Application page), Medical Statement, and Physician’s Consent Form.
  • C.Candidates will:
    • 1.print out the Medical Statement, and Physician’s Consent Form;
    • 2.ensure the forms are properly completed;
    • 3.return the properly completed forms to the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section by the close of the application period. Members will be disqualified from the continuing in the selection process if completed forms are not received by the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, by the close of the application period.
  • D.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances such as military duty. To submit a proxy application, the candidate’s proxy will contact the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, before the close of the application process.
VI.Selection Process
  • A.The selection process consists of four phases: a written test, a physical agility test, a yard inspection, and an oral interview. Candidates must pass the written test to proceed to the physical agility test. Candidates who pass both the written test and the physical agility test will be scheduled for a yard inspection. Candidates whose residences meet the criteria as delineated in this directive will then be scheduled to participate in the oral interview phase of the selection process. Eligible candidates will be notified of the date, time, and location to report for each phase of the selection process.
  • B.The written test consists of multiple choice questions based on reference material identified in Item VII of this directive.
  • C.Candidates who pass the written test and score in the top 50 percent will be scheduled to participate in the physical agility test. The physical agility test will consist of the following activities:
    • 1.lift a 75 pound weighted bag and walk 50 feet, proceed up and down one flight of stairs, walk another 50 feet, and place the weighted bag in the rear of a Department canine vehicle without stopping, dropping, or setting the weighted bag down to rest.
    • 2.lift and swing a 75 pound weight at the end of a six-foot leash for three consecutive revolutions.
    • 3.jog a course consisting of five marker cones set twenty-five yards apart in a back and forth configuration within 22.00 seconds.
    • 4.interact with a Department canine in a controlled situation without signs of excessive apprehension and/or fear.
    • 5.perform a “field track” over a one mile course consisting of various types of terrain with 15 minutes and 55 seconds.
  • D.Candidates who pass each activity in the physical agility test will be scheduled for a yard inspection. The yard inspection consist of the following criteria:
    • 1.the yard being free from hazards.
    • 2.the yard being suitable to install a five foot fence.
    • 3.the yard is suitable for a 4 x 12 kennel provided by the Department.
  • E.Candidates who pass the yard inspection will be scheduled for an oral interview. The interview will be conducted by the unit commander, or designee, a canine coordinator, a canine supervisor, and a canine trainer. Candidates will be graded on:
    • 1.prior canine or canine related experience.
    • 2.Department experience.
    • 3.willingness to accept the duties and responsibilities of a Canine Handler.
    • 4.appearance and demeanor.
    • 5.communication skills.
    • 6.attendance record.
    • 7.complimentary history.
  • F.Members will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled to report for any portion of the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will excuse a member who has been scheduled for testing during duty hours, provided that the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor was notified by the affected member at least 48 hours prior to the scheduled testing.
VII.Reference Material
The following list is a suggested study guide for the written test for the position of Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
  • A.Department directive entitled “Canines as a Force Option.”
  • B.Department directive entitled “Incidents Involving Animals.”
  • C.Department directive entitled “Canine Teams.”
  • D.Education and Training Bulletin 01-7, entitled “Canine Teams.”
VIII.Eligibility List
  • A.Candidates will be graded on a numerical scale of zero (below average) through six (above average) in each of the categories listed in Item VI-C of this directive.
  • B.The total score will be divided by seven to determine the candidate’s final score.
  • C.The top 50 scoring candidates on the oral portion of the selection process will be placed on the eligibility list in rank order by their final score on the oral interview. Ties will be resolved by seniority.
  • D.The eligibility list may be stricken at any time at the discretion of the Department. Placement on the eligibility list is not a guarantee of appointment to the Explosives Detection Canine Handler position.
  • E.Management reserves the right to fill 20 percent of the recognized vacancies at its discretion without regard to seniority or rank score, provided that the position is filled by a candidate on the eligibility list.
IX.Training
  • A.As an Explosives Detection Canine Handler vacancies occur, candidates will be selected in rank order from the eligibility list to attend the training program.
  • B.Candidates selected for training will be detailed to the Airport Law Enforcement Section, Public Transportation Section, or Special Functions Division for the duration of the training.
  • C.Certified TSA candidates will undergo a 10 week training at Lackland Air Force Base in San Antonio, Texas.
  • D.Candidates may waive appointment to the position of Explosives Detection Canine Handler position, as long as the list is active.
  • E.The candidate’s disciplinary record will be reviewed to ensure it meets the criteria as enumerated in Item III-E of this directive and a check will be made for indebtedness to the City of Chicago prior to selection to begin training, and again prior to being assigned as a Canine Handler.
  • F.Candidates selected to the position as an Explosives Detection Canine Handler will:
    • 1.take and successfully pass a drug screening and a thorough medical examination, the scope of which will be determined by the Medical Services Section, immediately prior to the start of training.
    • 2.purchase and maintain a utility uniform.
    • 3.adjust their furlough period(s) if any segment(s) conflicts with the training program.
    • 4.successfully complete all aspects of the training program.
  • G.If, at any time during training it becomes apparent to the commanding officer, a supervisor, and/or training staff that an officer exhibits problematic behavioral traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required duties, or fails training program testing, the member will be terminated from the training program, will have his or her detail cancelled, and will be returned to his or her district or unit of assignment. Reasons for termination include, but are not limited to the following:
    • 1.demonstrating a lack of physical coordination to the degree that the member cannot perform the duties of a Canine Handler.
    • 2.displaying anxiety, apprehension, or an obvious fear of canines.
    • 3.not having the physical strength necessary to safely handle the canine.
    • 4.failing to keep pace with the canine.
    • 5.failing to demonstrate a sincere interest and/or concern for the canine.
    • 6.any act of cruelty and/or abuse directed at or upon the canine.
  • H.Only those members who have successfully completed the training will be eligible to fill the recognized vacancies as an Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
  • I.Selection to participate in the Explosives Detection Canine Handler’s training program will not constitute an appointment to the position of an Explosives Detection Canine Handler. Selection to participate in the training program will constitute a detail only.
  • J.At the time training commences, members must be in a full-duty status.
X.Additional Conditions
If, at any time after assignment to the Airport Law Enforcement Section, Public Transportation Section, or Special Functions Division, a member demonstrates a lack of knowledge, skills, or abilities necessary for the assignment, is unable to perform the essential duties of the position, fails to properly care for the Department canine, or exhibits problematic behavior, the member will be subject to review and removal from assignment to the Canine Unit as an Explosives Detection Canine Handler.
