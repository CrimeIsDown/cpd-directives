Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S07-01-01
Inventorying Jewelry
Issue Date:06 April 2001Effective Date:07 April 2001
Rescinds:G81-01-02
Index Category:Processing Property
I.Purpose
This directive outlines procedures for the separate inventory of all jewelry from any other type of evidence and/or property.
II.Responsibilities
  • A.Arresting / Recovering Member
    When a member recovers jewelry, the member will:
    • 1.secure the jewelry without delay and ensure its safekeeping.
    • 2.transport the property:
      • a.to the district station or area center in which the jewelry was recovered, or;
      • b.if assigned to a specialized unit, to the facility of that unit.
    • 3.separate the jewelry and use different sets of inventory forms if items of jewelry from one case differ in respect to:
      • a.time of recovery;
      • b.place of recovery;
      • c.classification as evidence or non-evidence;
      • d.manner obtained (search warrant, seizure, recovery, etc.);
      • e.owners or possessors.
    • 4.record the information in the Property Inventory in accordance with the Department directive entitled, "Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody."
    • 5.describe the jewelry, using words such as "yellow metal," "white metal," or "clear stone," or similar language.
    • 6.obtain an Evidence-Property Envelope (CPD-34.599-A) or Evidence-Property Envelope (CPD-34.599-B) to secure the jewelry. Complete the required information on the face of the Evidence-Property Envelope as follows:
      • a.Complete the boxes marked "Unit No." and "Prop. Inventory No." with the appropriate information.
      • b.Complete the Box marked "Date Received" with the date of recovery.
      • c.Leave blank the box marked "Manner Received."
      • d.Complete the box marked "Delivering Officer" with the name of the member who is submitting the jewelry for inventory.
      • e.Leave blank the box marked "ERPS Receiving Officer."
      • f.Complete the box marked "Contents-Describe" with the same description used on the Property Inventory form.
    • 7.obtain the signature of the desk sergeant (or in specialized units, the sergeant responsible for the inventory book) to indicate approval of the completed inventory.
    • 8.place the jewelry in the Evidence-Property Envelope in the presence of the desk sergeant. The desk sergeant will verify its contents and place his or her signature on the Property Inventory form and in the vertical box on the right-hand side of the envelope (where the words "Seal within White Area" appear). The inventorying officer will then sign in that same box.
      NOTE:
      Jewelry items which do not fit in either of the approved Evidence-Property Envelopes will be hand-carried to the Evidence and Recovered Property Section (ERPS) by the officer who inventoried them.
    • 9.in the presence of the desk sergeant, seal the envelope with the jewelry inside by centering the box containing the two signatures in the electric heat sealer and pressing down on the handle, ensuring that the Evidence-Property Envelope is fully sealed across the signature box of the envelope.
    • 10.distribute the copies of the Property Inventory as described in the Department directive entitled, "Inventory System for Property Taken into Custody," after first securely stapling Copy 1 (white) to the sealed Evidence-Property Envelope.
    • 11.turn over custody of the to the Evidence-Property Envelope desk sergeant or, in specialized units, to the sergeant responsible for the inventory book.
    • 12.on the relevant case report and/or Arrest Report, record the associated Property Inventory number in the appropriate box(es).
  • B.The desk sergeant will:
    • 1.verify:
      • a.that the contents in the Evidence-Property Envelope correspond to the description of the contents of the face of the envelope;
      • b.the property is properly packaged, and;
      • c.the Property Inventory is complete. The desk sergeant will place his or her signature (or approve the Inventory):
        • (1)in the vertical box on the right-hand side of the envelope (where the words "Seal within White Area" appear), and
        • (2)in the box provided on the Property Inventory.
    • 2.provide safekeeping for the sealed Evidence-Property Envelope by securing it along with inventoried monies.
    • 3.be responsible for the security of the inventoried jewelry until removed by the Police Document Services Section officer.
    • 4.accept incorrect or incomplete Evidence-Property Envelopes from the Police Document Services Section officer and ensure that the inventorying officer is contacted to correct and/or re-inventory the jewelry. The desk sergeant is responsible for providing security for the evidence until such time as it is properly inventoried.
  • C.The Police Document Services Section officer will:
    • 1.take possession of all inventoried Evidence-Property Envelopes and examine each to ascertain if it is properly sealed and the contents have been inventoried correctly.
    • 2.if the property is not properly inventoried or packaged, inform the desk sergeant or designee about the discrepancy. The Police Document Services Section officer will return the Evidence-Property Envelope to the desk sergeant or designee for correction or re-inventory by the inventorying officer.
    • 3.provide removal information as required on the Property Inventory.
    • 4.transport the sealed Evidence-Property Envelopes to the Mail Room of Central Police Headquarters, where they will be deposited in the money safe.
  • D.The Evidence and Recovered Property courier will:
    • 1.transport all Evidence-Property Envelopes from the Mail Room of Central Police Headquarters to ERPS.
    • 2.turn over all Evidence-Property Envelopes to the operational sergeant or designee for processing by ERPS personnel.
III.Jewelry as Part of an Arrestees Personal Property
  • A.Jewelry belonging to an arrestee will continue to be inventoried on a Personal Property Receipt.
  • B.An arrestee's jewelry which remains in the custody of the Department 48 hours after the arrestee has left Department custody will be inventoried using the procedure described in this directive.
