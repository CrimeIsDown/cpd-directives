I.Information Systems Development Group (ISDG)
  • A.Membership of the ISDG will consist of the following:
    • 1.Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT); who will serve as Chairman.
    • 2.Command staff member of the Research and Development Division; who will serve as Vice-Chairman.
    • 3.Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division or designee; who will serve as Secretary.
    • 4.designees from the Assistant Superintendent Offices for Operations and Administration, and each of the bureau deputy superintendents.
    • 5.General Counsel to the Superintendent.
    • 6.Director, Finance Division.
  • B.The ISDG is responsible for:
    • 1.determining overall priorities regarding the development of Department information system projects and related applications.
    • 2.recommending projects that are in the best long term interests of the Department for improving operations and management consistent with the overall direction established by the Superintendent and the City’s Department of Information Technology.
    • 3.assessing and identifying user needs.
    • 4.reviewing security policy.
    • 5.recommending policies and strategies which promote data integrity.
    • 6.recommending strategies which will promote the development and sharing of regional, state, and federal information system resources.
    • 7.conferring with the Director, Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) regarding any issues affecting communications systems.
  • C.The ISDG must meet at least quarterly and will conduct business only when a quorum of the membership is present. A quorum will consist of the Chairman or Vice-Chairman and three members.
  • D.The Managing Deputy Director, PSIT, as Chairman of the ISDG, is responsible for the following:
    • 1.documenting the automated informational needs of the Department.
    • 2.presenting information concerning new and innovative computer technologies and information systems which will satisfy Departmental needs.
    • 3.ensuring that major new services and software are developed to meet the priorities identified by the ISDG and authorized by the Superintendent.
    • 4.reporting to the ISDG the cost and status of projects and proposals to determine feasibility.
    • 5.preparing and updating a 5-Year Information Technology Plan.
    • 6.acting as the primary liaison with the City's Department of Information Technology.
  • E.The command staff member of the Research and Development Division, as Vice-Chairman of the ISDG, is responsible for the following:
    • 1.ensuring policies and procedures developed by the ISDG and authorized by the Superintendent are reflected in the Department’s directives.
    • 2.identifying possible grant funding for improving technologies and computerized systems.
  • F.The Managing Deputy Director, PSIT is responsible for reporting the findings of the ISDG to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services who, through the Assistant Superintendent, Administration will make formal recommendations to the Superintendent.
