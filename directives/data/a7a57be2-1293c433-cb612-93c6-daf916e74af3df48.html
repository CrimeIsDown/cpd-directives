Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-03-04
City Service Requests
Issue Date:08 January 2009Effective Date:08 January 2009
Rescinds:G09-01-05
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.describes the Chicago Police Department (CPD) 311 City Service Request (CSR) system to mange requests for City and nonemergency police services.
  • B.outlines the:
    • 1.responsibilities for requesting, tracking, and reporting the status of City services as part of the CAPS problem solving process.
    • 2.automated method of:
      • a.requesting various city services.
      • b.obtaining and receiving requests for nonemergency police services called into the 311 system by citizens.
  • C.establishes the responsibility of:
    • 1.each district community policing office for the processing of police-related requests.
    • 2.the Inspection Division to monitor and audit unit response to 311 CSRs.
II.General Information
  • A.An important component of CAPS is the support of other City service agencies in addressing graffiti, abandoned vehicles, and other neighborhood problems that contribute to crime and the public’s fear of crime. The City Service Request system provides a tool for police personnel to access City services, monitor the response to the request, and provide follow up to the community.
  • B.The Bureau of Patrol will have the primary responsibility for the tracking and monitoring of all:
    • 1.police initiated requests, and
    • 2.citizen initiated requests and the resolution of nonemergency police service received by the 311 system.
  • C.Those situations that pose a public safety threat and require immediate attention (e.g. cave-ins, nonfunctioning traffic signals, down live wires, broken water mains) will not require the completion of a CPD City Service Request. Officers responding to such situations will inform the Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) via radio and their supervisor of the situation and the services required.
  • D.The Chicago Police Department City Service Request (CPD-21.208) is a form used by Department personnel to request the services of other City agencies to address public safety and crime-related problems. This form will not be:
    • 1.distributed to the community for completion.
    • 2.used to report problems within Department facilities.
    • 3.used to request assistance from other units within the Department.
  • E.The 311 City Service Request System is a computer application that provides Department personnel access to City service requests and is used to:
    • 1.enter new requests for services.
    • 2.obtain requests generated by citizens for nonemergency police services.
    • 3.enter resolutions and/or actions taken to resolve requests for service.
    • 4.create statistical reports by beat and district, and
    • 5.view the status of City service requests that have already been entered.
  • F.311 Service Requests are electronically generated forms, work order requests, and reports created by Department personnel trained on the 311 CSR system.
III.Responsibilities for Initiating and Tracking City Sservice Requests
  • A.Department members who identify and/or collect information from the community concerning conditions that contribute to crime and disorder will:
    • 1.complete a CPD City Service Request for those conditions that can be alleviated by assistance from other City agencies.
      NOTE:
      Only one CPD City Service Request will be completed for each service request need except in the case of multiple abandoned vehicles, when a request form will be completed for each vehicle.
    • 2.submit the CPD City Service Request form to the district community policing office.
    • 3.inform the community policing sergeant about service requests on their beat that remain unresolved within a reasonable time frame.
      NOTE:
      Certain City services are seasonal in nature and will be resolved as conditions permit.
    • 4.respond to members of the community concerning the status of City Service Requests.
  • B.Sector/rapid response sergeants will:
    • 1.provide direction to district personnel regarding the completion of the CPD City Service Request form.
    • 2.confer with community policing office personnel regarding requests that require additional follow-up.
    • 3.ensure beat team officers respond to members of the community concerning the status of City service requests.
  • C.Community policing sergeants will ensure:
    • 1.CPD City Service Requests are reviewed for completion and proper categorization on a daily basis.
    • 2.the appropriate data is entered in the 311 CSR system from the CPD City Service Requests and the reference number is recorded onto the form.
    • 3.CPD City Service Requests are retained in the unit file for 180 days after the service requested is completed.
    • 4.City Service Requests Status reports are generated and distributed to:
      • a.the beat team sergeant on a monthly basis, prior to scheduled Beat Community Meetings.
      • b.the district management team on a quarterly basis.
    • 5.the City Service Requests Status Report is reviewed to identify trends and chronic problems.
    • 6.incomplete or inappropriate requests for service are returned to the originating Department member.
    • 7.service requests that are not resolved within a reasonable time frame are reported to the CAPS Implementation Office.
    • 8.a current copy of the City Service Request Status Report is maintained in the community policing office.
    • 9.the maintaining and updating of the unit City Service Request file.
  • D.Area Deputy Chiefs will ensure that:
    • 1.district commanders meet as needed with City agency representatives to review problems and issues of concern as facilitated by the CAPS Implementation Office.
    • 2.the area CAPS lieutenant monitors the response to 311 service requests for the area under their command.
IV.Responsibilities for Resolution of Police-Related Service Requests
  • A.Requests for police-related service that are called into 311 by citizens will be entered into the 311 CSR system under the category of “Police Miscellaneous.”
  • B.Police miscellaneous service requests will not be forwarded to other CPD units for completion, even if the other unit will be requested by district personnel to provide assistance or specialized service (i.e., requests for narcotics or gang intervention). Requests for assistance or service from other CPD units will be requested through the use of the Intra-Departmental Support Service Request (CPD-21.224). Districts are responsible for ensuring that the service request is properly handled and that actions taken to resolve the request are properly documented in the 311 CSR system.
  • C.District commanders will ensure that:
    • 1.all police-related service requests within their district boundaries are processed accordingly.
    • 2.personnel assigned to the community policing office access the 311 system during each tour of duty and retrieve or open new requests for police-related services.
    • 3.an appropriate member of their unit is assigned to investigate, resolve, and respond to each Service Request Single Work Order (SRSWO).
      • a.The service request will be completed with an acceptable and sufficient action within fifteen (15) days of the request being assigned to an investigating member.
      • b.If applicable, a second request for additional time to investigate the service request will be approved.
  • D.Community policing sergeants will:
    • 1.assign a member from the community policing office to access the 311 system during each tour of duty and retrieve or open new requests for police-related services.
    • 2.ensure that:
      • a.the police-related service requests are queried by beats and a SRSWO is generated for each police-related Service Request Number.
      • b.the Service Request Query Report is generated and forwarded to the district commander.
      • c.all SRSWO(s) are printed and cross-referenced with the Service Request Query Report, in preparation for distribution to the investigating member.
    • 3.disseminate the SRSWO(s) to the appropriate district personnel for investigation.
    • 4.review the completed SRSWO(s) to ensure that acceptable and sufficient actions were attempted and/or accomplished.
      • a.If acceptable and sufficient actions were accomplished, the sergeant will ensure that the disposition is entered into the 311 CSR system and the service request is closed.
      • b.If acceptable and sufficient actions were not accomplished, the sergeant will resubmit the SRSWO to the investigating member with an explanation for additional follow-up.
    • 5.when appropriate, review and approve requests for extensions by the member investigating service requests. An extension will be approved for an additional fifteen (15) days and the SRSWO will be resubmitted to the assigned investigating member.
      • a.The reason for the extension will be entered into the 311 CSR system.
      • b.The service request will be completed within fifteen (15) days after the extension approval. Any further request for an extension will require the approval of the district commander.
  • E.The member assigned to investigate and respond to a City Service Request will:
    • 1.investigate the service request, and within fifteen days, indicate a written explanation of the action taken in the bottom half of the SRSWO, and submit the SRSWO to the community policing sergeant.
    • 2.when the investigation cannot be completed within fifteen days, request an extension by making a photocopy of the SRSWO, indicating in the bottom half of the SRSWO the reason for the extension, and forward the photocopy to the community policing sergeant for approval.
      NOTE:
      An approved extension will provide the investigating member with an additional fifteen days for the completion of action to resolve the request. The service request will be completed and resubmitted to the sergeant assigned to the community policing office within the fifteen (15) day extension.
    • 3.upon receipt of a prior SRSWO which has been returned by the community policing sergeant with an explanation to reinvestigate, review the explanation and reinvestigate the service request.
  • F.The Commander, Inspection Division, will:
    • 1.ensure the monitoring and auditing of the timeliness or failure of a unit to respond to a 311 City Service Request.
    • 2.notify the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Patrol, of the current status of all 311 police-related complaints on a monthly basis.
