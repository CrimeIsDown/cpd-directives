Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-07-04
Traffic Crashes - Damage To Governmental Property
Issue Date:21 March 2013Effective Date:22 March 2013
Rescinds: 01 April 1999 Version; G98-01-05A
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive informs Department members of procedures for completing the Illinois Traffic Crash Report when a traffic crash involves the property of any governmental agency.
II.General Information
  • A.In all traffic crashes involving damage to public property, the investigating member will complete the “Damage to Property” section of the Illinois Traffic Crash Report SR 1050 indicating the owner as the applicable governmental agency (e.g. City of Chicago, Chicago Park District, Chicago Police Department, State of Illinois, etc.).
  • B.The Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC) Section 8-4-120 states “No person shall cut, injure, mark, or deface any public building, sewer, water pipe, or other city property, fixture or personal property, or any tree, grass, shrub, or walk in any public way or public park.”
  • C.When the damage involves City of Chicago property (as defined in Item II-B) and such damage is a result of a traffic law violation, members will issue a Personal Service Citation for the traffic law violation and complete a Cost Recovery Incident Form (CPD-61.230) following procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Potential Cost Recovery Incident-City of Chicago."
    NOTE:
    Department members will NOT issue a Personal Service Citation or an Administrative Notice of Violation (ANOV) for damage to City of Chicago property(MCC 8-4-120).
  • D.When damage involves another governmental entity’s property (e.g. State, Federal, etc.) and such damage is a result of a traffic law violation, Department members will cite the appropriate charge for the traffic violation and note the name of the other governmental agency in the complainant/witness box of the Personal Service Citation.
    NOTE:
    The Potential Cost Recovery Incident procedures will not apply when damage involves another governmental entity’s property.
