Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-15
Application for Police Officer Assigned as a Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team Member
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 03 November 2011
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive delineates the duties, eligibility requirements, application procedures, selection process, and training for a Police Officer Assigned as a SWAT Team Member.
II.Duties
Police officers assigned to the SWAT Team will perform responsibilities as delineated below after successfully completing all required SWAT training, to include achieving and displaying a requisite competency in all SWAT operator related tasks, subject to determination and appointment by the SWAT Commanding Officer.
  • A.SWAT Team Incident Duties
    Police officers assigned as SWAT team members will:
    • 1.provide a tactical response to high-risk incidents for which the potential for injury or loss of life is present and the circumstances are unusual and beyond the capabilities of a normal police response.
      NOTE:
      SWAT team members may be asked to respond to SWAT team incidents regardless of duty hours or status. Members may be exposed to inclement weather for long durations of time. During the course of an incident, the member must be prepared to stand, sit, kneel, and lie prone for long periods of time while maintaining and carrying equipment assigned to the specific task. Tasks may include but are not limited to surveillance, perimeter coverage, emergency assault, equipment responsibilities, and overwatch.
    • 2.respond to high-risk incidents including, but not limited to:
      • a.hostage situations,
      • b.barricaded subjects,
      • c.suicidal subjects,
      • d.high-risk warrant service,
      • e.Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD),
      • f.Chemical, Biological, Radiological, Nuclear, and Explosive (CBRNE) incidents,
      • g.waterborne SWAT incidents,
      • h.active-shooter incidents,
      • i.clandestine laboratory responses, and
      • j.terrorist incidents.
  • B.SORT Duties
    SORT duties include but are not limited to:
    • 1.conducting regular patrol duties in assigned vehicles and designated areas;
    • 2.responding to various incidents involving acts or threats of acts of violence;
    • 3.responding to School Violence Incident Plan (SVIP) incidents;
    • 4.conducting Department of Homeland Security checks;
    • 5.escorting dangerous and/or sensitive materials to specific locations for units within the Department and outside agencies; and
    • 6.providing dignitary protection as directed.
  • C.Training
    Police Officers assigned as SWAT Team members will:
    • 1.comply with training standards as determined by the SWAT Commanding Officer.
    • 2.attend training to obtain and maintain certifications that meet local, state, and national standards.
    • 3.travel to locations within and outside the City limits to participate in training programs. Training is conducted under conditions that duplicate what may occur on an actual incident.
    • 4.complete training that includes, but is not limited to, the following:
      • a.weapon-retention techniques with the M4 rifle as well as empty-handed defense tactics.
      • b.qualification with a handgun in full SWAT gear wearing Level B Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and a Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (SCBA) or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • c.qualification with the M4 rifle in full SWAT gear wearing Level B PPE and a SCBA or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • d.annual exposure in a gas house where the SWAT Team member is in an enclosed structure with a live chemical agent.
      • e.SWAT firearms proficiency standards outlined in item VI-A of this directive.
      • f.SWAT physical performance standards as outlined in Item VI-B and VI-C of this directive.
    • 5.be trained in and maintain operational knowledge of numerous manual and power tools and have the ability to utilize them in dangerous environments. Tools include, but are not limited to, the following:
      • a.K12 (a gas-powered carbide-blade saw),
      • b.oxy-acetylene torch,
      • c.oxy-thermal torch,
      • d.rotary hammers,
      • e.lobster tools,
      • f.one- and two-man rams,
      • g.Hurst rabbit tool, and
      • h.Halligan bar.
    • 6.be trained in and maintain operational knowledge and proficiency with less-than-lethal weapons including, but not limited to, the following:
      • a.Taser devices,
      • b.40 mm launcher (sponge, OC, CS, etc.),
      • c.drag-stabilized shotgun, and
      • d.noise-flash diversionary devices.
III.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, members who are interested in applying must:
  • A.be a sworn member below the rank of sergeant.
  • B.have a minimum of three years of continuous service as a sworn member with the Chicago Police Department.
  • C.be willing and able to perform the duties of a police officer assigned as a SWAT team member, as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • D.have received performance ratings consistent with the minimum score necessary to qualify for a step increase as defined in the Department directive entitled "Performance Evaluation System."
  • E.have an acceptable disciplinary record. The candidate’s disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (C.R.) investigations for misconduct resulting in suspensions of more than seven (7) days during the preceding 12 month period; or a record of three (3) or more sustained C.R. numbers resulting in suspensions of any length of time within the past five years.
    NOTE:
    Sustained C. R. investigations with findings of “No Disciplinary Action” will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances or Police Board cases concerning discipline will be allowed to apply for the position of police officer assigned as a SWAT team member. Such applications will be considered if the candidate’s disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of the grievance or the Police Board hearing so that the member’s disciplinary record meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • F.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago.
  • G.be able to work in confined spaces.
  • H.not be afraid of heights.
  • I.not have a beard or facial hair that would preclude the member from wearing a gas mask.
  • J.be willing to forego days off on Saturdays, Sundays, and holidays as required by operational needs.
  • K.be able and willing to work any watch assignment and change work hours in accordance with the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement between the City of Chicago and the Fraternal Order of Police.
  • L.if currently appointed to a pay grade other than D-1, be willing to resign their current status and pay and accept D-1 status and pay upon assignment to the SWAT Team.
  • M.be willing to reschedule a furlough period if it conflicts with the selection process.
  • N.not have been previously removed from assignment as a police officer assigned as a SWAT team member for performance or disciplinary reasons.
  • O.be in full-duty status at the beginning and throughout the selection process.
  • P.purchase and maintain approximately $2,500 in uniform parts and accessories unique to the assignment of a police officer assigned as a SWAT team member.
  • Q.have an acceptable attendance record. The candidate’s attendance record cannot reflect any unauthorized absences within the preceding 12 month period.
  • R.meet acceptable medical roll usage guidelines. If the candidate’s medical roll usage reflects nine (9) or more medical incidents and/or 45 days or more on the medical roll within the preceding three (3) year period, absent exceptional circumstances, or demonstrates a pattern of medical roll abuse or misuse (i.e., the candidate goes on the medical roll when assigned to an involuntary detail; the medical roll use is linked to furloughs, RDOs, or other time due to extend time off; or the candidate is found to have used the medical roll in an unauthorized manner or demonstrates other behaviors demonstrating medical roll misuse), the candidate will be disqualified.
    NOTE:
    Medical time used in relation to approved FMLA leaves, ADA accommodations, or IOD injuries cannot be the basis for disqualification.
NOTE:
The candidate’s disciplinary record and indebtedness record to the City of Chicago will be checked at the close of the application period, prior to selection to begin training, and again prior to assignment to the SWAT Team.

V.Selection Process - General Information
  • A.The selection process will consist of two phases.
    • 1. Phase One will consist of five stages:
      • a.Stage One - Firearms Proficiency Test (50% of Phase One score)
      • b.Stage Two - Pursuit / Rescue Climb and Tactical Obstacle Course (30% of Phase One score)
      • c.Stage Three - Physical Performance Test (20% of Phase One score)
      • d.Stage Four - Confidence Tests – water, height, and confined spaces (pass / fail only, not factored into Phase One score)
      • e.Stage Five - Oral interview
    • 2.Candidates must successfully pass each stage to proceed to the next stage and continue in the selection process. Stages One, Two, and Three will be scored and weighted as listed above. Upon successful completion of Stage Four - Confidence Tests, the top-scoring candidates will be determined and ranked by combining the weighted scores of Stages One, Two, and Three. Ties will be resolved by seniority and date of birth. The 50 top-rated candidates will be placed in rank order and scheduled for Stage Five - Oral Interview.
    • 3.Candidates who successfully complete the five stages in Phase One and are ranked within the 35 top scoring candidates will be scheduled to participate in Phase Two – Training Phase of the selection process. Scores from Phase One will not be used during Phase Two of the selection.
    • 4.Phase Two of the selection process will consist of two stages.
      • a.Stage One - Rifle School
      • b.Stage Two - Basic SWAT School
      NOTE:
      Stage One must be successfully completed to proceed to Stage Two and continue in the selection process.
  • B.Candidates will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled to report for Phase One of the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will excuse a candidate who has been scheduled for testing during duty hours provided that the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor was notified by the candidate at least forty-eight hours prior to the scheduled testing.
  • C.Candidates who successfully move to Phase Two of the selection process will be carried Code 49 during Phase Two of the selection process. At the conclusion of the testing process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
VI.Selection Process - Overview
  • A.Stage One – Firearms Proficiency Test.
    The Firearm Proficiency Test is a timed, 50-round course designed to test marksmanship, ability to follow instructions, and performance under pressure. Candidates who pass the Firearms Proficiency Test will be scheduled for Stage Two.
    NOTE:
    To participate in the Firearms Proficiency Test, candidates will be required to use their personal Department-approved registered duty weapon, field duty belt, and holster and provide their own ammunition, soft body armor, and eye and ear protection.

    NOTE:
    To qualify, candidates must achieve a score of at least 80% with a minimum of two head shots in Stage 4. Only one attempt to qualify will be permitted.
  • B.Stage Two – Consists of two events:
    • 1.Event #1 - Pursuit / Rescue climb
      The candidate must come to a full hang from a standard horizontal pull-up bar, with palms facing away. Utilizing upper-body strength only (no "kipping"), the candidate must pull up to a position where the chin is above the bar. While doing the pull-ups, the candidate must wear a SWAT vest with plates totaling 25 pounds. The candidate must perform at least two (2) pull-ups.
    • 2.Event #2 - Tactical Obstacle Course
      Wearing personal exercise clothing, the candidate must complete an 880-yard course in four minutes and forty-five seconds or less. The course will contain a 40-yard running weave between nine cones, a 10-yard rescue drag of a supine victim or "dummy", a second 40-yard running weave which includes dropping to the prone position behind each of the nine alternately placed cones, and a six-foot wall climb.
  • C.Stage Three – Physical Performance Test.
    During the Physical Performance Test, candidates are required to:
    • 1.Complete a 1.5 mile run in 13 minutes
    • 2.Complete 40 push-ups in one minute
    • 3.Complete 40 sit-ups in one minute
    NOTE:
    The above benchmarks represent minimum standards required to pass the test and continue in the selection process. Candidate scores will be determined based on performance in each of the three tasks comprising the Physical Performance Test. Candidates are encourage to exceed the minimum performance standards for each of the three aforementioned tasks as extra points will be awarded for performance beyond the minimum performance standards.
  • D.Stage Four – Candidates are required to pass a series of confidence tests to include a:
    • 1.Water Confidence test that consists of a 50 meter swim, treading water for 5 minutes, swimming under water for a distance of 10 meters, and removing SWAT gear while in deep water.
    • 2.Height Confidence test in which the candidate starts at the bottom of a 30 foot ladder and climbs a simulated commercial building exterior to the third story, climbs over the ledge of the window into the structure, climbs back over the ledge onto the ladder, and proceeds back down the ladder.
    • 3.Confined Spaces test during which the candidate will wear a gas mask and move through a confined location in low-visibility conditions.
  • E.Stage Five - Oral Interview
    • 1.Candidates will be notified of the date, time, and place to report for the interview. Candidates who fail to appear for the interview on the assigned date, time, and location will be disqualified and will not be rescheduled.
    • 2.The interview will be conducted by a minimum of three interviewers who are familiar with the duties of the position of police officer assigned as a SWAT team member.
      NOTE:
      Each panel member must be trained on City interview and consensus meeting procedures prior to conducting any interviews and/or participating in any consensus meetings.
    • 3.Each panel member will independently determine whether the candidate demonstrates competency of each dimension of the job addressed in the interview by checking whether the candidate clearly demonstrates competency; demonstrates some aspects of competency; or does not demonstrate competency. Each panel member will independently provide an overall summary of the candidate based on the interview criteria and then will independently indicate whether he or she recommends the candidate for hire; recommends the candidate with some reservations; or does not recommend the candidate for hire.
    • 4.After all candidates have been interviewed, the panel and the hiring manager will meet to conduct a consensus meeting. A representative from the Human Resources Division will attend the consensus meeting. At the consensus meeting, the panel members will discuss each candidate and reach a consensus on whether the candidate will be eligible for hire. Those candidates eligible for hire will be ranked and all selections will be made based on the rankings of those selected for hire.
    • 5.Candidates will be rated in the areas of:
      • a.Department experience.
      • b.willingness to assume the duties and responsibilities of a police officer assigned as a SWAT team member.
      • c.demonstrated initiative.
      • d.decision making.
      • e.appearance and demeanor.
      • f.communication skills.
      • g.attendance.
  • F.At the conclusion of the oral interview consensus meeting, the names of the 50 top-rated candidates will be placed in rank order on the eligibility list. Ties will be resolved by seniority and date of birth.
  • G.Candidates who successfully complete the five stages in Phase One and are ranked within the 35 top scoring candidates will be scheduled to participate in Phase Two – Training Phase of the selection process. During Phase Two – Training Phase, candidates will:
    • 1.be required to take and pass a drug screening, a thorough medical examination, and a psychological examination, the scope of which will be determined by the Medical Services Section. All medical examinations conducted as part of this selection process are performed by medical personnel contracted and paid by the Chicago Police Department.
    • 2.be carried Code 49 by their respective unit of assignment. At the conclusion of Phase Two of the selection process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
    • 3.voluntarily adjust their furlough selection if a segment conflicts with the training program.
    • 4.be required to successfully complete all aspects of the training program including rifle school and the basic SWAT School.
  • H.At any time during Phase Two – Training Phase, if it becomes apparent to the SWAT Commanding Officer or training staff that a candidate exhibits problematic behavioral traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required duties or fails training phase testing, the candidate will be terminated from the training phase and returned to their unit of assignment or detail. Reasons for termination include, but are not limited to, the candidate demonstrating a lack of physical coordination, tactical competence, stamina, or strength to the degree that the member cannot perform the duties as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
VII.Eligibility List
  • A.The top scoring candidates from Phase Two – Training Phase of the selection process will be placed on an eligibility list in rank order based on their final scores from Phase Two of the SWAT Team selection process.
  • B.Selection from the eligibility list will be subject to current operational needs of the SWAT Unit.
  • C.Placement on the eligibility list is not a guarantee of assignment to the SWAT Unit.
  • D.Management reserves the right to fill 20 percent of the recognized vacancies at its discretion without regard to seniority or rank score provided that the position is filled by a candidate on the eligibility list as described in Item VII-A of this directive.
  • E.The eligibility list may be stricken at any time at the discretion of the Department.
VIII.Additional Conditions
At any time during the selection process and/or assignment to the SWAT Unit, if a member demonstrates a lack of knowledge, skills, or abilities necessary for the job, is unable to perform the essential duties of the position as mandated in the SWAT Standard Operating Procedures, or exhibits problematic behavior, the member will be subject to removal from the SWAT Unit by the SWAT Team Commanding Officer.
