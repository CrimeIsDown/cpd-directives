Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-05
Dependent, Neglected, or Abandoned Children Coming Under Department Control
Issue Date:31 December 1998Effective Date:01 January 1999
Rescinds:G98-11-05
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive establishes procedures for processing dependent, neglected, or abandoned children who come under Department control.
II.Responsibilities
  • A.In child abuse
    Abused Minor
    X
    705 Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) 405/2-3(2)(*): Those who are abused include any minor under 18 years of age whose parent or immediate family member, or any person responsible for the minor's welfare, or any person who is in the same family or household as the minor, or any individual residing in the same home as the minor, or a paramour of the minor's parent:
    • A.Inflicts, causes to be inflicted, or allows to be inflicted upon such minor physical injury, by other than accidental means, which causes death, disfigurement, impairment of physical or emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function.
    • B.Creates a substantial risk of physical injury to such minor by other than accidental means which would be likely to cause death, disfigurement, impairment of emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function.
    • C.Commits or allows to be committed any sex offense against such minor.
    • D.Commits or allows to be committed an act or acts of torture upon such minor.
    • E.Inflicts excessive corporal punishment.
    or neglect
    Neglected Minor
    X
    705 ILCS 405/2-3 (1)(*): Those who are neglected include:
    • A.Any minor under 18 years of age who is not receiving the proper or necessary support, education as required by law, or medical or other remedial care recognized under State law as necessary for a minor's well-being, or other care necessary for his or her well-being, including adequate food, clothing and shelter, or who is abandoned by his or her parents or other person responsible for the minor's welfare without a proper plan of care.
    • B.Any minor under 18 years of age whose environment is injurious to his or her welfare.
    • C.Any newborn infant whose blood or urine contains any amount of a controlled substance as defined in subsection (f) of Section 102 of the Illinois Controlled Substances Act, as now or hereafter amended, or a metabolite of a controlled substance, with the exception of controlled substances or metabolites of such substances, the presence of which in the newborn infant is the result of medical treatment administered to the mother or the newborn infant.
    • D.Any minor under the age of 14 years whose parent or other person responsible for the minor's welfare leaves the minor without supervision for an unreasonable period of time without regard for the mental or physical health, safety, or welfare of that minor.

  • B.In dependency
    Dependent Minor
    X
    705 ILCS 405/2-3(*): Those who are dependent include any minor under 18 years of age:
    • A.Who is without a parent, legal guardian or legal custodian.
    • B.Who is without proper care because of the physical or mental disability of his parent, guardian or custodian.
    • C.Who is without proper medical or other remedial care recognized under State law or other care necessary for his or her well-being through no fault, neglect or lack of concern by his parents, guardian or custodian, provided that no order may be made terminating parental rights.
    • D.Who has a parent, guardian or legal custodian who with good cause wishes to be relieved of all residual parental rights and responsibilities, guardianship or custody, and who desires the appointment of a guardian of the person with power to consent to the adoption of the minor under Section 2-29.
    cases, the preliminary investigating officer will:
    • 1.render assistance to the victim by providing protection and medical treatment as necessary.
    • 2.determine the circumstances contributing to the dependent status of the minor child.
      • a.If the dependent status has resulted from non-criminal inability of the parent, guardian or custodian to provide basic care:
        • (1)prepare a General Offense Case Report (Primary Classification: Offense Involving Children (OIC); Secondary Classification: Dependency).
        • (2)contact the Youth Investigations Section, explaining the pertinent details of the situation.
        • (3)notify DCFS to initiate emergency assistance.
      • b.If the dependent status has resulted from a "lockout" (the child is not permitted by his parents to remain in the home):
        • (1)take protective custody of the child.
        • (2)notify the Youth Investigations Section for assignment of an investigator to interview the parents and the child.
        • (3)if attempts to reunite the parents and child fail, prepare a General Offense Case Report (Primary Classification: OIC; Secondary Classification: Neglect).
        • (4)turn the child over to the assigned investigator for follow-up investigation and intervention by DCFS.
  • C.Officers arresting a parent/guardian for a crime other than abuse or neglect will attempt to find temporary alternative placement of a minor child with a relative, as directed by the parent. If this is not possible, the officer will transport the child to the district station to seek placement, with the assistance of the Youth Investigations Section, through DCFS.
