Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S09-03-05
Freedom of Information Act
Issue Date:27 May 2021Effective Date:27 May 2021
Rescinds:01 January 2010 Version
Index Category:Information Management
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.defines certain terms which pertain to the Freedom of Information Act.
  • B.updates responsibilities and procedures for processing requests for public records.
  • C.continues the use of the revised Freedom of Information Request form (CPD-31.509).
  • D.includes Resources as guidance for Department members.
  • E.satisfies CALEA Law Enforcement Standard Chapter 82.
II.General Information
  • A.The Illinois Freedom of Information Act (Illinois Compiled Statutes 5 ILCS 140/) requires Public Bodies, such as the Chicago Police Department, to grant members of the public access to public records upon request. The statute states that "all records in the custody or possession of a public body are presumed to be open to inspection or copying."
  • B.Individuals seeking access to examine or obtain copies of public records maintained by the Department may submit their written request either:
    • 1.via the US Postal Service addressed to the Chicago Police Department, Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Unit, Unit 114;
    • 2.in person, Monday through Friday, at the Public Safety Headquarters Building, Records Inquiry Section of the Chicago Police Department, located at 3510 South Michigan Avenue, First Floor, from 0800 to 1500 hours, except holidays;
    • 3.via email at foia@chicagopolice.org;
    • 4.via facsimile to (312) 745-6948.
  • C.Freedom of Information Request forms may be obtained from the Records Inquiry Section desk or online.
  • D.Upon submission of a completed Freedom of Information Request, the individual has the right to inspect or receive copies of a record within a statutorily defined timeframe, except as exempted by law.
  • E.The Department is not obligated to create, maintain, or prepare any public record that was not maintained or prepared prior to the effective date of the Freedom of Information Act, except as required by law.
  • F.In accordance with the Illinois Freedom of Information Act and other applicable Illinois statutes, certain information must be redacted from reports prior to release to non-law-enforcement agencies.
III.Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Unit
  • A.The Freedom of Information Act Unit, a component of the Legal Affairs Division, is staffed by civilian FOIA officers and sworn supervisor personnel.
  • B.Freedom of Information requests must be received in writing and may be submitted via the Freedom of Information Request form (CPD-31.509). However, written requests in any format that include sufficient information to identify a responsive record will be fulfilled.
  • C.Any Department member in receipt of a written request to examine or obtain copies of public records maintained by the Department will IMMEDIATELY :
    • 1.open request and scan all contents to foia@chicagopolice.org, AND
    • 2.forward the entire request, including the envelope, to the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Unit, Legal Affairs Division, Unit 114, via intra-departmental mail.
  • D.When a Freedom of Information request is received, the assigned FOIA officer will process the request in accordance with the requirements of the Freedom of Information Act (5 ILCS 140/7) and, when applicable the exemptions of the FOIA statute.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.select two members under their command to act as the unit's designee to respond to Freedom of Information Act requests from the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) Unit, one primary and one secondary.
    • 2.ensure the names and contact information of the designated primary and secondary designees are provided to the sworn supervisor(s) of the Freedom of Information Act Unit, and updated quarterly.
    • 3.ensure the unit designee checks emails during each tour of duty for requests from the FOIA Unit, consistent with the Department directive titled "Use of the Internet."
    • 4.ensure the designee responds to Freedom of Information Act requests for records in a thorough and timely manner.
    • 5.ensure responses to requests from the FOIA Unit are accurate, complete, and, if appropriate, include a DETAILED FACTUAL BASIS for the exemption of records under the FOIA.
    • 6.ensure all responsive records are forwarded to the requesting Freedom of Information Act Officer within three business days of receipt from FOIA officer.
    • 7.ensure the assigned Freedom of Information Act officer is contacted as soon as it is determined that more than three business days are necessary to research or fulfill the request.
    • 8.ensure a DETAILED FACTUAL BASIS is provided for the exemption of the records under the FOIA.
      NOTE:
      ALL AVAILABLE RESPONSIVE RECORDS MUST BE PROVIDED to the Freedom of Information Act officer or Freedom of Information Act supervisory staff, unaltered and in their entirety, even when a DETAILED FACTUAL BASIS for exemption has been provided. All responsive records and DETAILED FACTUAL BASES for exemptions receive a comprehensive legal review before a final decision to release or withhold them. After a review and further consultations with the involved unit commanding officer or designee, along with the Department and City legal professionals, the FOIA Unit and Legal Affairs Division supervisory staff will make the final decision to release or withhold records per applicable exemptions.
    • 9.ensure the designee accurately completes affidavits addressing the depth, breath, and manner of searches conducted for responsive records in a timely fashion upon request from FOIA officers or FOIA sworn supervisory staff.
    • 10. ensure the designee conducts an extensive search to locate requested records.
    • 11.ensure the designee notifies the requesting FOIA officer if no responsive records can be found or located within three business days of receipt from FOIA officer.
    • 12.ensure both primary and secondary designees are aware of all open FOIA requests assigned to their unit in the event that one or the other is absent.
  • B.Department members designated to respond to FOIA requests will:
    • 1.check email during each tour of duty for requests from the FOIA Unit, consistent with the Department directive titled "Use of the Internet."
    • 2.upon receiving a request for a public record from the FOIA Unit:
      • a.conduct an extensive search to locate requested record(s).
      • b.contact the assigned FOIA officer as soon as it is determined that more than three business days are necessary to research or fulfill the request.
      • c.review any and all records located for accuracy and responsiveness.
      • d.forward completed copies of the responsive record(s), if applicable, to the requesting FOIA officer within three business days of receipt from FOIA officer.
      • e.notify the requesting FOIA officer if no responsive records can be found or located within three business days of receipt from FOIA officer.
    • 3.accurately complete affidavits addressing the depth, breadth, and manner of searches conducted for responsive records in a timely manner, upon request from FOIA officers or FOIA sworn supervisory staff.
    • 4.consult directly with the assigned FOIA officer or sworn supervisory staff for questions or clarification regarding FOIA requests.
    • 5.be aware of all open FOIA requests assigned to their unit in the event one of the designees is absent.
V.Fees
The Public Body has the authority to charge fees for records in accordance with the Illinois Compiled Statutes 5 ILCS 140/6.
  • A.Except when a fee is otherwise fixed by statute, the Illinois Freedom of Information Act allows the Department to charge fees for reproducing and certifying public records. However, no copying fee is charged for the first 50 pages of black and white, letter or legal sized copies.
  • B.Any fee charged for copying public records will be limited to the actual cost of duplication or publication. The cost of search, examination, review, and the deletion and separation of exempt from non-exempt information will not be charged, unless allowed under current statute.
    NOTE:
    After the responsive record(s) has been approved for dissemination and it has been determined that a fee is due, the assigned Freedom of Information Act officer will contact the requester in writing with an accounting of all fees due for the requested public records. Upon receipt of funds, records will be released.
  • C.Fees may be waived by the Public Body, as provided by the current statute.
