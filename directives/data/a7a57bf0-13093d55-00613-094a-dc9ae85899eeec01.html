Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-05-09
Gun Offender Registration
Issue Date:23 May 2014Effective Date:23 May 2014
Rescinds:20 April 2013 Version
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of the Gun Offender Registration Ordinance, Chapter 8-26 of the Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC).
  • B.provides instructions for processing persons taken into custody under the provisions of this ordinance.
  • C.continue the use of the Gun Offender Registration Notification (CPD-23.210).
  • D.introduces the:
    • 1.Gun Offender Registration - CPD Notification (CPD-23.214).
    • 2.Chicago Gun Offender Registration (CPD-23.216).
II.General Information
  • A.A gun offender,
    Gun Offender
    X
    Any person convicted of a gun offense that is subject to the provisions of chapter 8-26 of the Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC).
    who: (i) resides within the city of Chicago and (ii) on or after 20 April 2013 is convicted of or discharged or released from a federal, state or local correctional center for incarceration for a gun offense as defined in Section 8-26-010 of the Municipal Code of Chicago (MCC), must register with the Criminal Registration Section (CRS) within five (5) days of either:
    • 1.being released, if the gun offender receives a sentence of imprisonment, or
    • 2.the time sentence is imposed, if the sentence does not include imprisonment (MCC 8-26-020(a)(1)).
  • B.The Superintendent has the authority to promulgate rules and regulations for the implementation of the Municipal Code of Chicago: Gun Offender Registration Ordinance (MCC 8-26-090) and to prescribe forms and the information required.
  • C.The Gun Offender Registration Rules and Regulations can be found on the Chicago Police Department website: www.chicagopolice.org.
  • D.Bureau of Detectives, CRS, will administer the Gun Offender Registration Ordinance for the Department.
  • E.Any person registered as a gun offender before 20 April 2013, shall continue to comply with the registration requirements of the ordinance until such time that the gun offender is no longer required to register.
  • F.Every gun offender subject to the ordinance shall be given individual notice, in writing, of the gun offender's duty to register (MCC 8-26-055). This individual notification will normally be made by county or city prosecutors and/or department of corrections personnel.
  • G.A gun offender:
    • 1.must register in person with the Criminal Registration Section (CRS) at the City of Chicago, Public Safety Headquarters, 3510 S. Michigan Avenue, Chicago Illinois, Monday through Friday between 0800 and 1430 hours (excluding holidays).
    • 2.who did not reside in the city at the time when registration would otherwise be required pursuant to the ordinance and subsequent to the time becomes a resident of the city shall register within five (5) days of becoming a resident of the city.
    • 3. who did not register within the time required by the ordinance because the offender did not receive notice of the duty to register shall register within five (5) days of receipt of such notice.
    • 4.shall report in person no later than one year after the date of the initial registration and thereafter no later than one year after the date of each annual registration until such time that the gun offender is no longer required to register.
    • 5.must register, in person, any change in residency to CRS within five (5) days of such change.
    • 6.shall comply with the requirements of the ordinance, including the registration requirement, for a period of four (4) years after the date of his or her first registration.
  • H.CRS may be contacted for assistance and/or clarification on the bell at 312-745-5075 or Pax 0248.
III.RESPONSIBILITIES
  • A.When a subject is taken into custody for a Gun Offender Registration Ordinance violation, Department members will:
    • 1.ensure there are valid basis for the charges being placed.
    • 2.verify all required registration data by downloading the subject's most recent Gun Offender Registration (CPD-23.216) from the Bureau of Detectives homepage via the CLEAR intranet.
    • 3.access the registration data by clicking on Bureau Sites, then click on Detective Division, then click on "Searches," and then click on "Sex Hard Card."
    • 4.enter the registration number in the "SOR Number" box and submit query.
    • 5.follow the instructions to view the front and back of the registration.
      NOTE:
      Members will use the registration number found on the Hot Desk response.
    • 6.process the arrestee in accordance with the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
  • B.Department members will check the status of a possible convicted gun offender via a name check through the Hot Desk. The response to the Hot Desk inquiry may include a "Submit Information Report" response. Members will follow the below listed procedures when the Hot Desk response indicate one of the following possible status:
    NOTE:
    Department members will not search, detain or arrest based solely on a Hot Desk response.
    • 1.Registered
      • a.A subject is in compliance provided he or she has not changed residence since last registration. Department members will verify the subject's residence address and "Last Contact Date." If all the information is consistent with the response, no police action is required.
      • b.If the subject resides within Chicago at a different address than the one listed on the Hot Desk response and he or she resided at that residence for more than five (5) days, upon verification of required registration data, the subject will be taken into custody for violating the Duty to Report Change of Information MCC 8-26-050 ordinance. Document the violation in a General Offense Case Report using the "Primary" classification: Other Offense; "Secondary" classification: Gun Offender - Duty to Report Change of Information. The I-UCR code will be 5112.
      • c.If the "Last Contact Date" listed is over one year and the subject resides within Chicago, the subject is overdue for registration. Upon verification of required registration data. The subject will be taken into custody and charged with an Annual Registration violation MCC 8-26-030(b). Document the violation in a General Offense Case Report using the "Primary" classification: Other Offense; "Secondary" classification: Gun Offender - Annual Gun Offender Registration. The I-UCR code will be 5111.
    • 2.Not Notified/To Be Notified
      • a.If a Department member comes in contact with a subject who was not given formal notification of his or her duty to register, the member will:
        • (1)request a field supervisor to complete and serve the Gun Offender Registration - CPD Notification (CPD-23.214).
        • (2)detain the subject for a reasonable period of time to serve the notification.
        • (3)orally inform the subject of the duty to register. Document the notification in a General Offense Case Report using the "Primary" classification: Other Offense; "Secondary" classification: Gun Offender - Duty to Register. The I-UCR code will be 5110.
      • b.The field supervisor will:
        • (1)complete and sign the Gun Offender Registration - CPD Notification (CPD-23.214).
        • (2)request the subject to read and sign each form-set. If the subject refuses to sign, the supervisor will write refused in the gun offender's signature box.
          NOTE:
          The supervisor will inform the subject a signature only acknowledges his or her duty to register.
        • (3)complete the "Notifying Department Member" box.
        • (4)serve the subject with the yellow carbon copy.
        • (5)immediately fax a copy of the original form to CRS at 312-745-6839.
        • (6)forward the white copy (original) to CRS, Unit 180.
    • 3.Notified/Not Registered
      • a.If a subject has been notified, but failed to register as required by the ordinance, Department members will:
        • (1)verify if the subject presently resides within Chicago.
        • (2)If the subject resides within Chicago, upon verification of required registration data, the subject will be taken into custody and charged with violating: Gun Offender - Duty to Register MCC 8-26-020.
        • (3)document the violation in a General Offense Case Report. The "Primary" classification: Other Offense; "Secondary" classification: Gun Offender: Duty to Register. The I-UCR code will be 5110.
        • (4)document in the narrative section of the case report a description of the circumstances justifying the failure to register charges.
      • b.inventory all physical evidence (e.g. proof of residency) in accordance with procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Property Under Department Control."
      • c.fax a copy of all reports to CRS at 312-745-6839.
      • d.forward a copy of all reports to CRS, Unit 180.
    • 4.Moving
      • a.The subject has notified CRS of his or her intention to move outside Chicago.
      • b.If the member verifies that the subject resides within Chicago and the subject has been residing at that address for more than five (5) days, upon verification of the required registration data, the subject will be taken into custody and charged with violating the Duty to Report Change of Information MCC 8-26-050.
      • c.Document the violation in a General Offense Case Report using the "Primary" classification: Other Offense; "Secondary" classification: Gun Offender - Duty To Report Change of Information MCC 8-26-050. The I-UCR code will be 5112
      • d.Department members will follow procedures outlined in Item III-B-3-b through d of this directive.
  • C.If a non-registered gun offender is encountered and there is no Hot Desk response, Department members will follow procedures outlined in III-B-2:
  • D.If the gun offender is taken into custody for an unrelated incident requiring the completion of a case report, members will NOT complete an additional case report. Document the gun offender's information in the narrative section of the case report.
  • E.The station supervisor will ensure:
    • 1.the arrestee is a gun offender.
    • 2.the arrestee has been notified of the duty to register as a gun offender.
    • 3.evidentiary proof has been documented within the reports which indicates the arrestee presently resides within Chicago.
    • 4.all physical evidence is properly inventoried.
    • 5.Department members are following procedures listed in Item III of this directive.
    • 6.the arrestee is processed in accordance with the procedures outlined in this directive and the Department directive entitled “Processing Persons Under Department Control."
IV.BUREAU OF DETECTIVES RESPONSIBILITIES
  • A.Bureau of Detectives, Area Criminal Investigators will NOT conduct follow-up investigations for subjects taken into custody for Gun Offender Registration Ordinance violations.
  • B.Commanding Officer, Criminal Registration Section will:
    • 1.coordinate with the Public Safety Information Technology to establish and maintain a gun offender registry database.
    • 2.ensure:
      • a.the database contains information on all subjects required to register under the ordinance.
      • b.member(s) are available Monday through Friday, 0800 hours to 1430 hours to assist with complying with the requirements of registration as defined in the ordinance.
      • c.member(s) are designated as the point of contact person(s) for all outside agencies inquiring information about the gun registration process.
  • C.Members assigned to CRS will:
    • 1.be available to register gun offenders complying with the ordinance, Monday through Friday, 0800 hours to 1430 hours.
    • 2.input gun offender registration data into the Criminal Registration Application.
    • 3.obtain proof of identification and residence addresses.
    • 4.print the required form(s) and obtain a signature and initials acknowledging, receiving, and understanding the form(s).
    • 5.fingerprint the offender and submit the fingerprints and a copy of the registration form to the Identification Section.
    • 6.photograph offenders at all registrations.
    • 7.assist with updating the Department's gun registration database.
V.PUBLIC SAFETY INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY
Public Safety Information Technology will:
  • A.post the name and address of every registered gun offender on the Department's website.
  • B.maintain a searchable registry database, available to the public, with a mapping system which identifies registered gun offenders within 1/8, 1/4, and 1/2 miles of an identified area.
VI.Research and Development Division
The Research and Development Division will run data searches at the request of CRS to identify convicted gun offenders that may be required to register and will submit the detailed results to CRS.
VII.RECORDS DIVISION
The Records Division, upon receiving fingerprints of a registered gun offender will:
  • A.compare fingerprints to the gun offender's criminal history to confirm identification.
  • B.update the gun offender's IR sheet to reflect registration status.
  • C.issue an IR Number to gun offenders with no criminal history on file with the Department.
  • D.notify CRS when a registered gun offender has been arrested.
