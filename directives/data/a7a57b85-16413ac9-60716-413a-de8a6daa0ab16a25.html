Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D18-03
Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program—011th District
Issue Date:18 June 2018Effective Date:09 July 2018
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program for the 011th District;
  • B.informs Department members of program eligibility, disqualifying factors, and processing procedures;
  • C.establishes responsibilities pertinent to the pilot program;
  • D.introduces the Log of Persons Referred to the Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program—011 District form (CPD-21.185); and
  • E.satisfies CALEA Law Enforcement Standard Chapter 1.
II.Policy
A Bureau of Patrol member that makes an arrest for a quantity of controlled substances under one gram in total weight within the boundaries of the 011th District will screen the arrestee for eligibility into the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program. The Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program directs an eligible arrestee, upon consent of the arrestee, to a Department-approved addiction counselor. The Department-approved addiction counselor will direct the arrestee to a Department-approved treatment provider or social service agency in lieu of criminal charges.
III.Narcotics Arrest Diversion Program Eligibility
An adult arrestee will be eligible for participation in the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program ONLY when:
  • A.the arrestee is 18 years of age or older;
  • B.the arrest occurred within the geographic boundaries of the 011th District;
  • C.the arrest occurred 0700–1900 hours, seven days a week;
  • D.the arrest is for possession of a controlled substance in the amount of one gram or less in total weight; and
  • E.the arrestee indicates an addiction problem and consents to a consultation with a Department-approved addiction counselor for screening and entry into a treatment program.
IV.Program Disqualifiers
An individual meeting the program requirements outlined in Item III of this directive will be considered ineligible if the arrestee:
  • A.has no legal identification and identity cannot be established via a Department database;
  • B.is being charged with Delivery of a Controlled Substance;
  • C.utilized the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program on a previous arrest;
  • D.has a serviceable arrest warrant from any jurisdiction;
  • E.is being charged with any additional arrestable offense;
  • F.requires the completion of a Tactical Response Report (TRR);
  • G.requires medical treatment unrelated to narcotics addiction;
  • H.is named as the target on a search warrant;
  • I.has been convicted of illegal possession of a firearm;
  • J.has a felony conviction for a crime of violence;
  • K.is a convicted sex offender; or
  • L.has been convicted of any domestic-violence-related offense.
V.Procedures
  • A.When arresting an offender that meets the criteria established in Item III of this directive, the arresting officer will:
    • 1.transport the arrestee to the 011th District for processing;
    • 2.run the offender's name in the Hot Desk and LEADS to determine eligibility;
    • 3.secure the arrestee, complete the first screen of the CLEAR Automated Arrest Report, and save the report in "Preliminary Booking" status;
    • 4.inventory suspect controlled substance consistent with the Department directive titled "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence."
    • 5.evaluate the arrestee, review the arrestee's criminal history, and review the disqualifiers to determine arrestee eligibility;
    • 6.verify an arrestee's eligibility into the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program via the Hot Desk and document the verification in the narrative portion of the arrest report;
    • 7.if program eligible, notify the district station supervisor that the arrestee qualifies for the program;
    • 8.inform the Department-approved addiction counselor that an arrestee meets the eligibility requirements of the program;
      NOTE:
      If there is no available on-site Department-approved addiction counselor, the arresting officer will process the arrest consistent with the Department directive titled "Processing Persons Under Department Control" and document the circumstances in the narrative portion of the case and arrest reports.
    • 9.complete the CLEAR Automated Arrest Report and:
      • a.state "This is a Narcotics Arrest Diversion Program eligible arrest" in the narrative portion of the report;
      • b.document the name of the Department-approved addiction counselor and the approved affiliated treatment facility in the narrative of the report; and
      • c.submit the report to the district station supervisor and watch operations lieutenant for review and approval.
    • 10.take the arrestee into the lock-up for processing.
      NOTE:
      The arresting officer will inform lockup personnel that the arrestee is eligible for the Narcotics Diversion Program and should be placed in the lockup interior visitation cell after processing.
  • B.Lockup personnel will:
    • 1.process the program-eligible arrestee without delay;
    • 2.place the arrestee in the lockup interior visitation cell after processing;
    • 3.inform the counselor that the arrestee has been processed and is available to interview; and
    • 4.inform the district station supervisor (DSS) that the arrestee has been processed and is being interviewed by the counselor.
      NOTE:
      When the interview is concluded, the counselor will inform the DSS whether or not the arrestee has been accepted into the diversion program. The DSS will inform the watch operations lieutenant of the decision.
  • C.The watch operations lieutenant will:
    • 1.if the arrestee has been accepted into the program:
      • a.release the arrestee without charging consistent with the Department directive titled "Releasing Arrestees Without Charging and Waving Fingerprint Results."
        NOTE:
        Once an arrestee is released without charging (RWOC), the arrestee is considered released to the on-site Department-approved addiction counselor and no longer in custody.
      • b.not tow or impound an arrestee's vehicle if a narcotic charge is the only arrestable offense.
    • 2.if the arrestee has not been accepted into the program, process the arrestee consistent with the Department directive titled "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
VI.Responsibilities
  • A.The district station supervisor will:
    • 1.review all arrests and ensure compliance with this directive;
    • 2.maintain documentation of all arrestees accepted into this program;
      NOTE:
      The district station supervisor will use the Log of Persons Referred to the Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program—011 District form (CPD-21.185) to document arrestees accepted into the program.
    • 3.ensure all arrestees meeting the diversion criteria are RWOC and released to the custody of the approved counselor; and
    • 4.ensure the arrestee's released without charges Clear Automated Arrest Report is forwarded via email to the Records Division at fieldservices@chicagopolice.org.
  • B.The watch operations lieutenant will:
    • 1.review all arrests submitted under this program and ensure compliance with this directive;
    • 2.document the reason for RWOC as the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program in the comments section of the CLEAR Automated Arrest Report; and
    • 3.forward a daily list of all program diversions to the district commander.
  • C.The district commander will:
    • 1.continuously monitor subordinate activity to ensure compliance with this directive; and
    • 2.forward a report to the Chief, Bureau of Patrol, at the beginning of each month. This report will list the previous month program diversions.
  • D.The Records Division will:
    • 1.receive the notification from the district station supervisor;
    • 2.verify participant eligibility into the Bureau of Patrol Narcotics Arrest Diversion Pilot Program based on the criteria established in Item III of this directive;
    • 3.maintain a running log of eligible and ineligible participants;
    • 4.ensure all pertinent information is entered into the Hot Desk; and
    • 5.monitor fieldservices@chicagopolice.org email for all diversion-program-related documents.
VII.Pilot Program Evaluation
This program will be subject to review by the Chief, Bureau of Patrol, on a quarterly basis to determine operational effectiveness.
