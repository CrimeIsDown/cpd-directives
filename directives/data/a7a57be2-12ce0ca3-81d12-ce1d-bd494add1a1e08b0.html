Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-25
Application for Sergeant (Assigned as a Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team Member)
Issue Date:04 March 2014Effective Date:04 March 2014
Rescinds:21 February 2012 Version
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members that internet applications for Sergeant (Assigned as a Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team Member) are being accepted until 4 April 2014 at 1500 hours.
  • B.delineates the duties, eligibility requirements, application procedures, and the selection process for the position of a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
  • C.introduces the use of the internet application.
II.Duties
Sergeants (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) will perform duties in areas including supervision, as well as other SWAT team responsibilities as delineated below, after having successfully completed all required SWAT training, to include achieving and displaying a requisite competency in all SWAT operator and SWAT supervisory related tasks, subject to determination and appointment by the SWAT Coordinator.
  • A.SWAT Team Incident Duties
    Ensure that operators conduct SWAT team incident duties and responsibilities as designated in the Department directive entitled "Application For Police Officer (Assigned As A Special Weapons And Tactics (SWAT) Team Member)."
  • B.Special Operations Response Team (SORT) Duties
    Ensure that operators conduct SORT patrol duties and responsibilities as designated in the Department directive entitled "Application For Police Officer (Assigned As A Special Weapons And Tactics (SWAT) Team Member)."
  • C.Training
    Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) will:
    • 1.comply with training standards as determined by the SWAT Commanding Officer.
    • 2.attend training to obtain and maintain certifications that meet local, state, and national standards.
    • 3.travel to locations within and outside the City limits to participate in training programs. Training is conducted in all conditions so as to duplicate what may occur on an actual incident. Members participate in training exercises regardless of weather factors, lighting conditions, and time constraints.
    • 4.complete training that includes, but is not limited to the following:
      • a.perform weapon-retention techniques with the M4 rifle as well as empty-handed defense tactics.
      • b.qualify with a handgun in full SWAT gear wearing Level B Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), while wearing Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (SCBA) or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • c.qualify with the M4 rifle in full SWAT gear wearing Level B PPE, while wearing SCBA or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • d.be tested yearly in a gas house where the Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) is in an enclosed structure with a live chemical agent.
      • e.meet and maintain SWAT firearms proficiency standards, as outlined in Item VI-A of this directive.
      • f.meet and maintain SWAT physical fitness testing standards, as outlined in Item VI-B and VI-C of this directive.
    • 5.be trained in and must maintain operational knowledge of numerous manual and power tools and have the ability to use them in high stress and dangerous environments. Tools include but are not limited to:
      • a.K12 (a gas-powered carbide-blade saw),
      • b.oxy-acetylene torch,
      • c.oxy-thermal torch,
      • d.rotary hammers,
      • e.lobster tools,
      • f.one- and two-man rams,
      • g.Hurst rabbit tool, and
      • h.Halligan bar.
    • 6.be trained in and must maintain operational knowledge and proficiency with less-than-lethal weapons including, but not limited to:
      • a.Taser devices,
      • b.40 mm sponge rounds and OC rounds,
      • c.drag-stabilized shotgun, and
      • d.noise-flash diversionary devices.
    • 7.schedule and attend training.
    • 8.review and approve lesson plans, training logs, and other training documentation.
    • 9.conduct, document, and submit individual performance evaluations.
  • D.Additional Squad Leader Duties
    Upon assignment as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) and successfully demonstrating knowledge and understanding of all duties and responsibilities of team members and equipment, the appointed candidate, upon determination made by the SWAT Commanding Officer, will be tasked with the additional responsibilities of:
    • 1.supervise a squad of SWAT operators in the performance of assigned duties.
    • 2.ensure that SWAT operators complete required training.
    • 3.supervise and participate in the maintenance and inspection of equipment.
    • 4.assist in ensuring the security of vehicles, equipment, and weapons at job sites and Department facilities.
    • 5.provide training to SWAT Team Members on the use of vehicles, tools and other equipment.
III.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, members who are interested in applying must:
  • A.be a sworn member, rank of Sergeant, with a minimum of one year in grade.
  • B.be willing and able to perform the duties of Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • C.be willing and able to work any watch assignment and change work hours in accordance with the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement between the City of Chicago and the Police Benevolent and Protective Association (PBPA).
  • D.have an acceptable disciplinary record. The candidate's disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (CR) investigations during the past three years resulting in a suspension of more than five days, or a record of two or more sustained CR investigations within five years resulting in suspensions of any length of time. The above time periods will begin with the date of the alleged infraction and not the date the CR number was sustained.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR investigations with findings of "No Disciplinary Action" will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances concerning discipline will be allowed to apply for the position of Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member). Such applications will be considered if the candidate's disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of a successful grievance such that the outcome of the grievance results in a disciplinary record that meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • E.have an acceptable attendance record. The candidate's attendance record cannot reflect any unauthorized absences within the preceding 12 month period.
  • F.meet acceptable medical roll usage guideline. If the candidate's medical roll usage reflects nine (9) or more medical incidents and/or 45 days or more on the medical roll within the preceding three (3) year period, absent exceptional circumstances, or demonstrates a pattern of medical roll abuse or misuse (i.e., the candidate goes on the medical roll when assigned to an involuntary detail; the medical roll use is linked to furlough, RDOs, or other time due to extend time off; or the candidate is found to have used the medical roll in an unauthorized manner or demonstrates other behaviors demonstrating medical roll misuse), the candidate will be disqualified.
    NOTE:
    Medical time used in relation to approved FMLA leaves, ADA accommodations, or IOD injuries cannot be the basis for disqualification.
  • G.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago.
    NOTE:
    The candidate's disciplinary record and indebtedness record to the City of Chicago will be checked at the close of the application period, prior to selection to begin training, and again prior to assignment as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
  • H.be willing and able to work in small and/or confined spaces.
  • I.be willing and able to work at heights.
  • J.be willing and able to work in and around water.
  • K.not have a beard or facial hair that would preclude the member from wearing a gas mask.
  • L.be willing to forego days off and holidays as required by operational needs.
  • M.if currently appointed to a D-4 position, be willing to forego the D-4 status and pay and accept a D-3 status and pay upon assignment as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
  • N.be willing to reschedule a furlough period if it conflicts with training.
  • O.purchase and maintain approximately $3,000 in uniform parts and accessories unique to the assignment of a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
  • P.at the time training commences, candidates must be in a full-duty status.
  • Q.not have been previously removed for cause from any assignment in the SWAT Unit).
  • R.if applicable, have received performance ratings above the minimum rating score necessary to qualify for a step increase as defined in the Department directives entitled "Performance Evaluation System" for each of the last four evaluations.
IV.Application Procedures
  • A.The application for a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) is available on the Department intranet. The application can be obtained from the Department's "Intranet Services" main page by selecting Bureau Sites, then under Bureau of Administration, select Human Resources Division, then select Applications. The member will:
    • 1.chose "Sergeant (Assigned as a Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team Member);"
    • 2.review and then accept or deny the Terms of Application; and
    • 3.enter his or her PC number and star number in the format requested. A confirmation page will be displayed after the member has successfully applied and should be printed for the member's records.
  • B.The on-line application for Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) consists of the on-line application (including the "Terms of Application" page), Medical Statement, and Medical Consent Form.
  • C.Candidates will:
    • 1.print out the Medical Statement and Medical Consent Form,
    • 2.ensure the forms are properly completed,
    • 3.return the properly completed original forms to the Human Resources Division (Unit 123), Career Development and Performance Management Section, by the close of the application period. Members will be disqualified from continuing in the selection process if completed original forms are not received by the Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, by the close of the application process.
  • D.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances such as military duty. To submit a proxy application, the candidate’s proxy will contact the Human Resources Division, Career Development and Performance Management Section, at 312-745-5225 before the close of the application period.
  • E.Members who have applied for the position of Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) must be willing and able to perform the duties as outlined in Item II of this directive.
  • F.Members who have applied for the position of a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) and who meet the eligibility criteria as delineated in Item III of this directive, will be scheduled to participate in the Firearms Proficiency Test. These candidates will be notified of the date, time, and location to report for the Firearms Proficiency Test at a later date. Members who fail to appear for their assigned Firearms Proficiency Test date, time and location will be disqualified and will not be rescheduled.
V.Selection Process - General Information
  • A.The selection process will consist of two phases.
    • 1. Phase One will consist of Five stages:
      • a.Stage One - Firearms Proficiency Test (50% of Phase One score)
      • b.Stage Two - Physical Fitness Test (20% of Phase One score)
      • c.Stage Three - Physical Performances Test (30% of Phase One score)
      • d.Stage Four - Confidence Tests – water, height, and confined spaces (pass / fail only, not factored into Phase One score)
      • e.Stage Five - Oral interview - Top 50 candidates will be interviewed
    • 2.Candidates must successfully pass each stage to proceed to the next stage and continue in the selection process. Stages One, Two, and Three will be scored and weighted as listed above. Upon successful completion of Stage Four - Confidence Tests, the top-scoring candidates will be determined and ranked by combining the weighted scores of Stages One, Two, and Three. Ties will be resolved by seniority and date of birth. The 50 top-rated candidates will be placed in rank order and scheduled for Stage Five - Oral Interview.
    • 3.At the conclusion of the Oral Interview, candidates who are ranked in the top 25 positions will be scheduled to participate in Phase Two - Training Phase of the selection process. Scores from Phase One will not be used during Phase Two of the selection process.
    • 4.Phase Two - Training Phase will consist of two stages.
      • a.Stage One - Rifle School
      • b.Stage Two - Basic SWAT School, to include the SWAT Leadership Assessment class.
      NOTE:
      Stage One must be successful to proceed to Stage Two and continue in the selection process.
  • B.Candidates will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled to report for Phase One of the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will excuse a candidate who has been scheduled for testing during duty hours provided that the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor was notified by the candidate at least forty-eight hours prior to the scheduled testing.
  • C.Candidates who move to Phase Two of the selection process will be carried Code 49 during Phase Two of the selection process. At the conclusion of the testing process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
VI.Selection Process - Overview
  • A.Phase One - Stage One – Firearms Proficiency Test
    The Firearms Proficiency Test is a timed, 50-round course designed to test marksmanship, ability to follow instructions, and performance under pressure. Candidates who pass the Firearms Proficiency Test will be scheduled for Phase One - Stage Two.
    NOTE:
    To participate in the Firearms Proficiency Test, candidates will be required to use their personal Department-approved registered duty weapon, field duty belt, and holster and provide their own ammunition, soft body armor, and eye and ear protection.

    NOTE:
    To qualify, candidates must score an 80% with a minimum of two head shots in Stage Four (4). Candidates will only be allowed one attempt to qualify.
  • B.Phase One - Stage Two – Physical Fitness Test
    During the Physical Fitness Test, candidates are required to:
    • 1.Complete a 1.5 mile run in 13 minutes
    • 2.Complete 40 push-ups in one minute
    • 3.Complete 40 sit-ups in one minute
  • C.Phase One - Stage Three – Physical Performance Test
    • 1.Event #1 - Pursuit / Rescue Climb
      The candidate must come to a full hang from a standard horizontal pull-up bar, with palms facing away. Utilizing upper-body strength only (no "kipping"), the candidate must pull up to a position where the chin is above the bar. While doing the pull-ups, the candidate must wear a SWAT vest with plates totaling 25 pounds. The candidate must perform at least two (2) pull-ups.
    • 2.Event #2 - Tactical Obstacle Course
      Wearing personal exercise clothing, the candidate must complete an 880-yard course in four minutes and forty-five seconds or less. The course will contain a 40-yard running weave between nine cones, a 10-yard rescue drag of a supine victim or "dummy", a second 40-yard running weave which includes dropping to the prone position behind each of the nine alternately placed cones, and a six-foot wall climb.
      NOTE:
      The above benchmarks represent minimum standards required to pass the test and continue in the selection process. Candidate scores will be determined based on performance in each of the tasks comprising the Physical Fitness Test and Physical Performance Test. Candidates are encourage to exceed the minimum performance standards for each of the aforementioned tasks as extra points will be awarded for performance beyond the minimum performance standards.
  • D.Phase One - Stage Four – Confidence Tests
    • 1.Water Confidence Test. Consist of a 50 meter swim, treading water for 5 minutes, swimming under water for a distance of 10 meters, and removing SWAT gear while in deep water.
    • 2.Height Confidence Test. The candidate starts at the bottom of a 30 foot ladder and climbs a simulated commercial building exterior to the third story, climbs over the ledge of the window into the structure, climbs back over the ledge onto the ladder, and proceeds back down the ladder.
    • 3.Confined Spaces Test. The candidate will wear a gas mask and move through a confined location in low-visibility conditions.
  • E.Phase One - Stage Five - Oral Interview
    • 1.Candidates will be notified of the date, time, and place to report for the interview. Candidates who fail to appear for the interview on the assigned date, time, and location will be disqualified and will not be rescheduled.
    • 2.The interview will be conducted by a minimum of three interviewers who are familiar with the duties of the position of Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
      NOTE:
      Each panel member must be trained on City interview and consensus meeting procedures prior to conducting any interviews and/or participating in any consensus meetings.
    • 3.Each panel member will independently determine whether the candidate demonstrates competency of each dimension of the job addressed in the interview by checking whether the candidate clearly demonstrates competency; demonstrates some aspects of competency; or does not demonstrate competency. Each panel member will independently provide an overall summary of the candidate based on the interview criteria and then will independently indicate whether he or she recommends the candidate for hire; recommends the candidate with some reservations; or does not recommend the candidate for hire.
    • 4.After all candidates have been interviewed, the panel and the hiring manager will meet to conduct a consensus meeting. A representative from the Human Resources Division will attend the consensus meeting. At the consensus meeting, the panel members will discuss each candidate and reach a consensus on whether the candidate will be eligible for hire. Those candidates eligible for hire will be ranked and all selections will be made based on the rankings of those selected for hire. Disputes will be resolved by the hiring manager.
    • 5.Candidates will be rated in the areas of:
      • a.Department experience.
      • b.willingness to assume the duties and responsibilities of a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
      • c.demonstrated initiative.
      • d.decision making.
      • e.appearance and demeanor.
      • f.communication skills.
  • F.At the conclusion of the oral interview consensus meeting, the names of the 25 top-rated candidates will be placed in rank order on the eligibility list. Ties will be resolved by seniority and date of birth.
  • G.Candidates who are ranked within the top 25 positions, will be scheduled to participate in Phase Two - Training Phase of the selection process. During Phase Two – Training Phase, candidates will:
    • 1.be required to take and pass a drug screening, a thorough medical examination, and a psychological examination, the scope of which will be determined by the Medical Services Section, immediately prior to the start of Phase Two. All medical examinations conducted as part of this selection process are performed by medical personnel contracted paid for and contracted by the Chicago Police Department.
    • 2.be carried Code 049 by their respective unit of assignment for only Phase Two of the selection process. At the conclusion of Phase Two of the testing process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
    • 3.adjust their furlough period(s) if a segment conflicts with the training program.
    • 4.be required to successfully complete all aspects of the training program including a rifle school, the basic SWAT School, and the SWAT Leadership Assessment Class.
  • H.At any time during Phase Two – Training Phase, if it becomes apparent to the SWAT Commanding Officer, a unit supervisor, or training staff that a member exhibits problematic behavioral traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required duties or fails Training Phase testing, the supervisor or instructor will request the member be terminated from the Training Phase. The request must document the reason(s) for termination from training and will be submitted through their chain of command to the appropriate exempt commanding officer. Reasons for termination include but are not limited to, the candidate demonstrating a lack of physical coordination, tactical competence, stamina, or strength to the degree that the member cannot perform the duties as enumerated in Item II of this directive.
  • I.The exempt commanding officer will review and, if appropriate, approve any requests to remove a candidate from training. When a member is terminated from the training program, they will be returned to their district or unit of assignment.
VII.Eligibility List
  • A.The top scoring candidates from Phase Two - Training Phase of the selection process will be placed on an eligibility list in rank order based on their final scores from Phase Two - Training Phase of the Sergeant (Assigned as SWAT Team Member) selection process.
  • B.Selection from the eligibility list will be subject to current operational needs of the SWAT Unit.
  • C.Placement on the eligibility list is not a guarantee of assignment to the SWAT Unit.
  • D.The eligibility list may be stricken at any time at the discretion of the Department.
  • E.Management reserves the right to fill 20 percent of the recognized vacancies at its discretion without regard to seniority or rank score provided that the position is filled by a candidate on the eligibility list as described in Item VII-A of this directive.
VIII.Additional Conditions
  • A.At any time during assignment to the SWAT Unit, if a member demonstrates a lack of knowledge, skills, or abilities necessary for the job, is unable to perform the essential duties of the position, or exhibits any behavior that conflicts with the goals or mission of the unit, the member will be subject to removal from assignment as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member) and will be returned to their career service rank of Sergeant.
  • B.All assigned members of the SWAT Unit are required to demonstrate SWAT Unit proficiency on an annual basis. Failure to maintain proficiency will subject the member to removal from assignment to the SWAT Unit as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
  • C.Members must remain in a full-duty status to be eligible for continued assignment as a Sergeant (Assigned as a SWAT Team Member).
