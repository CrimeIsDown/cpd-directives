  • B.The AMFN will:
    • 1.be utilized to transmit reports that previously would have been sent, via special messenger.
    • 2.not be utilized to transmit documents or reports that are of a non-immediate nature and suitable for delivery, via the Police Document Service Section.
  • C.AMFN is separate from and not related to the facsimile transmission of Arrest Reports (CPD-11.420), the Nippon Electronic Communication (NEC) fingerprint facsimile network and the Direct Electronic Fingerprinting Network (DEFN). Procedures concerning these networks are outlined in the Department directive entitled "Processing Persons Under Department Control" and remain unaffected by this directive.

    • 1.All messages or documents received over the AMFN will be immediately referred to the unit commanding officer/watch commander.

      • a.screen, initial and date messages that are to be read at roll calls.
      • b.ensure that copies of facsimile messages are made and distributed to all units in facilities housing multiple units sharing the AMFN terminal.
      • c.ensure that facsimile messages are delivered to individuals/units in a timely manner.
  • E.Retention

IV.TRAINING
  • A.Only Department members who are trained in the operation of the AMFN terminals will operate AMFN equipment.
  • B.Requests for training will be made in writing through the chain of command to the Commanding Officer, Alternate Response Section.
  • C.The AMFN Chairman will schedule members for training in the operation of AMFN terminals upon receipt of a written request.
V.ACQUISITION
Requests for new or additional facsimile equipment or the relocation of existing facsimile equipment will be made by the unit commanding officer in a To-From-Subject report, through the chain of command, to the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services. Each report will:
  • A.include the approval of the Deputy Superintendent of the requesting bureau.
  • B.justify the request.
  • C.indicate the:
    • 1.type of equipment desired.
    • 2.location of the installation.
    • 3.name a contact person familiar with the request.
