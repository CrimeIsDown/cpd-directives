Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-01-03
Arrestees' Property Inventory Procedures
Issue Date:06 June 2002Effective Date:07 June 2002
Rescinds:G02-03-06
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.General Information
  • A.Lockup personnel will immediately search each arrestee brought into the lockup. Searches will be conducted by Department members of the same sex.
  • B.Strip searches
    Strip Search
    X
    The removal or rearrangement of some or all of the clothing of an arrestee to permit a visual inspection of the genitals, buttocks, anus, female breasts, or undergarments.
    will be conducted in accordance with the Department directive entitled "Conducting Field and Strip Searches."
  • C.Lockup keepers or other lockup personnel will inventory all personal property described in Item II of this directive and any other property not found during the initial custodial search by the arresting officer.
II.Lockup Search Procedures
  • A.Lockup personnel will inventory all coin monies, valuables, lighters, matches, medications, and items that could be used to effect an escape, deface or damage the detention facility, or cause harm to the arrestee or other persons detained within the lockup facility. Either an itemized Personal Property Receipt (CPD-11.502) or the Receipt Copy of the Property Inventory (CPD-34.523), as appropriate, will be issued to the arrestee. Except for non-evidentiary bulk items in the possession of an arrestee which were inventoried on the Property Inventory by the recovering / arresting officer, the inventoried personal property will be packaged in an unused polyethylene Personal Property Envelope(s)(CPD-11.500-A or CPD-11.500-B), depending on the size and amount of an arrestee's personal property.
  • B.An arrestee's shoelaces, tie, belt, scarf, or anything that could be used as a ligature will be taken from the arrestee upon entry in the lockup and be secured by using the Personal Property Receipt and the polyethylene Personal Property Envelope.
III.Inventory Procedures of Arrestee's Property
  • A.The lockup keeper or other designated member receiving an arrestee's personal property will:
    • 1.print legibly with a ballpoint pen on both the Personal Property Receipt and the Personal Property Envelope.
    • 2.sign the receipt and the envelope in the appropriate space provided.
  • B.Lockup personnel taking the property into custody will:
    • 1.ensure that the property owner sign the Personal Property Receipt in two places:
      • a.On the line immediately following the last item listed on the property listing area on the Owner's Receipt copy;
      • b.On the signature line provided on the Officer's Receipt stub.
    • 2.ensure a district desk officer or Central Detention member signs the receipt in two places:
      • a.On the signature line provided in the Owner's Receipt copy;
      • b.On the signature line provided on the Officer's Receipt stub.
    • 3.give the property owner the Owner's Receipt copy.
    • 4.attach the Officer's Receipt to the Personal Property Envelope.
    • 5.obtain the date of birth of the property owner from legal documentation or verbally and print it in the space provided on the back of the Detention Facility copy. The member may also include other numbered personal identifiers (driver's license number or social security number).
  • C.When the Department member cannot obtain the property owner's signature (inability or unwillingness to sign), the member will:
    • 1.write "refused" on the Detention Facility copy of the Personal Property Receipt across the space provided for the person's name and address. Department members will also sign their name and star number in the location where the arrestees would normally sign their name.
    • 2.ensure that the receipt copy is not returned to the property owner, but kept with the rest of the paperwork.
IV.Returning Property
  • A.Before the designated district desk officer or Central Detention designated member returns personal property to the owner, the member will:
    • 1.request that the owner submit the Owner's Receipt copy.
    • 2.verify the owner's birth date with the date written on the reverse side of the Detention Facility copy.
    • 3.have the owner sign his name and print his address on the reverse side of the Owner's Receipt copy of the back of the Detention Facility copy.
      NOTE:
      If the owner refuses to sign, the property will be not be returned but inventoried according to the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled "Inventory System for Property Taken Into Custody."
    • 4.compare and verify the owner from the birth date and/or physical description documented on the original receipt or compare the claimant's signature on the Officer's Receipt stub.
    • 5.sign the reverse side of the Owner's Receipt copy in the appropriate space or the back of the Detention Facility copy if the Owner's Receipt copy is missing.
    • 6.return the property directly to the owner.
    • 7.attach the Owner's Receipt copy to the corresponding Detention Facility copy in the Personal Property Book.
  • B.Upon completion of this process, the Department member will return the Personal Property Receipt Book to the lockup keeper or other designated member.
