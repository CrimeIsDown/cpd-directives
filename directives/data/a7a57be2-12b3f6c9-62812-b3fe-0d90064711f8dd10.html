Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S08-03
Traffic Crash/Pursuit Review
Issue Date:02 August 2013Effective Date:02 August 2013
Rescinds:01 June 2003 Version
Index Category:Professionalism
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.establishes the traffic crash/pursuit review process conducted at the district level.
  • B.establishes the Mid-Level Review Board and delineates its responsibilities.
  • C.continues the Traffic Review Board and delineates its responsibilities.
II.General Information
  • A.All vehicle pursuits and traffic crashes involving Department vehicles will be reviewed by a review board as delineated in this directive.
    NOTE:
    A traffic crash that occurs as result of a pursuit will be reviewed under the motor vehicle pursuit process.
  • B.For the purposes of this directive, the following definitions apply:
    • 1.Serious personal injury—an injury that results in death or admittance to a hospital.
    • 2.Significant property damage—damage to any vehicle to such an extent that it must be towed from the scene, as determined by the on-scene supervisor.
  • C.A review of vehicle crashes and pursuits will consist of the following:
    • 1.all related reports
    • 2.in-car camera video
    • 3.GPS data
    • 4.radio transmissions
    • 5.other sources as necessary.
  • D.The review process will:
    • 1.investigate and determine whether the actions of the involved Department member(s) were either in compliance or noncompliance within the policy and procedures of the Department.
    • 2.determine the classification level of a Department vehicle crash consistent with the Department directive entitled "Traffic Crashes Involving Department Members."
    • 3.recommend subsequent action as appropriate, including training, disciplinary action, or complimentary Performance Recognition System entries.
  • E.Field supervisors will enter incidents into the Performance Recognition System when the involved member(s) is found to be in compliance with Department policy for all motor vehicle pursuits.
  • F.The Superintendent or the Superintendent's designee retains final authority to direct any traffic crash or pursuit investigation to be reviewed by the Traffic Review Board.
  • G.The Commander, Inspections Division, will ensure audits are conducted of motor vehicle pursuits and traffic crashes to ensure consistency in application and compliance with Department policy.
III.District Level Review
  • A.Motor Vehicle Pursuits
    • 1.The district of the location where the motor vehicle pursuit was initiated will review motor vehicle pursuits for policy compliance and proper vehicle operation that result in all of the following:
      • a.no serious personal injury; and
      • b.no significant property damage; and
      • c.the total duration of the pursuit is three minutes or less; and
        NOTE:
        If a pursuit is terminated and subsequently continued for the same wanted vehicle, all pursuit incidents will be counted to determine the total duration in time, inclusive of any gaps.
      • d.the entire pursuit occurred within the boundaries of one district.
    • 2.Direct entry will be made into the Performance Recognition System when involved members are found to be in compliance with Department policy for all motor vehicle pursuits.
    • 3.Consistent with the Department directive entitled "Emergency Vehicle Operations—Pursuits" and other applicable directives, if a supervisor determines that any involved member(s) was not in compliance with Department policy relative to a motor vehicle pursuit, take the following actions, as appropriate:
      • a.initiate Summary Punishment or a Complaint Register Investigation and/or
      • b.recommend and request retraining by submitting a To-From-Subject report through the chain of command to the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division.
  • B.Motor Vehicle Crashes
    • 1.The district of the location where the motor vehicle crash occurred will review all motor vehicle crashes involving Department vehicles for policy compliance and proper vehicle operation that result in the following:
      • a.no serious personal injury; and
      • b.no significant property damage.
    • 2.Appropriate classification and supervisory action will adhere to the Department directive entitled "Traffic Crashes Involving Department Members" for all motor vehicle crashes.
  • C.Districts will only initiate disciplinary action for the pursuits and crashes that fit the criteria for district-level review. Disciplinary action will not be initiated for pursuits and crashes that will be reviewed by the Mid-Level Review Board and Traffic Review Board.
IV.Mid-Level Review Board
  • A.Board Structure and Operation
    The Mid-Level Review Board will consist of the following members:
    • 1.Executive Officer, Bureau of Organizational Development;
    • 2. An exempt Bureau of Patrol executive officer to serve on a rotating basis as designated by the First Deputy Superintendent; and
    • 3.A deputy chief or commander to serve on a rotating basis as designated by the First Deputy Superintendent.
  • B.The Executive Officer of the Bureau of Organizational Development will serve as the chairperson.
  • C.All board members will be equal voting members.
  • D.A quorum of the board will consist of two voting members.
  • E.Decisions of the board will be made by majority vote and authenticated by the signature of the board chairperson.
    NOTE:
    In the event of a tie in a quorum situation, the chairperson's determination is final.
  • F.The Commanding Officer, Traffic Section, will provide administrative support for the Mid-Level Review Board.
  • G.Responsibilities
    The Mid-Level Review Board will:
    • 1.meet at least bi-monthly or more as needed, as determined by the Chairperson.
    • 2.review motor vehicle pursuits for policy compliance and proper vehicle operation that involve:
      • a.significant property damage and no serious personal injury; or
      • b.no significant property damage and no serious personal injury in addition to one or more of the following:
        • (1)the total duration of the pursuit is more than three minutes;
          NOTE:
          If a pursuit is terminated and subsequently continued for the same wanted vehicle, all pursuit incidents will be counted to determine the total duration in time, inclusive of any gaps.
        • (2)the pursuit was unmonitored by a supervisor;
        • (3)the pursuit involved supervisory intervention from more than one district/unit;
        • (4)the pursuit crossed district boundary lines;
        • (5)the members involved in the pursuit were assigned to different districts/units; or
        • (6)the pursuit involved multiple crash locations not involving significant property damage.
    • 3.review motor vehicle crashes for policy compliance and proper vehicle operation that involve:
      • a.significant property damage; and
      • b.no serious personal injury.
    • 4.review all applicable motor vehicle pursuits and related motor vehicle crashes consistent with the procedures stated in Item II of this directive.
    • 5.consistent with the Department directive entitled "Emergency Vehicle Operations—Pursuits" and other applicable directives, if it is determined that a vehicle operator was not in compliance with Department policy relative to a motor vehicle pursuit, take the following actions, as appropriate:
      • a.initiate Summary Punishment or Complaint Register Investigation and/or
      • b.request retraining via a To-From Subject report through the chain of command to the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division.
    • 6.as part of each bi-monthly meeting, review a sample of pursuit and crash investigations conducted on the district/unit level to ensure consistency in application and compliance with Department policy.
      NOTE:
      Sample reviews conducted by the board that result in a finding that is not consistent with the district-level determination will be forwarded to the appropriate exempt commanding officer for further investigation and resolution.
V.The Traffic Review Board
  • A.Board Structure and Operation
    • 1.The Traffic Review Board will consist of the following members:
      • a.Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division;
      • b.A deputy chief or other exempt member of the Bureau of Patrol;
      • c.Executive Officer, Special Functions Division; and
      • d.Commanding Officer, Traffic Section.
    • 2.On an annual basis, the First Deputy Superintendent will designate a deputy chief or other exempt member of the Bureau of Patrol to serve on the board.
    • 3.The designated deputy chief or other exempt member of the Bureau of Patrol will serve as chairperson of the board.
    • 4.All board members will be equal voting members.
    • 5.A quorum of the board will consist of two board members.
    • 6.Decisions of the board will be made by majority vote and authenticated by the signature of the board chairperson.
      NOTE:
      In the event of a tie, the chairperson's determination is final.
    • 7.The Commanding Officer,Traffic Section, will serve as the board secretary.
  • B.Responsibilities
    The Traffic Review Board will:
    • 1.meet at least bi-monthly or more as needed, as determined by the Chairperson.
    • 2.review traffic crashes and motor vehicle pursuits for policy compliance and proper vehicle operation that result in the following:
      • a.serious personal injury;
      • b.incidents that cross jurisdictional boundaries; or
      • c.other traffic-related incidents as directed by the Superintendent.
    • 3.review all applicable motor vehicle pursuits and crashes consistent with the procedures stated in Item II of this directive.
    • 4.consistent with the Department directive entitled "Emergency Vehicle Operations—Pursuits" and other applicable directives, if it is determined that a vehicle operator was not in compliance with Department policy relative to a motor vehicle pursuit, take the following actions, as appropriate:
      • a.initiate Summary Punishment or a Complaint Register investigation and/or
      • b.request retraining to the Deputy Chief, Education and Training Division.
    • 5.determine the appropriate classification and supervisory action for all vehicle crashes consistent with the Department directive entitled "Traffic Crashes Involving Department Members."
    • 6.as part of each meeting, review investigations conducted by the Mid-Level Review Board to ensure consistency in application and compliance with Department policy.
      NOTE:
      Reviews conducted by the Traffic Review Board that result in a finding that is not consistent with the Mid-Level Review Board determination will be forwarded to the chairperson for further investigation and resolution.
