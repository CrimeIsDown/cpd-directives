Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-01-04
Involuntary Delivery of a Controlled Substance
Issue Date:08 February 2000Effective Date:09 February 2000
Rescinds:S00-01
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of the statutes governing crimes involving the involuntary delivery of a controlled substance, commonly referred to as a "date rape drug."
  • B.outlines procedures for the preliminary investigator and evidence technician for cases in which the victim may have been drugged.
  • C.introduces the Illinois Attorney General Toxicology Screen formsets:
    • 1.Part One entitled "Consent to Toxicology Screen"
    • 2.Part Two entitled "Receipt of Evidence for Toxicology Screen"
    • 3.Part Three entitled "Revocation of Consent for Toxicology Screen."
II.References
The following sections of the Criminal Code of the Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) apply to "date rape drugs":
  • A.Aggravated Battery, 720 ILCS 5/12-4(c),
  • B.Aggravated Criminal Sexual Assault, 720 ILCS 5/12-14(a)(7),
  • C.Predatory Criminal Sexual Assault of a Child, 720 ILCS 5/12-14.1(a)(3),
  • D.Aggravated Criminal Sexual Abuse, 720 ILCS 5/12-16(a)(7),
  • E.Aggravated Robbery, 720 ILCS 5/18-5 (a-5),
  • F.General Provisions, 720 ILCS 5/12-18(f-g).
III.General Information
  • A.Although a "date rape drug" can be any controlled substance, the most common substances used include:
    • 1.GHB (Gamma Hydroxybutyric Acid), also known as Liquid G or G; a Schedule I controlled substance, which is usually found as a colorless, odorless liquid, but can also be found as a white powder.
    • 2.Rohypnol (Flunitrazepam), also known as roofies; a Schedule IV controlled substance, which usually comes in one milligram, dime-sized tablets with a single score on one side, and the name of the manufacturer Roche and the number 1 or 2 encircled on the other side.
    • 3.Ketamine, also known as Special K, Vitamin K, K and Cat; a Schedule III controlled substance used by veterinarians as an animal tranquilizer, usually seen as a liquid, but also marketed in powder or capsule form.
  • B.Although it may be difficult to determine if a victim has been administered a date rape drug based on the victims account of events, indicators include:
    • 1.appearance of intoxication disproportionate to the amount of alcohol consumption;
    • 2.unexplained drowsiness and impaired motor skills;
    • 3.dizziness, confusion, impaired judgment and loss of inhibition;
    • 4.amnesia, complete or partial; and/or
    • 5.coma.
  • C.The best chance of obtaining a positive result from a urine test is when the specimen is obtained soon after the substance was ingested. Depending on the substance, the test can be reliable on urine collected as little as six to eight hours after ingestion.
  • D.The toxicology screen tests for the presence of alcohol and all controlled substances, including prescription medications and illegal drugs, such as cocaine and marijuana.
  • E.When a victim provides a urine sample for a toxicology screen, the victim has 48 hours to consent to the testing or to revoke the consent.
  • F.The Illinois Attorney Generals Office has developed a Toxicology Screen formset with three sections to document the consent of the victim, receipt of evidence by a law enforcement agency, and/or revocation of consent. Hospitals will maintain a supply of Toxicology Screen formsets. These will be turned over to law enforcement personnel with the urine sample.
    NOTE:
    It is the hospitals responsibility to contact the Program Administrator of the Illinois State Police Crime Laboratory to obtain additional forms. Department members are not responsible for supplying the hospital with forms.
  • G.According to 410 ILCS 210/1, only a person 18 years of age or older may consent to the toxicology screen. A parent or guardian must sign to provide or revoke consent to the toxicology screen for anyone under 18 years of age. If a parent or guardian cannot be located at the time the urine sample is given, the formset may be signed by the parent or guardian anytime within the 48 hour time period.
  • H.During the 48 hour period after the victim provides the urine sample, the Forensic Services Division will retain the Toxicology Screen formsets.
IV.Procedures
  • A.When conducting a preliminary investigation of a case in which there is reasonable cause to believe the victim has been drugged, in addition to the procedures outlined in the directive entitled Preliminary Investigations, the preliminary investigator will:
    • 1.explain the need for medical evaluation and treatment (even if the crime occurred days/weeks prior to reporting).
    • 2.identify/protect the crime scene including, but not limited to:
      • a.physical evidence on the victim or victims clothing,
      • b.the location where the crime/assault occurred,
      • c.the location where the drug was administered (e.g. party or bar).
    • 3.advise the victim about preserving evidence including, but not limited to:
      • a.providing a urine sample for toxicology screening,
      • b.drinking glasses or bottles which may have been used to administer a date rape drug,
      • c.medicines which might be overlooked in other types of cases.
    • 4.if appropriate, follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled Crime Scene Protection/Processing to request an evidence technician to process the crime scene.
    • 5.advise the victim about the availability of a toxicology screen and describe the consent procedures as follows:
      • a.The victim should provide a urine sample to emergency medical personnel as soon as possible, even if the victim is unsure whether or not to have the urine tested.
      • b.The victim may provide written consent to the toxicology screen at the time the sample is given, or at any time within 48 hours of giving the sample by signing the Consent to Toxicology Screen, which accompanies the urine sample to the Forensic Services Division.
      • c.If the consent form is signed at the time the urine sample is given, the victim may revoke the consent at any time within 48 hours of giving the sample by signing the Revocation of Consent for Toxicology Screen, which accompanies the urine sample to the Forensic Services Division.
        NOTE:
        If the victim is unable to travel to the Forensic Services Division to provide written consent or to revoke consent, the victim may call the Forensic Services Division to request that an evidence technician be assigned to hand-deliver the formset for the victims signature.
      • d.The urine sample will not be tested until at least five days have passed and a Consent to Toxicology Screen is received that was signed by the victim within 48 hours of giving the urine sample.
    • 6.prepare a General Offense Case Report (CPD-11.380) and:
      • a.enter the appropriate Offense/Incident Classifications and Offense Codes using the following table:
        Offense/Incident ClassificationI-UCR
        Primary BoxSecondary BoxOffense Code

        Crim. Sexual AssaultAggravated-Other Dangerous Weapon0264

      • b.indicate "involuntary delivery of controlled substance" in Box 58 entitled "Unusual Characteristics of Offense."
      • c.document in the narrative of the case report whether or not the victim provided a urine sample for the toxicology screen.
  • B.The evidence technician will:
    • 1.accept the urine sample from hospital staff and complete and sign Part Two of the Toxicology Screen formset entitled "Receipt of Evidence for Toxicology Screen." The evidence technician will use:
      • a.star number for "Officer ID#,"
      • b.CPD for "Law Enforcement Agency," and
      • c.The Forensic Services Division phone number for law enforcement agency phone number.
        NOTE:
        The urine sample will not be placed in or included as part of a sexual assault kit, if taken.
    • 2.ensure that Part One of the Toxicology Screen formset entitled "Consent to Toxicology Screen" has been completed by the hospital staff, including the name of the victim, the name of the hospital, and the date and time the urine sample was given, even if the victim has not signed.
    • 3.receive all three parts of the Toxicology Screen formsets.
      • a.If the victim has already signed Part One, "Consent to Toxicology Screen," the victim and hospital copies of Parts One and Two will be removed and submitted to hospital staff. (Hospital staff will witness the consent when the victim signs at the hospital.) The evidence technician will receive Part Three in its entirety.
      • b.If the victim has not yet signed Part One, Consent to Toxicology Screen, the victim and hospital copies of Part Two only will be removed and submitted to hospital staff. Parts One and Three will remain intact.
    • 4.inventory the urine sample and hand-deliver it and the Toxicology Screen formsets to the Forensic Services Division.
    • 5.when assigned, report to the Forensic Services Division to pick up the Toxicology Screen formset for hand-delivery to the victims location for signature. After the victim signs, the evidence technician will:
      • a.sign as a witness and record the exact date and time on the form, and
      • b.return the signed formset to the Forensic Services Division.
  • C.The Commander of the Forensic Services Division will:
    • 1.develop written protocol for:
      • a.maintaining formsets for written consent or revocation of consent by the victim during the 48 hour period after the urine sample was given.
      • b.matching signed formsets with the appropriate urine sample.
      • c.submitting urine samples and the canary-colored lab copies of formsets to the Illinois State Police Forensic Science Laboratory at Westchester.
      • d.notifying the Illinois State Police Forensic Science Laboratory when Part Three, "Revocation of Consent for Toxicology Screen," is signed by the victim within 48 hours of giving the urine sample, when the urine sample has already been forwarded to the Illinois State Police Forensic Science Laboratory.
    • 2.designate Forensic Services Division personnel to:
      • a.instruct victim callers who wish to sign either Part One or Part Three of the formset to report to the Forensic Services Division. If the victim is unable to travel to the Forensic Services Division, the designated member will assign an evidence technician to pick up the form from the Forensic Services Division and deliver it to the victim for signature.
      • b.sign as a witness and record the exact date and time on the form when a victim reports to the Forensic Services Division to sign either Part One or Part Three.
      • c.at the conclusion of the 48 hour period after the urine sample was given, forward the white law enforcement originals of all three parts of the Toxicology Screen formsets to the appropriate Area Detective Division for inclusion in the investigative file.
V.Requests for Toxicology Screen After the Conclusion of the 48 Hour Period
  • A.Unconscious Victims
    • 1.In the event that an unconscious person is brought into the emergency room and emergency room personnel have reason to believe that the victim may have been given a controlled substance without consent, hospital personnel may take a urine sample from the victim.
    • 2.Department personnel will follow the procedures outlined in Item IV.
    • 3.If the victim becomes conscious within the 48 hour period after the urine sample was taken, the victim may sign the consent form by contacting the Forensic Services Division.
    • 4.If the victim does not become conscious within that time, the victim may seek a court order at a later time to extend the waiting period based on the inability to consent while the victim was unconscious.
    • 5.Any Department member who encounters this situation will refer the victim to the States Attorneys Office, Felony Review Unit to pursue a court order to have the urine tested.
  • B.All Other Requests After the Conclusion of the 48 Hour Period
    • 1.In accordance with State statute, no victim will be allowed to sign either Part One, Consent to Toxicology Screen, or Part Three, Revocation of Consent for Toxicology Screen, after 48 hours have passed since the urine sample was given.
    • 2.Any Department member who encounters a victim wishing to provide consent or to revoke consent after the conclusion of the 48 hour period, (other than a victim described in Item V-A), will advise the victim that the allotted time period has expired.
