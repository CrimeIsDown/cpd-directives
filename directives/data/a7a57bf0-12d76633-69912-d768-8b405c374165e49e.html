Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D11-01
Online Case Reporting
Issue Date:08 August 2014Effective Date:11 August 2014
Rescinds:09 May 2014 Version
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of the online case reporting system for public use.
  • B.explains the procedures and responsibilities of Department members in processing these online reports.
II.Scope
The following incidents may be reported online if the offender is unknown:
  • A.Lost Property (except a firearm),
  • B.Theft Under $500 (except a firearm),
  • C.Theft Over $500 (except a firearm),
  • D.Retail Theft,
  • E.Financial Identity Theft Under $300,
  • F.Financial Identity Theft Over $300,
  • G.Criminal Damage to Property,
  • H.Criminal Damage to Vehicle,
  • I.Criminal Defacement,
  • J.Simple Assault.
III.General Information and Procedures
  • A.Offender must be unknown in all incidents reported online.
  • B.The loss, theft, or turn-in of a firearm may NOT be reported via online case reporting.
  • C.The application is available to the public at www.chicagopolice.org from the Chicago Police Department’s Online Reporting System.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.The Alternate Response Section will use beat numbers 9131-9139 when obtaining Records Division (RD) numbers for online reports and will:
    • 1.receive information for the reports via the online data collection system.
    • 2.determine the validity of the report.
    • 3.generate a Records Division (RD) number.
    • 4.send the reporting person the Records Division (RD) number via email.
  • B.The Bureau of Detectives will receive the report from the Alternate Response Section through standard procedures. Any report received by the Bureau of Detectives with reporting beats of 9131-9139 will have originated in the online reporting system.
  • C.The Department will establish a committee which will meet as needed and make recommendations to continue, expand, modify, or terminate this program. The committee will consist of the following members or their designees:
    • 1.Chief, Bureau of Patrol;
    • 2.Chief, Bureau of Detectives;
    • 3.Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology;
    • 4.Director, Research and Development Division;
    • 5.Commanding Officer, Alternate Response Section.
