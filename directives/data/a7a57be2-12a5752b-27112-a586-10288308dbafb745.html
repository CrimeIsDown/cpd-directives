Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S10-02-01
Criminal Street Gang Arrest Information
Issue Date:24 August 2004Effective Date:25 August 2004
Rescinds:S04-11-02
Index Category:Gang and Narcotic Abatement
I.Purpose
This directive establishes responsibilities and procedures for the entry of High Level Gang Conflict Locations information into the CLEAR System.
II.Procedures on Completing a Gang Arrest Card
  • A.All sworn members and lockup keepers are authorized to create an Automated Gang Arrest Card and access the Automated Gang Arrest Card application that runs a query on the Gang Listing Report

    NOTE:
    For those units using Automated Arrest application, the Gang Arrest Card can be created directly through the Automated Arrest application by clicking on the "Create Gang Arrest Card" link.
  • B.All gang arrest cards will be generated using the I-CLEAR Automated Gang Arrest Card Login, no matter the unit of assignment of the inputting member.
    • 1.A sworn member who arrests a person for any offense whose membership in a criminal street gang is substantiated or self-admitted will create an automated Gang Arrest Card.
    • 2.Prior to creating an automated Gang Arrest Card; an arrest report must be completed and approved and a Central Booking (CB) number obtained for the arrestee. Once a CB number is obtained, the arresting or assisting officers or the lockup keeper will create the electronic Gang Arrest Card, enter the necessary information, and save the record.
    • 3.Arresting or assisting officers can create and enter information into the Gang Arrest Card database at any time after the arrest.
  • C.Members may obtain additional information on procedures to enter information into the Gang Arrest Card application by reviewing the "Gang Arrest Card User Guide" available on the Departments Intranet.
  • D.Members should follow the provided link when accessing the automated arrest to gang arrest.
III.Responsibilities for Data
The Managing Deputy Director, Public Safety Information Technology Group, will ensure that records on each gang member are maintained and returned as required by law.
