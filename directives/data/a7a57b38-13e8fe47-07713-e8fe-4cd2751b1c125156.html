Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D13-07
Social Media Outlet: Twitter - Pilot Program
Issue Date:10 May 2013Effective Date:15 May 2013
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive introduces the Social Media Outlet:Twitter - Pilot Program.
II.Social Media Outlet: Twitter
  • A.Twitter is an online social networking and microblogging service that enables its users to send and read text-based messages known as "tweets".
  • B.Twitter is a means to connect the Department to the community in real time. The Department will use Twitter as a tool to build relationships with community members.
III.Scope
The pilot program is effective 15 May 2013 and will continue until further notice in the 007, 011, and 018 Districts.
IV.General Information
  • A.The Chicago Police Department is committed to serving the community and recognizes that current technology, when utilized properly, can provide the Department and community with an essential channel of communication.
  • B.The identified volunteer members in each participating district will maintain and update a district-specific authorized Twitter account and use the account to communicate with and engage members of the community in this casual forum.
  • C.Participation in the Twitter program is on a voluntary basis. Volunteer members will participate in the program only during on-duty hours and will not receive additional compensation for participating in the program.
V.Guidelines
  • A.Twitter postings should provide information specific to the participating member's district. Examples include: beat community meeting schedules; beat profile information; problem-solving success stories; crime prevention tips; and information regarding community events.
  • B.Participating members must follow the guidelines set forth in the Rules and Regulations of the Chicago Police Department and the Department directives including, but not limited to, "Use of Social Media Outlets," "Use of the Internet," and "Department-Issued Electronic Communication Devices."
  • C.Unauthorized advertising of private projects, products, or services will not be allowed.
  • D.The identified volunteer members will attend a mandatory social media training course prior to participating.
  • E.The identified volunteer members will be acting as an official representative of the Department and are expected to act accordingly.
  • F.Those members authorized to post district-specific Twitter entries/responses will do so using only a Department electronic device.
  • G.The Twitter pilot program training course will be developed and conducted by the Office of News Affairs. Furthermore, ongoing advice and consultation will be provided by the Office of News Affairs.
VI.Procedures
  • A.District commanders will:
    • 1.identify two members from a pool of volunteers to function as the district Twitter representatives.
    • 2.submit the names, ranks, star numbers, employee numbers, log-on ID numbers, and Department e-mail addresses of the Twitter representatives to the Office of News Affairs.
  • B.The district CAPS sergeant will review the respective district-specific Twitter postings on a regular basis and ensure:
    • 1.those making the District's Twitter account entries post:
      • a.district beat meetings and special events.
      • b.district success stories.
      • c.district crime alerts.
    • 2.that any posted information is current, correct, valid, and appropriate.
    • 3.that any posting deemed inappropriate is discontinued.
  • C.Office of News Affairs will:
    • 1.provide participating members with the Social Media Outlet:Twitter - pilot program training material.
    • 2.oversee the Twitter account transmissions.
    • 3.issue an AMC message to the pilot districts to cease making and responding to any Twitter communications pertaining to any newsworthy event which calls for a cohesive Department response.
  • D.Public Safety Information Technology (PSIT) will be responsible for:
    • 1.any technical aspects of the program.
    • 2.ensuring the program is in compliance with all record retention requirements.
VII.Conflict Provision
If this directive conflicts with the existing policy concerning the use and prohibitions of social media or acting as an official representative of the Department, including the policies outlined in Department directives entitled "Use of Social Media Outlets," "Use of the Internet," and "News Media Guidelines," this directive will take precedence.
