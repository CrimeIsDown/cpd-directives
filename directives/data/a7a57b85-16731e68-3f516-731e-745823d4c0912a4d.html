I.PURPOSE
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Chicago Police Department Data Integrity Group;
  • B.outlines the duties of the Data Integrity Group; and
  • C.lists membership in the Data Integrity Group.
II.DATA INTEGRITY
Data integrity combines the disciplines of data quality, data management, data policy management, and data methodology that ensures important data assets are formally managed throughout the Department. A comprehensive data integrity plan will enable the CPD, over time, to set central rules to maintain data quality and to obtain synchronized, cleansed, consistent, accurate, enriched, and high-quality data, delivering both immediate and long-term key consistency and accuracy benefits. Improved data integrity will provide consistently accurate data to researchers and the public, enable collaboration and information sharing between the Department and other agencies, support the use of analytics to strengthen intelligence-led and preventive policing, and enable officers to securely access critical information.
III.MEMBERSHIP
Membership in the Data Integrity Group will consist of the following (or his or her designee):
  • A.Advisory Group:
    • 1.Chief, Bureau of Patrol;
    • 2.Chief, Bureau of Detectives;
    • 3.Chief, Bureau of Counterterrorism;
    • 4.Executive Director, Office of Constitutional Policing and Reform;
    • 5.Chief, Bureau of Internal Affairs;
    • 6.The General Counsel to the Superintendent, Legal Affairs Division;
    • 7.Commander, Office of Community Policing;
    • 8.Commander, Youth Investigations Division.
  • B.Working Group:
    • 1.Managing Deputy Director, Information Technology, Public Safety Administration, who will serve as Chairperson;
    • 2.Chief, Bureau of Crime Control Strategies, who will serve as Vice-Chairperson;
    • 3.Director, Communications Division;
    • 4.Director, Research and Development Division;
    • 5.Commanding Officer, Strategic Data Analytics Section;
    • 6.Director, Information Technology, Public Safety Administration, who will serve as secretary;
    • 7.Director of Strategy, Office of Constitutional Policing and Reform;
    • 8.Director, Professional Standards and Compliance Division;
    • 9.Assistant Director, Audits and Inspections Division;
    • 10.Commanding Officer, Crime Prevention and Information Center.
    NOTE:
    The Data Integrity Working Group will meet at least quarterly.
IV.DUTIES OF THE DATA INTEGRITY Group
The Data Integrity Group will:
  • A.create a process for ensuring that key crime and performance indicators are measured and reported in a consistent manner, thereby increasing public confidence in the accuracy of the information provided by the Department.
  • B.develop an agreed-upon set of measurable guidelines when producing data reports.
  • C.make data and statistics available to the public in an intelligent, proactive, and technologically adept manner.
  • D.proactively make public data accessible, thereby reducing the labor-intensive production of ad hoc and individual reports produced in response to specific requests, especially in cases of FOIA requests.
  • E.report the findings of the group to the First Deputy Superintendent and Superintendent.
