Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-25
Application for Sergeant Assigned as a Special Weapons and Tactics (SWAT) Team Member
Issue Date:21 February 2012Effective Date:21 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 09 November 2009; D09-45
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive delineates the duties, eligibility requirements, application procedures, selection process, and training for a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member.
II.Eligibility
By the close of the application period, members eligible to apply must:
  • A.be a sworn member, rank of Sergeant, with a minimum of one year in grade.
  • B.be willing and able to perform the duties of a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member, as enumerated in Item III of this directive.
  • C.have received performance ratings consistent with the minimum score necessary to qualify for a step increase as defined in the Department Performance Evaluation System.
  • D.have an acceptable disciplinary record. The candidate's disciplinary record cannot reflect any sustained Complaint Register (CR) investigations during the past three years resulting in a suspension of more than two days, or a record of two or more sustained CR investigations within five years in suspensions of any length of time. The above time periods will begin with the date of the alleged infraction and not the date the CR number was sustained.
    NOTE:
    Sustained CR investigations with findings of "No Disciplinary Action" will not be considered as sustained violations for purposes of this selection process. Candidates with pending grievances concerning discipline will be allowed to apply for the position of Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member. Such applications will be considered if the candidate's disciplinary record is modified or expunged as a result of a successful grievance such that the outcome of the grievance results in a disciplinary record that meets the disciplinary standards set forth in this directive.
  • E.have satisfied all indebtedness to the City of Chicago.
    NOTE:
    The candidate's disciplinary record and indebtedness record to the City of Chicago will be checked at the close of the application period, prior to selection to begin training, and again prior to assignment as a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member.
  • F.be able to work in small and/or confined spaces.
  • G.not be afraid of heights.
  • H.not have a beard or facial hair that would preclude the member from wearing a gas mask.
  • I.be willing to forego days off and holidays as required by operational needs.
  • J.be able and willing to work any watch assignment and change work hours in accordance with the provisions of the collective bargaining agreement between the City of Chicago and the Police Benevolent and Protective Association (PBPA).
  • K.if currently appointed to a D-4 position, be willing to forego the D-4 status and pay and accept a D-3 status and pay upon assignment as a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member.
  • L.be willing to reschedule a furlough period if it conflicts with training.
  • M.purchase and maintain approximately $2,500 in uniform parts and accessories unique to the assignment of a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member.
  • N.at the time training commences, candidates must be in a full-duty status.
III.Duties
Sergeants assigned as a SWAT team member will perform duties in areas including supervision, as well as other SWAT team responsibilities as delineated below, after having successfully completed all required SWAT training, to include achieving and displaying a requisite competency in all SWAT operator and SWAT supervisory related tasks, subject to determination and appointment by the SWAT Coordinator.
  • A.SWAT Team Incident Duties
    Ensure that operators conduct SWAT team incident duties and responsibilities as designated in the Department directive entitled "Application For Police Officer Assigned As A Special Weapons And Tactics (SWAT) Team Member."
  • B.SORT Duties
    Ensure that operators conduct SORT patrol duties and responsibilities as designated in the Department directive entitled "Application For Police Officer Assigned As A Special Weapons And Tactics (SWAT) Team Member."
  • C.Training
    Sergeants assigned as SWAT team members will:
    • 1.comply with training standards as determined by the SWAT Coordinator.
    • 2.attend training to obtain and maintain certifications that meet local, state, and national standards.
    • 3.travel to locations within and outside the City limits to participate in training programs. Training is conducted in all conditions so as to duplicate what may occur on an actual incident. Members participate in training exercises regardless of weather factors, lighting conditions, and time constraints.
    • 4.complete training that includes but is not limited to the following:
      • a.perform weapon-retention techniques with the M4 rifle as well as empty-handed defense tactics.
      • b.qualify with a handgun in full SWAT gear wearing Level B Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), while wearing Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus (SCBA) or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • c.qualify with the M4 rifle in full SWAT gear wearing Level B PPE, while wearing SCBA or a gas mask, in low-light and other adverse conditions.
      • d.be tested yearly in a gas house where the Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member is in an enclosed structure with a live chemical agent.
      • e.meet and maintain SWAT firearms proficiency standards, as outlined in Item VI-A of this directive.
      • f.meet and maintain SWAT physical fitness testing standards, as outlined in Item VI-B of this directive.
    • 5.be trained in and must maintain operational knowledge of numerous manual and power tools and have the ability to use them in high stress and dangerous environments. Tools include but are not limited to:
      • a.K12 (a gas-powered carbide-blade saw),
      • b.oxy-acetylene torch,
      • c.oxy-thermal torch,
      • d.rotary hammers,
      • e.lobster tools,
      • f.one- and two-man rams,
      • g.Hurst rabbit tool, and
      • h.Halligan bar.
    • 6.be trained in and must maintain operational knowledge and proficiency with less-than-lethal weapons including, but not limited to:
      • a.Taser devices,
      • b.40 mm sponge rounds and OC rounds,
      • c.drag-stabilized shotgun, and
      • d.noise-flash diversionary devices.
    • 7.schedule and attend training.
    • 8.review and approve lesson plans, training logs, and other training documentation.
    • 9.conduct, document, and submit individual performance evaluations.
  • D.Additional Squad Leader Duties
    Upon assignment as a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member and successfully demonstrating knowledge and understanding of all duties and responsibilities of team members and equipment, the appointed candidate, upon determination made by the SWAT Coordinator, will be tasked with the additional responsibilities of:
    • 1.supervise a squad of SWAT operators in the performance of assigned duties.
    • 2.ensure that SWAT operators complete required training.
    • 3.supervise and participate in the maintenance and inspection of equipment.
    • 4.assist in ensuring the security of vehicles, equipment, and weapons at job sites and Department facilities.
    • 5.provide training to SWAT team members on the use of vehicles, tools and other equipment.
IV.Application Procedures
  • A.The application for a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member can be obtained from the Department's "Intranet Services" main page by selecting Bureau Sites, then under Bureau of Administration, select Human Resources Division. The member will:
    • 1.choose "SWAT Application";
    • 2.review and then accept or deny the terms of application; and
    • 3.enter their PC number and star number in the format requested. A confirmation page will be displayed after the candidate has successfully applied and should be printed for the candidate's records.
  • B.The on-line application for Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member consists of the on-line application (including the Terms of Application page), Medical Statement, and Physician's Consent form.
  • C.Candidates will:
    • 1.print out the Medical Statement and Physician's Consent form;
    • 2.ensure the forms are properly completed; and
    • 3.return the forms to the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, by the close of the application period. Candidates will be disqualified from continuing in the selection process if completed forms are not returned by the close of the application process.
  • D.Proxy applications will be allowed under limited circumstances such as military duty. To submit a proxy application, the candidate's proxy will contact the Human Resources Division, Career Development Section, before the close of the application period.
  • E.Candidates who have applied for the position and meet the eligibility criteria listed in Item II of this directive will be notified and:
    • 1.report to the SWAT Unit Monday through Friday, 0900-1700 hours, to receive a candidate information packet. Questions can be directed to the SWAT Unit.
    • 2.be scheduled for the firearms proficiency test. Eligible candidates will be notified of the date, time, and location to report for each phase of the selection process.
V.Selection Process - General Information
  • A.The selection process will consist of two phases.
    • 1.Phase one will consist of three stages:
      • Stage One - Firearms proficiency test (40%)
      • Stage Two - Physical performance test (30%)
      • Stage Three - Oral interview (30%)
      NOTE:
      Each stage must be successfully passed in order to proceed to the next stage and continue in the selection process. Each stage will be scored and carry the weight listed above. Upon completion of the oral interview stage, the top-scoring candidates will be determined and ranked by combining the weighted total scores of the first three stages for each candidate.
    • 2.Candidates who score in the top ten (10) positions will be allowed to attend phase two and continue in the selection process. Scores from phase one will not be used during phase two of the selection.
    • 3.Phase two of the selection process will consist of three stages.
      • Stage One - Rifle school
      • Stage Two - Confidence testing (water, height, and confined space)
      • Stage Three - Basic SWAT school, to include the SWAT Leadership Assessment class
      NOTE:
      Each stage must be successfully passed in order to proceed to the next stage and continue in the selection process.
  • B.Candidates will not be compensated for overtime when scheduled to report for phase one of the selection process during off-duty hours. The station supervisor / designated unit supervisor will excuse a candidate who has been scheduled for testing during duty hours, provided that the station supervisor / designated unit supervisor was notified by the candidate at least forty-eight (48) hours prior to the scheduled testing.
  • C.Candidates who successfully move to phase two of the selection process will be carried Code 049 while completing phase two of the selection process. At the conclusion of the individual testing process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
VI.Selection Process - Overview
  • A.Stage One - The firearm proficiency stage is a timed 50-round course designed to test marksmanship, ability to follow instructions, and performance under pressure. Candidates who pass the firearms proficiency phase will be scheduled for the physical performance phase.
    NOTE:
    Candidates will be required to use their own Department-approved / registered duty weapon, Department-approved field duty belt, provide their own ammunition, soft body armor, and eye and ear protection to participate in the firearms proficiency test.
    Firearms Qualification
    This is a fifty (50) round course that requires three (3) magazines.

    Candidates must score an 80% with a minimum of two head shots. Candidates will only be allowed one attempt to qualify.
  • B.Stage Two - During the physical performance stage candidates will be required to:
    • 1.Complete a mile and a half run in 13 minutes
    • 2.Complete 40 push-ups in one minute
    • 3.Complete 40 sit-ups in one minute
    NOTE:
    The above standards are the minimum to pass the test; additional points may be awarded for superior performance.
  • C.Stage Three - The oral interview will be conducted by bona fide SWAT operators selected by the SWAT Coordinator to include, the SWAT Coordinator, the Assistant SWAT Coordinator and a minimum of two (2) outside SWAT representatives. Candidates will be graded in the areas of:
    • 1.Department experience;
    • 2.SWAT team experience and/or military equivalent, SWAT schools and/or military equivalent;
    • 3.willingness to assume the duties and responsibilities of a Sergeant assigned as a SWAT team member;
    • 4.demonstrating initiative;
    • 5.decision-making;
    • 6.appearance and demeanor;
    • 7.communication skills;
    • 8.complimentary history, education, attendance, and additional skills.

    Preferred candidates will have demonstrated prior law enforcement or military training and/or experience in SWAT operations and skills that fall within the prescribed duties outlined in Item III.
  • D.Candidates who successfully complete phase one and are ranked within the top ten (10) positions will move on to phase two of the selection process. During phase two, candidates will be required to voluntarily:
    • 1.take and pass a drug screening, a thorough medical examination, and a psychological examination, the scope of which will be determined by the Medical Services Section, immediately prior to the start of phase two. All medical examinations conducted as part of this selection process will be performed by medical personnel paid for and contracted by the Chicago Police Department.
    • 2.be carried Code 049 by their respective unit of assignment for only phase two of the selection process. At the conclusion of phase two of the testing process, candidates will return to their unit of assignment.
    • 3.adjust their furlough period(s) if a segment conflicts with the training program.
    • 4.pass a series of confidence tests to include:
      • a.a basic water confidence test which consists of a 50 meter swim, treading water for 5 minutes, swimming under water for a distance of 10 meters, and removing SWAT gear while in water.
      • b.a height confidence test in which the candidate will start at the bottom of a 30 foot ladder and climb the simulated commercial building exterior to the 3rd story, climb over the ledge of the window into the structure. The candidate will then climb back over the ledge out of the structure and onto the ladder, climbing back down the ladder.
      • c.a confined space test in which the candidate will be required to wear a gas mask, within a confined location, where smoke is deployed to assist in determining the candidate's tolerance for confined areas.
    • 5.successfully complete all aspects of the training program including a rifle school and the basic SWAT school and SWAT Leadership Assessment Class.
  • E.At any time during the training phase, if it becomes apparent to the SWAT Coordinator or training staff that a candidate exhibits problematic behavioral traits and/or is physically or psychologically incapable of performing required duties or fails training phase testing, the candidate will be terminated from the training phase and returned to their unit of assignment. Reasons for termination include, but are not limited to, the candidate demonstrating a lack of physical coordination, tactical competence, stamina, or strength to the degree that the member cannot perform the duties as enumerated in Item III of this directive.
VII.Eligibility List
  • A.The top scoring candidates from phase two of the selection process will be placed on an eligibility list, in rank order, based on their final scores from phase two of the Sergeant assigned as SWAT team member selection process.
  • B.The eligibility list will be subject to current operational needs of the SWAT team.
  • C.Placement on the eligibility list is not a guarantee of assignment to the SWAT team.
  • D.The eligibility list may be stricken at any time at the discretion of the Department.
VIII.Additional Conditions
At any time during the selection process and/or assignment to the SWAT Team, if a member demonstrates a lack of knowledge, skills, or abilities necessary for the job, is unable to perform the essential duties of the position as mandated in the SWAT Standard Operating Procedures, or exhibits problematic behavior, the member will be subject to removal from assignment to the SWAT team by the SWAT Coordinator.
