Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E04-03
Watch Personnel System
Issue Date:12 March 2004Effective Date:13 March 2004
Rescinds:S04-03
Index Category:Assignments and Details
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the automated Watch Personnel System, a part of the Office Automation application.
  • B.establishes responsibilities and procedures for the:
    • 1.recording and updating of information in the Watch Personnel System.
    • 2.maintenance of system security and access to the Watch Personnel System.
II.Description of System
  • A.The Watch Personnel System is the designated personnel program to be used to maintain information on assigned and detailed unit personnel, sworn and civilian. All use of other databases containing personnel information will be discontinued upon issuance of this directive.
  • B.The Watch Personnel System will contain unit members' personal information and other information related to their employment with the Department, such as furlough assignments, court key, day off group, and other similar types of information.
  • C.The Watch Personnel System can be queried by last name, star number, employee number, PC Logon identification number, title code, or in alphabetical order by unit. Using available software, reports can be written that include star number rosters, alpha rosters, address / telephone rosters, seniority rosters, and other similar attributes.
III.Responsibilities for Institution / Maintenance of the Watch Personnel System
  • A.District commanders and unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.ensure that data previously entered into other source databases (Paradox, Quattro Pro, Word Perfect) is entered into the Watch Personnel System.
    • 2.designate a member or members who will enter applicable watch or unit personnel information into the Watch Personnel System on a timely basis. In the Bureau of Patrol, the responsibility for this task will lie with the district or unit secretaries and the secretaries to the Deputy Chiefs.
    • 3.ensure that the information fields listed in the Watch Personnel System are populated and updated to the fullest extent possible. Designated unit personnel can access all information contained in the bottom box of the screen; changes to information contained in the upper box of the screen must be directed to the Human Resources Division.
    • 4.upon discovery of the maintenance or use of unauthorized personnel programs or databases, order their removal from Department-issued computers.
  • B.Unit commanding officers will ensure that a brief but appropriate description of the assignments of all personnel under their command is generated and made available to the member(s) designated to maintain the Watch Personnel System to be used in the "Nature of Assignment" Field.
IV.Procedures for the Entering of Information into the Watch Personnel System
  • A.Members designated in Item III-A-2 of this directive will maintain and update watch or unit personnel information whenever changes occur or, minimally, on the first day of each police period.
  • B.Unless indicated otherwise, each of the following fields will be populated and maintained:
    • 1.Watch Assignments
      • a.All units and districts will use numbers "1" through "4" to designate the watch assignments. Additionally, Districts 001 and 018 may use "5" to denote foot patrol officers.
      • b.No other type of entry is authorized for use.
    • 2.Unit of Detail
      If relevant, the unit losing the member will enter the 3 digit unit designator of the unit the member is detailed to. This includes the movement of a member already detailed to a unit to a different unit on a subsequent detail.
    • 3.Date of Detail
      If relevant, the unit losing the member will enter the date the detail becomes effective. This includes the movement of a member already detailed to a unit to a different unit on a subsequent detail.
    • 4.Unit Telephone Number
      • a.Bureau of Patrol units will enter the 10 digit unit telephone number for:
        • (1)Deputy chiefs;
        • (2)District and watch commanders;
        • (3)Field lieutenants, tactical lieutenants, and CAPS management team leaders;
        • (4)Administrative managers, district / unit secretaries, and tactical secretaries;
        • (5)Sergeants and police officers assigned to district community policing offices.
      • b.Units other than the Bureau of Patrol will enter the 10 digit unit telephone number for personnel as designated by the unit commanding officer.
    • 5.Furlough Selection
      • a.Units will use either both the "F1" and "F2" fields or the "Full" field to indicate a members furlough selection.
      • b.For split segment furloughs, units will enter the number and letter of the current year furlough selections in the "F1" and "F2" fields in chronological order (not the order of selection).
      • c.For full period furloughs, units will enter the number of the police period for the current year furlough selection.
      • d.Furlough Year
        Units will enter the 4 digit year of the furloughs listed.
    • 6.Day Off Group
      • a.Units will indicate the day off group by entering a number from one to fourteen, as described below. No other type of entry is authorized.
      • b.Numbering of Day Off Groups
        • (1)For members working a day off group as noted on the Department Operations Calendar (CPD-11.143), the number coordinating to their day off group (1 through 7) will be entered.
        • (2)For members working other types of schedules, the following day off group numbers apply:
          Saturday/Sunday=8;
          Friday/Saturday=9;
          Sunday/Monday=10;
          Monday/Tuesday=11;
          Tuesday /Wednesday=12;
          Wednesday/Thursday=13;
          Thursday/ Friday=14;
          and any other combination=15.
    • 7.Locker Number
      If a locker has been assigned to a member, the locker number will be entered in this field.
    • 8.Court Key
      The Traffic Court Section is responsible for entering and maintaining court key data for all sworn personnel, including probationary police officers who are preparing for assignment to field duties.
    • 9.Start Time 1
      • a.Units will enter a time in the "Start Time 1" field for all members.
      • b.When a member's starting time fluctuates (i.e., relief or tactical assignment), the unit will enter:
        • (1)the starting time that is valid at the beginning of the upcoming police period or
        • (2)the new starting time if it is anticipated to extend for longer than seven days.
    • 10.Start Time 2
      • a.In districts, this field will be used for recording the afternoon starting time for crossing guards.
      • b.Other units may use this field as necessary or leave blank.
    • 11.Performance Evaluation
      Once the temporary suspension of performance evaluations has been lifted, this field will be a required field for completion.
      • a.For the "Jan-Jun" and "Jul-Dec" fields, units will enter the year that the evaluation covers.
      • b.Units will enter the evaluation rating for the relevant rating periods for all assigned personnel (sworn and civilian).
      • c.Crossing guards are only rated once per year. Districts will enter the evaluation rating for the entire year in the "Jan-Jun" field.
      • d.District and unit secretaries will enter the performance evaluation information for probationary police officers who are detailed to the unit and are evaluated by unit supervisors. This data entry will be completed prior to the return of the Performance Evaluation Rating Cards to Unit 044.
    • 12.Beat
      • a.Units will enter the beat assignment number for the member. When the member is assigned to a relief position and the assigned beat number fluctuates, the unit will enter:
        • (1)the beat assignment that is valid at the beginning of the upcoming police period or
        • (2)the new beat assignment if it is anticipated to extend for longer than seven days.
      • b.For crossing guards, units will enter the geographic beat where the assigned crossing is located.
    • 13.Vehicle Number
      The unit will enter the vehicle number primarily used by the member.
      • a.When the member is assigned to a relief position and the assigned vehicle number fluctuates, or when there is no car specifically assigned for the member, this field will be left blank.
      • b.When a member is assigned a take-home vehicle, the vehicle number will be entered here.
    • 14.Lunch Period
      To the extent possible, a lunch period will be entered for all members.
    • 15.Type of Vehicle
      • a.The type of vehicle indicated in Item IV-B-13 of this directive will be indicated by selecting from the entries provided in the drop-down menu.
      • b.If the vehicle fits more than one selection, the selection which is the most descriptive will be used. If the "vehicle number" field has been left blank, no entry is necessary in this field.
    • 16.Gas Mask Number
      As members are trained and issued gas masks, the number of the mask will be entered in this field.
    • 17.Bid / Management Data
      • a.Each year immediately following the watch selection process, units taking part in the process will enter the results in the designated fields. Units not taking part in the watch selection process will leave this field blank.
      • b.The format to be used is: Unit-Watch-"B" (for "Bid") or "M" (for "Management")- Position (based on seniority on that watch) (e.g. 001-1-B-005).
      • c.For district members assigned to watch 4 or 5 (i.e., tactical and gang tactical team personnel, 001 and 018 foot patrol officers), this field will be completed as specified by the Commander, Management and Labor Affairs Section.
    • 18.Nature of Assignment
      • a.An option from the drop-down menu will be selected to indicate the nature of assignment for all personnel, sworn and civilian.
      • b.These entries were generated by the Finance Division with the input of unit commanding officers. Requests for the addition of additional entries to this list should be directed through chain of command to the Director, Finance Division.
    • 19.Location
      This field will be used to indicate the street locations for assignments such as school crossing corners or foot patrol.
    • 20.Pub/Pri/Parochial
      These check boxes will be used to indicate the type of school crossing identified in Item IV-B-12-b.
V.Related Procedures
  • A.All units will continue to:
    • 1.maintain a Personnel Card (CPD-11.601) for each assigned or detailed sworn and civilian member that depicts personal data (e.g., home address, telephone number, district of residence, or change in the Police Training and Special Qualifications section).
    • 2.for probationary police officers and other new arrivals to the unit, verify that all relevant data from the Personnel Card or gathered during the unit orientation is entered into the Watch Personnel System. This includes all data listed in Item IV-B.
    • 3.ensure that sections of the Personnel Card that list information of a work-related nature, (e.g., firearms and equipment, performance ratings, awards, commendations, and citations) are current.
    • 4.forward the Personnel Card to a members new unit of assignment when the member is transferred.
    • 5.forward the Personnel Card to the Human Resources Division for insertion in a members personnel jacket when the member is terminated or will be absent from duty for longer than thirty days due to suspension or leave of absence.
  • B.All Department reports and copies thereof pertaining to unit personnel will be retained in accordance with the Forms Retention Schedule. Reports that have reached their retention period will be boxed, marked, and moved to a records storage room pending destruction as described in the Department directive entitled "Records Management." Reports will not be retained beyond their authorized retention.
