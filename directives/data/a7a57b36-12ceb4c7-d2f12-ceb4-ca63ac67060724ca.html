Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-04-08
Processing School Absentees
Issue Date:10 September 2013Effective Date:10 September 2013
Rescinds:22 August 2013 Version
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive outlines procedures for:
  • A.handling school absentees.
  • B.processing School Absentee Reports (CPD-24.116).
II.Field Procedures
  • A.When a sworn member observes a juvenile between the ages of 7 and 17 years of age (unless the juvenile has already graduated from high school) during periods of time when school is in session, the juvenile will be questioned to ascertain the reason for the absence.
  • B.If there is no valid reason for the absence, the member will:
    • 1.advise the dispatcher of the location and nature of the stop.
    • 2.complete a School Absentee Report.
    • 3.name check the juvenile and record the results in the section entitled "ADDITIONAL INFORMATION."
  • C.When a school absentee is not involved in a criminal or quasi-criminal offense, the member will, based on their investigation, determine if the school absentee should be taken to school or home. Members will take one of the following actions:
    • 1.transport the school absentee to the school of attendance when the school is located in:
      • a.the district of occurrence, or
      • b.an adjacent district and a supervisor authorizes the transport.
        NOTE:
        The name and address of the school official, parent, legal guardian, or other adult accepting responsibility for the school absentee is required on the School Absentee Report. A copy of the report will not be left at the school.
    • 2.transport the school absentee home when a responsible adult is present in the home and the home is located in:
      • a.the district of occurrence, or
      • b.an adjacent district and a supervisor authorizes the transport.
        NOTE:
        If the person accepting responsibility for the school absentee refuses to give a name, the member will attempt to establish the relationship of the person to the absentee and write the relationship of the person and the word refused (e.g., "Uncle Refused") in the section entitled "IF RELEASED, GIVE NAME AND ADDRESS OF PERSON RELEASED TO." A copy of the report will not be left at the residence.
    • 3.transport the school absentee to the district station for processing when:
      • a.the school or home is not located within the district of occurrence or an adjacent district, or
      • b.the home address or school cannot be determined.
        NOTE:
        The transport of a school absentee will additionally be governed by the provisions of Department directives concerning the requirement for proper supervisory authorization to leave an assigned location (e.g., fixed post, mission site, beat/sector).
  • D.If the school absentee is transported to the district station, the member will:
    • 1.complete the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody (CPD-24.518).
    • 2.contact the appropriate area Violent Crimes Section and request a name check for the juvenile.
    • 3.make every effort to contact the parent or legal guardian of the absentee, recording the efforts in the narrative section entitled "ADDITIONAL INFORMATION" (e.g., called grandmother's home, 123-4567 at 1300 hours, no answer).
    • 4.allow sufficient time for the parent or legal guardian to respond to the district.
    • 5.if a parent or guardian cannot be located or has not responded within a reasonable amount of time:
      • a.notify the appropriate area Violent Crimes Section and inform the detective:
        • (1)a school absentee is being processed in the district station, and
        • (2)efforts to contact a parent or legal guardian have failed and sufficient time for a response has passed.
      • b.turn the absentee over to the detective, Violent Crimes Section for a disposition.
  • E.When the school absentee has also been apprehended for a criminal or quasi-criminal offense, members will:
    • 1.process the school absentee according to the Department directives entitled "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" and "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
    • 2.complete a School Absentee Report.
    • 3.check "YES" in the "Other Offense" box.
    • 4.record the charge(s) placed against the juvenile in the "ADDITIONAL INFORMATION" section.
III.Other Responsibilities
  • A.Supervisors will:
    • 1.ensure all necessary information is contained in each School Absentee Report.
    • 2.indicate approval by signing the School Absentee Report in the lower-right corner of the report.
    • 3.forward all reports to the district citation clerk.
  • B.If the school absentee is transported to the district station:
    • 1. the station supervisor will ensure:
      • a.the member completes the Daily Log of Juveniles Taken Into Custody (CPD-24.518).
      • b.ensure all necessary information is contained in each School Absentee Report.
      • c.all reasonable efforts to contact a parent or legal guardian are exhausted and the parent or legal guardian is given a sufficient amount of time to respond to the district station.
      • d.if applicable, transportation is provided for the juvenile to the parent/legal guardian or alternate placement after the detectives completed their investigation.
    • 2.the Area Violent Crimes Section will process the school absentee according to the Department directive entitled "Processing of Juveniles and Minors Under Department Control" and the Youth Investigations Division directives.
IV.Disposition
  • A.The district citation clerk will:
    • 1.review all School Absentee Reports for accuracy and completeness.
    • 2.prepare a Transmittal/Curfew Violation/School Absentee (CPD-24.112) form in duplicate and forward the form with all approved School Absentee Reports, attach both copies of all School Absentee Reports, and forward via the Police Documents Section, to the Youth Investigations Division, Unit 184.
    • 3.retain the duplicate copy of the Transmittal/Curfew Violation/School Absentee form in the district in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
  • B.The Youth Investigations Division will:
    • 1.establish procedures to inform the Board of Education of all documented school absentees.
    • 2.retain copies of the School Absentee Reports in accordance with existing records retention requirements.
