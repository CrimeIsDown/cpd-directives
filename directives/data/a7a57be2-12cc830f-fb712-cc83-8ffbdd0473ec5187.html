Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S04-01-01
Alternate Response Section Procedures
Issue Date:28 April 2021Effective Date:29 April 2021
Rescinds:03 February 2013 Version
Index Category:Preliminary Investigations
I.Purpose
This directive explains specific procedures related to the Alternate Response Section (ARS). Department members will refer to the Department directive titled "Alternate Response Section Case Reporting Policy" for guidelines on the preliminary investigation of incidents included in the ARS.
II.Procedures
  • A.For all field "on-view" and district "walk-in" incidents, Department members will follow the procedures enumerated in the Department directives titled "Preliminary Investigations," "Preliminary Investigations: Required Immediate Notifications and Investigations Conducted at Suburban Hospitals," and "Miscellaneous Incident Reporting Procedures."
  • B.The Alternate Response Section will be operational between 0500-0030 hours. During non-operational hours (0030 - 0500 hours) members of the public have additional options for submitting a report, including online or visiting a district station.
    NOTE:
    During ARS non-operational hours, when requested, district personnel will complete case reports normally completed by ARS via telephone or in person.
  • C.During ARS operational hours (0500-0030 hours), district desk personnel who receive a telephone inquiry and determine the completion of a case report is appropriate will:
    • 1.keep the caller on the line, and
    • 2.forward the call to ARS.
      NOTE:
      Desk officers should familiarize themselves with their particular phone's ability to transfer calls to ARS.
  • D.All telephone calls for service received from any source by OEMC that meet the criteria outlined in the Department directive titled "Alternate Response Section Case Reporting Policy" during ARS operational hours (0500-0030 hours) will be forwarded to an ARS call-taker, who will determine if:
    • 1.the caller is requesting information only.
    • 2.the preparation of a police report is required.
    • 3.the dispatch of a field unit is needed based upon additional information furnished to the member by the caller.
      :
      An ARS supervisor must approve any decision to send a call back to OEMC for dispatch. The name and star number of the approving supervisor will be included in the comments section under the event number.
  • E.The OEMC call-takers will not dispatch a call that meets the criteria for ARS as outlined in the Department directive titled "Alternate Response Section Case Reporting Policy" unless ARS is non-operational.
  • F.The ARS call-taker will:
    • 1.receive assignments and beat numbers from the ARS watch supervisor.
    • 2.answer all incoming calls.
    • 3.determine the nature of the call for service.
    • 4.prepare a computer-aided dispatch (CAD) event record as required.
    • 5.provide the appropriate police service within the guideline of the ARS.
    • 6.create PCAD referral for an evidence technician when warranted.
    • 7.notify the Bureau of Detectives when required or warranted (e.g., major theft over $10,000).
    • 8.perform other duties as assigned by a supervisor.
  • G.Supervisors assigned to the ARS will:
    • 1.evaluate the ARS requests for dispatch of a field unit and, as appropriate, approve or deny the request.
    • 2.ensure that approved requests for field response are promptly directed to the appropriate dispatch position.
    • 3.review all submitted reports for accuracy and completeness and, if satisfactory, affix his or her signature as appropriate.
    • 4.return case reports or other reports that have not been properly completed to the reporting ARS member for corrections.
    • 5.ensure the preparation of a Case Report Transfer List (Non-Traffic) (CPD-11.417) at the conclusion of each watch.
  • H.The Commanding Officer, ARS, will:
    • 1.coordinate the administrative activities of the section.
    • 2.evaluate the on-going staffing, equipment, and training requirements for the section.
    • 3.develop and prepare the appropriate statistical reports to provide a basis for an evaluation of the efficiency of the section.
