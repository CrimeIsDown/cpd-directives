Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-03-01
Departmental Unit CAPS Responsibilities
Issue Date:08 January 2009Effective Date:08 January 2009
Rescinds:G09-01-02
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive establishes the responsibilities of Departmental units for the implementation of CAPS.
II.Department-wide Responsibilities
  • A.Assistant Superintendents will ensure the overall management, monitoring, and auditing of the implementation of CAPS for the bureaus/unit under their command.
  • B.Each bureau deputy superintendent will ensure support of the implementation of CAPS. This support will include, but is not limited to:
    • 1.coordinating the Intra-Departmental Support Service Requests (IDSSR).
    • 2.designating unit members to attend meetings or other community events, upon request.
    • 3.addressing community concerns.
    • 4.responding to requests for resources and additional support for the implementation of a District Plan.
    • 5.consulting with the Director, CAPS Implementation Office, to recommend modifications in the implementation of CAPS, CAPS-related duties, or the CAPS Manual.
    • 6.actively participating in the problem-solving process.
  • C.Unit commanding officers, Department-wide, will access the CAPS Implementation Office server to retrieve and review the current District Plans submitted by each district, for which they have geographical responsibilities, for the purpose of:
    • 1.examining the current strategies outlined in the District Plans,
    • 2.recommending unit-specific strategies, use of unit resources, or additional district strategies to supplement the effectiveness of the District Plans, and
    • 3.providing the necessary unit resources and support for the successful implementation of the District Plans.
III.Office of the Assistant Superintendent, Operations
  • A.The Director, CAPS Implementation Office, will:
    • 1.coordinate with the Office of Management Accountability (OMA) to ensure the overall management, monitoring, and auditing of the Department’s implementation of CAPS
    • 2.provide administrative support, training, and materials needed for the successful implementation of CAPS
    • 3.ensure District Plans are submitted, reviewed, monitored, and available for review by appropriate Department members
    • 4.maintain and update the CAPS Manual as necessary in order to promote a consistent and efficient implementation of CAPS.
  • B.The Director, CAPS Implementation Office, will ensure:
    • 1.district law enforcement is supported by actively organizing community members to become involved in the problem-solving process.
    • 2.CAPS Implementation Office community organizers participate in Beat Community and DAC Subcommittee Meetings.
    • 3.CAPS community activities and events are developed, scheduled, and conducted consistent with the Department’s implementation of CAPS.
    • 4.the overall promotion and management of the community Block Club Program.
    • 5.City service requests are initiated and followed-up upon with the appropriate agency/unit.
    • 6.that CAPS materials are available as promotional items for the purpose of promoting the CAPS problem-solving process.
    • 7.community flyers are distributed to the intended target markets in a timely manner, when authorized.
    • 8.there is a prominent presence of CAPS community organizers in those communities wherein the active law enforcement phase of problem solving evolves towards community stabilization.
    • 9.community maintenance strategies are developed and conducted for the successful implementation of the District Plans.
    • 10.each district commander is notified, in writing, of the available DAC Funds, and make immediately available to the district commander a sum of one-half of the notified amount to be used and managed as outlined in the CAPS Manual.
IV.Bureau of Patrol
  • A.The Patrol Field Group Chiefs will be accountable for the implementation of CAPS within their areas of command.
  • B.Area and Central Control Group deputy chiefs will:
    • 1.provide for the overall management, monitoring, and auditing of the area strategy for the implementation of CAPS.
    • 2.attend one Beat Community Meeting per week in their area of assignment and record their attendance in the CLEAR exempt reporting application.
    • 3.designate an area CAPS lieutenant.
    • 4.review district plans submitted by district commanders under their command.
  • C.Area CAPS Lieutenant
    For the districts in their area of assignment, the area CAPS lieutenant will:
    • 1.coordinate with the beat team sergeants, community policing sergeants, and community policing officers and provide support for the implementation of CAPS at the district level.
    • 2.provide assistance in obtaining responses to:
      • a.requests for outside unit resources and support.
      • b.submitted Intra-Departmental Support Service Requests.
    • 3.on behalf of the deputy chiefs review, audit, and ensure the proper submission of the district plans.
    • 4.monitor the Community Concerns, CLEARpath, and automated Beat Log databases.
    • 5.monitor and audit Beat Community Meetings which can include, but is not limited to:
      • a.attending Beat Community Meetings and completing Beat Community Meeting Evaluation Forms.
      • b.monitoring Department and community attendance.
      • c.ensuring the problem solving, information sharing, follow-up, and accountability aspects of the Beat Community Meetings are maintained.
    • 6.analyze area crime data and district plans to:
      • a.identify district crossover or area-wide patterns.
      • b.recommend resource deployment, additional strategies, or modifications of a district plan.
    • 7.coordinate with the Deployment Operations Center Section (DOC) and the Detective Division to ensure identified area-wide community-related concerns, problems, and patterns are addressed.
    • 8.be available to act as a watch commander at the discretion of the area deputy chief.
  • D.District commanders will:
    • 1.attend one Beat Community Meeting per week within their district of assignment and report their attendance in the CLEAR exempt reporting application and on the Beat Community Meeting Log.
    • 2.provide for the overall management, monitoring, and auditing of the district’s implementation of CAPS.
    • 3.designate Department members to serve as the community policing sergeant and the beat team sergeant.
    • 4.develop and continually monitor the District Plan with input from the community and the district strategic management team.
  • E.District watch commanders will:
    • 1.provide for the overall management of the implementation of CAPS for their watch.
    • 2.monitor, assign, and ensure the closure of CLEARpath Community Concerns assigned to their watch.
    • 3.maintain beat assignment integrity on their respective watch, when possible based on operational needs.
    • 4.when assigned to the 3rd watch, attend all Beat Community Meetings in their district of assignment and record their attendance on the Watch Commander’s Log and the Beat Community Meeting Log.
      NOTE:
      If there is a field or tactical lieutenant working the watch on the evening of the meeting, the watch commander may delegate the responsibility to attend the Beat Community Meeting to a lieutenant. This substitution will be noted on the Watch Commander’s Log.
  • F.Community policing sergeants will:
    • 1.provide for the overall management of the assigned community policing office personnel.
    • 2.ensure the responsibilities of the beat team sergeant are completed when the beat team sergeant is unavailable (e.g., furlough, medical).
    • 3.provide for the overall management of the community policing office activities including, but not limited to:
      • a.support of the District Advisory Committee (DAC) and subcommittees.
      • b.coordination and management of district-level funding (i.e. Allstate grant, DAC monies).
      • c.coordination of the Troubled Building Program.
      • d.managing and responding to Community Concerns, City Service Requests, 311 work orders, and CLEARpath Community Concerns.
      • e.maintaining the CLEARpath, Business, Community Concerns, Automated Beat Community Log, and Critical Facility databases.
      • f.Extreme Weather Operation Plan responses.
      • g.coordination of and participation in district events including, but not limited to:
        • (1)Community marches.
        • (2)Court Advocacy trips.
        • (3)Domestic Violence Prevention events.
        • (4)National Night Out activities.
        • (5)Police-Partnership Day activities.
        • (6)Positive Loitering activities.
        • (7)Senior Services events.
        • (8)Other city-wide and district-planned community events designated by the district commander (i.e. job fairs, child safety seat seminars, charitable drives, fishing tournaments, haunted police stations)
      • h.responding to issues and requests by:
        • (1)City officials.
        • (2)Citizens, businesses, and community organizations.
        • (3)Educational facilities.
      • i.completion of required management reports and notifications, including but not limited to:
        • (1)Sex Offender Registration.
        • (2)Safe School Information Packages.
        • (3)Senior Bracelet Files.
        • (4)Intra-Departmental Support Service Requests.
        • (5)Crime Watch.
        • (6)Meetings Hosted by the Department Report.
        • (7)Community Participation Report.
        • (8)Court Advocacy Report.
        • (9)Monthly Senior Service Officer Report.
        • (10)Monthly Domestic Violence Liaison Officer Report.
        • (11)CAPS Weekly Event Report.
        • (12)Other reports as directed or as identified in the CAPS Procedural Guidelines.
  • G.Beat team sergeants will:
    • 1.ensure the responsibilities of the community policing sergeant are completed when the community policing sergeant is unavailable (e.g., furlough, medical).
    • 2.attend all district beat community meetings and DAC meetings when not on furlough.
    • 3.provide for the overall management of the district’s Beat Community Meetings as outlined in Department directive entitled “Building Partnerships with the Community.”
    • 4.coordinate with the beat facilitator when developing the agenda and topics for the Beat Community Meetings.
    • 5.maintain and review the District Plan as outlined in the addendum to this directive entitled “District Plans.”
    • 6.serve as the district-level CAPS training coordinator and reinforce previous training topics such as the crime triangle and proven problem-solving methods.
    • 7.manage community concerns received from Beat Community Meetings and ensure they are entered into the appropriate database.
    • 8.update and maintain the district mission boards.
    • 9.coordinate with the watch commanders, tactical lieutenant, and beat team officers to discuss issues raised at the Beat Community Meetings.
  • H.Beat team officers will:
    • 1.attend Beat Community Meetings and community events, when notified.
    • 2.coordinate with the beat team sergeant to discuss problems raised at the Beat Community Meetings or identified through other means (e.g., officer observations).
  • I.All district field personnel (e.g., beat team officers, rapid response officers, gang and tactical officers, sector sergeants) will actively participate in the CAPS problem-solving process by using problem-solving techniques to develop and implement strategies to combat identified crime and disorder problems and community concerns.
V.Bureau of Investigative Services
  • A.The Chief, Detective Division, will ensure:
    • 1.Community Alerts and Area Crime Patterns are developed, processed, and disseminated using established procedures.
    • 2.each area commander designates a liaison for each district community policing office to:
      • a.serve as a resource to address community issues including:
        • (1)domestic violence.
        • (2)senior citizens.
        • (3)juveniles.
        • (4)community alerts and crime patterns.
      • b.attend Beat Community Meetings, Area and District Strategic Management Meetings, and the Management Accountability Review Sessions.
  • B.The Chief, Organized Crime Division, will ensure a liaison is designated for each district community policing office to serve as a resource to address community issues including:
    • 1.narcotics, gang, and prostitution activity.
    • 2.enforcement of liquor and other license violations.
  • C.The Chief, Counter-Terrorism and Intelligence Division, will ensure the Commander, Deployment Operations Center Section, assigns a DOC analyst as a liaison for each area to serve as a resource for the district and area management teams when developing, monitoring, and evaluating District Plans.
VI.Bureau of Professional Standards
  • A.The Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, will ensure topic-specific training is developed and conducted to enhance the efficient implementation of CAPS.
  • B.The Commander, Inspection Division, will ensure audits are conducted of the following programs to ensure unit compliance:
    • 1.CLEARpath Community Concerns.
    • 2.Beat Community Meetings.
    • 3.Community Concerns database.
    • 4.Automated Community Beat Log database.
    • 5.311-CSR database.
  • C.The Commanding Officer, Office of Management Accountability, will ensure management accountability review sessions are conducted to audit the overall implementation of CAPS at all levels, including the evaluation of district community policing activities and the district plans.
VII.Bureau of Administrative Services
The Commander, Public Safety Information Technology, will ensure the maintenance of the CLEARpath webportal, the CAPS Implementation Office server, and appropriate databases.
