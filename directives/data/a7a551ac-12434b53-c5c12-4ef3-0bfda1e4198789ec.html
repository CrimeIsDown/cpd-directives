Abandoned Vehicle
  • A vehicle in a state of disrepair which renders it incapable of being driven, or any vehicle that has not been moved or used for seven consecutive days or more and is apparently deserted.
    NOTE:
    Abandoned vehicles will not be towed as an immediate tow by Department members. A City Service Request will be utilized for the purpose of having an abandoned vehicle removed from the public way.
Abuse
Physical abuse, harassment, intimidation of a dependent, interference with personal liberty or willful deprivation. Abuse does not include reasonable direction of a minor child by a parent or person in loco parentis (acting on behalf of a parent).
A pattern of use of alcohol or other drugs with the potential of leading to immediate functional problems or to addiction or the use of alcohol or other drugs solely for purposes of intoxication.
Abused child
325 Illinois Compile Statutes (ILCS) 5/3: ...a child whose parent or immediate family member, or any person responsible for the child's welfare, or any individual residing in the same home as the child, or a paramour of the child's parent:
  • A.inflicts, causes to be inflicted, or allows to be inflicted upon such child physical injury, by other than accidental means, which causes death, disfigurement, impairment of physical or emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function;
  • B.creates a substantial risk of physical injury to such child by other than accidental means which would be likely to cause death, disfigurement, impairment of physical or emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function;
  • C.commits or allows to be committed any sex offense against such child, as defined in the Criminal Code of 1961, as amended, and extending those definitions of sex offenses to include children under 18 years of age;
  • D.commits or allows to be committed an act or acts of torture upon such child; or
  • E.inflicts excessive corporal punishment.
Abused Minor
705 Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) 405/2-3(2)(*): Those who are abused include any minor under 18 years of age whose parent or immediate family member, or any person responsible for the minor's welfare, or any person who is in the same family or household as the minor, or any individual residing in the same home as the minor, or a paramour of the minor's parent:
  • A.Inflicts, causes to be inflicted, or allows to be inflicted upon such minor physical injury, by other than accidental means, which causes death, disfigurement, impairment of physical or emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function.
  • B.Creates a substantial risk of physical injury to such minor by other than accidental means which would be likely to cause death, disfigurement, impairment of emotional health, or loss or impairment of any bodily function.
  • C.Commits or allows to be committed any sex offense against such minor.
  • D.Commits or allows to be committed an act or acts of torture upon such minor.
  • E.Inflicts excessive corporal punishment.
Academic Training Phase
The Academic Training Phase is conducted at the Timothy J. O'Connor Education and Training Academy where police recruits are trained in law, physical skills, theories of policing, and firearms use and safety. The performance objectives of the Academic Training Phase are the standards set by the Illinois Law Enforcement Training and Standards Board and the Chicago Police Department.
Access Route
An access route shall be clearly defined and kept open for the unrestricted passing of emergency vehicles. This route will be used for emergency evacuation in the event the incident escalates.

Active Criminal
An active criminal (suspect person) is an individual with an assigned Identification Record (I.R.), Illinois State Identification (S.I.D.) and/or Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) number, and a history of repeated investigations and arrests, usually related to a particular criminal specialty or which support organized criminal activity.
Active Neglect
The intentional failure to fulfill a caretaking obligation, including a conscious and willful attempt to inflict physical or emotional stress or injury on a senior citizen, e.g., deliberate abandonment, depriving of dental prosthesis or eyeglasses, deliberate denial of food or health-related services.
Active Resister
Includes subjects who secret themselves and fail to comply with sworn members' orders to reveal themselves.
Active Shooter Incident
A violent incident, not limited to a threat involving a firearm, wherein an armed assailant(s) has remained on or near the scene and continues to present a threat to the safety of citizens that requires rapid deployment as opposed to containment and negotiation.
Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN)
The Chicago Police Department's facsimile message network system.
Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) Ledger Book
Record of all facsimile message numbers kept by the Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) Center. This record will indicate the Facsimile Message Number, number of pages, the date and time the message was sent.
Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) Number
A unique number assigned by the Administrative Message Facsimile Network (AMFN) Center which:
  • A.Consists of the last two digits of the current year and a sequential number.
  • B.Starts at 000001 for the first message of the year and continues in ascending order until the end of the year. (ie. A97-000001 would be the first facsimile message of 1997 issued by the AMFN Center).
Administrative Message Facsimile Network Center or AMFN Center
A section responsible for issuing Facsimile Message Numbers and transmitting Facsimile Messages.
Adult
A person seventeen years of age or older.
Adult With Disabilities
An elder adult with disabilities or a high-risk adult with disabilities who is a person, age 18 and over, prevented by physical or mental disability or advanced age from taking appropriate action to protect himself from abuse inflicted by a family or household member.

Alcoholism
An illness characterized by preoccupation with alcohol which is typically associated with physical disability and impaired emotional, occupational or social adjustments as a direct consequence of loss of control over consumption of alcohol, and demonstrated by persistent and excessive use of alcohol such as usually leads to intoxication if drinking is begun, by chronicity, by progression; and by tendency towards relapse.
Alien
Any person who is not a citizen or national of the United States.

Alternate Response Section (ARS)
The non-emergency service section within the Information Services Division, Bureau of Administrative Services. The Alternate Response Section (ARS) is a city-wide telephonic system for preliminary investigations of calls for service. The Alternate Response Section services as the direct liaison to 311 City Services, as well as, functions as the Back-up Call facility (BCF) for the Office of Emergency Management and Communication 911 center (OEMC).
The non-emergency service section designed to improve the efficiency of the 9-1-1 emergency system and serve as a call-screening and report-taking entity. The ARS provides alternate police service whenever a citizen's call for service is of a non-emergency nature or is a request for services provided by other City service agencies.
Alzheimer's Disease
The condition in which a person suffers changes in memory, thought, personality, language, and other characteristics; becoming progressively incapacitated and dependent until death occurs, usually from a secondary cause.
Ambulatory Recuperation
Allows a member who is recuperating from an illness or injury to leave their residence without notifying their unit of assignment or detail. A member assigned to this category will be given appointment dates for review of their illness or injury.
Annual furlough

Application for Authority to Dispose of Local Records
A request to the Local Records Commission for approval to dispose of records or series of records that have accumulated and are not needed in the transaction of current business and do not have sufficient administrative, legal, or fiscal value to warrant their further preservation.
Approval of Charges
The entering on an Arrest Report of the watch commander's signature indicating approval of the formal filing of charges against an arrestee.
Archive
The process by which requested 9-1-1 telephone calls and radio transmissions associated with a particular incident will be transferred from a computerized logging device to a magneto optical. The selected audio that will be archived to the disk will be accompanied with a text file that contains details of the voice transmissions, such as date, start time, stop time, name and address of caller, comments, and related identification numbers, (ie, event number, RD number, CR number, etc.), and will be stored indefinitely.
Area CAPS Lieutenant
A lieutenant designated by and assigned to each Bureau of Patrol Area Deputy Chief’s office, who manages the implementation of CAPS in their area of assignment, and analyzes area crime data to ensure area resources support the District Plans.
Area Domestic Violence Coordinator
A Detective Division sergeant appointed by the Detective Division area commander to be responsible for the conduct of all domestic violence investigations within the area and for ensuring that the Area Domestic Violence Related Incident File is maintained.
Area Management Team
A management team, headed by the Bureau of Patrol area Deputy Chief, consisting of the district commanders from within the area; the Detective Division area commander; area CAPS Lieutenant; and other individuals as designated by the area Deputy Chief.
Area Strategic Management Meeting
A meeting chaired by the area Deputy Chief and attended by the Area Management Team members and other personnel as designated by the area Deputy Chief. The meeting provides a forum for area management team members to confer and share information for the development and monitoring of the District Plans.
Arm/Arming
Removing a carbine from a Department vehicle and chambering a round.
Arrest
