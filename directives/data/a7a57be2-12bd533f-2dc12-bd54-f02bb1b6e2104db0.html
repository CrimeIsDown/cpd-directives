Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S03-14
Operation Protect Youth Program
Issue Date:23 February 2012Effective Date:23 February 2012
Rescinds:Version dated 22 June 2010; S10-07
Index Category:Field Operations
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.continues the Operation Protect Youth Program.
  • B.identifies the scheduling procedure for the program.
  • C.delineates unit and individual responsibilities.
II.General Information
  • A.The Operation Protect Youth program provides special attention near the vicinity of City schools to address youth violence, prevent crime citywide, and provide safe passage for everyone, especially during dismissal times at City high schools. The Operation Protect Youth Program also focuses directed patrols in an attempt to reduce and eliminate violence in designated Hot Spots and High Level Gang Conflict Locations throughout the city.
    NOTE:
    While members assigned to Operation Protect Youth will not receive district radio assignments, they are expected to respond to radio calls within the district of deployment, as appropriate.
  • B.The Operation Protect Youth Program will operate on a continuous basis. The program requires police officers and sergeants from all units working the 2nd watch assigned to administrative functions, including officers assigned to D2 and D2A pay positions and assigned to Central Police Headquarters, to work one week in each of the four deployment sessions.
    NOTE:
    Department members who are assigned to administrative duties in District Law Enforcement or successful bidders to unit bid positions are EXCLUDED from assignment to the program.
  • C.The program week will run Monday through Friday, excluding Department holidays.
    NOTE:
    A deployed member’s regular day-off group and previously approved elective time will not be affected by participation in the program.
  • D.Members will report for roll call and check-off at the designated program location. Duty hours are 0900–1730 hours.
  • E.Deployed members will not be considered as a part of the personnel complement of the district to which they are deployed. Program supervisors will not be assigned to supervisory tasks that are the responsibility of district supervisors.
  • F.Members in an Injury-On-Duty status during their scheduled week of deployment will not be required to make up their absence from the program.
  • G.Members will reschedule additional days to compensate for each day missed due to placement on non-IOD medical status or use of elective time during their scheduled/assigned week.
  • H.Department members assigned to the program will use the “4500" series of radio call numbers.
III.Affected Personnel
  • A.The following Department members will participate in the program:
    • 1.Sworn Department members of the rank of sergeant and below who are:
    • 2.in full duty status, and
    • 3.currently assigned:
      • a.to a nonfield duty, or
        NOTE:
        Personnel that have successfully obtained a unit duty bid position, as described in Section 23.9 of the Agreement between the City of Chicago, Department of Police, and the Fraternal Order of Police Chicago Lodge No.7, are excluded from assignment to the Program.
      • b.to field duty, but performing a staff or nonfield duty task the majority of time during the total work day.
  • B.Affected members will:
    • 1.access the CLEAR system — Automated Deployment link, and create their deployment requests.
    • 2.submit by the date required, which will be specified in a quarterly Department Administrative Message Center (AMC) message, a minimum of five selections (weeks) indicating when they are available for assignment to the program.
      NOTE:
      Assignments will be made on the basis of seniority; however, the Department reserves the right to schedule to meet operational needs. Affected members are responsible for submitting their selections prior to beginning their furloughs. Members who fail to submit selections will be assigned at the discretion of the program commanding officer.
    • 3.not select to work in the program for any week they are scheduled for a furlough or baby furlough. Days in which the program is not operational will count toward fulfillment of a member’s participation if they fall within the member’s scheduled week.
    • 4.contact the program commanding officer to schedule additional days to compensate for each day missed due to placement on non-IOD medical status or elective-time use during their scheduled week.
      NOTE:
      All requests for the use of elective time off that may affect a member’s scheduled participation in the Operation Protect Youth Program must be submitted to the First Deputy Superintendent for approval.
IV.Responsibilities
  • A.The Office of the First Deputy Superintendent will:
    • 1.identify a lieutenant as the Operation Protect Youth Program commanding officer.
    • 2.identify required units and/or members not participating at the expected level and provide this information to the appropriate commanding officer for corrective action.
    • 3.develop a plan for the deployment and logistical support of the personnel assigned to the program.
    • 4.ensure personnel are notified for the upcoming quarter/session via the court notification system and Department email prior to the quarter/session beginning.
    • 5.ensure that district commanders and station supervisors are informed of the number of units deployed to their districts and watches on a daily basis.
    • 6.have sole authority to grant an affected member’s exemption or schedule change from the program.
  • B.Unit commanding officers will:
    • 1.ensure each eligible member of their command completes a one-week assignment to the program in each of the quarters/sessions.
    • 2.submit all requests for exemptions and schedule changes due to operational needs to the First Deputy Superintendent for approval.
    • 3.ensure the status of deployed members is properly recorded on the Assignment and Attendance Record.
  • C.The program commanding officer will:
    • 1.schedule and assign members based on directed missions as identified by the Deployment Operations Section.
    • 2.assign members to dismissal times at focus high schools identified by the Deployment Operations Section and consistent with the Bureau of Patrol Safe School Passage Program.
  • D.Program watch coordinators will:
    • 1.ensure that both the appropriate Office of Emergency Management and Communications (OEMC) zone and the district station supervisor(s) in the district(s) of deployment are notified of each member’s beat number and vehicle assignment.
    • 2.forward daily summary reports submitted by deployed personnel to the program commanding officer.
    • 3.maintain the Supervisor’s Management Logs (CPD-11.455) in accordance with current Department directives.
    • 4.notify a deployed member’s unit timekeeper if a member uses elective time or is excused from duty for any reason.
  • E.Supervisors deployed to the program will:
    • 1.obtain a radio and vehicle from the designated program location.
    • 2.ensure that members assigned to the program wear the regulation field uniform of the day, including soft body armor, and have their helmets and raincoats immediately accessible.
    • 3.ensure appropriate OEMC zone notification upon their arrival at their assignment.
    • 4.ensure the deployed units work within the identified parameters of their directed mission.
    • 5.approve, as appropriate, submitted reports from program officers.
      NOTE:
      Case reports will be processed through the district of occurrence. Other documents, such as Contact Information Cards (CPD-21.101), Personal Service Citations, Violation Notices, Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violations, and School Absentee Reports (CPD–24.116), will, after supervisory approval, be submitted to the program commanding officer who will ensure they are processed consistent with current Department directives.
    • 6.submit a completed Supervisor’s Management Log at the conclusion of each tour of duty.
  • F.Department members below the rank of sergeant deployed to the program will:
    • 1.be issued Personal Service Citations, Violation Notices, Administrative Notice of Ordinance Violations (ANOV), Contact Information Cards, and School Absentee Reports at the designated program roll call location.
    • 2.obtain a radio and vehicle from the designated program roll call location.
    • 3.proceed to their assigned deployment location and use one or more of the following tactics to address observed conditions and take appropriate police action:
      • a.assist in safe-passage efforts during school dismissals;
      • b.perform mobile patrols within a radius of the school with special attention to suspicious activity and in-progress crimes that either involve youths or have the potential to harm the school at large (e.g., drug and gang activity);
      • c.be assigned to peripheral zones, concentrating on the safe passage of students. Members will pay special attention to transit locations, after-school gathering points, libraries, parks, and other locations where youths may congregate within designated areas;
      • d.identify and process school absentees;
      • e.remain highly visible at all times while conducting Operation Protect Youth Program missions;
      • f.be deployed to school after-hour activities when police intelligence shows a potential for violence.
    • 4.monitor the calls for service on the radio zone frequency assigned to the district of their deployment location and respond, as appropriate, to support the assigned officers.
    • 5.submit all completed reports to their program sergeant, as necessary.
    • 6.request placement on the medical roll, as necessary, by notifying their unit of assignment/detail.
      NOTE:
      The notified unit will immediately inform the Operation Protect Youth Program watch coordinator of the member’s absence.
V.Court Scheduling
Per order of Court, court appearances will be scheduled as follows:
  • A.Traffic citations will be scheduled according to the member’s assigned court-key date, time, and room number.
  • B.Arrests will be scheduled according to the Department directive entitled “Court Call Schedule.”
  • C.Deployed members assigned to a unit not covered by the Department directive entitled “Court Call Schedule” will schedule court appearances as follows:
    • 1.If the member is the complainant or victim and:
      • a.resides north of Madison Street, misdemeanor cases will be scheduled for Branch 29 and felony cases will be scheduled for Branch 42.
      • b.resides south of Madison Street, misdemeanor cases will be scheduled for Branch 34 and felony cases will be scheduled for Branch 48.
    • 2.If the citizen is the complainant or victim, court will be scheduled by the location of the district of arrest according to the Department directive entitled “Court Call Schedule.”
