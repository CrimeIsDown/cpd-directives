Chicago Police DepartmentEmployee Resource E05-06-01
Removal Process for D-2, D-2A, and D-3 Positions
Issue Date:11 September 2020Effective Date:11 September 2020
Rescinds:12 March 2019 version
Index Category:Career Development
I.Purpose
This directive continues:
  • A.procedures for removing a sworn member below the rank of sergeant from D-2, D-2A, and D-3 assignments.
  • B.the use of the:
    • 1.Notice Of Hearing Before The Review Board (CPD-12.151).
    • 2.Review Board Process—Voting Member Determination (CPD-12.152).
    • 3.Review Board Determination Notice (CPD-12.153).
    • 4.Determination In The Appeal Of The Review Board Decision (CPD-12.154).
II.Policy
  • A.It is the policy of the Department to ensure that sworn members below the rank of sergeant assigned to D-2, D-2A, and D-3 assignments are adequately performing the functions of their assignment.
  • B.If a unit commanding officer requests a performance review of a member under his or her command, the Review Board may convene a hearing to determine whether the member is adequately performing their functions of the assignment and ensure the member receives sufficient due process.
  • C.The Review Board process will only be utilized in cases involving the review of an officer's performance. The review process will not apply if a member is being removed from his or her assignment for a reason unrelated to performance (e.g., budget modification, reduction in force initiative).
III.Request for Review
  • A.The process of removing a sworn member below the rank of sergeant from a D-2, D-2A, and D-3 assignment will be initiated by a supervisor via To-From-Subject Report through the chain of command to the affected member's chief. The report requesting removal will include documentation supporting the request.
  • B.The affected member's chief, or designated exempt member, will review and forward the review request to the Legal Affairs Division for a determination of legal sufficiency. If the removal request is:
    • 1.legally sufficient, the Legal Affairs Division will initiate the Review Board process.
    • 2.not legally sufficient, it will be returned to the initiating supervisor with additional instructions. Such instructions may include, but not be limited to, requests for additional information or recommendations that the affected member receive additional training, counseling, or placement into the Behavioral Intervention System.
IV.Notice of Hearing Before the Review Board
  • A.The Legal Affairs Division will:
    • 1.assign a date for the Review Board to convene a hearing to review the affected member's performance;
    • 2.enter the assigned date and time in the daily Court Notification system;
    • 3.prepare a Notice Of Hearing Before The Review Board (CPD-12.151) form; and
    • 4.forward the form to the affected member's unit of assignment.
  • B.The affected member must be notified of the:
    • 1.possible removal of the member's D-2, D-2A, or D-3 position before the Review Board;
    • 2.potential consequences of his or her conduct. The notification is satisfied by Department directives, Rules and Regulations of the Chicago Police Department, and Standard Operating Procedures (SOP).
    • 3.Review Board hearing date, time, and location posted on the Notice Of Hearing Before The Review Board (CPD-12.151).
    • 4.Review Board date, via the Daily Court Notifications system, following the acknowledged receipt of the Notice Of Hearing Before The Review Board (CPD-12.151).
  • C.The member's immediate supervisor will:
    • 1.serve the member the Notice Of Hearing Before The Review Board;
    • 2.ensure the member acknowledges receipt of the notice by signing the form;
    • 3.inform the affected member of the option to submit a To-From-Subject Report as described in Item IV-D; and
    • 4.forward copies of the notice based on the distribution list on the form.
  • D.Within seven working days of being served the notice, the member must inform the Review Board if he or she requests a hearing by submitting a To-From-Subject Report to the Commander, Labor Relations Division. The To-From-Subject Report must include a written response to the allegation(s) contained in the notice.
    NOTE:
    If no request for a hearing is received, the affected member will be deemed to have accepted notice and waived his or her right to a hearing.
  • E.The Labor Relations Division will forward the affected member's original To-From-Subject Report to the Legal Affairs Division.
V.Review Board Process
  • A.The Review Board will be the forum for hearings regarding the removal of a sworn member below the rank of sergeant from D-2, D-2A, and D-3 assignments.
  • B.The Review Board will be comprised of the following members:
    • 1.Chief, Office of Operations;
    • 2.Chief, Bureau of Detectives;
    • 3.Chief, Office of Constitutional Policing and Reform;
    • 4.Chief, Bureau of Internal Affairs;
    • 5.Chief, Bureau of Counterterrorism;
    • 6.A representative, Legal Affairs Division, who will serve as the Review Board secretary as a nonvoting member; and
    • 7.A representative, Labor Relations Division, who will serve as a nonvoting member.
  • C.The affected member's chief or his or her designated exempt member will serve as chairperson and a nonvoting member.
  • D.A Review Board hearing will be conducted only when a quorum of the membership is present. A quorum will consist of at least the chairperson and three voting members.
  • E.The affected member's commanding officer will provide all relevant documents to the Review Board secretary for distribution to members of the Review Board. The affected member's unit commanding officer will also present the case for removal to the Review Board.
  • F.The member appearing before the Review Board will have the right to advocate on his or her behalf or to have an advocate of their choice (attorney, Fraternal Order of Police representative, etc.).
    • 1.The advocate may present witnesses.
    • 2.The advocate may not cross-examine or question members of the Review Board.
  • G.The member appearing before the Review Board may offer documentation and testimony.
  • H.Upon receiving all relevant documentation and testimony, the Review Board will deliberate and arrive at a determination by conducting a poll of voting Review Board members. A majority of the Review Board members comprising a quorum is required for consensus.
VI.Conduct of The Hearing
  • A.The basis for convening the Review Board will be read at the hearing.
  • B.An audio recording will be made of all testimony given at a Review Board hearing.
  • C.Review Board members will have the option of questioning the affected member.
  • D.When arriving at a determination, the Review Board will consider all evidence, written and oral.
  • E.The board's options include, but are not limited to:
    • 1.removing the affected member from their assignment;
    • 2.establishing a period of supervision after which the Review Board will reconvene for further determination;
    • 3.scheduling training, counseling, or mentoring the affected member to address concerns;
    • 4.other remediation as deemed appropriate prior to removal; or
    • 5.no action necessary. The affected member will maintain his or her current assignment.
  • F.The board's decision will be provided to the Commander, Labor Relations Division.
  • G.Within five business days, the Labor Relations Division will inform the affected member, in writing, of the determination.
VII.Appeal Process
  • A.Within seventy-two hours of receiving the Review Board's decision, the affected member may submit a To-From-Subject Report through his or her chain of command to the Office of the Superintendent to appeal the determination.
  • B.The Superintendent will have the authority to affirm or reverse the Review Board decision within fourteen days after the meeting of the Review Board. The Superintendent's decision will be communicated via the Legal Affairs Division form titled "Determination In The Appeal Of The Review Board Decision (CPD-12.154)."
  • C.The final decision will be provided to the Director, Human Resources Division, for appropriate action(s).
