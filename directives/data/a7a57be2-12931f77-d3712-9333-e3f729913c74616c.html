Chicago Police DepartmentGeneral Order G05-06
Active Shooter Incident Plan
Issue Date:15 August 2008Effective Date:16 August 2008
Rescinds:G08-02
Index Category:Extraordinary Responses
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the Active Shooter Incident Plan (ASIP).
  • B.outlines procedures and responsibilities in the event an ASIP is initiated.
Department members will refer to the Special Order titled "Active Shooter Incident Plan" for training and evaluation responsibilities.
II.Policy
In the event that an active shooter incident
Active Shooter Incident
X
A violent incident, not limited to a threat involving a firearm, wherein an armed assailant(s) has remained on or near the scene and continues to present a threat to the safety of citizens that requires rapid deployment as opposed to containment and negotiation.
is identified, the Department will respond immediately; assess and contain the situation; ensure that any further threat is neutralized; protect the lives of victims threatened by the assailant(s); and secure medical aid for the injured.
III.General Information
The Active Shooter Incident Plan (ASIP) is designed to be utilized universally in response to any violent incident wherein an armed assailant(s) has remained on or near the scene and continues to present a threat to the safety of the citizens that requires rapid deployment as opposed to containment and negotiation. The Department's initial responsibility when responding to an Active Shooter Incident
Active Shooter Incident
X
A violent incident, not limited to a threat involving a firearm, wherein an armed assailant(s) has remained on or near the scene and continues to present a threat to the safety of citizens that requires rapid deployment as opposed to containment and negotiation.
is to mitigate the violence and neutralize the threat.
NOTE:
Locations in which the Active Shooter Incident Plan can be utilized include, but are not limited to educational facilities, shopping malls, train stations, large building complexes, public venues, businesses, etc.

7.
Safe School Information Package
-
A package that contains information on a particular school including, but not limited to, the Safe School Plan Information Sheet, floor plans for the school buildings, and a map of the surrounding area.
8.
EMS Triage Area
-
A secure position where the Chicago Fire Department (CFD) will establish a triage area.
9.
Victim Area
-
A secure location near the incident site where victims can be transported.
10.
Media Briefing Area
-
An area designated for the media to assemble and receive information concerning the incident.
11.
Family Briefing Area
-
A secure position outside the inner perimeter for the briefing of victim's family members by Department personnel.
12.
Secure Area
-
An area within the incident site that is secured to serve as a safe haven for victims until secure evacuations can be completed and where additional incident information can be obtained.
13.
Facility Information Management System (FIMS)
-
A database used within the CLEAR System as the depository for information on locations throughout the City of Chicago. Information stored in FIMS includes, but is not limited to, contact information of key personnel, information about the location structure and contents, and plans and/or photographs of the location. Information in FIMS is accessible on a need to know basis to personnel approved by the Commander, Deployment Operations Center Section.
14.
Staging Area
-
  • A.(Crime Scene) A designated area within the outer perimeter of a crime scene where field members assigned to investigate and process the scene of a crime can confer and be briefed and debriefed by supervisory personnel.
  • B.(General Incident) A secure location where all responding units will report for briefing, assignment, and equipment. The manager, whose primary function is to provide intelligence and direction to the responding units, is designated by the Incident Commander.
  • C.(Hazardous Material Incident (Haz-Mat)) A location designated for use as a stand-by location for personnel not actually committed to responsibilities at the incident.
15.
Incident Command Post
-
A secure position where the Incident Commander directs the operation.
16.
Information Area
-
  • A.A secure area within or in close proximity to the incident site where additional information can be obtained to assist responding officers.
    NOTE:
    This may be a security office, administrator's office, or engineer's office and this information area may be a distance away from where the violent incident is occurring or where the assailant(s) is located.
