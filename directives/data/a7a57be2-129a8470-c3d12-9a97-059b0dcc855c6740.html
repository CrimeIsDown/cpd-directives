Senior Citizens at Risk
Issue Date:14 July 2006Effective Date:15 July 2006
Rescinds:S06-06
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.states the Department policy regarding assisting senior citizens at risk.
  • B.specifies:
    • 1.signs and indicators of a senior citizen at risk.
    • 2.general responsibilities of Department members when dealing with incidents involving senior citizens.
  • C.provides:
    • 1.the procedures for:
      • a.handling involuntary admissions of a senior citizen to an approved geriatric medical facility.
      • b.assisting senior citizens who are not in Department custody, but seek voluntary admission to an approved geriatric medical facility.
      • c.assisting senior citizens in finding temporary shelter in certain circumstances.
    • 2.information on the handling of official records dealing with incidents involving senior citizens.
  • D.identifies:
    • 1.elder abuse and protective services.
    • 2.approved geriatric medical facilities.
  • E.references:
    • 1.the Senior Citizen Assistance Card (CPD-21.837).
    • 2.the Senior/Disabled at Risk Checklist (CPD-21.838).
    • 3.the General Instructions for the Senior/Disabled at Risk Checklist (CPD-21.838A).
    • 4.the Office of the Assistant Superintendent, Operations, Extreme Weather Report Results (CPD-20.003).
    • 5.relevant State statues dealing with senior citizens.
    • 6.the Petition for Involuntary/Judicial Admission.
II.Policy
It is the policy of the Department to develop and implement new strategies to address crimes against the senior population within the City of Chicago. The Departments goal is to assist the City of Chicago Department of Aging in recognizing and assisting those senior citizens that may be at risk. The Chicago Police Department will provide direct and indirect services to senior citizens to ensure that they obtain and maintain dignity, respect, independence, and personal safety.
III.Signs and Indicators of a Senior Citizen at Risk
Members encountering a situation involving a senior citizen will assess the situation for signs or indicators of abuse or neglect including, but not limited to:
  • A.Physical
    • 1.Malnutrition.
    • 2.Old and new bruising, suggesting repeated injury.
    • 3.Bruising in the shape of an object.
    • 4.Imprint bruising, indicating rough handling.
    • 5.Frequent injuries, with implausible explanations.
    • 6.Poor personal hygiene.
    • 7.Damaged prostheses, including broken eyeglasses, dental prosthesis, and hearing aids.
  • B.Environmental
    • 1.Unclean living arrangements (e.g., filth, pests, etc.).
    • 2.Unsafe conditions in the home.
    • 3.Lack of heat, electricity, water, or food.
  • C.Senior Citizens Behavior
    • 1.Depression, withdrawal, or apathy.
    • 2.Anti-social, agitated, isolated, and fear and reluctance to talk openly.
    • 3.Hostility toward caregivers (yelling, shoving).
  • D.Caregivers Behavior
    • 1.Hostility toward the senior citizen (yelling, shoving).
    • 2.Alcohol and/or drug abuse.
    • 3.Reluctance to permit the senior citizen to speak in private with others, leave the home, or receive services.
  • E.Financial
    • 1.Unusual activity in bank accounts.
    • 2.Basic needs not met when income appears adequate.
    • 3.Exploitative use or conversion of personal/real property.
IV.General Responsibilities
  • A.The role of the Chicago Police Department in senior citizen abuse cases varies depending on the type of abuse. Members will protect the victim(s), prevent and stop abuse, and enforce the law, arrest the offender, and provide referrals to other agencies and resources that can address non-police- related incidents.
  • B.Preliminary investigators will:
    • 1.investigate the complaint, and, if bonafide, complete the appropriate case report consistent to Department directives entitled, "Preliminary Investigations: Required Immediate Notifications and Investigations Conducted At Suburban Hospitals."
      NOTE:
      The officer who initially discovers any suspected abuse, neglect, or exploitation of a senior citizen must report the offense within 24 hours to the Illinois Department on Agings Elder Abuse Hotline, and note the notification in the narrative of the case report.
    • 2.ensure immediate medical treatment in cases of physical abuse.
    • 3.contact the  Department of Human Services (DHS)
      Department of Family and Support Services
      X
      312-743-0300
      , available 24 hours a day in cases of unsafe conditions, involuntary commitment to a medical facility, neglect abuse, exploitation, lack of heat, electricity, food, or water.
  • C.When a member believes an incident should be documented, but has not been provided with an adequate amount of information to substantiate a crime, the member will:
    • 1.complete a General Offense Case Report (CPD-11.380), using Non-Criminal in the primary classification box, and whichever is appropriate, either the Other Non-Criminal Persons (I-UCR offense code 5080) or Other Non-Criminal Property (I-UCR offense code 5081) for the secondary classification.
    • 2.state only the facts in the narrative and omit any conclusions.
      NOTE:
      A General Offense Case Report is prepared to document information and provide follow-up investigators with possible patterns of criminal activity in an effort to prevent the victim from suffering future abuse, neglect, or exploitation.
  • D.Community policing officers assigned to work with senior and disabled citizens will:
    • 1.identify:
      • a.any crime patterns focusing on senior citizen victims.
      • b.the crime prevention programs which would provide useful information to reduce victimization of senior citizens.
    • 2.distribute public awareness and education materials.
    • 3.conduct a follow-up visit or phone call to senior citizen crime victims when additional information would aid in the apprehension of the offender, or reduce the trauma or victimization.
    • 4.participate in the district senior citizen advisory subcommittee.
    • 5.educate senior citizens on crime prevention and outside agencies.
    • 6.provide police services in accordance with the special needs of senior citizens.
    • 7.actively seek out and enroll senior citizens in the Emergency Identification Bracelet Program.
  • E.District commanders will ensure:
    • 1.their review officers separate and evaluate all case reports that record crimes against persons sixty years of age and older.
    • 2.that aggressive enforcement action is taken to abate an identified crime pattern by utilizing the services available through the Bureau of Patrol, Office of the Assistant Superintendent, Operations, and the Senior Services Unit.
    • 3.that a supply of the Petition for Involuntary/Judicial Admission is kept in the district.
  • F.The district review officer will:
    • 1.use crime analysis techniques to determine the frequency and similarity of incidents involving senior citizens and bring specific incidents to the attention of the district commander.
    • 2.provide the Senior Citizens Services Officers with copies of all case reports of incidents involving senior citizens.
  • G.The deputy chiefs of the area detective divisions will direct the Criminal Analysis Unit to identify recurring or similar crimes directed against senior citizens. The identified crime pattern will be distributed to affected units including the Bureau of Patrol, the Office of the Assistant Superintendent, Operations, and the Senior Services Unit, so enforcement action can be taken.
  • H.The senior citizens services officers will:
    • 1.provide assistance to the senior citizens advisory councils in all districts.
    • 2.analyze statistical information involving crimes against senior citizens and conduct a follow-up visitation program in selected cases.
    • 3.assist the Education and Training Division in developing senior citizen education training programs.
  • I.The Assistant Deputy Superintendent, Education and Training Division, will ensure that all recruits are trained to identify crimes and special problems involving senior citizens.
V.Procedures for Handling Involuntary/Voluntary Admission
Department members will follow the procedures as outlined in the Department directive entitled, "Persons Subject To Involuntary or Voluntary Admission Nonarrestees" when handling involuntary/voluntary admission of seniors.
VI.Temporary Shelter for Senior Citizens
  • A.When an individual sixty years of age or older is in need of temporary shelter, Department members will:
    • 1.arrange for the persons removal to a hospital approved by the Department of Health when medical care of an emergency nature is needed.
    • 2.arrange for transportation to a shelter by contacting the Department of Human Services and, if applicable, prepare the appropriate case report.
  • B.The Department of Human Services will provide temporary shelter to individuals sixty years of age or older who:
    • 1.have been evicted and cannot provide for their own immediate shelter.
    • 2.have lost their home through fire, demolition, or condemnation.
    • 3.have been temporarily removed from their home due to emergency repairs, fumigation; and assistance from family members, friends, or neighbors is not readily available.
    • 4.are found wandering, lost and/or disoriented, and are in need of temporary shelter.
    • 5.are bonafide crime victims/witnesses who have suffered emotional trauma, physical injury, or property loss as a result of being a victim/witness of a crime and whose well-being may be in jeopardy by staying home.
VII.Official Records
  • A.All records concerning reports of senior citizen abuse, neglect, and financial exploitation may be disclosed only as permitted by law.
  • B.Access to such records, but not access to the identity of the person(s) making a report of alleged abuse, neglect, or financial exploitation as contained in such records, shall be allowed to law enforcement officials investigating known or suspected senior citizen abuse, neglect, or financial exploitation.
    NOTE:
    Where a provider agency has reason to believe the death of a senior citizen may be the result of abuse or neglect, the provider agency shall immediately provide law enforcement officials with all records pertaining to the senior citizen.
VIII.Elder Abuse and Protective Services
  • A.Per Illinois Compiled Statute 320 ILCS 20/1 entitled Elder Abuse and Neglect Act, any sworn Department member who has reason to believe that a senior citizen has been subject to abuse, neglect, or exploitation and is unable to seek assistance on their own, is required to report such information within twenty-four hours to the State of Illinois Elder Abuse Prevention Program.
    • 1.Notification will be to the Illinois Department on Agings 24-hour Elder Abuse Hotline. Subsequently, the notifications will be routed to the appropriate service provider agency.
    • 2.If conditions or circumstances warrant, a call may be made directly to the provider agency that serves the area in which the senior citizen lives.

IX.Approved Medical Facilities
  • A.For a listing of approved medical facilities members will refer to the Department directive entitled "Approved Medical Facilities."
  • B.Senior citizens not under arrest who are brought to an approved medical facility by Department members will be accepted at any time on a no-decline basis. In the event an approved medical facility refuses to evaluate a senior citizen, the following actions will be taken:
    • 1.Members will request through the OEMC that a supervisor respond to the medical facility.
    • 2.The responding supervisor will request that the senior citizen be evaluated.
    • 3.If the medical facility still refuses to evaluate a senior citizen, the supervisor will notify the watch commander.
      • a.The watch commander will determine the next course of action if the issue cannot be resolved at the scene.
      • b.All involved members will then follow the procedures outlined in the Department directive entitled, "Liaison with Hospitals, Detoxification Facilities and Mental Health Facilities."
X.Senior Citizen Assistance Card
Department members will provide senior citizens with a Senior Citizen Assistance Card containing a list of various telephone numbers that are helpful in notifying proper authorities of lost or stolen credit cards and other personal documents.
