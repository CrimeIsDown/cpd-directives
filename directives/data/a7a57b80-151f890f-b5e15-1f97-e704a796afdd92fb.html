Chicago Police DepartmentDepartment Notice D15-13
Cook County Drug Analysis Field Test Pilot Program
Issue Date:31 December 2015Effective Date:03 January 2016
Rescinds:
Index Category:Department Notice
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.introduces the State of Illinois Public Act 099-0425 The Cook County Drug Analysis Field Test Pilot Program.
  • B.introduces the mandatory requirement that all police officers assigned/detailed to District 006, be trained in the use of the X-CAT Field Test Narcotic Kit, and use the kit for the processing of all narcotics arrests for cocaine and heroin. For District 006 personnel, the Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908 will be used when making cannabis arrests for any amount of cannabis
    NOTE:
    Members will be automatically registered for the X-CAT Field Test Narcotic Kit training in the eLearning training application under enrolled learning modules.
  • C.Introduces the use of the X-CAT Field Test Narcotic Kit Log (CPD-21.243).
  • D.Introduces the use of the X-Cat Field Test Narcotic Kit List (CPD-21.244).
II.Scope
The Cook County Drug Analysis Field Test Pilot Program is operational for members assigned/detailed to District 006.
III.Policy
  • A.It is the policy of the Chicago Police Department to follow Illinois State Public Act 099-0425 regarding the use of a narcotic identification kit to field test cocaine, heroin, and cannabis. The "X-CAT" field test narcotic kit will be used to test cocaine and heroin. The "Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908" field test kit will continue to be used to test cannabis. For purposes of the preliminary examination only, the field test results shall be used in lieu of drug chemistry laboratory reports from the Department of State Police Division of Forensic Services.
  • B.The testing of suspected cocaine, heroin, and marijuana will only be conducted in accordance with training and instructions given by the Department.
  • C.Members will use only the "X-CAT" and the "Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908" field test narcotic kits in conjunction with their official duties.
    NOTE:
    For District 006 personnel, the Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908 will be used when making cannabis arrests for any amount of cannabis. Department members will use the appropriate ILCS Possession of Cannabis charge. Members will refer to the Department directive "Alternative Cannabis Enforcement Program," when recovering under 15 grams of cannabis.
IV.GOALS OF THE COOK COUNTY DRUG ANALYSIS FILED TEST PILOT PROGRAM
The Cook County Drug Analysis Field Test Pilot Program shall assess whether the use of field tests in Cook County will:
  • A.reduce the number of days a person would otherwise remain in custody awaiting drug chemistry reports;
  • B.result in expedited preliminary examinations for cannabis, cocaine, and heroin offenses; and
  • C.reduce the overall Cook County Jail population at a substantial cost savings to Cook County taxpayers.
V.Test Kit Equipment
The pilot program will utilize the following:
  • A. X-CAT device and sampling cards with specifically formulated optical detection inks and software to identify characteristics unique to each individual or group of chemically related substances undergoing chemical analysis.
  • B.The X-CAT field test kits will be located in a secure area and will only be issued to Department members by the station supervisor.
VI.X-CAT DEVICE TESTING PROCEDURES FOR COCAINE AND HEROIN
All police officers assigned/detailed to District 006 will be trained and certified in the use the X-CAT device, and be required to use the X-CAT device when making arrests for heroin and cocaine. Department members will follow the below guidelines when administering the field test narcotic kits:
  • A.When using the field test narcotic kit, members will use one of the following methods to collect the sample:
    • 1.Direct Method-Grinding: grinding the card's white sampling paper directly into the suspect narcotics specimen.
    • 2.Direct Method-Swiping: the card's white sampling paper must be firmly swiped against the suspect narcotics specimen.
    • 3.Indirect Method: using a cotton swab or glove to remove suspect narcotics from the specimen and place it on the bottom of the white sampling paper.
  • B.Members will always administer the test in a police facility to prevent cross contamination and for personal safety.
  • C.The X-CAT field test narcotic kit sampling card will be inventoried separately and sent to ERPS. The remaining substance will be inventoried and sent to the Illinois State Police Forensic Science Center (ISP-FSC-C) for testing. Department members will refer to Department directive "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence," when the X-CAT detects a positive narcotic substance.
  • D.When the result of the test kit is negative, and no additional charges apply, the officer will immediately notify the district station supervisor to release the subject without charging, following the procedures outlined in the Department directive "Releasing Arrestees Without Charging and Waiving Fingerprint Results." The arresting officer will prepare an automated arrest report indicating the negative test result and:
    • 1.inventory the sampling card in one Evidence - Property Envelope (blue) (CPD34.559), ensuring the test kit is packaged securely and:
      • a.select "Cocaine/Heroin - Negative" for the "Item Type" in eTrack for the sampling card, indicating a negative test result;
      • b.indicate that the sampling card will be disposed of by the ERPS custodian.
    • 2.inventory the remaining unknown substance separately in one Evidence - Property Envelope (blue) (CPD.34.559) and:
      • a.select "Other" for the "Item Type" in eTrack for the remaining unknown substance, if no other controlled substance is present;
      • b.indicate in the inventory that the substance will be returned to owner;
      • c.return any left over substance to the owner when a negative test indicates no suspect narcotics is present and no additional charges apply;
      • d.document the subject's name and demographics in the "Recovered From" fields.
  • E.All narcotics will be inventoried in its original packaging consistent with the Department directive "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence."
VII.DUQUENOIS-LEVINE REAGENT SYSTEM TESTING PROCEDURES FOR MARIJUANA
  • A.All police officers assigned/detailed to District 006 making an arrest for any amount of cannabis, will use the Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908. Members are required to be trained and certified, and will:
    • 1.request a cannabis test kit from the district of occurrence station supervisor;
    • 2.conduct the cannabis test following the training provided and written instructions.
  • B.If the subject is arrested for a cannabis offense (15 grams or more unless aggravating factors exist per Department directive, "Alternative Cannabis Enforcement Program") and the cannabis test result is positive, the charging member will inventory the cannabis and the cannabis test kit together in one Evidence bag (green) (CPD-11.511) ensuring the test kit is packaged securely and:
    • 1.select " Cannabis Test Kit - Positive" for the item type in eTrack for the cannabis test kit, indicating a positive test result;
    • 2.select "Cannabis" for the "Item Type" in eTrack for the rest of the suspect narcotic(s), any/all packaging;
    • 3.indicate the appropriate ILCS "Possession of Cannabis" charge;
    • 4.send to Illinois State Police Crime Lab for testing in accordance with the Department directive "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence."
  • C.If the subject is arrested for a cannabis offense (15 grams or more unless aggravating factors exist per Department directive, "Alternative Cannabis Enforcement Program") and the cannabis test result is negative and no additional charges apply, the officer will immediately notify the district station supervisor to release the subject without charging, following the procedures outlined in the Department directive "Releasing Arrestees Without Charging and Waiving Fingerprint Results." The arresting officer will prepare an Automated Arrest report indicating the negative test result and:
    • 1.inventory the cannabis test kit in one Evidence - Property Envelope (blue) (CPD-34.559) ensuring the test kit is packaged securely and:
      • a.select "Cannabis Test Kit - Negative" for the "Item Type" in eTrack for the cannabis test kit, indicating a negative test result;
      • b.indicate that the inventory will be disposed of by the ERPS custodian;
    • 2.inventory the remaining unknown substance separately in one Evidence - Property Envelope (blue) (CPD-34.559) and:
      • a.select "Other" for the "Item Type" in eTrack for the remaining unknown substance, if no other suspected controlled substance is present;
      • b.indicate in the inventory that the substance will be returned to owner;
      • c.document the subject's name and demographics in the "Recovered From" fields.
    NOTE:
    Members can refer to Department directive entitled "Alternate Cannabis Enforcement Program" for further clarification on above listed testing procedures.
VIII. RESPONSIBILITIES
  • A.The executive officer will ensure:
    • 1.sworn members under their command are trained in this policy and certified in using the X-CAT field test narcotic kit;
    • 2.sworn members under their command are trained in this policy and certified in using the Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908.
  • B.The district station supervisor will ensure:
    • 1.the X-CAT and sampling cards, along with the Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908 are in adequate supply and available for use.
    • 2.the X-CAT Field Test Narcotic Kit Log (CPD-21.243) is maintained and kept up to date, including a current list of those officers trained in the use of the X-CAT field test narcotic kit.
    • 3.cleaning and calibration of the X-CAT Device after every 20 tests.
    • 4.before the end of their tour of duty, scan and submit a copy of the X-Cat Field Test Narcotic Kit List (CPD-21.244), to the Cook County Sheriff, Records Division, Lieutenant Charles Luna at: Charles.luna@cookcountyIl.gov.
    • 5.the X-Cat Field Test Narcotic Kit List (CPD-21.244) will be forwarded to the district review office and will be retained for the duration of the pilot program and evaluation.
    • 6.all procedures are followed in accordance with Department directive, "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence."
    • 7.all procedures are followed in accordance with Department directive, "Releasing Arrestees Without Charging and Waiving Fingerprint Results," when notified by an the arresting officer of a negative narcotic result and no additional charges apply.
  • C.The arresting officer assigned/detailed to District 006 will:
    • 1.participate in mandatory X-CAT Field Test Narcotic Kit training available in the eLearning application.
    • 2.use the kit for the processing of all narcotics arrests for cocaine and heroin.
    • 3.request the X-CAT or Duquenois-Levine Reagent System #908 field test narcotic kit from the district station supervisor when making narcotics arrests.
    • 4.perform the test using the appropriate field test narcotic kit sample card or cannabis test kit, in accordance with Department training.
    • 5.for positive field test narcotic kit results, inventory the suspect narcotics sample card in a blue inventory bag and send the used sampling card to ERPS. Members will inventory the rest of the suspect narcotic and any/all packaging, then forward to the the Illinois State Police Forensic Science Center (ISP-FSC-C) for testing in accordance with Department directive entitled "Inventorying Narcotics Evidence."
    • 6.list in the beginning of the Arrest Report "this an X-CAT related arrest," and the method of collection, i.e. grinding method, swiping method, or the indirect method.
