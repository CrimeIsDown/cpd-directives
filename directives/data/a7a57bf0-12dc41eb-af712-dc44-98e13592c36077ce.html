Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S02-05-06
Sex Offender Registration and Community Notification
Issue Date:24 April 1998Effective Date:25 April 1998
Rescinds:S98-02
Index Category:Human Rights and Community Partnerships
I.Purpose
This directive:
  • A.informs Department members of pertinent provisions of the Sex Offender and Child Murderer Community Notification Law (730 Illinois Compiled Statutes (ILCS) 152/101 et seq) and Sex Offender Registration Act (730 ILCS 150/1 et seq).
  • B.modifies procedures for retention and distribution of sex offender data.
  • C.provides instructions for processing persons taken into custody under the provisions of this Act.
  • D.assigns Departmental responsibilities for community notification as provided under the Sex Offender and Child Murderer Community Notification Law.
  • E.introduces the Criminal Registration Receipt form (CPD-23.205).
II.General Information
  • A.Any person convicted of an Illinois Statute listed under 720 ILCS 5; or Federal, or law of another State that is equivalent to the Sexually Dangerous Person Act is required to register. A sex offender will re-register every year in person for a period of 10 years from conviction or release from a penal institution, hospital or other institution or facility, whichever is later.
  • B.A sex offender, upon release from a court, prison, or other institution, will be advised of his responsibility to register, as provided by the Sex Offender Registration Act. The offender will sign the Illinois State Police Sex Offender Notification form prior to release stating that he is aware of his duty to register and that the proper procedures have been explained to him.
  • C.The court, prison, or other institution will retain the original Illinois State Police Sex Offender Notification form for its files and supply a copy to the offender.
  • D.The court, prison, or other institution will be responsible for entering the information contained on the Illinois State Police Sex Offender Registration form into the Law Enforcement Agency Data System (LEADS).
  • E.A sex offender:
    • 1.has 10 days after establishing residency to register in person with local law enforcement officials. If the offender is establishing a temporary residence within a city, but for a time period longer than 10 days, he must also register.
    • 2.is required to report a change of address in writing to the local law enforcement agency of registration within 10 days of establishing a new residence. The offender must appear in person within 10 days of moving to the local law enforcement agency of jurisdiction to re-register.
  • F.Any person required to register with the Department under the Sex Offender Registration Act will be directed to the Chicago Police Department Headquarters Building.
III.Illinois State Police Responsibilities
The Illinois State Police (ISP) will:
  • A.administer the Sex Offender Registration Act.
  • B.develop a system of informational computer screens for the LEADS system. These computer screens will inform law enforcement personnel, when conducting a name inquiry, that the subject is a convicted sex offender. The computer screens will list the current status of the offender as:
    • 1.Registered; or
    • 2.Moving to --- County; or
    • 3.Moving out of State; or
    • 4.Not Notified / To Be Notified; or
    • 5.Notified / Not Registered; or
    • 6.Incarcerated.
  • C.provide the Department with a quarterly listing of all schools and child care facilities eligible to receive community notifications of registered Sex Offenders.
IV.Department Responsibilities
  • A.A Department member who receives a LEADS computer informational report on a sex offender whose status is:
    • 1.Registered - will verify the offenders registrational status with the Submit Information Report (SIR) file (located on the Hot Desk computer network) when the offender is a resident of Chicago. The offender will be taken into custody if:
      • a.the offenders current stated address is different from the SIR file address and he has resided at that address for over 10 days . (730 ILCS 150/6 - The offender failed to notify the local law enforcement agency of his change of address), or
      • b.the "Last Contact Date" listed in the SIR file is over 1 year and two weeks ( 730 ILCS 150/3 - The offender had a duty to register annually and failed).
    • 2.Moving to --- County - will take no police action. This is a LEADS caution file and supplied for informational purposes only.
    • 3.Moving Out of State - will take no police action. This is a LEADS caution file and supplied for informational purposes only.
    • 4.Not Notified / To Be Notified - will inform the subject of his duty to register, as stated in Item III of this directive, and submit an Information Report (CPD-11.461). This report will detail the reason for the stop, date, time, location, and the Department member making the notification. After orally notifying the subject, the Department member will attempt to obtain the subjects signature in the narrative section of the Information Report, affirming notification. In the event that the subject refuses to sign, the Department member will document the refusal on the Information Report. The Information Report will be submitted to the Commander, Deployment Operations Center, as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Information Report System." The Commander, Deployment Operations Center will forward the original to the Detective Division Headquarters attention Career Criminal Liaison-Sex Offender Registration Unit, and a copy to the LEADS desk.
    • 5.Notified / Not Registered - will take the offender into custody for a violation of 730 ILCS 150/3.
    • 6.Incarcerated - This is a LEADS caution file and supplied for informational purposes only.
  • B.A Department member taking a sex offender into custody will:
    • 1.prepare a General Offense Case Report (CPD-11.380) detailing the reasonable suspicion for the name inquiry and the response received. The "Primary" classification will be Other Criminal Offenses; Secondary classification Failure to Register. The I-UCR code will be 4650. If the person is taken into custody for an unrelated incident requiring the preparation of a case report, no additional case report is required.
      NOTE:
      An offender will be released without charging as outlined in the Department directive entitled, "Processing Persons Under Department Control," if registration verification is unsuccessful.
    • 2.prepare an Arrest Report (CPD-11.420) as outlined in the Department directive entitled, "Processing Persons Under Department Control."
    • 3.notify the appropriate Detective Division area for follow -up investigation and charging of the offender.
    • 4.be required to appear in court.
V.Detective Division Responsibilities
  • A.The Detective Division is responsible for follow-up investigation of offenders taken into custody for non-compliance with the Sex Offender Registration Act. The appropriate Detective Division Violent Crimes office will assign a detective upon notification to complete the investigation and charging of the offender.
  • B.The Commanding Officer, Detective Division, Headquarters Support Section, will ensure that:
    • 1.a Department member(s) is available on the second watch (Monday through Friday) to assist persons in complying with the requirements of registration as defined in the Act.
    • 2.a sex offender database is established and maintained. This database will contain information on all persons:
      • a.for whom the Department has received an ISP Sex Offender Notification.
      • b.complying with the Act and currently residing within the City of Chicago, or maintaining a temporary residence for a period over 10 days and registered according to the Act.
    • 3.a separate listing, by police districts, is made available to the Records Inquiry Section for public inspection. This listing will contain all sex offenders complying with the Act.
    • 4.a Department member is designated as the point of contact for all outside agencies inquiring about the Sex Offender Registration Program.
    • 5.all monies collected under the Sex Offender Registration Act are deposited.
  • C.The Department member(s) assigned to processing persons and correspondence in accordance with the Sex Offender Registration Act will:
    • 1.be available to register Sex Offenders complying with the Act, Monday through Friday, between 0700 and 1500 hours (excluding holidays).
    • 2.maintain a supply of ISP Sex Offender Registration formsets.
    • 3.obtain positive proof of identification and residency from the sex offender.
    • 4.provide the sex offender with a Criminal Registration Receipt form.
    • 5.collect the registration fee.
    • 6.distribute the Criminal Registration Receipt as follows:
      • a.original to registrants file.
      • b.second copy to registrant.
      • c.third copy to the Finance Division.
    • 7.assist the offender in completing the ISP Sex Offender Registration formset and match it with the ISP Notification. These forms will be retained until the offenders requirement to register has expired or the offender has moved to a new jurisdiction.
    • 8.photograph the offender.
    • 9.fingerprint the offender and submit the fingerprints to the Identification Section along with a copy of the registration formset.
    • 10.forward the offenders photograph to the ISP within 3 days of registration.
    • 11.maintain the Departments sex offender registration database.
    • 12.forward all criminal registration fees to the Finance Division.
    • 13.prepare a listing of all sex offenders required to register and currently residing in the City of Chicago. This listing will be forwarded monthly to all Detective Division, Violent Crime Offices, Youth Investigations Section, and Area Youth Offices.
    • 14.prepare the distribution list of sex offenders required to register. This listing will have a banner line across the top of every page stating the information is only current on the date of preparation. The listing must state the sex offenders:
      • a.name,
      • b.address,
      • c.date of birth, and
      • d.offense or adjudication.
    • 15.forward quarterly (Feb / May / Aug / Nov) to each district commander, the listing of sex offenders along with the listing of schools and child care facilities to be notified.
    • 16.forward a copy of the citywide listing of Sex Offenders eligible for community notification to the following:
      • a.President, Chicago School Reform, Board of Trustees
      • b.Archdiocese of Chicago, Director of Administrative Affairs, Office of Catholic Education, (Archdiocese Schools)
      • c.Executive Secretary, District Three - Christian Schools International, (Christian Schools of Michigan)
      • d.Superintendent, Associated Talmud Torahs (Jewish Schools)
      • e.Executive Director-ISACS, (Independent Schools)
      • f.Education Executive, Northern Illinois District, Lutheran Church-Missouri Synod, (Lutheran Schools-Missouri Synod)
      • g.Director of Schools, Evangelical Lutheran Schools of America (Lutheran Schools-American Synod)
      • h.Superintendent, Seventh Day Adventist, (Seventh Day Adventist)
      • i.Headmaster, Association of Christian Schools International, (Christian Schools International)
      • j.Superintendent, Greek Orthodox Schools, (Greek Orthodox)
      • k.Executive Director, Illinois Association of Christian Schools, (Christian Schools Illinois)
      • l.Superintendent, Evangelical Lutheran Church in America, Concordia University, (Lutheran Church America)
      • m.President, Illinois Montessori Society, (Montessori Schools)
      • n.Superintendent of Schools, Seventh Day Adventist (Lake Region Conference)
    • 17.forward a copy of the entire listing of sex offenders to the Point of Contact person identified by the Department of Children and Family Services.
VI.Finance Division Responsibility
The Finance Division will:
  • A.audit and retain all bank deposit slips and Criminal Registration Receipts.
  • B.ensure all monies collected under the Sex Offender Registration Act are returned for use by the Department.
VII.Records Services Division Responsibilities
  • A.The Identification Section, upon receiving the fingerprints of a registered sex offender, will:
    • 1.compare the fingerprints to the offenders past criminal history to confirm identification.
    • 2.update the offenders IR sheet to reflect registration compliance with the Act.
    • 3.issue an IR number to those offenders who do not have a criminal history on file with the Department.
    • 4.notify the Detective Division, Headquarters Support Section, that a registered sex offender has been arrested.
  • B.The Field Inquiry Section will:
    • 1.notify the Detective Division, Headquarters Support Section, upon receipt of a sex offender notification transmitted via the LEADS system. This notification will be done on a daily basis.
    • 2.upon receipt of a Sex Offender Registration Form, enter all required data into the LEADS system within 3 days.
    • 3.enter a LEADS add-on message into the system indicating that a sex offender has been informed of his duty to register whenever an Information Report is received. This add-on message will include the date, time and location of contact, as well as the Department members name and star number.
  • C.The Records Inquiry Section will:
    • 1.allow for public inspection of the citywide sex offender listing.
    • 2.permit members of the public to copy information from the sex offender listings, but will not reproduce or disseminate the list.
    • 3.maintain a record of persons viewing this list as outlined in Items VIII-C-1 through 6.
VIII.District Commander Responsibilities
The district commander, upon receipt of a sex offender listing, will ensure that the neighborhood relations sergeant:
  • A.provides a copy of the entire listing to the following entities located within his district:
    • 1.the principal or other appropriate administrative officer of each non-public school as listed.
    • 2.the manager of each child care facility as listed.
  • B.maintains the district sex offender listing. This listing will be open for public inspection during regular business days and hours. Members of the public may hand copy information from the list. However, the list will not be reproduced or disseminated.
  • C.maintains a record of all persons viewing the sex offender listing. This record will contain the persons:
    • 1.name,
    • 2.address,
    • 3.date of birth,
    • 4.sex,
    • 5.race, and
    • 6.form of photo identification presented.
  • D.discloses the information in Items V-C-14-a through d to any person or entity who, in the district commanders opinion, is likely to encounter a sex offender.
IX.Restrictions
  • A.Information regarding registered sex offenders (Items V-C-14-a through d) will be open for inspection by the public, as authorized by the Sex Offender and Child Murderer Community Notification Law.
  • B.Information required by the Sex Offender Registration Act will not be open to inspection by the public or by any other person, other than a law enforcement officer or other individual authorized by law. It is a Class B misdemeanor to permit the unauthorized release of any information required by the Sex Offender Registration Act.
  • C.Sworn members are authorized access to the Sex Offender Registration Act database with the approval of their unit commanding officer and concurrence by the Chief of the Detective Division.
