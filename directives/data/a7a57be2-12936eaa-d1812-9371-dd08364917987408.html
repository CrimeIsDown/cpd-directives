I.Purpose
This directive designates responsibilities for developing, updating, and providing appropriate training for emergency action plans

II.Responsibilities
  • A.The Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services, is responsible for:
    • 1.developing and maintaining an emergency action plan

    • 2.coordinating the development and maintenance of emergency action plans for all other Department facilities.
    • 3.distributing emergency action plans to the appropriate divisions and units.
    • 4.completing an annual report to the Superintendent on the status of all Emergency Action Plans.
  • B.Command staff members and unit commanding officers, at facilities other than Central Police Headquarters, will develop or assist in the development of an emergency action plan for their facility as directed by the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services.
  • C.The Commander, Bomb and Arson Section and the command staff member of the Research and Development Division will assist the Deputy Superintendent, Bureau of Administrative Services in reviewing emergency action plans.
  • D.Command staff members, unit commanding officers, and watch commanders will ensure compliance with the Emergency Action Plan pertaining to their facility with respect to both preparedness for emergency situations and implementation of the plan during an emergency.
  • E.To ensure access to Department facilities for emergency vehicles and responders, command staff members responsible for Department facilities will evaluate and implement parking restrictions to be enforced at all times at the front entrance of each Department facility. All Department supervisory personnel will be responsible for enforcing the parking restrictions.
III.Drills and Training
  • A.Drilling is the responsibility of the command staff member who has primary responsibility for each Department facility. In a shared Department facility, the command staff member ordering a drill will consult with any other command staff members of equal or higher rank assigned to the facility.
  • B.The Inspection Division will:
    • 1.schedule and monitor additional drills at each Department facility, for the purposes of evaluation, according to the following schedule:
      • a.Even-numbered years:
        • (1)Even-numbered districts
        • (2)Homan Square Combined Police facility
        • (3)Education and Training Division facility
        • (4)Equipment and Supply Section facility
        • (5)Evidence and Recovered Property Section Bulk Storage facility
        • (6)All public housing facilities
        • (7)All Auto Pound facilities.
      • b.Odd-numbered years:
        • (1)Odd-numbered districts
        • (2)Central Police Headquarters
        • (3)South Loop Police Complex
        • (4)Canine Training facility
        • (5)Marine Unit facility
        • (6)Mounted Unit facility
        • (7)Airport North facility
        • (8)Alternate Response Section
    • 2.complete a report to the Superintendent summarizing the results of each evaluation drill performed.
  • C.Unit commanding officers will ensure that emergency action plan

  • D.Mock arrestees will be used when conducting evacuation drills at detention facilities. Actual arrestees will be transported to an alternate detention facility prior to the drill.
  • E.Department facilities may be drilled in whole or by section. However, all sections must undergo an evaluation drill consistent with Item III-B-1 of this directive.
