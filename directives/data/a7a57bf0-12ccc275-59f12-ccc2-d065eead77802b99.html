Chicago Police DepartmentSpecial Order S06-01-10
Placing Additional Charges on Inmates in Cook County Jail
Issue Date:26 August 2002Effective Date:27 August 2002
Rescinds:S02-14
Index Category:Processing Persons
I.Purpose
This directive describes procedures for processing an inmate in Cook County Jail who will be charged with a separate crime, either by the filing of a new criminal complaint or the serving of a criminal warrant, and:
  • A.the inmate will not be taken out of the custody of the Cook County Department of Corrections (CCDOC).
  • B.the investigation has been completed.
  • C.new felony charges will be placed, when those charges have been approved by an Assistant States Attorney (ASA) from Felony Review, as outlined in the Department directive entitled "Felony Review by Cook County State's Attorney."
II.Policy
Only authorized Detective Division personnel and Traffic Specialists assigned to the Bureau of Patrol will be allowed to obtain an additional charge for inmates in the custody of the Cook County Department of Corrections.
III.Procedures
  • A.When it is determined that an inmate in the custody of the Cook County Department of Corrections will be charged with another crime, the investigating Department member seeking to add the charge will:
    • 1.prepare an Arrest Report (CPD-11.420) and any other related paperwork necessary to complete the booking process, indicating in the narrative portion of the Arrest Report that the arrestee is in the custody of the CCDOC.
    • 2.present the Arrest Report to the on-duty 10th District watch commander for the initial approval of probable cause.
    • 3.prepare a Defendant Hold Notification form (CPD-23.186).
    • 4.contact the Cook County Department of Corrections Special Operations Response Team (SORT) to schedule a time to fingerprint and photograph the inmate to be charged.
    • 5.request an evidence technician to meet at the scheduled time for fingerprinting and photographs.
    • 6.inform Cook County Department of Corrections SORT personnel of the additional charge and present them with the Defendant Hold Notification form and a copy of the Arrest Report.
    • 7.receive the completed fingerprint card from the evidence technician and hand-carry the fingerprint cards to the Field Services Section.
    • 8.notify the 10th District desk sergeant that the prisoner has been fingerprinted, and the prints have been taken to the Field Services Section.
  • B.The responding evidence technician will:
    • 1.meet the requesting investigating Department member at the pre-arranged time at Cook County Jail.
    • 2.fingerprint and photograph the inmate.
    • 3.turn the completed fingerprint card over to the investigating Department member.
  • C.The on-duty 10th District watch commander will:
    • 1.review the Arrest Report, and if appropriate, approve the "Initial Approval of Probable Cause" and ensure that the narrative contains sufficient facts to substantiate the charges.
    • 2.ensure that a Central Booking (CB) number is obtained.
    • 3.notify the Field Services Section to expedite the processing of the fingerprints once they are received.
    • 4.indicate final approval of the charges on the Arrest Report when the arrestee is formally charged (booking process completed).
  • D.The on-duty 10th District desk sergeant will ensure that all court-related documents, including the Criminal History Sheet, are processed expeditiously.
  • E.The 10th District 2nd watch desk sergeant will ensure that the court-related documents are hand-carried to the next Central Bond Court call.
    NOTE:
    Members preparing the Court Complaint Transmittal Listing (CPD-11.551) will record on the transmittal that the arrestee is in the custody of the CCDOC.
